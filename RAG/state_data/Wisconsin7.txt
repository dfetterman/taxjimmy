State: Wisconsin



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Wisconsin exempts from sales and use tax the sale, storage, use, or other consumption of food or food ingredients, except candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, and prepared food. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Nat'l Amusement Co. v. Wisconsin Dept. of Rev., 41 Wis. 2d 261, 163 N.W.2d 625 (Wis. 1969); Wisconsin Publication 220. BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.1.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined “Food and food ingredient” is defined as a substance in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that is sold for ingestion, or for chewing, by humans and that is ingested or chewed for its taste or nutritional value. This does not include alcoholic beverages or tobacco. Wis. Stat. §77.51(3t). Wisconsin incorporates the definition of food and food ingredients as found in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Wis. Stat. §77.51(3t); Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – Jan. 27, 2010. BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Grocery Items

Exempt Grocery items are generally tax-exempt, with the exception of candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, and prepared food. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Natl. Amusement Co. v. Wisconsin Dept. of Rev., 41 Wis. 2d 261, 163 N.W.2d 625 (Wis. 1969); Wisconsin Publication 220; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). If a combination of taxable and nontaxable products that are distinct and identifiable, one or more of which is an exempt food or food ingredient, are packaged together and:• more than 50 percent of the retailer's purchase price or sales price as determined by the seller (but not a combination of both) of the products in the package are attributable to products that are subject to Wisconsin sales or use tax, the total sales price of the package is subject to Wisconsin sales or use tax;but • if 50 percent or less of the retailer's purchase price or sales price as determined by the seller(but not a combination of both) is attributable to products that are subject to Wisconsin sales or use tax, the total sales price of the package is exempt from Wisconsin sales or use tax. Wisconsin Publication 220. For a detailed list of taxable and exempt sales by grocers, see Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.51(2)(a) and Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.51(2)(b). BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Candy

Taxable Candy is specifically excluded from tax-exempt food in Wisconsin, and is therefore subject to sales tax. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). “Candy” is defined as a preparation of sugar, honey, or other natural or artificial sweetener combined with chocolate, fruit, nuts, or other ingredients or flavorings in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. ”Candy”does not include a preparation that contains flour or that requires refrigeration. Wis. Stat. §77.51(1fm); Wisconsin Publication 220. However, candy is exempt from taxation under the following circumstances:• when sold by hospitals, sanatoriums, nursing homes, retirement homes, community-based residential facilities, or day care centers; Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(b); Wisconsin Publication 248. • when sold by a public or private institution of higher education to a student or a national football league team; Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(c). or • when furnished for no consideration by a restaurant to the restaurant's employee during the employee's work hours. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20r). BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Dietary Supplements and Vitamins

Taxable Dietary supplements are specifically excluded from tax-exempt food in Wisconsin, and are therefore subject to sales tax. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). A “dietary supplement”is a product, other than tobacco, that is intended to supplement a person's diet, that:• contains any of the following ingredients: Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a); Wisconsin Publication 220. ○ a vitamin; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a)(1). ○ a mineral; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a)(2). ○ an herb or other botanical; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a)(3). ○ an amino acid; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a)(4). ○ a dietary substance intended for human consumption to supplement the diet by increasing total dietary intake; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a)(5). or ○ a concentrate, metabolite, constituent, or extract; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(a)(6). • is intended for ingestion in tablet, capsule, powder, soft-gel, gel-cap, or liquid form, or, if not intended for ingestion in such forms, is not represented as conventional food and is not represented for use as the sole item of a meal or diet; Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(b). and • is required to be labeled as a dietary supplement as required under 21 CFR 101.36. Wis. Stat. §77.51(3n)(c). However, dietary supplements are exempt from taxation under the following circumstances:• when sold by hospitals, sanatoriums, nursing homes, retirement homes, community-based residential facilities, or day care centers; Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(b); Wisconsin Publication 248. • when sold by a public or private institution of higher education to a student or a national football league team; Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(c). or • when furnished for no consideration by a restaurant to the restaurant's employee during the employee's work hours. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20r). BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.3.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Alcoholic Beverages

Taxable Alcoholic beverages are excluded from the definition of food and are therefore taxable in Wisconsin. Wis. Stat. §77.51(3t); Wis. Stat. §77.52(1)(a); Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.51(2)(a); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). “Alcoholic beverages”are beverages suitable for human consumption that contain 0.5 percent or more of alcohol by volume. Wis. Stat. §77.51(1b); Wisconsin Publication 220. Sales of “cooking wine” are exempt from Wisconsin sales and use tax, however. Wisconsin Publication 220. Any federal excise tax on the sale of alcohol is included in the taxable price of an alcoholic beverage. Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.26(2)(a). BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.4.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Non-Alcoholic Beverages

Depends Unsweetened, non-alcoholic beverages are tax-exempt. Wis. Stat. §77.51(17w); Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Wisconsin Publication 220. Additionally, non-alcoholic beverages that contain milk, milk products, milk substitutes (soy milk, etc.), or more than 50 percent fruit or vegetable juice by volume are exempt, whether or not such beverages contain sweeteners. Wis. Stat. §77.51(17w); Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Wisconsin Publication 220. However, naturally or artificially sweetened beverages that contain 50 percent or less of fruit or vegetable juice or that do not contain milk or milk substitutes are taxable soft drinks. Wis. Stat. §77.51(17w); Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Wisconsin Publication 220. BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.5.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Soft Drinks

Taxable Soft drinks are specifically excluded from tax-exempt food in Wisconsin, and are therefore subject to sales tax. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). A “soft drink”is a beverage that is less than 0.5 percent alcohol in content and that contains natural or artificial sweeteners. It does not include a beverage that contains milk or milk products; soy, rice or similar milk substitutes; or more than 50 percent vegetable or fruit juice by volume. Wis. Stat. §77.51(17w); Wisconsin Publication 220. Soft drinks are exempt from taxation under the following circumstances:• when sold by a public or private institution of higher education to a student or a national football league team; Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(c). and • when furnished for no consideration by a restaurant to the restaurant's employee during the employee's work hours. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20r). For information on excise taxes on soft drinks in Wisconsin, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Wisconsin 14.1. BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Ice

Depends Ice sold by a food retailer in cubed or crushed form to be ingested for taste are sales of tax exempt food. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.80(3); Wisconsin Publication 220. Ice, including dry ice, that is sold for refrigeration is taxable. Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.80(1); Wisconsin Publication 220. Ice can be purchased as a nontaxable sale for resale when purchased by a restaurant or similar establishment to be used in drinks. Ice purchased by the same establishment to cool bottled drinks or to preserve food is taxable. Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.80(1). Ice is also nontaxable when sold to manufacturers, producers or food processors for use inside the shipping cases of merchandise being transferred to a customer, and sales to manufacturers which is consumed or destroyed in the manufacture of tangible personal property in any form destined for sale. Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.80(3). If ice is sold to a person who will use it both for a taxable and nontaxable purpose the total charge shall be divided between taxable and nontaxable use. If the ice is not divided between taxable and nontaxable, then sales tax applies to the total sale. Wis. Admin. Code Tax § 11.80(4)(a). BNA-SUTN WI 11.1.3.12.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

Taxable The sale, storage, use, or other consumption of prepared food is taxable in Wisconsin. Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m); Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0); Wisconsin Publication 220. BNA-SUTN WI 11.2.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined “Prepared food” is defined to include food and ingredients that are sold in a heated state; heated by the retailer; sold with eating utensils that are provided by the retailer; or two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by a retailer for sale as a single item. Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(a). Notwithstanding the above definition, “prepared food” does not include the following:• two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by a retailer for sale as a single item, if the retailer's primary classification in the North American Industry Classification System, 2002 edition, is manufacturing under subsector 311, not including bakeries and tortilla manufacturing under industry group number 3118; Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(b)(1). • two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by a retailer for sale as a single item, sold unheated, and sold by volume or weight; Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(b)(2). • bakery items made by a retailer, including breads, rolls, pastries, buns, biscuits, bagels, croissants, donuts, Danishes, cakes, tortes, pies, tarts, muffins, bars, cookies, and tortillas; Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(b)(3). • food and food ingredients that are only sliced, repackaged, or pasteurized by a retailer; Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(b)(4). or • eggs, fish, meat, or poultry, or foods containing raw eggs, fish, meat, or poultry that require cooking by the consumer, as recommended by the Food and Drug Administration. Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(b)(5). Additionally, effective Sept. 23, 2017, prepared food that meets all of the following conditions is exempt from tax:• the prepared food is not candy, soft drinks, or dietary supplements; • the retailer manufactures the prepared food in a building assessed as manufacturing property according to Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(b)(5); • the retailer makes no retail sales of prepared food at the building described above; and • either the retailer freezes the prepared food prior to its sale and sells the prepared food at retail in a frozen state, and the prepared food is not sold with eating utensils that are provided by the retailer or the prepared food consists of more than 50 percent yogurt. Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(d), as added by 2017 Wis. A.B. 64, effective Sept. 23, 2017. BNA-SUTN WI 11.2.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Prepared Food Items Sold With Eating Utensils Provided by Seller

Taxable Prepared food items sold with eating utensils provided by the seller are subject to tax. Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(a)(3); Wis. Stat. §77.54(20n)(a); Wisconsin Publication 220; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). “Eating utensils”include plates, bowls, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(a)(3). A retailer is deemed to have “provided” eating utensils if:• the utensils are available to purchasers and the retailer's sales of prepared food and food for which plates, bowls, glasses, or cups are necessary to receive the food account for more than 75 percent or the retailer's total sales of food and food ingredients; Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(a)(3)(a). or • it is the retailer's customary practice to physically give or hand the utensils to the customer, except that plates, bowls, glasses, or cups need only to be available to the customer if such items are necessary to receive the food. Wis. Stat. §77.51(10m)(a)(3)(b). BNA-SUTN WI 11.2.5.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Restaurant Equipment

Depends Generally, purchases of equipment by restaurants are subject to sales or use tax. Wisconsin Publication 220. Bakeries, however, are considered manufacturers for Wisconsin sales and use tax purposes and, therefore, may purchase certain machinery and equipment without Wisconsin sales and use tax. Wisconsin Publication 220. The machinery and equipment must be used exclusively and directly in the manufacturing process to qualify for the exemption. Examples of items which may be purchased by a bakery without Wisconsin sales and use tax if used exclusively and directly in manufacturing include:• bread pans, pie pans, and other pans for baking; • bread slicers, cookie cutters, and glazing screens; • scales used to weigh dough; • rolling pins, scoops, measuring cups, and can openers; • ovens, mixers, gas fryers, and donut machines; • thermometers used to check the temperature of dough; • wearing apparel used by employees to prevent contamination of the manufactured product; and • cleansers and solvents used for cleaning manufacturing machinery. Wisconsin Publication 220. Ingredients used to produce baked goods which are sold may be purchased without paying sales and use tax. If the ingredients qualify for the exemption for food and food ingredients for human consumption, the bakery does not need to provide the supplier with an exemption certificate. Wisconsin Publication 220. BNA-SUTN WI 11.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Depends Purchases by a caterer, restaurant, or other eating establishments, of certain food and food ingredients (e.g., potato chips, meats, soup mixes) and beverages (e.g., milk) are exempt from sales and use tax because the items qualify as exempt food and food ingredients for human consumption. Wisconsin Publication 220; Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – May 10, 2016, Catering Services Are Taxable. The purchaser is not required to provide an exemption certificate for such purchases. Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 192 - Articles; Wisconsin Sales and Use Tax Report 4-15; Wisconsin Publication 220; Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – May 10, 2016, Catering Services Are Taxable. Purchases by a restaurant or caterer of certain food and food ingredients and beverages for resale may be exempt, provided the restaurant or caterer provides the supplier with a fully completed resale exemption certificate. Examples of items a restaurant or caterer may purchase for resale include, candy, potato salad that is prepared food, soft drinks, and beer. Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 192 - Articles; Wisconsin Sales and Use Tax Report 4-15; Wisconsin Publication 220; Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – May 10, 2016, Catering Services Are Taxable. Similarly to restaurants, caterers may purchase certain packaging materials used to transfer merchandise to a customer exempt from sales and use tax. Additionally, packaging and shipping materials used by a caterer in packing, packaging, or shipping meat or meat products are exempt regardless of whether the items are used to transfer merchandise to customers. Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 192 - Articles; Wisconsin Sales and Use Tax Report 4-15; Wisconsin Publication 220; Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – May 10, 2016, Catering Services Are Taxable. Purchases by a caterer of disposable items (e.g., tablecloths, napkins, straws) provided to customers with the required purchase of taxable products (i.e., alcoholic beverages, prepared food) are not subject to Wisconsin sales or use tax. The caterer is reselling the disposable items, even if the caterer does not separately charge the customer for the disposable items. To claim the resale exemption, the caterer must provide the supplier with a fully completed exemption certificate. Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 192 - Articles; Wisconsin Sales and Use Tax Report 4-15; Wisconsin Publication 220; Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – May 10, 2016, Catering Services Are Taxable. However, purchases of non-disposable items (e.g., silverware, tablecloths, pots) used by a caterer to prepare, transport, and serve meals are subject to Wisconsin sales and use tax. Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 192 - Articles; Wisconsin Sales and Use Tax Report 4-15; Wisconsin Publication 220; Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – May 10, 2016, Catering Services Are Taxable. BNA-SUTN WI 11.10.
