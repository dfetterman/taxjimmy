Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Depends Biodiesel that is used on the highway is subject to a special fuel excise tax and is exempt from sales and use tax. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(g); Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64E, §4. However, if biodiesel is not subject to a special fuel excise tax, it will be subject to sales and use tax. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(g); Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64E, §4. For more information on Massachusetts' special fuels excise tax, see Excise Tax Navigator at Massachusetts 8.2. BNA-SUTN MA 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends Sales of electricity are taxable, subject to a few exemptions. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §1, “tangible personal property”; Massachusetts Administrative Decision C277094 (Jan. 17, 2007) (a purchaser of electricity may not appeal a sales tax assessment because the incidence of tax falls upon the vendor). However, the commonwealth exempts from sales and use tax the sales, furnishing, or service of:• water; Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(i)(1). • gas, steam, or electricity used for residential purposes; Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(i)(2). • gas, steam, or electricity that is consumed and used directly and exclusively in an industrial plant in the actual manufacture of tangible personal property to be sold or in the heating of such industrial plant. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(i)(3). The exemption for gas, steam or electricity that is consumed for the purpose of manufacturing or heating is only allowed if at least 75 percent of the gas, steam or electricity at the industrial plant is used for those purposes. The term “industrial plant” means a factory at a fixed location primarily engaged in the manufacture, conversion, or processing of tangible personal property to be sold in the regular course of business. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(i). Massachusetts also offers an exemption for electricity purchased for use by a small business that has 5 or fewer employees and that had gross income of less that$1 million for the preceding calendar year, and that reasonably expects gross income of less that $1 million for the current calendar year. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(qq); Mass Regs. Code tit. 830, § 64H.6.11(3)(a). Qualifying small businesses must register for a Small Business Exemption certificate through MassTaxConnect. Mass Regs. Code tit. 830, § 64H.6.11(3)(a)(4); see Massachusetts Dept. of Rev., New Rules for Small Business Energy Exemption (last visited March 26, 2019). A vendor will be relieved of the burden of proving that purchases made by a qualifying small business are exempt and will be presumed to have acted in good faith if it takes a copy of the small business's current certificate or by verifying the small business's current Small Business Exemption registration. Mass Regs. Code tit. 830, § 64H.6.11(3)(b)(1). BNA-SUTN MA 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Depends Sales of equipment directly relating to any solar, wind powered, or heat pump system, which is being utilized as a primary or auxiliary power system for the purpose of heating or otherwise supplying the energy needs of an individual's principal residence in the commonwealth, are exempt. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(dd). Solar Energy Massachusetts has a sales and use tax exemption for the sale of solar systems. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(dd). Sales of equipment directly relating to any solar system which is being used as a primary or auxiliary power system for the purpose of heating or supplying the energy needs of an individual's principal residence in Massachusetts is exempt from sales and use tax. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(dd). Leases of such systems also qualify for the exemption. Massachusetts Letter Ruling 84-88 (Sep. 25, 1984). Further, sales of fans and duct work that are components of a solar heating system for use in a principal residence in Massachusetts are also exempt from the sales and use tax. Massachusetts Directive 86-2. However, structural components of a residence such as glass used in a solar sunspace are taxable. Massachusetts Directive 86-2; Massachusetts Letter Ruling 83-11. Also, solar water heating units qualify for the exemption, but traditional water heaters and tanks do not qualify for the exemption. Massachusetts Letter Ruling 81-36; Massachusetts Letter Ruling 82-87 Wind Energy Massachusetts has a sales and use tax exemption for the sale of windpowered systems. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(dd). Sales of equipment directly relating to any windpowered systems which is being used as a primary or auxiliary power system for the purpose of heating or supplying the energy needs of an individual's principal residence in Massachusetts is exempt from sales and use tax. Mass. Gen. L. ch. 64H, §6(dd). BNA-SUTN MA 30.5.
