State: South Dakota



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

11.1.1.Taxable South Dakota imposes sales tax on sales of food and food products, as those are sales of tangible personal property. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-2. BNA-SUTN SD 11.1.1.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined South Dakota defines “food” as any substance, whether in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that is sold for ingestion or chewing by humans and is consumed for its taste or nutritional value. Food does not include alcoholic beverages, tobacco, or prepared food. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-1(5). South Dakota incorporates the definition of food and food ingredients as found in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. § 10-45-1. The definition of prepared food was amended effective July 1, 2005, to comport with the Agreement. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. § 10-45-1(8), as amended by 2005 S.D. H.B. 1043, enacted Feb. 9, 2005. BNA-SUTN SD 11.1.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Candy

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales tax on sales of grocery items, as those are sales of tangible personal property. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-2. “Candy” means any preparation of sugar, honey, or other natural or artificial sweeteners in combination with chocolate, fruits, nuts or other ingredients or flavorings in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. The term candy does not include any preparation containing flour and does not require refrigeration. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. § 10-45-1(3). BNA-SUTN SD 11.1.3.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Alcoholic Beverages

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales tax on the gross receipts from all sales at retail of alcoholic beverages, as defined by S.D. Codified Laws Ann. § 35-1-1(4). S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:04; see also South Dakota Dept. of Rev., Wine Direct Shipping. Sales tax is imposed regardless of whether the alcoholic beverages are mixed or unmixed at the time of sale or thereafter and whether sold for consumption on the premises or through off-sale outlets for consumption off the premises. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:04; see also South Dakota Dept. of Rev., Wine Direct Shipping. BNA-SUTN SD 11.1.3.4.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Ice

Nontaxable South Dakota does not impose sales and use tax on ice that becomes an ingredient part of food or drink to be sold at retail. Ice not sold for food and drink purposes is taxable. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:20. Sales of ice include sales for household use as well as for refrigeration purposes. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:20. BNA-SUTN SD 11.1.3.12.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Coffee

Taxable Coffee is subject to South Dakota's sales and use tax as tangible personal property. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-2. Coffee that meets the definition of prepared food is also subject to tax. The prepared food, such as coffee, is taxable whether purchased for consumption on-premises or as take-out. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:26; South Dakota Tax Facts—Prepared Food; South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, South Dakota (version 2018.0). Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales tax on meals furnished by restaurants, hotels, and boarding houses, including when the meals are furnished to an employee. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:26; South Dakota Tax Facts—Prepared Food; South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, South Dakota (version 2018.0). Other businesses that must collect tax on sales of prepared food include casinos, bars, drugstores, drive-in restaurants, lunch counters, caterers, cafeterias, snack bars, sandwich shops, cafés, and similar places of business. South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars. The prepared food is taxable whether purchased for on-premise consumption or as take-out. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:26; South Dakota Tax Facts—Prepared Food; South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, South Dakota (version 2018.0). Restaurants and bars must also collect tax on all sales of soft drinks and alcoholic beverages. South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars. BNA-SUTN SD 11.2.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined Prepared food” includes any food sold in a heated state or heated by the seller; two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by the seller for sale as a single item; or food sold with eating utensils provided by the seller, including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-1(8); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, South Dakota (version 2018.0). Prepared food does not include:• food that is only cut, repackaged or pasteurized by the seller and eggs, fish, meat, poultry; and • foods containing these raw animal foods requiring cooking by the consumer to prevent food-borne illnesses. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-1(8); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, South Dakota (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN SD 11.2.2.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Restaurant' or 'Eating Establishment'

Not Defined An “eating establishment” is a business that sells prepared food for immediate consumption that the public can purchase, eat there, or take with them. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-1; South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars. Eating establishments may be mobile or immobile and may or may not provide seating for their customers. South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars. Examples of eating establishments are bagel shops, bars, cafeterias, caterers, coffee or donut shops, concession stands at a fair or a mall, convenience shops, delis, diners, drive-ins, fast food restaurants, hot dog carts, lunch counters, night clubs, pizzerias, private and social clubs, refreshment stands, restaurants, sandwich shops, sidewalk vendors, snack bars, taverns, yogurt or ice cream stores/stands, or other places where food is served and is meant to be eaten soon after it is served. South Dakota Tax Facts—Restaurants and Bars. BNA-SUTN SD 11.2.3.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Immediate Consumption'

Not Defined South Dakota defines the term “immediate consumption” to include any prepared food that can be consumed immediately and is generally meant to be eaten soon after it is purchased. South Dakota Tax Facts—Prepared Food. BNA-SUTN SD 11.2.4.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Prepared Food Items Served at Nursing Homes and Other Long-term Care Facilities

Taxable Prepared food items served at nursing homes and other long-term care facilities are taxable, unless another exemption applies. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-14.6; S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:72. Generally, sales tax on food furnished to residents of retirement and nursing homes must be computed on a base of $9.66 per resident per day. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:59; S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:72. Sales of meals to patients and residents are exempt from tax if the meals are paid for by law or by contract, by the U.S., South Dakota, or a political subdivision. Specifically, meals provided to Medicare, Medicaid, CHAMPUS, Indian health service, or county poor relief patients are exempt. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-14.6. The sale of food to visitors, employees, and others is subject to sales tax. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:72. BNA-SUTN SD 11.2.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on food sold via a vending machine. South Dakota Department of Revenue Newsletter - January 2015. If another company owns or services the machine, that company may be responsible for the tax depending on the contract. Any commission received for the vending machine is not subject to sales tax. If a company owns and services the machine, or if it is responsible for reporting the tax under the contract, then the tax can be backed out of the total vending sales by dividing the gross sales by 1 + the applicable tax rate. Items to be sold in a vending machine can be purchased exempt from tax using an exemption certificate. South Dakota Department of Revenue Newsletter - January 2015. BNA-SUTN SD 11.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Depends Railway and Airline Meals Sales of meals and alcoholic beverages on railway trains or airplanes being operated through South Dakota are taxable if the meal is ordered or delivered within the boundary lines of South Dakota. It is immaterial whether or not the meal is consumed within South Dakota. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:37. BNA-SUTN SD 11.10.
