State: Mississippi



Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Taxable Mississippi imposes sales tax on retail sales of aircraft at a reduced rate of 3 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(1)(d); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(3). Retail sales of private carriers of passengers and light carriers of property are subject to an additional sales tax at the rate of 2 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(2). Sales of equipment used or designed to assist disabled persons, such as wheelchair equipment and lifts for mounting on, or attachment to, or installation on private carriers of passengers or light carriers of property, are also subject to an additional sales tax at the rate of 2 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(1)(k); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(2). However, sales of aircraft used predominantly to transport passengers and property either to or from offshore oil or natural gas exploration or production platforms or vessels are exempt from sales tax, as are sales of engines, accessories, and spare parts associated with such aircraft. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-101(1)(c), as amended by 2014 Miss. H.B. 1555, effective July 1, 2014. All sales of aircraft by Mississippi dealers are presumed to have been made in Mississippi unless the dealer can provide factual evidence proving otherwise. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.03.501. Aircraft Leases and Rentals For information about rentals generally in Mississippi, see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Mississippi 7.11. Income from renting or leasing tangible personal property, such as an aircraft, is taxed as the same rates as sales of such property. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.03.301. An aircraft is not rented when an instructor accompanies a student for instructional purposes. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.03.301. Aircraft Withdrawn from Inventory No tax is due on the cost of aircraft withdrawn from inventory by dealers for lease or rental. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.03.303. The cost of aircraft withdrawn for demonstration purposes, that otherwise remains in the dealer's inventory is exempt from tax. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.03.201. However, non-sales-related use, whether personal or for a charter in which a taxpayer only recoups its operating costs, is subject to tax. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-5; Mississippi. Dept. of Rev. v. Johnny Reb Aviation, LLC, 2014 BL 286957 (Miss. Ct. App. 2014). Temporary Presence in State Mississippi exempts from use tax the use, storage, or consumption of jet aircraft engines temporarily located within Mississippi and brought into the state for research and/or testing purposes at a jet aircraft engine research and testing facility. Miss. Code Ann. §27-67-7(o). “Aircraft” Defined Aircraft means any contrivance now known, or hereafter invented, used or designed for navigation of or flight in the air or airspace, manufactured by mass production or individually constructed or assembled, that are subject to registration with the Federal Aviation Administration. Miss. Code Ann. §61-15-3. Property and Passenger Transport Parts Used to Repair Aircraft Operating in Foreign or Interstate Transportation Mississippi provides a tax exemption covering sales of parts used in repairing and servicing aircraft not registered in Mississippi, but engaged exclusively in the business of foreign or interstate transportation to businesses engaged in aircraft repair and maintenance. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-101(1)(ee). Aircraft Used for Offshore Oil-Related Transportation Sales of aircraft engines, accessories, and spare parts for aircraft used predominantly to transport passengers and property either to or from offshore oil or natural gas exploration or production platforms or vessels are exempt from sales tax, as are sales of engines, accessories, and spare parts associated with such aircraft. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-101(1)(c), as amended by 2014 Miss. H.B. 1555, effective July 1, 2014. Special Rates, Excise Taxes For more information on any applicable excise taxes, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Mississippi 9. BNA-SUTN MS 29.1.1.

Taxable Mississippi imposes tax on the retail sales of repair parts for aircraft at the regular retail rate. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.03.102. Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Reduced rate Retail sales of automobiles, trucks, truck-tractors, semitrailers, and manufactured or mobile homes are subject to the state sales tax at the rate of 3 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(1)(d). Retail sales of private carriers of passengers and light carriers of property are subject to an additional sales tax at the rate of 2 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(2). Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.101 defines “automobile, truck, and truck-tractor” to include motor homes that are self-propelled, but excludes mobile homes, campers, trailers, semi-trailers, motorcycles, warehouse trucks, draglines, golf carts and similar vehicles. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.101. Sales of equipment used or designed to assist disabled persons, such as wheelchair equipment and lifts for mounting on or attachment to, or installation on private carriers of passengers or light carriers of property, are also subject to an additional sales tax at the rate of 2 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(1)(k); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(2). Sales price or purchase price for tax purposes means the full amount received from the sale of property, including delivery charges, manufacturers excise tax and any other additions to the selling price, unless specifically excluded by statute. The Federal Retailers Excise Tax on truck chassis and bodies and on truck-trailer and semi-trailer chassis and bodies is exempt from sales tax. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.103. Effective July 1, 2016, sales tax is imposed at the reduced rate of 3 percent on income from the rental or lease of truck-tractors and semitrailers not used in interstate commerce and not registered under the International Registration Plan or any other similar reciprocity agreement. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.05.03. Automobile, truck, and truck-tractor dealers titling a vehicle for wholesaler will be required to report sales tax based on the NADA value of the vehicle, absent any valid sales invoice from the wholesaler. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.209. Rebates made directly by the manufacturer that are assigned to the dealer are taxable. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.602. Automobiles or Trucks Withdrawn from Inventory The withdrawal from inventory of property purchased by a seller for resale for any other use, is subject to tax as a retail sale. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-7. Moreover, no tax is imposed on for the withdrawal from inventory of autos or trucks by dealers who remove for lease or rental. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.801. Motor Vehicle Trade-ins When any motor vehicle is taken in trade as a credit or part payment on the sale of a motor vehicle, tax is due on the net difference, that is, the true value of the motor vehicle sold minus the credit for the motor vehicle taken in trade. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-201(4). A trade-in is limited to property of the same kind and character as that normally carried in inventory for sale. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.103. “Motor Vehicle” Defined Motor vehicle means every vehicle that is self-propelled, including trackless street or trolley cars. Miss. Code Ann. §27-19-3(a)(3). The term includes motor homes (self-propelled)but does not include mobile homes, campers, trailers, semi-trailers, motorcycles, warehouse trucks, drag lines, golf carts and similar vehicles. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.03.02.102. Sales Between Siblings For sales occurring on or after July 1, 2019, transfers of legal ownership of motor vehicles between siblings are exempt from sales tax, unless the transferor is a licensed dealer of motor vehicles and the transfer of the motor vehicle is made in the regular course of business. Prior to July 1, 2019, sales between siblings are taxable. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-201(7)(d), as amended by 2019 Miss. S.B. 2229, effective July 1, 2019. Towing and Wrecker Services Charges for towing and wrecker services are not subject to tax if they do not include otherwise taxable charges, for example, charges for repairs or storage. Separately invoicing charges for such services does not affect the taxability. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.04.01.104; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.400. Fuel Gross proceeds from the sale of motor fuel are exempt from sales tax. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-111(n). A sale of equipment mounted on a vehicle so that it can be transported from place to place to perform a special function while stationary is taxable at the regular retail rate of tax whether sold as a complete unit or separately. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.11.02.203. Special Rates, Excise Taxes For more information on any applicable excise taxes, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Mississippi 9. BNA-SUTN MS 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Short-term Motor Vehicle Rentals

Increased rate Mississippi imposes tax on the gross income from a short term rental of motor vehicles at a rate of 11 percent. Miss. Reg. §35.IV.05.03.401. Generally, a 5 percent tax is imposed on the gross income from the rental of a motor vehicle. In addition to the normal 5 percent tax, Mississippi imposes an additional 6 percent rental tax on gross income from short-term rentals (i.e., rentals for 30 continuous days or less). Therefore, the total tax on gross income from short-term motor vehicle rentals is 11 percent, while the total tax due on gross income from long-term motor vehicle rentals of 31 days or more is 5 percent. Miss. Reg. §35.IV.05.03.401. Income from rentals of “you-drive-it”or “you-haul-it” vehicles are taxable to the lessor, even though the lessee may use the property in multiple cities in Mississippi or in other states, and even though the charges may be collected by or with another person. Miss. Reg. §35.IV.05.03.403. The tax rate is determined by the gross vehicle weight:• Greater than 10,000 pounds:3 percent; • 10,000 pounds or less:5 percent plus a 6 percent rental tax. Miss. Reg. §35.IV.05.03.403. Motor fuel charges are not taxable rental income when separately stated from the charge or rental or lease. Miss. Reg. §35.IV.05.03.404. BNA-SUTN MS 29.3.7.
