Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Exempt Receipts from sales of biodiesel fuel are subject to New Mexico's specials fuels tax, and are therefore not subject to gross receipts or compensating use tax. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-26; N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-16A-2(Q); N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-16A-3; N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-13. New Mexico also provides a biodiesel blending tax credit, which may be applied against the taxpayer's gross receipts tax liability or compensating tax liability. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-79.2. “Biodiesel” means renewable, biodegradable, monoalkyl ester combustible liquid fuel that is derived from agricultural plant oils or animal fats and that meets American society for testing and materials D 6751 standard specification for biodiesel B100 blend stock for distillate fuels. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-79.2. For a detailed discussion on biodiesel blending facility credits, see Credits and Incentives Navigator, at New Mexico 5.2. BNA-SUTN NM 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Butane

Depends Butane and other liquefied petroleum gases used primarily to power motor vehicles are exempt from gross receipts and compensating use taxes, but are subject to tax as gasoline, special fuel, or alternative fuel. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-26. Receipts from the sales of fuel, specially prepared and sold for use in turboprop or jet type-engines, including butane, however, are subject to the gross receipts tax and are not taxable under New Mexico's Gasoline Tax Act. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.114.9. Further, receipts from the sale of butane used in the operation of a “production unit” are subject to gross receipts tax. Only the value of the oil, natural gas or liquid hydrocarbon, individually or any combination thereof, produced on a “production unit” and used as fuel on the same “production unit” is exempt from provisions of the gross receipts and compensating use tax. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.121.8. A “production unit” is a unit of property designated by the Department from which products of common ownership, such as oil, natural gas, or liquid hydrocarbon, are severed. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-31-2(B). For information concerning the alternative fuel tax imposed on special fuels in New Mexico, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at New Mexico 8.2. BNA-SUTN NM 30.1.2.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Heating Oil

Taxable New Mexico provides an exemption from gross receipts and compensating use taxes for petroleum products commonly used to propel motor vehicles. Heating oil is not used to propel motor vehicles; therefore heating oil is taxable. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-3(J); N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-26. No gross receipts tax or compensating tax applies to receipts from processor storing or using crude oil natural gas or liquid hydrocarbons, individually or any combination, when stored or used in New Mexico or by a person engaged in the business of refining oil, natural gas or liquid hydrocarbons who stores or uses the crude oil, natural gas or liquid hydrocarbons in the regular course of his refining business. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-34. BNA-SUTN NM 30.1.11.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Taxable Gross receipts derived from sale of utilities, including electricity, are subject to New Mexico's gross receipts tax. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-3(J); N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-3.5; N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-4; N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-5(A); N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-7. Receipts of a utility from installation charges are subject to the gross receipts tax. However, if the utility is operated by a government entity the receipts are exempt from the gross receipts tax. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.1.18(W)(1). The definition of tangible personal property includes electricity. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-3(J). Thus, electricity is subject to tax unless another exemption applies. Local Governments Receipts from the sale of gas or electricity by a utility owned or operated by a county, municipality, or other political subdivision of a state are not exempted from the gross receipts tax. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-13(B). Electricity Connection Receipts of a private electric utility from connection fees are subject to the gross receipts tax. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.1.18(W)(3). BNA-SUTN NM 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Depends Any person who sells nonfissionable natural resources other than for subsequent sale in the ordinary course of business or for use as an ingredient or component part of a manufactured product is subject to gross receipts or compensating use taxes in addition to being subject to the Resources Excise Tax Act (N.M. Stat. Ann. § 7-25-1) on such sales. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-25-8. Exempted from the gross receipts tax are receipts from the sale or processing of natural resources. The severance or processing of natural resources are subject to the taxes imposed by the Resources Excise Tax Act under N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-25-1. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-35. New Mexico also exempts from gross receipts and compensating tax chemicals or reagents sold to any mining, milling, or oil company for use in processing ores or oil in a mill, smelter, or refinery or in acidizing oil wells. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-65. Effective July 1, 2019, New Mexico only exempts sales of chemicals or reagents in lots in excess of eighteen tons that are sold to any hard-rock mining or milling company for use in any combination of extracting, leaching, milling, smelting, refining or processing ore at a mine site. Prior to July 1, 2019, sales of chemicals or reagents sold in lots in excess of eighteen tons are exempt regardless of the purchaser. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-65, as amended by 2019 N.M. S.B. 549, effective July 1, 2019. For the purposes of these exemptions, “chemical” means a substance used for producing a chemical reaction. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.223.7.2. See Peabody Coalsales Co. v. New Mexico Taxn. & Rev. Dept., No. A-1-CA-36632), 2019 BL 253846 (N.M. Ct. App. July 12, 2019) (denying a refund on tax paid for coal in excess of eighteen tons because coal is not a “chemical” for the purposes of the statute). Explosives, blasting powder, and dynamite are not eligible for these exemptions. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-65. BNA-SUTN NM 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Production

Depends Receipts from sales of equipment to the New Mexico Renewable Energy Transmission Authority are deductible from taxable gross receipts; other sales of energy production equipment are taxable. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-101. Electricity used in the production, conversion, and transmission of electricity, including transmission using voltage source conversion technology, is exempt from the compensating tax. N.M. Stat. Ann. § 7-9-38, as amended by 2012 N.M. H.B. 116, effective July 1, 2012; N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.126.8(A). Electricity used in the production, conversion, and transmission of electricity includes electricity used: • directly in the operation of an electricity generating plant in New Mexico, and • directly in the operation of transmission facilities of an electric utility in New Mexico. N.M. Stat. Ann. § 7-9-38, as amended by 2012 N.M. H.B. 116, effective July 1, 2012; N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.126.8(A). Electricity used in the operation of offices, warehouses, or any facility of an electric utility in New Mexico, other than directly in a generating plant or transmission facility, is not used in the production or transmission of electricity and the value is subject to the compensating tax. N.M. Stat. Ann. § 7-9-38, as amended by 2012 N.M. H.B. 116, effective July 1, 2012; N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.126.8(B). Receipts from selling equipment to the New Mexico renewable energy transmission authority or an agent or lessee of the authority may be deducted from gross receipts if the equipment is installed as part of an electric transmission facility or an interconnected storage facility acquired by the authority pursuant to the New Mexico Renewable Energy Transmission Authority Act. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-101. BNA-SUTN NM 30.4.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Depends Receipts from the sale of wind and solar energy generation equipment to a government for the purpose of installing a wind or solar electricity generation facility are deductible from taxable gross receipts. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-54.3(A). Other sales of alternative energy equipment are taxable. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-4. “Solar generation equipment” includes:• solar energy collection, concentration, heat transfer, and conversion equipment; • solar tracking hardware and software; • photovoltaic panels and inverters; • support structures; • turbines and associated electrical generating equipment; and • related equipment. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-54.3(C)(3). Wind generation equipment includes:• turbines; • blades; • nacelles; • rotors;and • supporting structures. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-54.3(C)(4). BNA-SUTN NM 30.5.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Wastewater Treatment

Taxable New Mexico does not provide any deductions or exemptions for equipment used in wastewater treatment activities; therefore such equipment is taxable. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-9-4. Biomass materials, including wastewater treatment gas and biosolids, may be deducted in computing the compensating tax due, when used for processing into biopower, biofuels or biobased products. N.M.
