State: Georgia



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Depends Generally, the sale of food and food ingredients for off-premises human consumption or use is exempt from state sales and use tax in Georgia. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(57)(A); Georgia Food SUT-2013-10-24-01 Food (Oct. 24, 2013); Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0). This exemption does not extend to food and food ingredients purchased by businesses or certain nonprofit organizations. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(57)(C); Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.115(3)(b)(4). Sales of food and food ingredients are not exempt from most local sales and use taxes. However, the exemption for food and food ingredients does apply to the imposition of the equalized homestead option sales tax under Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-109.1, et seq. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(57)(D)(i), as amended by 2017 Ga. S.B. 156, §1, effective May 8, 2017. Food and food ingredients that are subject to local tax but exempt from state tax include:• most prepackaged or repackaged items sold for ingestion by humans for taste or nutritional value such as prepackaged boxed cereal, cartons of milk, peanut butter ground in the store, prepackaged juice chewables and nutrition bars, deli meat sliced by the seller and sold by the pound, and deli salads purchased in bulk by the seller and sold by the pound; • raw animal foods such as eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these products that require cooking by the consumer to prevent food borne illness; • packaged beverages of all kinds including bottled water; • candy; • vending machine items; • liquids used in cooking with more than 0.5 percent alcohol that are not intended for drinking; and • dietary supplements. Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0); 2011 Ga. H.B. 168, effective April 27, 2011 (dietary supplements are explicitly included from the definition of “food and food ingredients”); see Georgia Letter Ruling SUT 2016-13 (June 6, 2016) (products that bear a “Supplemental Facts” label constitute dietary supplements, and are therefore subject to sales and use tax). BNA-SUTN GA 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined “Food and food ingredients” means substances, whether in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that are sold for ingestion or chewing by humans and are consumed for their taste and nutritional value. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(16). See also Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0). This definition does not include alcoholic beverages, dietary supplements, or tobacco. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(16). See also Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0); but see 2011 Ga. H.B. 168, effective April 27, 2011 (dietary supplements are explicitly excluded from the definition of “food and food ingredients”); see e.g. Georgia Letter Ruling SUT 2016-13 (June 6, 2016) (products bearing a “Nutritional Facts” label constitute food and food ingredients and are therefore exempt from sales and use tax;however, products bearing a “Supplemental Facts” label constitute dietary supplements, and are therefore subject to sales and use tax). Georgia incorporates the definition of food and food ingredients as found in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(16); Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018. BNA-SUTN GA 11.1.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

Taxable Prepared food is not included in the definition of “food and food ingredients”and is subject to both state and local tax. Ga. Prepared food is exempt from sales tax under the following circumstances:• food and food ingredients and prepared food sold and served to pupils and employees of public schools as part of a school lunch program; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(12), as amended by 2011 Ga. H.B. 168, §2, effective April 27, 2011. • sales of prepared food consumed by pupils and employees of bona fide private elementary and secondary schools approved by the commissioner; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(13). • through June 30, 2021, prepared food donated to an entity exempt from taxation under I.R.C. §501(c)(3) for hunger relief; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(57.2), as amended by 2009 Ga. H.B. 1221, effective Jan. 1, 2011; 2016 Ga. H.B. 763, effective July 1, 2016. • through June 30, 2020, prepared food donated following a natural disaster for disaster relief purposes; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(57.3), as amended by 2009 Ga. H.B. 1221, effective Jan. 1, 2011; 2015 Ga. H.B. 426, effective July 1, 2015. • food and food ingredients sold by the Girl Scouts or Boy Scouts for fund-raising purposes; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(59), as amended by 2009 Ga. H.B. 1221, effective Jan. 1, 2011. • food and food ingredients sold to an airline for service to crew and passengers and food and ingredients served to crew and passengers without charge whether in flight or on the ground; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(81), as amended by 2009 Ga. H.B. 1221, effective Jan. 1, 2011. For purposes of this exemption, “food and food ingredients”means substances, whether in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that are sold for ingestion or chewing by humans and are consumed for their taste or nutritional value. All beverages and tobacco are not exempt. Prior to amendment by 2009 Ga. H.B. 1221, nonalcoholic beverages were included in the airline exemption. and • meals served at fraternities, sororities, other student societies, with members residing at a common location and jointly sharing household expenses, including meals. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.65(2). Combination Meals When combination meals are sold containing food items taxable at different rates, the total price charged is subject to tax at the highest applicable rate unless the seller can identify the portion of the price attributable to items subject to tax at the lower rate. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.115(3)(d). BNA-SUTN GA 11.2.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined “Prepared food” means food: sold in a heated state or heated by the seller;with two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by the seller for sale as a single item; or sold with eating utensils provided by the seller, including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(27)(A); Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0). A plate does not include a container or packaging used to transport the food. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(27)(A); Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0). “Prepared food”does not include food:• that is only cut, repackaged, or pasteurized by the seller, and eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these raw animal foods requiring cooking by the consumer to prevent food borne illnesses; • sold by a food manufacturer with the primary NAICS code subsector 311, except for bakeries and tortilla manufacturing with NAICS industry group code 3118, if sold without eating utensils provided by the seller;or • sold by beverage manufacturers with the primary NAICS code 3121. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(27)(B); Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011). BNA-SUTN GA 11.2.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Complimentary Meals

Taxable Complimentary meals and beverages provided to employees without charge by an employer are taxable to the employer. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.115(4)(b). BNA-SUTN GA 11.6.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Mobile Food Vendors

Taxable Prepared food items sold by mobile food vendors are subject to sales tax. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(27)(A); Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN GA 11.7.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Restaurant Equipment

Taxable Machinery, equipment, and supplies used or consumed by the restaurant are not exempt from sales tax. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.115(6)(e)(1). Restaurants are statutorily excluded from being considered as manufacturers for purposes of manufacturing exemptions. Georgia Letter Ruling SUT-2018-06-Restaurants (July 26, 2018). BNA-SUTN GA 11.8.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Taxable Prepackage items sold from vending machine are only subject to local taxes. Georgia Informational Bulletin SUT-2010-12-23 (Jan. 1, 2011); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Georgia (version 2018.0). When tangible personal property is sold by means of a vending machine, the person owning the property contained in the machine is the retail seller and is liable for the tax on the total gross receipts therefrom without any deduction for commissions paid to the person on whose premises the machine is located. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.06. BNA-SUTN GA 11.9.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Exempt School lunches sold to students and employees of public schools are exempt. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(12). In addition, the food and drinks that become component parts of these lunches may be purchased under an exemption certificate. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.79(5). BNA-SUTN GA 11.10.
