State: Tennessee



Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Tangible Personal Property

Origin Through June 30, 2021, Tennessee uses origin-based sourcing rules for intrastate retail sales. See Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-702(a); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-2-.05(1); Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018; Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Effective July 1, 2021, Tennessee will implement destination-based sourcing rules for intrastate retail sales. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902, as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2009, as amended through 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). See also Tennessee Notice No. 15-09 (June 2015) (announcing the delay and identifying the delayed provisions). Under Tennessee's current origin-based sourcing rules, the location where the vendor receives the order for the good is the place of sale. The jurisdiction where the order is received will collect the tax revenue on the sale. Bloomberg Tax Survey of State Tax Departments. Under the destination sourcing provisions effective July 1, 2021, the rules are as follows for retail sales:• if a product is received by its purchaser at a business location of the seller, then Tennessee sources the sale to that business location; • if a product is not received by its purchaser at a business location of the seller, then Tennessee sources the sale to the location where receipt by the purchaser, or donee of the purchaser, occurs, including the location indicated by instructions for delivery to the purchaser or donee, known to the seller; • if the above two rules do not apply, then Tennessee sources the sale to the location indicated by the purchaser's address available from the business records of the seller that are maintained in the ordinary course of the seller's business, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith; • if the above three rules do not apply, then Tennessee sources the sale to the location indicated by the purchaser's address obtained during the consummation of the sale, including the address of a purchaser's payment instrument, if no other address is available, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith; and • if no other rules apply, including a circumstance in which a seller is without sufficient information to apply the previous rules, then Tennessee sources the sale using the address from which tangible personal property was shipped, from which the digital good or the computer software delivered electronically was first available for transmission by the seller, or from which the service was provided, disregarding for these purposes any location that merely provided the digital transfer of the product sold. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(a)(1) through Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(a)(5), as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Sourcing Remote Sales Sales made by out-of-state dealers are sourced to the location of the purchaser's receipt. Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. Prior to Oct. 1, 2019, dealers with no location in Tennessee have the option to collect a standard 2.25 percent local rate in lieu of the rate applied at the purchaser's location of receipt. However, this option is no longer available effective Oct. 1, 2019. Following this date, remote sellers must collect local sales and use tax using the rate applicable at the purchaser's location of receipt. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-702(f), as repealed by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 326, effective Oct. 1, 2019; Tennessee Notice No. 19-05 (June 2019). Effective Oct. 1, 2019, out-of-state dealers must also provide the department of revenue with a report of each incorporated municipality or unincorporated area of a county into which they ship or deliver a sale. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-504(m), as added by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 326, effective Oct. 1, 2019. For more information on reporting requirements, see Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Tennessee Section 3.2. Water Sales A seller of water must collect the local option tax at the local rate for the location where the water is delivered to the customer. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-702(a)(4) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §164, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021); Tennessee Notice No. 15-22 (Oct. 2015). Internet Sales Use tax is due on items purchased over the internet and delivered within Tennessee if Tennessee sales tax was not paid at the time of purchase. Tennessee Dept. of Rev., Are Sales Made on the Internet Subject to Sales or Use Tax? BNA-SUTN TN 4.1.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Services

Destination Under Tennessee's origin-based sourcing rules, the location where the vendor receives the order for the service is the place of sale. The jurisdiction where the order is received will collect the tax revenue on the sale. Bloomberg Tax Survey of State Tax Departments. Effective July 1, 2021, Tennessee will implement destination based sourcing for telecommunications services. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-905, as enacted by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2021. Under the destination sourcing provisions effective July 1, 2021, sales of telecommunications services sold on a call-by-call basis are sourced to each level of taxing jurisdiction where the call:• originates and terminates in that jurisdiction; or • either originates or terminates and in which the service address is also located. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-905(a)(1); Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-905(a)(2), as enacted by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2021. Likewise, a sale of ancillary services or telecommunications services sold on a basis other than a call-by-call basis, is sourced to the customer's place of primary use. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-905(b), as enacted by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2021. BNA-SUTN TN 4.1.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Leases and Rentals

Destination Under Tennessee's origin-based sourcing rules, the location where the vendor receives the order for the service is the place of sale. The jurisdiction where the order is received will collect the tax revenue on the sale. Bloomberg Tax Survey of State Tax Departments. Effective July 1, 2021, Tennessee implements destination based sourcing for leases and rentals of tangible personal property, motor vehicles, and transportation equipment. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(b) through Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(d), as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Under the destination sourcing provisions effective July 1, 2021, the rules are as follows for leases and rentals of tangible personal property other than motor vehicles or transportation equipment:• for a lease or rental that requires recurring periodic payments, the first periodic payment is sourced in the same manner as a retail sale. Periodic payments made after the first payment are sourced to the primary property location for each period covered by the payment. The primary property location is the location indicated by an address for the property provided by the lessee that is available to the lessor from its records maintained in the ordinary course of business, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith. The property location is not changed by intermittent use at different locations, such as use of business property that accompanies employees on business trips and service calls; and • for a lease or rental that does not require recurring periodic payments, the payment is sourced in the same manner as a retail sale. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(b)(1)(A) and Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(b)(1)(B), as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Under the destination sourcing provisions effective July 1, 2021, the rules are as follows for leases and rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semi-trailers, or aircraft that do not qualify as transportation equipment, and watercraft with a displacement of under 50 tons:• for a lease or rental that requires recurring periodic payments, each periodic payment is sourced to the primary property location. The primary property location is indicated by an address for the property provided by the lessee that is available to the lessor from its records maintained in the ordinary course of business, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith. This location is not altered by intermittent use at different locations; and • for a lease or rental that does not require recurring periodic payments, the payment is sourced the same as a retail sale. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(c)(1)(A) and Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(c)(1)(B), as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). The retail sale, including lease or rental, of transportation equipment is sourced the same as a retail sale. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-902(d)(1), as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §174, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). BNA-SUTN TN 4.1.3.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Local Sourcing

Depends A sale of a taxable item is sourced to the local tax jurisdiction in which the sale is consummated. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-2-.05(1). Dealers are liable for remitting local sales tax when sales are made, or delivery to a U.S. post office for delivery within the state is made, within the boundaries of a local tax jurisdiction. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-2-.05(1). Vendors located out-of-state are liable for local sales tax if they are subject to the jurisdiction of the state and they sell tangible personal property or services within the boundaries of a local tax jurisdiction. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-2-.05(2). Effective until July 1, 2021, dealers without presence in the state of Tennessee may opt to pay local tax at the rate of 2.25 percent of the sales price on all sales made in Tennessee in lieu of the local sales and use tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-702(f), as repealed by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 326, effective Oct. 1, 2019. BNA-SUTN TN 4.1.4.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Credit for Tax Paid to Another Jurisdiction

Yes Tennessee allows a reciprocal use tax credit up to the amount of any sales or use tax paid to another state. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-507(a); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-1-.91(1); Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Tennessee (version 2018.0). In addition to state taxes paid, Tennessee allows a taxpayer to take a credit against Tennessee state taxes due for local taxes paid as well as taxes paid to the District of Columbia. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-507(a); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-1-.91(1); Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Tennessee (version 2018.0); see 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). BNA-SUTN TN 4.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property First Used Out-of-state

Exempt Personal automobiles, personal manufactured homes, personal effects, or the household furnishings to be used in the residence of a person who was a bona fide resident of another state who moved to Tennessee who caused such property to be imported into Tennessee are exempt from use tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-210(b). BNA-SUTN TN 4.3.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Temporarily Stored In-state

Exempt Temporary storage pending shipping or mailing of tangible personal property to nonresidents of Tennessee shall not constitute a taxable use in Tennessee. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(88) (effective July 1, 2021, the definition for “storage” is located in Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(90)). BNA-SUTN TN 4.3.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Purchased for Use Out-of-state

Taxable If a nonresident of Tennessee purchases taxable tangible personal property or taxable services and the sale is delivered in Tennessee, the sale is taxable even if the property will be transported out of state. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 1320-5-1-.29(1). BNA-SUTN TN 4.3.3.
