State: Mississippi



Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Taxation of Property

Taxable Every person engaging or continuing to engage within the State of Mississippi in the business of selling tangible personal property is assessed a tax of 7 percent of the gross proceeds of the retail sales of the business. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(1)(a). A complementary use tax is imposed at the rate of 7 percent on the use, storage, or consumption of tangible personal property within Mississippi. Miss. Code Ann. §27-67-5. Tax also is imposed on the gross income of every person engaging or continuing in Mississippi in the business of selling, renting, or leasing specified digital products. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-26. More information on the taxation of property is available at 1300 T.M., Sales and Use Taxes: General Principles and 1360 T.M., Sales and Use Taxes: Retail Sales Issues. BNA-SUTN MS 9.1.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Taxation of Services

Taxable Sales of various services, including services performed in connection with the sale and installation of tangible personal property are subject to tax. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-23. Mississippi levies a 7 percent tax on individuals engaged in the following businesses:• air conditioning installation and repairs; • automobile, motorcycle, boat, or any other vehicle repairing or servicing; • billiards, pool, or domino parlors; • bowling or tenpin alleys; • burglar and fire alarm systems or services; • car washing, regardless of whether it is automatic, self-service, or manual; • computer software sales and services; • cotton compresses or cotton warehouses; • custom creosoting or treating, custom planning, and custom sewing; • custom meat processing; • electricians, electrical work, wiring, and all repairs or installation of electrical equipment; • elevator or escalator installing, repairing, or servicing; • film developing or photo finishing; • foundries, machine, or general repairing; • furniture repairing or upholstering; • grading, excavating, ditching, dredging, or landscaping; • hotels, motels, tourist courts or camps, and trailer parks; • insulating services or repairs; • jewelry or watch repairing; • laundering, cleaning, pressing, or dyeing; • marina services; • mattress renovating; • office and business machine repairing; • parking garages and lots; • plumbing and pipe fitting; • public storage warehouses; • refrigerating equipment repairs; • radio or television installing, repairing, or servicing; • renting or leasing personal property used within the state; • services performed in connection with geophysical surveying, exploring, developing, drilling, producing, distributing, or testing of oil, gas, water, and other mineral resources; • shoe repairing; • storage lockers; • telephone answering or paging services; • termite and pest control services - including treating termites or pests, the replacement of flooring, sills, or other damaged portions of the building, and annual or semiannual inspection fees; • tin and sheet metal shops; • TV cable systems, TV subscriptions services, and other similar activities; • vulcanizing, repairing, or recapping of tires and tubes; • welding;and • woodworking or wood turning shops. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-23. Sales of any enumerated, taxable services performed for electric power associations in the ordinary and necessary operation of their generating or distribution systems are not subject to tax at the normal 7 percent rate, but rather at a reduced rate of 1 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-23. Also, sales of enumerated, taxable services performed on materials for use in track or track structures to a railroad whose rates are fixed by the Interstate Commerce Commission of the Mississippi Public Service Commission are taxed at a reduced rate of 3 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-23. BNA-SUTN MS 9.2.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Mixed and Bundled Transactions, Application of the True Object Test Mixed Transactions and the True Object Test

Taxable When nontaxable services, such as design, engineering or other professional services, are related to the sale of taxable tangible personal property, charges for both the service and tangible personal property are subject to tax. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.05.07.100 - Miss. Regs. §35.IV.05.07.106. Mississippi does not otherwise specifically address the application of the true object test for mixed transactions. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.05.07.100. BNA-SUTN MS 9.3.1.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Mixed and Bundled Transactions, Application of the True Object Test Bundled Transactions

Taxable Bundled transactions involving the sale of tangible personal property are fully taxable. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(e)(vii)(1); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-3(h); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.02.209. A bundled transactions is defined as a transaction that comprises distinct and identifiable properties or services which are sold for a single nonitemized price but are treated differently for tax purposes. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(e)(vii)(1); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.02.121. Mississippi tax law defines “gross proceeds of sale” as the value proceeding or accruing from the full sale price of tangible personal property, including installation charges, carrying charges, or any other addition to the selling price on account of deferred payment by the purchaser, without any deduction for delivery charges, cost of property sold, other expenses or losses, or taxes of any kind except those that are expressly exempt. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-3(h). Telecommunications Services When a bundled transaction includes nontaxable and taxable services, the entire charge is subject to tax unless the vendor can reasonably identify the nontaxable portion from its books and records kept in the regular course of business. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(e)(vii)(2); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.02.209. For bundled transactions involving telecommunications services, the tax due from the sale of component parts must be reported separately by using the appropriate tax codes. The preferred method is using the actual breakdown of the charges. The price is allocated by identifying the portion of the price attributable to each service from its books and records kept in the regular course of business or based on a reasonable allocation methodology approved by the Department of Revenue. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(e)(vii)(3); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.02.1005. Meeting Rooms If the charge for a meeting room is bundled with other taxable items, tax applies to the total invoice amount, unless the meeting room charge is separately stated. Mississippi Dept. of Rev., Sales Tax Frequently Asked Questions. BNA-SUTN MS 9.3.2.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Local Jurisdictions, Taxability Distinctions

Conforms to State Mississippi municipalities are authorized to impose a special sales tax of not more than 1 percent on the same tax base that is taxed at the rate of 7 percent under the state's sales tax law, Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-1 et seq. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-241(2)(a). The state sales tax applies to sales of tangible personal property, specified digital products, and enumerated services. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17(1)(a); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-23; Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-26. BNA-SUTN MS 9.4.
