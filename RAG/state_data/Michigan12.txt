State: Michigan



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Michigan is a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Michigan (version 2018.0). As a full member, Michigan maintains a seat on the governing board and may vote on amendments and interpretations of the Agreement. Mich. Comp. Laws §205.171; Mich. Comp. Laws §205.801;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Michigan Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Michigan (version 2018.0). On June 28, 2004, Michigan enacted H.B. 5502 through 5505 to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, effective July 1, 2004. The streamlined provisions took effect September 1, 2004. All sellers registering under the Streamlined Sales Tax system are required to collect and remit taxes on sales into Michigan. Mich. Comp. Laws §205.171; Mich. Comp. Laws §205.801;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Michigan Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Michigan (version 2018.0);Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN MI 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Michigan is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Michigan Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Michigan was not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). On Oct. 1, 2005, Michigan became a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement. Mich. Comp. Laws §205.91; Mich. Comp. Laws §205.51;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Important Documents, Michigan; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Michigan (version 2018.0). As a member state, Michigan has changed its sales tax administration laws so they are fully compliant with the provisions of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Michigan Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). Michigan must also collect sales and use tax for all other member states. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Frequently Asked Questions. Sellers and certified service providers are relieved from tax liability to Michigan and its local jurisdictions for having charged and collected the incorrect amount of sales and use tax resulting from the seller or certified service provider relying on the Library of Definitions section of the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board Taxability Matrix, but not the Tax Administration Practices Section. Mich. Comp. Laws §205.819(9); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Michigan (version 2018.0); User Guide: Online Taxability Matrix and Certificate of Compliance. BNA-SUTN MI 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Nonmember By enacting 2014 Mich. S.B. 156, 2014 Mich. S.B. 156 on Sept. 10, 2014, Michigan retroactively repealed the Multistate Tax Compact effective January 1, 2008. 2014 Mich. S.B. 156, effective Sept. 10, 2014; Michigan Treasury Update - February 2016. Prior to Sept. 10, 2014, Michigan was a member of the Multistate Tax Compact. It had enacted the Compact's provisions into Michigan law. Mich. Comp. Laws §205.581(1) repealed. The purposes of the compact included:• facilitating proper determination of state and local tax liability; • equitably apportioning the tax bases and settling disputes; • promoting uniformity; • facilitating taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration; and • avoiding duplicative taxation. Mich. Comp. Laws §205.581(1) repealed. BNA-SUTN MI 2.2.
