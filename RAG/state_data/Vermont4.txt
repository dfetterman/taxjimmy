Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Depends Vermont exempts purchases and leases of motor vehicles from the state sales and use tax if the transaction was taxed under the motor vehicle purchase and use tax imposed under Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, ch. 219. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(12). The sale and purchase of a motor vehicle includes lease transactions, including types of leases and transactions whereby the possession of the property is transferred but the seller retains the title as security for the payment of the purchase price. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §8902(3). The motor vehicle purchase and use tax is imposed on Vermont residents on the purchase or use of a motor vehicle. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §8902(5). The purchase and use tax is imposed for the sale and use of motor vehicles at the rate of 6 percent for pleasure cars, motorcycles, motor homes, and other vehicles that weigh up to 10,999 pounds. For other motor vehicles there is a tax imposed at the lesser amount of 6 percent or $1,850. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §8903(a); Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §8903(b). The purchaser or lessor of a motor vehicle must file the purchase and use return at the time of sale or lease with the commissioner of Motor Vehicles and pay the applicable motor vehicle purchase and use tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §8904. For more information about Vermont's motor vehicle use tax, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Vermont 9.1. BNA-SUTN VT 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Repair and Replacement Parts

Taxable Motor vehicle repair and replacement parts that are resold to a customer as part of a repair service are subject to Vermont's sales and use tax as tangible personal property. Vt. Stat. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet GB-1161. The following automotive parts and supplies are typically considered resold to the customer. Automotive repair shops may purchase these items for resale without paying tax, but must collect sales tax from their customers on the final bill:• brake pads, rotors, shocks, and struts; • tires; • filters; • fan belts; • electronic sensors; • brake fluid; • headlights; • brake lights; • starters; • alternators; • windshields; • rear-view mirrors; • doors; • fenders; • antifreeze; • motor oil; and • automatic transmission fluid. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet GB-1161. Effective July 1, 2019, Vermont exempts from sales and use tax motor vehicle parts purchased by a registered dealer and used to recondition a used motor vehicle held by the dealer in its resale inventory. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32 §9741(12), as amended by, 2019 Vt. H. 514, effective July 1, 2019; Vermont Tax Fact Sheet GB-1161. This exemption does not apply to supplies. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet GB-1161. BNA-SUTN VT 29.3.2.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Short-term Motor Vehicle Rentals

Nontaxable Under Vermont law, special provisions are made for the short-term leases or rentals of motor vehicles. Vermont Dept. of Transp., Taxation of Motorboats. Alaska, Montana, Delaware, New Hampshire and Oregon do not have a sales and use tax. • the applicant for the Motorboat Registration is a non-resident of Vermont and purchased the vessel in a state other than Vermont (while a non-resident); Vermont Dept. of Transp., Taxation of Motorboats. • the applicant for the Motorboat Registration is a non-resident of Vermont and purchased the vessel in Vermont, but received title or possession(or both) in another state; Vermont Dept. of Transp., Taxation of Motorboats. • the applicant for the Motorboat Registration is a non-resident of Vermont and submits a copy of a valid registration from his/her home state; Vermont Dept. of Transp., Taxation of Motorboats. or • the vessel is/was acquired through a transfer to an individual (relative or not) for no consideration. Vermont Dept. of Transp., Taxation of Motorboats. If a person sells a motorboat or vessel and buys another vessel within three months, then the sales price on the new vehicle or vessel excludes the lesser of the first vessel's sale price Vt. Stat. Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Railroad Rolling Stock, Interstate and Common Carrier Oil, Filters, and Other Maintenance Items

Depends Service providers may generally purchase oil, filters, and like maintenance items exempt from tax under Vermont's resale exemption. Vermont Technical Bulletin TB-58 (Jan. 3, 2011). Amounts charged by a service provider applying these items to rolling stock will generally be taxable as sales of tangible personal property. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9771. BNA-SUTN VT 29.8.4.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Railroad Rolling Stock, Interstate and Common Carrier Fuel

Taxable Fuel used to propel railroad rolling stock is subject to Vermont's sales and use tax as tangible personal property. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9771. A tax of $0.03 per gallon is also imposed on each gallon of railroad fuel used in Vermont. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 23, §3003(c). Railroad fuel means diesel fuel and any blend of diesel and other fuel used or suitable for use for the generation of power to propel a railroad train. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 23, §3002(8). For more information about fuel taxes in Vermont see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Vermont 8.1. BNA-SUTN VT 29.8.6.
