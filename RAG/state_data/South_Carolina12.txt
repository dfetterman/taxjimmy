State: South Carolina



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Nonmember South Carolina is an advisory state to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project, but is neither a full member nor an associate member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through December 16, 2016. BNA-SUTN SC 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

Nonmember South Carolina has enacted the Simplified Sales and Use Tax Administration Act, which required the South Carolina Department of Revenue to enter into multistate discussions for the purpose of simplifying the sales and use tax system. S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-30; S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-40. Additionally, the Act was enacted for the purpose of and reducing, with a goal to eliminate, the burden and cost of compliance with the requirement to collect and remit South Carolina's sales and use tax. S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-30; S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-40. While South Carolina has yet to enter into the Agreement, the South Carolina Department of Revenue has laid out specific restrictions and criteria that the Agreement must comply with before the state will enter into the Agreement. S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-70. The legislature has enacted a statute that provides that even if the state does adopt the Agreement, such adoption will not constitute an invalidation, amendment, or modification of any South Carolina law in effect as of the time of the adoption. S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-60. Additionally, the legislature has enacted a statute that provides that if the state does become a member state, the state is only intended as a beneficiary of the Agreement, which will work to bind and inure only to the benefit of the state, and that no law or application of law can be declared invalid because of the Agreement. S.C. Code Ann. §12-35-90. BNA-SUTN SC 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate/Project Member South Carolina is an associate and project member of the Multistate Tax Commission. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. Associate members are states that participate in Commission meetings and otherwise consult and cooperate with the Commission and its other member states, or participate in Commission programs or projects as project members. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. BNA-SUTN SC 2.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

International Fuel Tax Agreement South Carolina is a member state of the International Fuel Tax Agreement(IFTA). South Carolina Dept. of Motor Vehicles, International Fuel Tax Agreement; International Fuel Tax Association, Inc., About Us. IFTA created a streamlined system for reporting fuel use by interstate motor vehicle carriers. IFTA operators receive licenses and file quarterly reports used to determine net tax or refund due and redistribute the funds collected to the appropriate jurisdictions. The tax is required for “qualified motor vehicles,” defined as motor vehicles used, designed, or maintained for transportation of people and property:• having two axles and a gross vehicle weight or registered gross vehicle weight exceeding 26,000 pounds or 11,793 kilograms; • having three or more axles regardless of weight; or • used in combination when the weight of such combination exceeds 26,000 pounds or 11,793 kilograms gross vehicle or registered gross vehicle weight. Qualified motor vehicles do not include recreational vehicles. International Fuel Tax Association, Inc., About Us;South Carolina Dept. of Motor Vehicles, International Fuel Tax Agreement. BNA-SUTN SC 2.3.
