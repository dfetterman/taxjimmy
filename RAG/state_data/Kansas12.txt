State: Kansas



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Kansas is a full member state of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement and all of the provisions required by the agreement are in effect. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3665;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Kansas (version 2018.0). Kansas became a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement on Oct. 1, 2005. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3653; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3665;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Kansas; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kansas (version 2018.0). On May 19, 2003, Kansas enacted H.B. 2005 in order to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, effective July 1, 2003. Although the agreement's destination-based sourcing rules were set to take effect July, 1, 2003, Kansas enacted legislation (S.B. 147) on May 20, 2004, which allowed retailers to use origin-based sourcing until January 1, 2005. The Kansas Department of Revenue will relieve sellers and certified service providers from tax liability to Kansas and its local jurisdictions for having charged and collected the incorrect amount of sales and use tax resulting from the seller or certified service provider relying on erroneous data in the Library of Definitions and the Tax Administration Practices section of the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board Taxability Matrix for Kansas. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3677(c); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kansas (version 2018.0); Kansas Notice 15-13 (Sept. 14, 2015); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). If the department amends an existing provision of the Library of Definitions or Tax Administration Practices section of the taxability matrix, the department will relieve sellers and certified service providers from liability to the department and local jurisdictions for having charged and collected incorrect tax until the first day of the calendar month that is at least 30 days after notice of a change to the Tax Administration Practices section of the taxability matrix is submitted to the governing board of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, provided the seller or certified service provider relied on the prior version of the taxability matrix in charging such incorrect tax. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3677(c); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kansas (version 2018.0); Kansas Notice 15-13 (Sept. 14, 2015); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN KS 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Kansas is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Kansas (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a Compliance Review Report finding that Kansas is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN KS 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member Kansas is a compact member state of the Multistate Tax Commission by enacting the provisions of the Multistate Tax Compact into Kansas law. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-4301. The purposes of the Multistate Tax Compact are to: • facilitate proper determination of state and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers, including the equitable apportionment of tax basis and settlement of apportionment disputes; • promote uniformity or compatibility in significant components of tax systems; • facilitate taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration; and • avoid duplicative taxation. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-4301. BNA-SUTN KS 2.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

Midwest Border States Compact Kansas is a member state of the Midwest Border States Compact along with Illinois, Iowa, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota, and South Dakota. Midwest Border States Compact, Facts You Need to Know to Protect You and Your Customers. The compact seeks to eliminate unfair competition and increase compliance by informing consumers about the use tax and seeking voluntary registration from out-of-state businesses. Kan. Dept. of Rev., Midwest Border States Compact, Facts You Need to Know to Protect You and Your Customers. BNA-SUTN KS 2.3.
