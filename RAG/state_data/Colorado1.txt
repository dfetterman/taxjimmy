State: Colorado



Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Tangible Personal Property

Destination Except as otherwise provided by law, Colorado follows destination based sourcing rules for all sales at retail, which includes tangible personal property and taxable services. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(a), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9(3), as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. See Colorado Dept. of Rev., Colorado Sales Tax Guide (Rev. June 2019). In determining the sourcing of a sale of tangible personal property, the following rules apply:• if received at the business of the seller, the sale is sourced to that location; • if not received at the seller's business location, the sale is sourced to the location where receipt by the purchaser occurs, including the location indicated by delivery instructions, if known by the seller; • if the above rules do not apply, the sale is sourced to the location indicated by an address for the purchaser available from the business records of the seller that are maintained in the ordinary course of the seller's business, when the use of this address does not constitute bad faith; • if the above rules do not apply, the sale is sourced to the location indicated by an address for the purchaser, including the address of the purchaser's payment instrument, when the use of this address does not constitute bad faith; or • if the above rules do not apply or if the seller is without sufficient information to apply them, the sale is sourced to the location indicated by the address from which the tangible personal property was shipped. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(a), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, §16, effective June 1, 2019; Colorado Dept. of Rev., Colorado Sales Tax Guide (Rev. June 2019). “Receipt” or “receive”means taking possession of tangible personal property or commodities or making first use of services, and does not include possession by a shipping company on behalf of the purchaser. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(d)(II), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, §16, effective June 1, 2019 Small Retailer Threshold Most sales in Colorado are sourced according to the destination of the purchased property. However, small retailers ($100,000 in sales or less in the previous calendar year, and before surpassing $100,000 in the current calendar year) source their sales to the business location of the retailer regardless of where the purchaser receives the tangible personal property or services. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(c)(I), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; Colorado Dept. of Rev., Colorado Sales Tax Guide (Rev. June 2019). If this rule results in the sale being sourced to a location outside of Colorado, sales tax does not apply. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(c)(II), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; Colorado Dept. of Rev., Colorado Sales Tax Guide (Rev. June 2019). This special rule for the sourcing of sales is effective until 90 days after the Department of Revenue notifies the revisor of statutes that a Global Information System is available for retailers to use to determine the taxing jurisdiction in which an address resides. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(c)(III), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; Colorado Dept. of Rev., Sales Tax Information for In-State Retailers. BNA-SUTN CO 4.1.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Leases and Rentals

Destination Effective Dec. 1, 2018, except for leases or rentals of certain transportation equipment, for leases or rentals that require recurring periodic payments, the first periodic payment is sourced the same as a retail sale, but periodic payments made subsequent to the first payment are sourced to the primary property location for each period covered by the payment. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(b), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019; Colorado Sales Tax Topics: Leases (revised June 2019). Prior to Dec. 1, 2018, Colorado treats leases under the same rules as sales of property. Thus, the lease or rental tangible personal property is sourced to the location of the delivery, use, or storage of the leased tangible property. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-713(1)(a); Colorado FYI Sales 56. The tax was collected on leases or rentals requiring recurring periodic payments at the rate applicable to the first payment until the end of the lease, regardless of the primary property location, or any local rate changes taking effect during the lease term. See 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-105.1(A)(1)(a), as repealed by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018 (emergency rule), by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018 (emergency rule re-adopted), and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019 (final repeal). For a lease or rental that does not require periodic payments, the payment is sourced the same as a retail sale. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(b), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. The retail sale, including the lease or rental, of transportation equipment is sourced the same as a retail sale. Transportation equipment includes locomotives and railcars used in interstate commerce, trucks and truck-tractors with a Gross Vehicle Weight Rating of 10,001 pounds or greater, trailers, semi-trailers, or passenger buses that are registered through the International Registration Plan and operated under authority of a certified carrier, aircraft operated by a certified air carrier, and containers designed for use on the foregoing equipment. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(b)(III), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. The lease or rental of motor vehicles, trailers, semi-trailers, or aircraft that do not qualify as transportation equipment where the lease requires recurring periodic payments is sourced to the primary property location. The primary property location is the location indicated by an address for the property provided by the lessee that is available to the lessor from its records maintained in the ordinary course of business, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith. The primary property location is not altered by intermittent use at different locations. For a lease or rental of such property that does not require recurring periodic payments, the payment is sourced the same as a retail sale. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-104(3)(b)(II), as added by 2019 Colo. H.B. 1240, effective June 1, 2019; 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. BNA-SUTN CO 4.1.3.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Local Sourcing

Destination Local sales tax applies to the purchase or sale if the transaction is subject to state sales tax and consummated within a county, or incorporated city and town borders that imposes a local sales tax. Colo. Rev. Stat. §29-2-105(1)(d)(I). Taxable transactions are sourced to the localities using the same sourcing method used to source the state sales and use tax. Colo. Rev. Stat. §29-2-105(1)(d)(I). In-state Retailers Effective Dec. 1, 2018, retailers with physical locations in Colorado must collect and remit sales tax based on the local tax rate at the point of delivery, rather than the rate at the retailer's physical location or the taxes that are in common between the customer's address and the retailer's location. This includes any applicable state-administered local and special district taxes, as well as taxes for home-rule jurisdictions. Colorado Dept. of Rev., Information for In-state Retailers (last visited Oct. 30, 2018). For each location an in-state retailer makes deliveries to, the retailer must add a non-physical location to their Revenue Online account. In-state retailers must create a non-physical location for every location to which they deliver taxable goods. Colorado Dept. of Rev., Information for In-state Retailers (last visited Oct. 30, 2018). Specifically, when tangible personal property or services are received by the purchaser at a business location of the seller, the sale is sourced to that business location. When services are not received by the purchaser at a business location of the seller, the sale is sourced to the location where receipt by the purchaser (or the purchaser's donee, designated as such by the purchaser) occurs, including the location indicated by instructions for delivery to the purchaser (or donee), if that location is known to the seller. 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. If the purchaser does not receive tangible personal property or services at the seller's business location or the seller does not know where receipt by the purchaser occurs, the sale is sourced to the location indicated by an address for the purchaser that is available from the business records of the seller that are maintained in the ordinary course of the seller's business when use of this address does not constitute bad faith. 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. If none of the previous conditions apply, the sale is sourced to the location indicated by an address for the purchaser obtained during the consummation of the sale, including the address of a purchaser's payment instrument, if no other address is available, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith. 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-102.9, as amended by Colo. Reg. Vol. 41, No. 19 (Oct. 10, 2018), effective Dec. 1, 2018, by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 2 (Jan. 25, 2019), effective Dec. 18, 2018, and by Colo. Reg. Vol. 42, No. 6 (March 25, 2019), effective April 14, 2019. If the seller is without sufficient information to apply the previous rules, then the location of the sale will be determined by the address from which tangible personal property was shipped. 39 Colo. Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Credit for Tax Paid to Another Jurisdiction

Yes Colorado allows a use tax credit for the amount of tax paid to another state prior to the storage, use, or consumption of tangible personal property in Colorado. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-203(1)(k); 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-713.2(f). The amount of the credit is equal to the tax paid by the taxpayer to another state by reason of the imposition of a similar tax on the purchase or use of the property. The amount of credit cannot exceed the tax imposed in Colorado. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-203(1)(k); 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-713.2(f). BNA-SUTN CO 4.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property First Used Out-of-state

Exempt Colorado exempts from sales and use tax property that was used out of state and for which the use tax has already been paid. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-704(2). Further, Colorado exempts from sales and use tax tangible personal property acquired outside Colorado and brought into Colorado by a nonresident acquiring residency. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-704(2). The storage, use, or consumption of a motor vehicle is not subject to use tax if the owner is or was, at the time of purchase, a nonresident of Colorado and the owner purchased the vehicle outside of Colorado for use outside Colorado and actually used it in the manner for a substantial and primary purpose for which it was acquired and registered, titled, and licensed said motor vehicle outside of Colorado. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-719(2)(a). BNA-SUTN CO 4.3.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Purchased for Use Out-of-state

Depends Sales of tangible personal property located within Colorado at the time of sale and delivered within Colorado are taxable, irrespective of the ultimate destination of the property sold, or where the parties to the contract of sale are located, or where the contract was made or accepted or the funds paid. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-704(3). Tangible personal property sold to an out-of-state buyer is not subject to sales tax when shipped by a common carrier hired by the vendor to a destination outside Colorado for use outside Colorado. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-704(2); Colorado Private Letter Ruling PLR-18-001 (Feb. 22, 2018). Colorado provides a temporary presence exemption for a purchase of aircraft that satisfies the conditions as set forth in Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-711.5. Purchase By a Non-Resident for Use Outside the State (‘Fly-away’ exemption) The purchase of a new or used aircraft will not be subject to Colorado sales and use tax if the aircraft:• is sold to a non-resident of Colorado; Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-711.5(1)(a). • will be removed from the state within the longer of: ○ 120 days after the date of sale; Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-711.5(1)(b)(I), as amended by 2016 Colo. H.B. 1119, §1, effective July 1, 2016. or ○ 30 days after completing maintenance, interior refurbishment, paint, or engine work associated with the sale of the aircraft; Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-711.5(1)(b)(II), as amended by 2016 Colo. H.B. 1119, §1, effective July 1, 2016. and • will not be in the state more than 73 days in any of the three calendar years following the calendar year in which the aircraft is removed from the state. Colo. Rev. Stat. §39-26-711.5(1)(c). BNA-SUTN CO 4.3.3.
