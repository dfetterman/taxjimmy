Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Interstate and Common Carrier Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Exempt Arizona exempts navigational and communication instruments from retail transaction privilege and use taxes if the instruments are sold to entities holding certain permits and/or certificates. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(B)(7)(a); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(B)(7)(a). Specifically, Arizona exempts from retail transaction privilege and use tax aircraft, navigational and communication instruments, and other accessories and related equipment sold to a person:• holding, or exempted by federal law from obtaining, a federal certificate of public convenience and necessity for use as, in conjunction with, or becoming part of, an aircraft to be used to transport persons for hire in intrastate, interstate, or foreign commerce; • holding a supplemental air carrier certificate under federal aviation regulations for use as, in conjunction with, or becoming part of, an aircraft to be used to transport persons for hire in intrastate, interstate, or foreign commerce; or • holding a foreign air carrier permit for air transportation for use as or in conjunction with or becoming a part of aircraft to be used to transport persons, property, or United States mail in intrastate, interstate, or foreign commerce. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(B)(7)(a), as amended by 2016 Ariz. H.B. 2533, effective July 1, 2017, and 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1010, effective July 21, 2017; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(B)(7)(a), as amended by 2016 Ariz. H.B. 2533, effective July 1, 2017, and 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1010, effective July 21, 2017; see also American Helicopters, LLC v. Arizona Dept. of Rev., 2015 BL 22951, No. 1 CA-TX 14-0001 (Ariz. Ct. App. 2015) (taxpayer did not meet the requirements of the exemption because it did not hold either a CPCN, a supplemental air carrier certificate, or a foreign air carrier permit for air transportation). Effective July, 1, 2017, Arizona also exempts from retail transaction privilege and use tax aircraft, navigational and communication instruments, and other accessories and related equipment sold to a person:• that is certificated or licensed under federal aviation regulations as a scheduled or unscheduled carrier of persons for hire for use as or in conjunction with or becoming part of an aircraft to be used to transport persons for hire in intrastate, interstate, or foreign commerce; • operating an aircraft to transport persons in any manner for compensation or hire including as an air carrier, a foreign air carrier, or a commercial operator or under a restricted category, within the meaning of 14 Code of Federal Regulations, regardless of whether the operation or aircraft is regulated or certified under Part 91, 119, 121, 133, 135, 136, or 137, or another part of 14 Code of Federal Regulations; and effective Dec. 31, 2017, operating an aircraft for use in a fractional ownership program that meets Federal Aviation Administration requirements under 14 Code of Federal Regulations 91, subpart K; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(B)(7)(a)(iv), as amended by 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1416, effective Dec. 31, 2017; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(B)(7)(a)(iv), as amended by 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1416, effective Dec. 31, 2017. or • that will lease or otherwise transfer operational control, within the meaning of Federal Aviation Administration Operations Specification A008, or its successor, of the aircraft, instruments or accessories to one or more persons described above. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(B)(7)(a), as amended by 2016 Ariz. H.B. 2533, effective July 1, 2017, and 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1010, effective July 21, 2017; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(B)(7)(a), as amended by 2016 Ariz. H.B. 2533, effective July 1, 2017, and 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1010, effective July 21, 2017. In the case of persons that will lease or otherwise transfer operational control of an aircraft to one or more of the first five above categories of eligible persons, the Department of Revenue will prescribe the form of a certificate to be used to document eligibility for the exemption. The purchaser must provide the certificate and documentation that the aircraft's operational control has been transferred or will be transferred immediately after the purchase. The operational control must be at least 50 percent of the aircraft's flight hours. If the 50-percent operational control does not transfer during the recapture period, then the aircraft's owner must pay any tax that the seller or purchaser would have been required to pay, plus penalty and interest. The recapture period begins when the operational control transfers, and it ends on the later of the date on which the aircraft fully depreciates for federal income tax purposes or five years after operational control transfers. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5009(Q), as amended by 2017 Ariz. S.B. 1010, effective July 21, 2017. For purposes of these exemptions, the definition of aircraft includes an “airplane flight simulator that is approved by the federal aviation administration for use as a phase II or higher flight simulator” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(V)(2)(a); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(H)(1)(a). or “tangible personal property that is permanently affixed or attached as a component part of an aircraft that is owned or operated by a certificated or licensed carrier of persons or property.” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(V)(2)(b); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(H)(1)(b). Other accessories and related equipment are defined as “aircraft accessories and equipment such as ground service equipment that physically contact aircraft at some point during the overall carrier operation. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(V)(2). Arizona also provides an exemption for leases or rentals of aircraft, flight simulators and similar training equipment to students or staff by nonprofit, accredited educational institutions that offer associate or baccalaureate degrees in aviation or aerospace related fields. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5071(A)(6). BNA-SUTN AZ 29.2.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Taxable Generally, gross proceeds from the sale of motor vehicles required to be licensed in Arizona are subject to transaction privilege tax under the retail classification or use tax. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(C)(5); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(C)(5). Motor vehicle is defined as (1) a self-propelled vehicle or (2) “for the purposes of the laws relating to the imposition of a tax on motor vehicle fuel, a vehicle that is operated on the highways of this state and that is propelled by the use of motor vehicle fuel.” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-101(37)(a); Arizona Publication No. 610. “Motor vehicle” does not include “a motorized wheelchair, an electric personal assistive mobility device or a motorized skateboard.” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-101(37)(b). “Vehicle” is defined as “a device in, on or by which a person or property is or may be transported or drawn on a public highway, excluding devices moved by human power or used exclusively on stationary rails or tracks.” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-101(67). Rules for Specific Types of Motor Vehicle Sales Use by Motor Vehicle Dealer Motor vehicles removed from inventory by a motor vehicle dealer and used directly in the motor vehicle dealer's primary business and returned to the dealer's active sales inventory within one year after the date of the initial removal from inventory, are subject to use tax on a monthly basis. The use tax applies to one thirty-ninth of the value of each:• new motor vehicle as determined by the manufacturer's suggested retail price;and • used motor vehicle as determined by any industry-wide publication in common use and devoted to listing used car values. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5157; Arizona Publication No. 610. However, Arizona has a use tax exemption for motor vehicles removed from inventory by a motor vehicle dealer and given to a charitable or educational institution, public education institutions, or state universities or affiliated organizations of a state university if no part of the organization's net earnings inures to the benefit of any private shareholder or individual. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(A)(32); Arizona Publication No. 610. Use by Motor Vehicle Manufacturer Use tax is imposed on a motor vehicle manufacturer for the storage, use or consumption of its maintenance, support or service motor vehicles. The tax is levied on a monthly basis and applies as a percentage of one thirty-ninth of the dealer net price of the motor vehicles. This tax cannot be imposed for more than thirty-nine months on any motor vehicle. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5158; Arizona Publication No. 610. Motor vehicles and component parts used for testing or development by motor vehicle manufacturers are not subject to use tax. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5158. Purchase by Nonresidents Gross proceeds of motor vehicles sold to nonresidents for use outside of Arizona, if the dealer ships or delivers the motor vehicle outside Arizona, are exempt from tax under the retail classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(14); Nonresident State Tax Rate Schedules and Arizona County Tax Rate Schedules, effective Aug. 1, 2016; Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax Changes & News January 2015; Arizona Publication No. 610; Arizona Publication No. 622. Further, sales of motor vehicles to a nonresident if the purchaser's state of residence does not allow a corresponding use tax exemption and if the nonresident has secured a special 90 day nonresident registration permit for the vehicle is exempt from tax under the retail classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(28)(a); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-2154; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-2154(1). Sales of motor vehicles to nonresidents from reciprocal states including California, Connecticut, Florida, Idaho and Illinois, that have higher tax rates than Arizona's 5.6 percent transaction privilege tax rate, are subject to Arizona taxes if the nonresident purchaser takes possession of the vehicle in Arizona. Residents of such states will be allowed a credit against their state taxes for the state transaction privilege tax paid in Arizona, when the nonresident registers the vehicle in his or her home state. For a information on states exercising reciprocity with Arizona for taxes paid on such purchases see Nonresident State Tax Rate Schedules and Arizona County Tax Rate Schedules, effective Aug. 1, 2016; Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax Changes & News January 2015. Sales to Indian Tribe Members Sales of motor vehicles to an enrolled member of an Indian tribe, residing on the Indian reservation established for that tribe are exempt from tax under the retail classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(28)(b). Alternative Fuel Vehicles A retail transaction privilege and use tax exemption is available for certain alternative fuel vehicles manufactured as diesel fuel vehicles and then later converted to operate on alternative fuel, as well as equipment that is installed on a diesel fuel vehicle to convert it to operate on alternative fuel. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. 42-5159(A)(44); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(49). Heavy Trucks and Trailers Transaction privilege tax on sales of heavy trucks and trailers does not include any amount attributable to federal excise taxes imposed by I.R.C. §4051 entitles “Imposition of tax on heavy trucks and trailers sold at retail.” Sales at Auction Sales of motor vehicles to nonresidents at an auction for use outside of Arizona, if the vehicle is shipped or delivered outside of Arizona, are exempt from tax under the retail classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(44). Motor Vehicle Leases Generally, motor vehicles are treated the same as any other tangible personal property for purposes of the taxation of leases and rentals under the personal property rental classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5071(A); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5001(21), as renumbered by 2019 Ariz. H.B. 2757, effective Sept. 30, 2019; see Arizona Publication No. 606. The tax base for the lease and rental of a motor vehicle does not include any amounts attributable to the car rental surcharge. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5071(D); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-5810; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §48-4234. Further, vehicle and registration fees, license plate fees, special plate fees, historic value license plate fees, and the constitutional license tax for vehicles are not included in the tax base under the rental classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5071(B)(1). In the case of a lease or rental of a motor vehicle by a dealership, the dealership may reduce gross receipts by amounts received for the first month of a lease payment, if the lease and the lease payment for the first month of the lease are transferred to a third party leasing company. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5071(B)(5). Donations to a Charitable Or Educational Institutions Motor vehicles that are removed from inventory by a motor vehicle dealer and provided to charitable or educational institutions exempt from taxation under §501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, public education institutions, or state universities or affiliated organizations if no part of the organization's net earnings benefit any private shareholder or individual are exempt from tax. Arizona Publication No. 610. BNA-SUTN AZ 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Fuel

Nontaxable Motor vehicle fuel and use fuel that are subject to a valid single-trip use fuel tax are exempt from retail transaction privilege and use tax. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(22); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(A)(5). “Motor vehicle fuel”is defined as “all products that are commonly or commercially known or sold as gasoline, including casinghead gasoline, natural gasoline and all flammable liquids, and that are composed of a mixture of selected hydrocarbons expressly manufactured and blended for the purpose of effectively and efficiently operating internal combustion engines. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-101(40). Motor vehicle fuel does not include “inflammable liquids that are specifically manufactured for racing motor vehicles and that are distributed for and used by racing motor vehicles at a racetrack, use fuel, aviation fuel, fuel for jet or turbine powered aircraft or the mixture created at the interface of two different substances being transported through a pipeline, commonly known as transmix.” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-101(40). “Use fuel” is defined as “all gases and liquids used or suitable for use to propel motor vehicles, except fuels that are subject to the motor vehicle fuel tax.” Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-5601(38). Also, sales of natural gas or liquefied petroleum gas used to propel a motor vehicle are exempt from tax. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(35). There is a complementary use tax exemption as well. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(33). For more information on any applicable fuel excise taxes, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Arizona Section 8.1. BNA-SUTN AZ 29.3.6.

Ann. §42-5071(B)(4). Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-5852; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-5851(4). Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-5432(C)(2). • motor vehicles owned by a nonprofit organization in Arizona and used exclusively for disaster or search and rescue assistance, which have been approved by the director of the division of emergency management for exemption from gross weight fees under Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-318; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §28-5432(C)(3); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §26-318. • motor vehicles owned by a foreign government, by the United States, by a state or political subdivision of a state, or by an Indian tribal government, and used exclusively for government purposes; Ariz.
