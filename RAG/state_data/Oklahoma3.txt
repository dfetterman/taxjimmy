Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Undyed Diesel

Nontaxable Oklahoma does not impose sales and use taxes on sales of motor fuels for which an excise tax has been paid. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-180(a). This includes diesel fuel, although not specifically with respect to undyed diesel. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-180(b). Diesel fuel users whose purchases of diesel fuel in bulk quantities are primarily for delivery by them into the fuel supply tanks of motor vehicles in Oklahoma, must apply for a Sales Tax Permit issued by the Oklahoma Tax Commission and assume direct liability for the payment of state and local taxes on all withdrawals from bulk storage of fuel not used “on-the-road”and therefore not subject to motor fuel or gasoline excise taxes. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-180(b). BNA-SUTN OK 30.1.4.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends Oklahoma imposes sales tax on sales of electricity. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354(A)(2); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-19-341(a). Residential Electricity Oklahoma exempts from state sales tax the sale of natural or artificial gas sold exclusively for residential use. However, such sales still may be subject to local sales tax. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1357(8); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-19-341(b). “Residential use” means the use of gas or electricity by the customer exclusively for domestic purposes in a private home or individual living area that is served through a single meter or a master metered multi-unit apartment, condominium, or townhome used exclusively for “domestic purposes.” Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-120(a). “Domestic purposes” include:• lighting; • refrigeration; • cooking; • water; • heating; • space heating; and • air conditioning. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-120(a). Electricity for Enhanced Oil Recovery Operations Sales of electricity to the operator of a spacing unit or lease when oil is produced or is attempted to be produced using enhanced recovery methods are exempt from the sales tax. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-123(a). BNA-SUTN OK 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Natural Gas

Depends Oklahoma imposes sales tax on sales of natural or artificial gas. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354(A)(2); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-19-341(a). Residential Gas Exemption Oklahoma exempts from state sales tax the sale of natural or artificial gas sold exclusively for residential use. However, the transaction may be subject to local sales tax. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1357(8); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-19-341(b). “Residential use”means the use of gas or electricity by the customer exclusively for domestic purposes in a private home or individual living area that is served through a single meter or a master metered multi-unit apartment, condominium, or townhome used exclusively for “domestic purposes.” Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-120(a). “Domestic purposes” include:• lighting; • refrigeration; • cooking; • water; • heating; • space heating; and • air conditioning. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-120(a). BNA-SUTN OK 30.2.2.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Exempt Oklahoma exempts from sales and use tax sales of machinery, electricity, fuels, explosives, and materials (excluding chemicals) used to mine coal in Oklahoma. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-55(a). Extractive industrial activities(such as mining) do not qualify for Oklahoma's manufacturing exemption, because those activities are not manufacturing activities. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1352(14); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-150.1(a)(4). BNA-SUTN OK 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Oil and Gas Machinery and Equipment

Nontaxable Oklahoma does not provide an exemption for machinery and equipment used in oil and gas extraction operations. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354(A). Also, extractive industrial activities (such as oil and gas extraction) do not qualify for Oklahoma's manufacturing exemption, because those activities are not manufacturing activities. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1352(14); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-150.1(a)(4). BNA-SUTN OK 30.3.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Oil and Gas Services to Wells

Nontaxable Services to wells are not among the enumerated services listed in Oklahoma, so sales of these services are not taxable. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354(A). Sales of hydraulic fracturing and cementing services are also nontaxable. Oklahoma Letter Ruling LR-15-009 (March 31, 2016). BNA-SUTN OK 30.3.2.2.

==================================================

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Oil and Gas Other Property and Services

Nontaxable Other oil and gas services are not among the enumerated services listed in Oklahoma, so Oklahoma does not impose sales and use tax on those services. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354(A). Hydraulic fracturing and cementing services are also nontaxable. Oklahoma Letter Ruling LR-15-009 (March 31, 2016). However, sales and use tax is imposed on tangible personal property, such as sand, proppants, chemicals, and other materials, sold by a vendor providing fracturing services where the parties' contract transfers title to the property to the taxpayer and the vendor's invoices separately stated cost of such property. Oklahoma Commission Decision 2017-03-14-12 (March 14, 2014). BNA-SUTN OK 30.3.2.3.

==================================================

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Timber

Nontaxable Logging services are not among the enumerated services listed in Oklahoma, so sales of these services are not taxable. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354(A). The same applies to sales of hydraulic fracturing and cementing services. Oklahoma Letter Ruling LR-15-009 (March 31, 2016). Also, extractive industrial activities (such as logging) do not qualify for Oklahoma's manufacturing exemption, because these activities are not manufacturing activities. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1352(14); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-150.1(a)(4). BNA-SUTN OK 30.3.3.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Production

Taxable Oklahoma's manufacturing exemption does not cover sales of machinery and equipment used to produce energy (i.e., extractive industrial activities such as mining, quarrying, logging, and drilling for oil, gas, and water), but the exemption includes processes subsequent to extraction if the processes result in a change of the form or use of the material extracted. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1352(14); Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-13-150.1(a)(4). BNA-SUTN OK 30.4.
