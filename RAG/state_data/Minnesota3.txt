Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Other Fuel

Depends Effective July 1, 2017, special fuel used for certain motor vehicles is exempt from Minnesota's sales and use tax. Minn. Stat. §297A.68(19), as amended by 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, First Special Session, Art. 3, § 22, effective July 1, 2017. Minnesota exempts special fuel used:• to power a refrigeration unit mounted on a licensed motor vehicle, provided that the unit has an engine separate from the one used to propel the vehicle and the fuel is used exclusively for the unit; • to power an unlicensed motor vehicle that is used solely or primarily to move semitrailers within a cargo yard, warehouse facility, or intermodal facility; or • to operate a power take-off unit or auxiliary engine in or on a licensed motor vehicle, whether or not the unit or engine is fueled from the same or a different fuel tank as that from which the motor vehicle is fueled. Minn. Stat. §297A.68(19), as amended by 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, First Special Session, Art. 3, § 22, effective July 1, 2017; see Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 116. In general, however, fuel used in construction activities or for heating commercial buildings is subject to Minnesota's sales and use tax. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 116. BNA-SUTN MN 30.1.12.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends The sale of electricity, gas, water, or steam for use or consumption is subject to Minnesota's sales and use tax, except when sold for residential use as a primary heat source during enumerated months. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(e); see Minnesota Local Governments Industry Guide. Specifically, the following utilities are not taxable when sold for residential use natural gas or electricity used as a primary source of residential heat during the billing months of November, December, January, February, March, and April: fuel oil, coal, wood, steam, hot water, propane, LP gas, and water services. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(e); Minn. Stat. §297A.99; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 157. All charges associated with furnishing utilities or making utility services available are considered part of sales price and subject to tax except for mandated federal law programs and taxes legally imposed directly on the consumer that are separately stated on the purchaser's bill. Minn. R. 8130.1100(4). Examples of taxable charges are:• demand charge; • fixed or basic monthly charge; • franchise fee; • fuel clause adjustment; • minimum charge; • reconnection fee; • service charge; • service connection charge; • standby fee;and • surcharge. Minn. R. 8130.1100(4)(A) - Minn. R. 8130.1100(4)(J). Minnesota offers an exemption to qualified data centers for electricity used to operate and maintain the qualified data center. Minnesota Dept. of Rev., Qualified Data Centers (Oct. 1, 2016). If a taxpayer has a separate utility meter for data center equipment, the taxpayer's exemption percentage on that meter is 100 percent. However, in most situations a predominant use or energy study is required if taxable and nontaxable uses are made through a single meter. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 129. For more information on Qualified Data Centers, see Sales and Use Tax Navigator at Minnesota 19.1. BNA-SUTN MN 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Timber

Depends Logging equipment, including chain saws used for commercial logging, is exempt from sales and use tax. Minn. Stat. §297A.69(4). Logging equipment means new or used machinery, equipment, implements, accessories, and contrivances used directly and principally in the commercial cutting or removal or both of timber or other solid wood forest products intended to be sold ultimately at retail. Logging equipment includes machinery used for bucking, bunching, debarking, delimbing, felling, forwarding, loading, piling, skidding, topping, and yarding operations performed on timber and chain saws. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(37). For sales and use tax purposes, logging is considered to be agricultural production. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 153. For more information about property used in agricultural production, see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Minnesota 25.1. BNA-SUTN MN 30.3.3.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Exempt Sales of solar energy systems and wind energy conservation systems are exempt from sales and use tax in Minnesota. Minn. Stat. §297A.67(29); Minn. Stat. §297A.68(12). In addition, materials used to manufacture, install, construct, repair, or replace wind energy conservation systems are also exempt. Minn. Stat. §297A.68(12). A “solar energy system”is a set of devices whose primary purpose is to collect solar energy and convert and store it for useful purposes, or to produce generated power by means of any combination of collecting, transferring, or converting solar-generated energy. “Useful purposes” include heating and cooling buildings or other energy-using processes. Minn. Stat. §216C.06(17). A “wind energy conversion system” (WECS) is any device, such as a wind charger, windmill, or wind turbine, which converts wind energy to a form of usable energy. Minn. Stat. §216C.06(19). BNA-SUTN MN 30.5.
