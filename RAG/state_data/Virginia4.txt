Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Depends Aircraft are not subject to Virginia's retail sales and use tax, if they are subject to the Virginia Aircraft Sales and Use Tax. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-609.1(5). The Virginia Aircraft Sales and Use Tax is imposed on the sale, use, or lease of aircraft required to be licensed by the Department of Aviation. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-1502; see also Va. Code Ann. §5.1-5; Va. Regs. §10-220-10. Virginia does not impose state sales and use tax or local sales and use taxes that are levied pursuant to Va. Code Ann. §58.1-605 and Va. Code Ann. §58.1-606, on any tangible personal property sold or leased to an airline operating in intrastate commerce as a common carrier that provides scheduled flights on a continuing basis to at least one Virginia airport at least once a week. To be exempt, the property must be used or consumed by the airline directly in its rendition of common carrier service. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-609.3(6), as reenacted and amended by 2017 Va. H.B. 1738, effective July 1, 2017. For more information about any applicable excise taxes, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Virginia 9. BNA-SUTN VA 29.1.1.

Exempt For sales on or after July 1, 2018 to July 1, 2022, parts, engines, and supplies used for maintaining, repairing, or reconditioning aircraft or any aircraft's avionics system, engine, or component parts are exempt from sales tax. Prior to July 1, 2018, repair and replacement parts and accessories are subject to sales and use tax unless purchased by a dealer and installed on an aircraft which is being resold. Va. However, an exemption from state and local sales and use tax is provided for tangible personal property used or consumed directly in the rendition of common carrier service by an airline regularly operating in intrastate commerce in Virginia. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-609.3(6), as reenacted and amended by 2017 Va. H.B. 1738, effective July 1, 2017. BNA-SUTN VA 29.1.2.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Exempt Motor vehicles, trailers, and semitrailers are not subject to the retail sales and use tax. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-609.1(2). However, the sale, use, or rental of motor vehicles is subject to the motor vehicle sales and use tax. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-2402. The motor vehicle sales and use tax is imposed upon the sale or use of motor vehicles in Virginia, other than vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating or gross combination with rating of more than 26,000 pounds, or a sale to or use by a person for rental as an established business or part of an established business. The tax is imposed at a rate of 4.15 percent, with a minimum tax of$75. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-2402, as amended by 2019 Va. H.B. 1679, effective Oct. 1, 2018. All-terrain Vehicles, Mopeds, and Off-road Motorcycles Effective Oct. 1, 2018, all-terrain vehicles, mopeds, and off-road motorcycles are included in the definition of “motor vehicle” and are thus exempt from retail sales tax, but subject to the motor vehicle sales tax. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-602, as amended by 2018 Va. S.B. 249, effective Oct. 1, 2018; Virginia Dept. of Taxn., 2018 Legislative Summary (June 2018). All-terrain vehicles, mopeds, and off-road motorcycles may be subject to a higher motor vehicle sales and use tax rate if sold in certain locations, including the Historical Triangle. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-2402, as amended by 2019 Va. H.B. 1679, effective Oct. 1, 2018. Prior to Oct. 1, 2018, all-terrain vehicles, mopeds, and off-road motorcycles as defined in Va. Code Ann. §46.2-100 are not considered motor vehicles subject to motor vehicle tax, and are therefore subject to the retail sales and use tax. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-2403(27), as amended by 2013 Va. S.B. 1038, effective July 1, 2013, and by 2019 Va. H.B. 1679, effective Oct. 1, 2018. For more information on the motor vehicle sales tax, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Virginia 9.1. Trailers Virginia has passed legislation lowering the minimum tax on purchases of trailers weighing less than 2,000 pounds to $35. 2018 Va. H.B. 680, effective July 1, 2018; 2019 Va. H.B. 1679, effective Oct. 1, 2018. Leases Leases of motor vehicles are not subject to the retail sales and use tax if they are subject to the motor vehicle sales and use tax. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-609.1(2); Va. Regs. §10-210-990 A lease is considered a transfer of possession without a transfer of ownership for 12 months or more. The motor vehicle sales and use tax does not differentiate between a Terminal Rental Adjustment lease and other leases with purchase price options. See Va. Code Ann. §58.1-2401. The motor vehicle sales and use tax is paid up front on motor vehicle leases. and is paid by the purchaser and is collected by the Division of Motor Vehicles. Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2404; Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2402(A)(2); Va. Code Ann. §58.1-2402(A)(2); Virginia Ruling of the Commissioner PD 12-206 (Dec. 13, 2012). The tax base for motor vehicle leases is the gross amount of the total lease payments. Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2405(A). When a vehicle is sold by a lessor to the lessee pursuant to a purchase option price at the termination of the lease, tax is not due if the lessee paid the tax levied when the vehicle was leased. If it is determined that tax was not paid on the remaining value of the vehicle, that amount is subject to tax. Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2405(B) and (C); Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2404; Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2402; Va. Code. Ann. §58.1-2403.26. For more information on any applicable excise taxes, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Virginia 9. BNA-SUTN VA 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Short-term Motor Vehicle Rentals

Taxable Virginia imposes a motor vehicle rental tax on the rental of motor vehicles in Virginia, whether from a rental business or from an automobile dealership or other establishment, for a period of less than 12 months. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-1735; Virginia Ruling of the Commissioner PD 13-109 (July 1, 2013). The 10-percent tax is comprised of: a 4-percent “rental tax” imposed on the cost for rental of motor vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating or gross combined weight rating of 26,000 pounds or less; another 4-percent component is levied on the rental cost for motor vehicles, regardless of weight, but which does not apply to motorcycles and mobile homes; and a 2-percent “rental fee” imposed without regard to vehicle weight, and which also does not apply to motorcycles or mobile homes. Va. Code Ann. §58.1-1735; Virginia Ruling of the Commissioner PD 13-109 (July 1, 2013). For motor vehicle rentals occurring after June 30, 2013, gross proceeds does not include:• charges for violations, citations, fines, or other penalties or fees; • delivery charges, pickup charges, recovery charges, or drop-off charges; • pass-through charges; • transportation charges; • third-party service charges; and • refueling surcharges. Virginia Ruling of the Commissioner PD 13-109 (July 1, 2013). For additional information about Virginia's motor vehicle and rental tax and fee, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Virginia 9.2. BNA-SUTN VA 29.3.7.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Other Transportation Property and Services

Exempt Other Railroad Property The taxability or exemption of railroad property depends on the railway activities for which the property is used. Va. Regs. §10-210-382. The four primary railway activities are:• way and structures activity, which entails repair, maintenance, and acquisition of right-of-way and track, structures, buildings, and facilities; • equipment activity, entailing the repair, maintenance, and acquisition of transportation and other operating equipment, including locomotives, freight train cars, passenger train cars, highway revenue equipment, floating equipment, and work equipment; • transportation activity, entailing the operation, servicing, inspecting, weighing, assembling, and switching of trains; operation of highway revenue services; operation of facilities in connection with transportation operations (coal and ore terminals, intermodal terminals, and terminal grain elevators for example); operation of floating equipment and related facilities; and the operation of communications systems which primarily support train operations; and • general and administrative activity, which entails the provision of overall administrative and other general support for carrier operations. Va. Regs. §10-210-381. Tangible personal property used in way and structures activity is subject to tax to the extent it is not an integral part of a railway's roadbed. Taxable property includes: materials used to construct or erect most structures (station and office buildings, roadway buildings, sidewalks, driveways, etc.); structures carrying public roads; railway tunnels (except rails and roadbed); and items used in administrative support of the activity. Examples of exempt property used directly in the rendition of common carrier service are: rails, ties, and roadbed materials. Va. Regs. §10-210-382(1). Tangible personal property other than administrative supplies, employee comfort supplies, and equipment and structures of all types, used in this activity is exempt from the tax. Examples of exempt property are fire extinguishers used on revenue equipment;locomotives; passenger and tool cars; and tangible personal property used in cleaning and painting (including lettering) revenue equipment. Examples of taxable property include: arm rests and cab cushions (other than for passenger use); instructional cars; scale test cars; and supplies and equipment used to maintain or repair company automobiles and general purpose trucks. Va. Regs. §10-210-382(2). Transportation activity property is generally exempt from the tax as this activity relates primarily to the direct operation of railways. Examples of exempt property are: equipment used in switching trains; equipment used in fueling, lubricating, maintaining, and repairing revenue and service equipment; and tangible personal property used in receiving, sorting and loading freight, containers, or trailers. Examples of taxable property are crew meals and lodging and property used for the comfort of employees. Va. Regs. §10-210-382(3). Note that tangible personal property in way and structures, equipment, and transportation activities that is used in the servicing, maintenance or repair of tangible personal property is exempt from the tax only to the extent that the tangible personal property serviced, maintained or repaired is exempt from the tax. Va. Regs. §10-210-382. Tangible personal property used in the general and administrative activity is taxable, including office supplies, office equipment, furniture, tariff rate schedules, billing supplies and equipment, payroll supplies and equipment, and similar items. Va. Regs. §10-210-382(4).
