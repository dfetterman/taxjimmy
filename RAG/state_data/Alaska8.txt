State: Alaska



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

No Tax Alaska does not impose a state sales and use tax. Federally Subsidized Food A borough may not levy or collect sales tax on any purchases made with food coupons, stamps, or any other allotment issued under the Federal Food Stamp Program or on food instruments, vouchers, or other type of certificate issued under the Federal Special Supplemental Food Program for Women, Infants, and Children. Alaska Stat. §29.45.650(f). A borough assembly may not authorize a city to levy or collect sales tax on any purchases made with food coupons, stamps, or any other allotment issued under the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program or on food instruments, vouchers, or other type of certificate issue under the Federal Special Supplemental Food Program for Women, Infants, and Children. Food stamp allotments paid in the form of wage subsidies, as authorized by state law under Alaska Stat. §47.25.975(b), are not regarded as an allotment issued under the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program. Alaska Stat. §29.45.700(d). BNA-SUTN AK 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Alcoholic Beverages

No Tax Alaska does not impose a state sales and use tax. Municipalities are prohibited from imposing a tax on alcoholic beverages with the following exceptions:• sales tax on alcoholic beverages sales if sales taxes are imposed on other sales within the municipality; • sales tax on alcoholic beverage sales that was in effect before July 1, 1985; and • sales and use tax on alcoholic beverages if the sale of alcoholic beverages within the municipality has been prohibited under the authority of Alaska Stat. §04.11.491. Alaska Stat. §04.21.010. For more information on taxes on alcohol and other beverages in Alaska, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Alaska 1. BNA-SUTN AK 11.1.3.4.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

No Tax Alaska does not impose a state sales and use tax. Boroughs may levy and collect sales tax on sales, rents, and on services provided in the borough and are authorized generally by the state to levy taxes and special assessments. Alaska Stat. §29.35.010(6). These authorizations to tax allow boroughs in Alaska to either subject sales of meals to tax under a general sales and use tax, or to impose a special meals tax, either in addition to or in replacement of a general sales tax. See Alaska Stat. §29.45.650(a). For more information on meals taxes in Alaska, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Alaska 10.1. BNA-SUTN AK 11.2.1.
