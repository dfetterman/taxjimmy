Depends Motor Fuel Through Aug. 31, 2021, sales of compressed natural gas (including liquefied natural gas), E85, and hydrogen fuel for use in motor vehicles are exempt from sales tax. A 20-percent partial exemption is available for B-20 biodiesel. N.Y. Tax Law § 1115(a)(42), as amended by 2015 N.Y. S.B. 6409, effective April 13, 2016; New York TSB-M-16(3)M (July 22, 2016); New York TSB-M-16(4)S (July 22, 2016). Uncompressed natural gas purchased for conversion into compressed natural gas and used or sold for use or consumption directly and exclusively in the engine of a motor vehicle is through Aug. 31, 2021. N.Y. Tax Law § 1115(a)(42), as amended by 2015 N.Y. S.B. 6409, effective April 13, 2016; New York TSB-M-16(3)M (July 22, 2016); New York TSB-M-16(4)S (July 22, 2016). Solar Energy System Equipment and Services Effective Dec. 1, 2015, retail sales of equipment for commercial or residential solar energy systems and of the service of installing such systems are exempt from New York's sales and use tax and the Metropolitan Commuter District Tax. N.Y. Tax Law §1115(ee)(1), as amended by 2015 N.Y. A.B. 3009, effective Dec. 1, 2015; N.Y. Tax Law §1115(ii)(1), as amended by 2015 N.Y. A.B. 3009, effective Dec. 1, 2015; New York Publication 718-CS; New York Publication 718-S. Also effective Dec. 1, 2015, electricity sold under qualifying power purchase agreements by persons primarily engaged in selling solar energy system equipment, electricity generated by such equipment, or both are also exempt. The electricity must be generated according to the power purchase agreement using solar energy system equipment that is: (1) owned by a person other than the purchaser of the electricity; (2) installed on the purchaser's residential or commercial premises; and (3) used to provide heating, cooling, hot water or electricity to the purchaser's premises. N.Y. Tax Law §1115(ee)(2), as amended by 2015 N.Y. A.B. 3009, effective Dec. 1, 2015; N.Y. Tax Law §1115(ii)(2), as amended by 2015 N.Y. A.B. 3009, effective Dec. 1, 2015; New York TSB-M-15(5)S (Nov. 4, 2015); New York Publication 718-PPA. Fuel Cell Generated Electricity Beginning on June 1, 2016, receipts from the sale of hydrogen gas or electricity by a seller primarily engaged in the business of selling fuel cell generating systems equipment are exempt from sales and use tax. Primarily engaged in the business of selling fuel cell generating systems equipment means that 50 percent or more of the person's receipts are derived from such sales. New York TSB-M-16(3)S (June 1, 2016). In addition, gross receipts paid or payable for the sale of such equipment, its installation and services to maintain the equipment are also exempt from sales and use tax. N.Y. Tax Law § 1115(kk), as amended by 2015 N.Y. S.B. 6409, Part WW, § 1, effective June 1, 2016. Receipts from the sale of electricity produced by commercial fuel cell generating equipment pursuant to the terms of a written agreement may also be exempt from sales and use tax as long as the equipment is owned by a person other than the purchaser of the electricity, and the equipment is actively in use for the purpose of providing heat, hot water, electricity or cooling services to nonresidential premises. N.Y. Tax Law § 1115(kk), as amended by 2015 N.Y. S.B. 6409, Part WW, § 1, effective June 1, 2016. Fuel cell electricity generating systems equipment consist of electric generating arrangements or combinations of components installed in nonresidential premises that use solid oxide, molten carbonate, proton exchange membrane or phosphoric acid fuel. N.Y. Tax Law § 1115(kk), as amended by 2015 N.Y. S.B. 6409, Part WW, § 1, effective June 1, 2016; New York TSB-M-16(3)S (June 1, 2016). Cities and counties may also elect to exempt fuel cell generating systems equipment. N.Y. Tax Law § 1115(kk); New York TSB-M-16(3)S (June 1, 2016); New York Publication 718-FC. BNA-SUTN NY 30.5.
