State: Arkansas



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Arkansas became a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement on Jan. 1, 2008. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Important Documents, Arkansas. Initially, Arkansas was admitted as an Associate Member of the agreement on July 1, 2005. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Important Documents, Arkansas; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Recertification Letter, Arkansas; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Arkansas (version 2018.0). As a full member, Arkansas maintains a seat on the governing board and may vote on amendments and interpretations of the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Arkansas (version 2018.0). On April 11, 2003, Arkansas enacted 2003 S.B. 483 to amend the state's existing sales and use tax law to conform with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. The provisions were originally scheduled to take effect the first day of the first quarter following the effective date of the Agreement; however, through subsequent legislation, 2005 S.B. 1173, 2007 S.B. 180, 2007 S.B. 273, and 2007 S.B. 281, the provisions were amended to allow the finance department and local businesses additional time to prepare for the changes. The purpose of the Agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration in order to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance. This is achieved by improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections and having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, uniformity of major tax base definitions, uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, and having a central electronic registration system for all member states. Additional purposes of the Agreement are to simplify state and local tax rates, simplify administration of tax returns, tax remittances, and tax exemptions, and protection of consumer privacy. See Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). All sellers registering under the Streamlined Sales Tax system are required to collect and remit tax on sales into Arkansas. Ark. A seller or certified service provider using a database provided by the Arkansas Department of Revenue will not be liable to the state or its local jurisdictions for charging and collecting the incorrect amount of sales or use tax, if the seller or the certified service provider relied on erroneous data provided by the department on sales or use tax rates, boundaries, taxing jurisdiction assignments, or the taxability matrix. Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Arkansas is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Certificate of Compliance, Arkansas (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, User Guide: Online Taxability Matrix and Certificate of Compliance (Nov. 20, 2018). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Arkansas was not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member Arkansas is a compact member of the Multistate Tax Commission, and has entered the provisions of the Multistate Tax Compact into the law of the state. Ark. Code Ann. ยง26-5-101, et seq.. Created by the Multistate Tax Compact, the Multistate Tax Commission is an intergovernmental state tax agency that works on behalf of states and taxpayers to equitably and efficiently administer tax laws that apply to multistate and multinational enterprises. The Commission facilitates the proper determination of state and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers, including the equitable apportionment of tax bases and settlement of apportionment disputes; promotes uniformity or compatibility in significant components of tax systems; facilitates taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration;and helps to avoid duplicative taxation. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Multistate Tax Commission. As a compact member state, Arkansas has enacted the Compact into its state law, is represented by the head of its department of taxation for the administration of its sales and use taxes to govern the Commission, and participates in a wide range of projects and programs. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Multistate Tax Commission. National Nexus Program The Commission created the National Nexus Program for the purpose of fostering increased state tax compliance by businesses that are engaged in multi-jurisdictional commerce and establishing national cooperation in the administration of state tax issues arising the in nexus area. Included in the program's focus is the possible development of a uniform nexus standard that satisfies requisite constitutional requirements, the identification of businesses involved in multi-jurisdictional commerce that are no longer in compliance with applicable state tax laws, and the establishment of a national information network with uniform confidentiality standards, along with other similar activities. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Nexus Program. Another goal of the Commission is to facilitate taxpayer compliance through education, teaching taxpayers their state tax reporting responsibilities once involved in the systematic development of a market in a specific state. The Commission also seeks to provide cooperative services to multistate taxpayers to reduce compliance burdens and to simplify the compliance process, as well as to promote fair, even-handed, and consistent state tax enforcement in the nexus area. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Nexus Program. Multistate Voluntary Disclosure Program The Commission's National Nexus Program also assists businesses that are involved in multistate commerce to voluntarily resolve potential state sales and use tax liabilities where nexus is the central issue. Taxpayers benefit from participation in this program by resolving potential state tax disputes before states issue assessments of taxes from prior years, as well as interest and penalties. This multistate resolution effort saves time and money for taxpayers;furthermore, the Commission's staff perform this work at no cost to the participating taxpayer. Multistate Tax Commission, Multistate Voluntary Disclosure Program. BNA-SUTN AR 2.2.
