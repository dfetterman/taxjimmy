Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Depends Gross receipts or gross proceeds derived from the sale of biodiesel fuel are exempt from gross receipts tax. If the fuels tax has not been paid, such sales will be subject to sales and use tax. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-401(11)(A)(iv)(a). “Biodiesel fuel”is defined as “a diesel fuel substitute produced from nonpetroleum renewable resources.” Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-401(11)(A)(iv)(b). For more information on motor fuel and special fuel excise tax in Arkansas, see Excise Taxes Navigator, at Arkansas 8. BNA-SUTN AR 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Taxable The sale of gas, electricity, water, ice, steam, or any other tangible personal property sold as a utility or provided as a public service are subject to the state of Arkansas gross receipts and compensating use tax. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-301(2); Ark. Code Ann. §26-53-106(a); Ark. Regs. GR-6(A); Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20151202 (Aug. 18, 2017) (purchases of solar cooperative shares that entitle member access to solar power output and act as a credit against member's purchase of electricity are taxable as a sale of electricity). Low Income Residential Utility Customer Tax Exemption The first 500 kilowatt hours of electricity per month sold to a residential customer whose household income does not exceed $12,000 per year is exempt from the tax. Ark. Regs. GR-6(B)(2)(a). Also exempt from taxation is the total franchise taxes billed to each residential customer whose household income does not exceed $12,000 per year. Ark. Regs. GR-6(B)(2)(b). The exemption must be claimed by March 1 or the year following the year in which the customer's income did not exceed $12,000. Ark. Regs. GR-6(B)(4). When the household income for the year exceeds $12,000, the customer is disqualified from the exemption. Ark. Regs. GR-6(B)(5). BNA-SUTN AR 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Exempt Arkansas exempts the full purchase price for machinery and equipment used directly in mining and quarrying. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-402(b)(1) . Mining and quarrying qualify as manufacturing or processing activities, thus, machinery and equipment used directly in such activities, and catalysts, chemicals, reagents, and solutions consumed or used in these activities, are eligible for exemption from the sales and use tax. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-402(b)(1); Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-401(35); Ark. Code Ann. §26-53-114(b)(1); Ark. Regs. GR-55(F)(4); Ark. Regs. GR-59. Exempt machinery includes machinery and equipment used to wash, grade, and separate the mined or quarried articles of commerce if such operation is carried on at the same site and as part of the continuous mining operation. Additionally, machinery used to strip overburden, drills used to prepare rock for using explosives, quarry trucks used to deliver rocks to the primary crusher if not capable of being registered for use on the highways, and primary crushers that reduce the size of the rock are exempt from tax. However, dump trucks and other transportation vehicles that are capable of being registered for use on highways generally do not qualify for the exemption. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-402(b)(1); Ark. Code Ann. §26-53-114(b)(1); Ark. Regs. GR-55(F)(4); Ark. Regs. GR-59; Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20160414 (April 15, 2016). See Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20160914 (March 6, 2018) (dump trucks and transportation vehicles that are capable of being registered for use on highways could qualify for the exemption depending on the facts). BNA-SUTN AR 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Timber

Exempt Effective July 1, 2014, Arkansas exempts the full purchase price for machinery, equipment, and attachments relating to the harvesting of timber. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-431, as amended by 2013 Ark. S.B. 299, effective July 1, 2014. Before July 1, 2014, Arkansas only provided an exemption for the first $50,000 put toward the purchase of machinery, equipment, and attachments relating to the harvesting of timber. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-431(a). To qualify for the exemption, the purchaser must be engaged primarily in the activity of harvesting timber and the machinery must be used exclusively in the off-road activity of harvesting timber. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-431(b). Furthermore, the exemption does not apply to any purchases relating to repairs or replacement parts. Ark. Code Ann. §26-52-431(c). BNA-SUTN AR 30.3.3.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Wastewater Treatment

Exempt Sales of machinery and equipment for use in a city or county wastewater treatment plant or by a manufacturing or process plant are exempt from tax if the equipment is used to remove contaminants from wastewater. Ark. Regs. GR-66(E). Examples of exempt wastewater treatment machinery includes: pipes, pumps, valves, screens, screen baskets, gates, blowers, fans, skimmers, aerators, diffusers, equalization basins, concrete flumes, conveyor belts, flow meters, grit separators, grit removal equipment, back flow preventers, chlorination disinfection equipment, digester equipment, vacuators, and air eductors. Ark. Regs. GR-66(E); see Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20170309 (June 26, 2017) (headworks bar screen is exempt when used to actively remove wastewater contaminants;however, corrosion resistant coatings do not actively remove or alter impurities in water, and thus, are not exempt, event though coatings are applied to equipment that removes contaminants from water). To qualify for the wastewater tax exemption, the taxpayer must meet three initial criteria:• the taxpayer must be either a manufacturing or processing plant or facility or a city or town in Arkansas; • the taxpayer must secure certification from state or federal pollution control authorities that the machinery or equipment is required for pollution control purposes; and • the machinery or equipment for which the exemption is being claimed must be used to remove contaminants from wastewater during the treatment process. Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20180919 (April 23, 2019); Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20180222 (April 9, 2018) (a private, tax-exempt homeowner association purchasing equipment and supplies for improvements to wastewater treatment plants does not qualify for the wastewater treatment plant exemption). Lab equipment used to test wastewater does not qualify for exemption. Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20170113 (May 12, 2017). Aerators and associated ancillary equipment necessary for wastewater treatment qualify for the exemption. Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20180172 (April 24, 2018); Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20170816 (Sept. 5, 2017). Also qualifying are clarification equipment, cloth media filtration systems, disinfection equipment, and ancillary equipment other than that used to control the flow of wastewater into a facility. Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20180172 (April 24, 2018). Additionally, the Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration has ruled that ultraviolet radiation disinfection systems qualify for the exemption. Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20180172 (April 24, 2018); Arkansas Revenue Legal Counsel Opinion No. 20170209 (May 17, 2017). BNA-SUTN AR 30.7.
