Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Exempt The sale of electricity by public utilities is exempt from sales and use tax, but utilities are subject to a separate utility privilege and license tax in Alabama. See Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(8) (exemptions from sales tax); Ala. Code §40-21-82 (application of privilege and license taxes). Alabama also exempts from taxation the gross receipts of utility products sold by others who are not public utilities. Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(8); Ala. Code §40-23-62, as amended by 2018 Ala. H.B. 242 effective April 6, 2018. However, utility products sold by others who are not public utilities are subject to the state's utility privilege and license tax. Ala. Code §40-21-80(a)(8); Ala. Code §40-21-82. For more information on taxes on public utilities in Alabama, see Excise Taxes Navigator, at Alabama 11.1. BNA-SUTN AL 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Taxable Sales of machines, machine parts, and machine attachments used in mining, quarrying, compounding, processing, and manufacturing of tangible personal property are taxed at a reduced rate in Alabama. Ala. Code §40-23-2(3); Ala. Code §40-23-61(b). The term “machine”includes machinery which is used for mining, quarrying, compounding, processing, or manufacturing tangible personal property, and the parts of the machines, attachments, and replacements which are made or manufactured for use on or in the operation of the machines and which are necessary to the operation of the machines in Alabama. Ala. Code §40-23-2(3); Ala. Code §40-23-61(b). For more information regarding machines, see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Alabama 27.1.5 Alabama imposes a uniform severance tax on natural minerals severed from the ground within Alabama and sold as tangible personal property. For more information about this tax, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Alabama 12.1. BNA-SUTN AL 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Oil and Gas Machinery and Equipment

Exempt Alabama exempts from sales and use tax gross proceeds from sales of machinery and equipment used exclusively used for the exploration for or production of oil, gas, sulphur, or other minerals in offshore federal waters. Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(43)b; Ala. Code §40-23-62, as amended by 2018 Ala. H.B. 242 effective April 6, 2018. Sales of materials, equipment, and machinery which become a component part of ships, vessels, towing vessels or barges, or drilling ships, rigs or barges, or seismic or geophysical vessels of over five tons load displacement as registered with the U.S. Coast Guard and licensed by the Alabama Department of Conservation and Natural Resources, are exempt from tax. Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(13). Fuel used in foreign, international, or interstate commerce aboard watercraft, including ships, vessels, towing vessels or barges, drilling ships, rigs or barges, or seismic or geophysical vessels, is exempt from tax in Alabama. Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(10). Alabama imposes an annual oil and gas privilege tax on all persons engaged in the business of producing or severing oil or gas from beneath the soil or water. Alabama also imposes an oil and gas production tax on the production of oil or natural gas severed from any well or wells in Alabama. BNA-SUTN AL 30.3.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Timber

No Guidance Alabama does not have any specific authority relating to timber. Ala. Code §40-23-2. Alabama imposes a forest products severance tax on severing of timber or any other forest product from the soil for sale, profit or commercial use and on every processor or manufacturer (in-state or out-of-state) using the forest products. An additional privilege tax is levied on processors and manufacturers using timber. For more information, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Alabama 12.4 BNA-SUTN AL 30.3.3.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Production

Exempt Alabama exempts the sale of wood residue, coal and/or coke to manufacturers and certain companies for certain uses including producing by-products or generating heat or power used in manufacturing tangible personal property for sale. Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(9); Ala. Code §40-23-62, as amended by 2018 Ala. H.B. 242 effective April 6, 2018. Specifically, Alabama exempts the sale of wood residue, coal, and/or coke to manufacturers and certain companies for use or consumption in producing by-products, or generating heat or power used in manufacturing tangible personal property for sale, for generating electric power or energy for use in manufacturing tangible personal property for sale or for resale, or for generating motive power for transportation. Ala. Code §40-23-4(a)(9); Ala. Code §40-23-62, as amended by 2018 Ala. H.B. 242 effective April 6, 2018. BNA-SUTN AL 30.4.
