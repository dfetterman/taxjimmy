State: Hawaii



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Nonmember Hawaii is an advisory state to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project and is not a member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, although it has enacted legislation authorizing it to enter into the Agreement. Haw. Rev. Stat. ยง255D-1 et seq. Hawaii enacted 2005 Haw. H.B. 1224 on July 12, 2005, in order to work toward bringing the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Although the measure did little to change Hawaii's existing sales tax laws, the state Legislature had found that few amendments would be necessary to bring Hawaii into compliance. 2005 Haw. H.B. 1224, effective July 12, 2015; see also Hawaii Announcement No. 2003-11 (June 30, 2003). On January 23, 2015, legislation was introduced in the Hawaii Senate that would conform Hawaii's general excise tax with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. As of December 17, 2015, the bill was carried over into the 2016 regular session where it died in committee. 2015 Haw. S.B. 259, introduced Jan. 23, 2015. BNA-SUTN HI 2.1.1.
