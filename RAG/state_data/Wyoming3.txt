Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Nontaxable Wyoming levies an excise tax on gas and diesel fuel based on the volume of gas or diesel sold, used or distributed for sale or use within the state. Fuel subject to the excise tax is not subject to sales and use taxes. Wyo. Stat. §39-17-101 - Wyo. Stat. §39-17-111; Wyo. Stat. §39-17-201 - Wyo. Stat. §39-17-211; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(v)(C). Diesel fuels refer to those combustible gases and liquids commonly referred to as diesel fuel or any other volatile liquid of less than 46 degrees American petroleum industry gravity test, except liquid petroleum gas, when actually sold for use in motor vehicles for operation upon public roads and highways. Diesel fuels include jet fuel, natural gas, oil, shale or coal and sold under the name of jet fuel and kerosene and any type of additive when the additive is mixed or blended into diesel fuel, excluding a pour point depressant. Wyo. Stat. §39-17-201(a)(xxi). BNA-SUTN WY 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Taxable Charges by public utilities for the furnishing of gas, electricity or heat services for domestic, industrial, or commercial consumption are generally taxable. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-103(a)(i)(E). However, the following charges by utilities are exempt from taxation:• sales of water delivered by pipeline or truck; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(vi)(D); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-105(a)(vi)(D). • sales of fuel used as boiler fuel in the production of electricity; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(iii)(G); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-105(a)(iii)(C). • metered sales of water by a landlord to tenants, when there are no additional charges added by the landlord. Wyo. Stat. §37-1-101(a)(vi)(H)(VIII), as amended by 2015 Wyo. H.B. 31, effective July 1, 2015. • sales of power and fuel for direct use in manufacturing, processing or agriculture. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(iii)(D). The sale of gas, electricity, or heat for domestic, industrial or commercial consumption is generally subject to the state of Wyoming sales and use tax, with exceptions, and the county and resort district local sales and use taxes apply to the same base as the state sales tax. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-103(a)(i)(E); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-202(a); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-203(a)(i)(E); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-205; Wyo. Stat. §39-16-203(a)(i)(E). Therefore, the sale of gas, electricity, or heat for domestic, industrial, or commercial consumption is subject to the county and resort district local sales and use taxes. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-103(a)(i)(E). BNA-SUTN WY 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Nontaxable Extracting ore from the earth is a service to real property, relates to the work of a contractor, and is not subject to sales and use tax. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication - Mining Industry. Wyoming provides that services to real property are not subject to sales and use tax. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication - Mining Industry. However, purchases of property for use in performing such services are subject to sales and use tax. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication - Mining Industry. Note that subsequent processing may qualify for the manufacturing exemption. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication - Mining Industry. BNA-SUTN WY 30.3.1.

Taxable Services associated with well site development are subject to tax. This includes exploration, seismographic activities, geophysical activities, bringing power to the site, locating power poles, dirt work, rigging up, drilling, coring, logging, and testing. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(B); Wyoming Dyed Diesel Bulletin; In re Bar S Servs., Inc., Wyoming Administrative Decision 2014-09 (Mar. 31, 2015); Wyoming Taxing Issues - March 2016. H.B. 51, effective July 1, 2015; Wyoming Taxing Issues - March 2016. Services provided to an oil, gas, or coal drill site before production has begun are not subject to tax. Services are not subject to tax until completion of under-reaming or the attainment of total depth of the oil or gas well. After that period, all third-party services performed in an area where production equipment is installed to store or prepare oil or gas for transportation are subject to tax. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(B); Wyoming Dyed Diesel Bulletin; In re Bar S Servs., Inc., Wyoming Administrative Decision 2014-09 (Mar. 31, 2015); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication - Oil, Conventional Natural Gas and Coal Bed Methane Industries. Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Taxable Sales of machinery and equipment used to generate power from renewable sources are subject to tax in Wyoming. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. However, from Jan. 1, 2010 until December 31, 2011, Wyoming exempts from taxation the sale of equipment used in generating electricity from renewable resources. The exemption is limited to acquiring equipment to be used in making the project operational so that it may connect with an existing transmission grid. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. Sales of equipment used to generate electricity from renewable resources with a total net rating capacity of not more than twenty-five (25) kilowatts, or where the entire renewable energy system is to be for off-grid use, are exempt until June 30, 2012. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. Renewable resources include, but are not limited to:• wind generation; • solar; • biomass; • landfill gas; • hydro; • hydrogen;and • geothermal energy. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. Existing transmission grids include:• turbines; • generating equipment; • control and monitoring systems; • power lines; • lighting; • pipes;and • other equipment for locating power lines and poles. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. The exemption is limited to the acquisition of equipment used in a project to make it operational up to the point of interconnection with an existing transmission grid including wind turbines, generating equipment, control and monitoring systems, power lines, substation equipment, lighting, fencing, pipes and other equipment for locating power lines and poles. The exemption shall not apply to tools and other equipment used in construction of a new facility, contracted services required for construction and routine maintenance activities and equipment utilized or acquired after the project is operational. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. This is applicable as follows: • On and after Jan. 1, 2010, the exemption shall apply to sales of equipment for projects where either the project developer is the landowner for the project prior to Jan. 1, 2010, or where the project developer has, prior to Jan. 1, 2010, entered into a written contract with a landowner that describes the project with specificity, including a description of equipment to be purchased and placed on the landowner's land and has made payment to the landowner under terms of the contract. To be eligible for the exemption, the project developer shall have received all required permits and approvals from all governmental agencies for the development and construction of the project. Evidence of compliance with this subparagraph shall be submitted to the department with the request for an exemption. Equipment eligible for the exemption shall be purchased and delivered within the state of Wyoming on or before Dec. 31, 2011 for storage, use or consumption by the developer to qualify for the exemption. The exemption provided under this subdivision of this subparagraph is repealed effective Dec. 31, 2011. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. • On and after Jan. 1, 2010, the exemption shall apply to sales of equipment used to generate electricity from renewable resources with a total net rating capacity of not more than twenty-five (25) kilowatts, or where the entire renewable energy system is to be for off-grid use. The exemption provided under this subdivision of this subparagraph is repealed effective June 30, 2012. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(viii)(N), as repealed by 2017 Wyo. H.B. 36, §2, effective July 1, 2017. BNA-SUTN WY 30.5.
