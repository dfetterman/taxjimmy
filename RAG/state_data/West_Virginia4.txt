Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Repair and Replacement Parts

Taxable Sales of aircraft repair and replacement parts used in transportation are subject to sales and use taxes in West Virginia. W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15-64.2. However, sales of parts and materials used to maintain, repair, rebuild, or recondition aircraft used exclusively for rental purposes are exempt when tax is charged on the rental of the aircraft. W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15-129.11.5. Additionally, effective Sept. 1, 2018, tangible personal property that is permanently affixed or permanently attached, as part of a repair, remodeling, or maintenance service, as a component part of an aircraft operated under a fractional ownership program is exempt from West Virginia's sales and use tax. W. Va. Code §11-15-9p, as added by 2018 W. Va. H.B. 4022, effective May 31, 2018; W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15k-3, effective April 6, 2019 through April 9, 2024. A fractional ownership program is a system of aircraft ownership and exchange that consists of:• management services provided by a single program manager on behalf of fractional owners; • two or more airworthy aircraft; • one or more fractional owners per program aircraft, with at least one program aircraft having at least one owner; • possession of at least a minimum ownership interest in one or more program aircraft by each fractional owner; • a dry-lease aircraft exchange arrangement among all the fractional owners; and • multi-year program agreements covering the ownership, program management services, and dry-lease aircraft exchange aspects of the program. W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15k-2.2.f, effective April 6, 2019 through April 9, 2024 BNA-SUTN WV 29.1.2.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Fuel

Taxable Sales of gasoline and special fuel are subject to tax in West Virginia. W. Va. Code §11-15-18(a). Sales of aviation gasoline and jet fuel are exempt from the applicable flat rate that applies to sales of motor fuels. However, they are subject to sales and use taxes at the same variable rate applied to sales of gasoline, which is set every year according to state calculation of 5 percent of the average wholesale price per gallon of gasoline. W. Va. Code §11-14C-1; W. Va. Code §11-14C-5(a); W. Va. Code §11-14C-9(a)(2). The variable fuel tax rate is 12.7¢ per gallon from Jan. 1, 2016, through Dec. 31, 2016. West Virginia Administrative Notice 2015-26 (Nov. 20, 2015). The variable fuel tax rate was 14.1¢ per gallon from Jan. 1, 2015, through Dec. 31, 2015. West Virginia Administrative Notice 2014-26 (Nov. 25, 2013). The variable fuel tax rate was 15.2¢ per gallon from Jan. 1, 2014, through Dec. 31, 2014. West Virginia Administrative Notice 2013-26 (Nov. 25, 2013). BNA-SUTN WV 29.1.6.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Depends Motor vehicles sales to West Virginia residents are subject to a 5 percent sales tax in West Virginia. W. Va. Code §11-15-3c(e). West Virginia exempts from tax the sale of vehicles required by law to be titled with the Division of Motor Vehicles, and which are subject to the 5 percent motor vehicle tax set forth in W. Va. Code §17A-3-4 for certification of title, or a similar tax. W. Va. Code §11-15-9(a)(4). West Virginia's sales tax does not apply to:• sales of passenger vehicles offered for rent in the normal course of business; • certain intercompany vehicle transfers; • sales of motor vehicles acquired by a qualified, exempt senior citizen service organization; • sales of active duty military personnel stationed outside West Virginia who purchase a motor vehicle within nine months from the date the person returns to West Virginia; • sales of motor vehicles acquired by dealers for resale only; • sales of motor vehicles acquired by the state of West Virginia or a political subdivision thereof, or by nonprofit volunteer fire departments or rescue or ambulance squads; • sales of motor vehicles to an exempt urban mass transit authority; and • the registration of vehicles owned by West Virginia residents who can show proof of residency and of registration of the vehicle, in another state. W. Va. Code §11-15-3c(f). Rentals of motor vehicles for a period greater than 30 consecutive days are considered leases and are exempt from the sales tax. However, a separate tax is imposed on the monthly payments for the lease of any motor vehicle under a written contract by a resident of West Virginia for a contractually specified continuous period exceeding 30 days. This tax is equal to 5 percent of the amount of the monthly payment, applied to each payment, and continues for the entire term of the initial lease period. The tax must be remitted to the Division of Motor Vehicles on a monthly basis by the lessor of the vehicle. Rentals of 30 days or less are subject to sales tax. W. Va. Code §11-15-3c(f)(10); W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15-45.5. “Motor vehicle” means every propellable device in or on which any person or property is or may be transported or drawn on a highway. W. Va. Code §11-15-3c(e). Sales and use taxes do not apply to sales of trucks, truck tractors, or road tractors registered at a gross weight of 55,000 pounds or greater (Class B), or to sales of trailers, semitrailers, full trailers, pole trailers, or converter gear having a weight of 2,000 pounds or greater (Class C). W. Va. Code §11-15-3c(f)(9). BNA-SUTN WV 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Fuel

Taxable Sales of motor vehicle fuels such as gasoline and diesel fuel are subject to the flat rate tax component of motor fuel excise tax and are also subject to sales and use taxes at a rate that equals the variable rate component. W. Va. Code §11-15-18b(a); West Virginia Publication TSD-410; see also West Virginia Administrative Notice No. 2015-26 (Nov 20, 2015) (providing the average wholesale price of conventional motor fuels and “alternative” motor fuels for the 2016 calendar year). “Motor fuel”means gasoline, blended fuel, aviation fuel, and any special fuel. W. Va. Code §11-14C-2(61), as renumbered by 2013 W. Va. S.B. 454, effective July 1, 2014; West Virginia Administrative Notice No. 2013-26 (Nov. 25, 2013). Beginning Jan. 1, 2014, motor fuel includes alternative fuel, which is defined as butane, compressed natural gas, liquefied natural gas, liquefied petroleum gas, and other specified fuels. W. Va. Code §11-14C-2(4). “Special fuel” means any gas or liquid, other than gasoline, used or suitable for use as fuel in an internal combustion engine. W. Va. Code §11-14-2(17). Examples of special fuel include products commonly known as natural or casing head gasoline, but do not include alternative fuel or any petroleum product or chemical compound such as alcohol, industrial solvent, heavy furnace oil, lubricant, or so on not commonly used nor practicably suited for use as fuel in an internal combustion engine. W. Va. Code §11-14-2(17), as amended by 2013 W. Va. S.B. 454, effective July 12, 2013; West Virginia Office of Tax Appeals Decision Docket No. 13-337 MFE (June 17, 2014) (propane does not need to be used exclusively in an internal combustion engine in order to be considered special fuel). For more information about the Motor Fuel Excise Tax, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at West Virginia 8. BNA-SUTN WV 29.3.6.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Railroad Rolling Stock, Interstate and Common Carrier Fuel

Taxable Sales of gasoline or special fuel for use or consumed in railroad diesel locomotives is exempt from special fuel excise tax, but subject to motor fuels tax at the flat rate, and sales and use taxes at the variable component rate. W. Va. Code §11-14-5(15). In West Virginia, sales of motor fuels may be subject to a flat rate tax component of the motor fuel excise tax, to a variable tax rate component, or to both. Sales of motor vehicle fuels such as gasoline and diesel fuel are subject to the flat rate tax component of motor fuel excise tax and are also subject to sales and use taxes Jan. 1 at a rate that equals the variable rate component. W. Va. Code §11-15-18b(a); West Virginia Publication TSD-410; see also West Virginia Administrative Notice No. 2015-26 (Nov. 20, 2015) (providing the average wholesale price of conventional motor fuels and “alternative” motor fuels for the 2016 calendar year). Motor fuel means gasoline, blended fuel, and any special fuel. Beginning Jan. 1, 2014, motor fuel includes alternative fuel, which is defined as butane, compressed natural gas, liquefied natural gas, liquefied petroleum gas, and other specified fuels. W. Va. Code §11-14C-2(4), as renumbered by 2013 W. Va. S.B. 454, effective July 1, 2014; West Virginia Administrative Notice No. 2013-26 (Nov. 25, 2013). Sales of fuel used by rolling stock transporting passengers or freight in West Virginia is subject to sales and use taxes on the average wholesale price of all gallons of gasoline or special fuel, but only on that portion of fuel used in the transportation operation within West Virginia. Locomotive unit miles should be used by railroads for calculations. W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15-11.4; W.Va. Code R. tit. 110, §110-15-2.52. Beginning Jan. 1, 2018, sales of motor fuel used or consumed in railroad diesel locomotives are eligible for a refundable exemption of the variable rate component of the motor fuel excise tax. The refundable exemption is limited to an annual aggregate amount of $4,300,000 to all taxpayers claiming the exemption. W. Va. Code §11-14C-9(14), as amended by 2016 W. Va. S.B. 582, effective June 3, 2016. BNA-SUTN WV 29.8.6.
