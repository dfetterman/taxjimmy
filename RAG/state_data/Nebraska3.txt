State: Nebraska



Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Depends Biodiesel for use on the highways is not subject to sales and use tax. However, biodiesel sold for other purposes would be subject to sales and use tax. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2704.05; Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2701.16. Nebraska levies an excise tax on all motor fuels such as biodiesel based on the volume of fuels sold, used or distributed for sale or use within Nebraska. Motor fuels subject to the excise tax are not subject to sales and use taxes. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2704.05. For more information on motor fuel excise taxes, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Nebraska Section 8.1. BNA-SUTN NE 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Taxable Utilities are subject to the state of Nebraska sales and use tax. Nebraska imposes sales and use tax on the gross receipts from the furnishing of electricity services, other than electricity supplied to customer-generators. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2701.16(2)(c). A customer-generator is an end-use electricity customer that generates electricity on the customer's side of the meter. For customer-generators, only the net energy use upon billing or statements is subject to tax. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2701.16(2)(c)(ii), , as amended by 2013 Neb. L.B. 90, effective June 5, 2013. See also Nebraska Revenue Ruling 01-16-2 (revoking Nebraska Revenue Ruling 01-10-3, issued Aug. 4, 2010, which had concluded that the gross amount was subject to tax). For additional discussion of utilities tax in Nebraska, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Nebraska 11.1. BNA-SUTN NE 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Steam

Nontaxable Nebraska does not subject the sale of heat in the form of steam to sales and use tax providing the steam is returned to the utility company through a closed system. Nebraska Revenue Ruling 1-76-13. Where the steam is piped to the customer, circulated through the customer's heating system, and then piped back to the supplier there is neither a sale of tangible personal property nor a sale of a taxable service. Nebraska Revenue Ruling 1-76-13. BNA-SUTN NE 30.2.3.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Production

Depends Nebraska does not provide an explicit exemption for machinery and equipment purchased for energy production purposes, but authorizes a refund of sales and use tax paid by qualifying taxpayers using renewable energy to produce electricity for sale. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2701.47(1). Nebraska Advantage Act Nebraska allows a refund of all sales and use tax paid by a taxpayer that uses one or more sources of renewable energy to produce electricity for sale and that meets the investment and wage requirements under the Nebraska Advantage Act . Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-5725(1)(e)(ii); see Nebraska Revenue Ruling 29-17-2 (Dec. 13, 2017). A refund is allowed for sales and use tax paid on all purchases and rentals of:• qualified property used as a part of the project; • property, excluding motor vehicles, based in Nebraska and used in both the state and another state in connection with the project, except when the property is to be used for fundraising for or for the transportation of an elected official; • tangible personal property by a contractor or repair person after appointment as a purchasing agent of the owner of the improvement to real estate when such property is incorporated into real estate as a part of a project. The refund is based on 50 percent of the contract price excluding any land, as the cost of materials subject to the sales and use tax; • tangible personal property by a contractor or repair person after appointment as a purchasing agent of the taxpayer when such property is annexed to, but not incorporated into, real estate as a part of a project. The refund is based on the cost of materials subject to the sales and use tax that were annexed to real estate; and • tangible personal property by a contractor or repair person after appointment as a purchasing agent of the taxpayer when such property is both (A)incorporated into real estate as a part of a project and (B) annexed to, but not incorporated into, real estate as a part of a project. The refund is based on 50 percent of the contract price, excluding any land, as the cost of materials subject to the sales and use tax. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-5725(2)(a). Applicants filing for the renewable energy project benefit on or after Jan. 1, 2017, must meet an investment requirement of $20 million. Nebraska Revenue Ruling 29-16-1 (Nov. 21, 2016); Nebraska Revenue Ruling 29-18-1 (Nov. 15, 2018). Applicants filing between Jan. 1, 2016, and Dec. 31, 2016 were required to meet the same $20 million investment. Nebraska Revenue Ruling 29-15-1 (Dec. 17, 2015). An application guide and 2015 and 2016 Nebraska Advantage Applications are available on the Department of Revenue's website. The Department has also issued a ruling on making multiple Nebraska Advantage applications. Nebraska Revenue Ruling 29-17-2 (Dec. 13, 2017). Failure to maintain an average number of employees greater than or equal to the number of employees required in the base year results in a partial recapture of the benefits. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-5725(1)(e). For more information on the Nebraska Advantage Act, see Credits and Incentives Navigator, at Nebraska 6.1. BNA-SUTN NE 30.4.
