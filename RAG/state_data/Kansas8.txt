State: Kansas



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Taxable Kansas imposes sales tax upon the sale of food and food ingredients, candy, soft drinks, alcohol, tobacco, food sold through vending machines, and prepared food. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3603(a); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kansas (version 2018.0). Kansas incorporates the definition of food and food ingredients as found in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Kan. Stat. Ann. § 79-3602; Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018. Food Sales Tax Rebate for Qualifying Individuals and Families Effective Jan. 1, 2013, the food sales tax refund statutes were repealed and the refund is no longer allowed. Kansas Notice 12-15. Until Jan. 1, 2013, Kansas allowed each household having at least one claimant with an income of less than $17,500 to receive a retailer's sales tax refund of $90 per member of the household per year. Where the household had at least one claimant with an income between $17,500 and $35,000, the household was eligible to receive a sales tax refund of $45 per member of the household per year. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3635(a)(1)(Repealed). If the household so chose, rather than receiving a refund, the amount of refund entitled to each member of the household could be used to offset each member's individual income tax liability. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3635(a)(2)(Repealed). A claimant was any person who has filed a claim for refund with the state, who during the previous calendar year was a resident of Kansas and was a member of a household. The claimant was not permitted to have income exceeding $35,000 in the previous calendar year and had to be: Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3633(c)(Repealed). • a person with a disability; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3633(c)(1)(Repealed). • a person who is at least 55 years old; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3633(c)(2)(Repealed). or • a person with dependent children under the age of 18 residing with them. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3633(c)(3)(Repealed). The claimant was required to provide the Department of Revenue with reasonable proof of age or disability, as well as household income. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3637 (Repealed). In 2012, the food sales tax refund was available to individuals who were Kansas residents for the entire year, whose household income was less than $35,401, and who were 55 years of age or older, or totally and permanently disabled or blind, or who have a dependent under the age of 18 living with them the entire year. Kansas Notice 12-15. BNA-SUTN KS 11.1.1.

Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • the product contains any of the following ingredients or any combination of any of the following ingredients: Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • a vitamin; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • a mineral; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • an herb or other botanical; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • an amino acid; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • a dietary substance intended for human consumption to supplement the diet by increasing total dietary intake; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). or • a concentrate, metabolite, constituent, or extract. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(1); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). • the product is intended for ingestion in tablet, capsule, powder, soft-gel, gel-cap, or liquid form, or, if not intended for ingestion in such forms, is not represented as conventional food and is not represented for use as the sole item of a meal or diet; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(2); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). and • the product is required to be labeled as a dietary supplement as required under 21 CFR 101.36. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(jjj)(3); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3704(d). BNA-SUTN KS 11.1.3.3.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Alcoholic Beverages

Depends Whether alcoholic beverages are taxable depends on the type of beverage and where they are sold. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(n); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(a); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-4101. Kansas imposes an excise tax on certain alcoholic beverages in lieu of sales tax. Specifically, Kansas exempts from sales tax all alcoholic beverages that are subject to excise taxes, except for cereal malt beverages and malt products. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(hhh), as amended by 2017 Kan. H.B. 2502, effective April 1, 2019; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(n); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(a); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-4101. Alcohol is specifically excluded from the definition of “food and food ingredients.” Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(n). Effective April 1, 2019, retailers licensed under the cereal malt beverage act and retail liquor stores may sell both cereal malt beverages and beer. Retailers licensed under the cereal malt beverage act, commonly including grocery stores, convenience stores, and drug stores, must charge state and local sales tax on their sales of commercial malt beverages and on their sales of beer. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(hhh), as amended by 2017 Kan. H.B. 2502, effective April 1, 2019; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(n); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(a); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-4101; Kansas Notice 18-04 (July 1, 2018). Effective April 1, 2019, retail liquor stores should not charge sales tax on the sale of alcoholic liquor, cereal malt beverages, and non-alcoholic malt beverages. Other goods or services sold by a retail liquor store on their licensed premises (excluding the sale of lottery tickets) are subject to state and local sales tax. Sales of beer and cereal malt beverages by clubs are drinking establishments continue to be subject to a liquor drink tax. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(hhh), as amended by 2017 Kan. H.B. 2502, effective April 1, 2019; Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3602(n); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(a); Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-4101, as amended by 2019 Kan. H.B. 2035, effective April 1, 2019; Kansas Notice 19-01 (April 19, 2019); Kansas Notice 18-04 (July 1, 2018). For more information about excise taxes on alcohol, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Kansas 1.1. BNA-SUTN KS 11.1.3.4.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Depends Boarding homes are required to pay sales tax upon their purchase of food. Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-19-21(a); Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-20-4. When a boardinghouse serves a meal only to its residents, a meal that is not open to the public, no sales tax need be collected on the price of the meal. However, if the meal was open to the public, the boardinghouse must collect sales tax on each meal served. Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-19-21(a); Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-20-4. Schools When an educational school or institution operates its own lunchroom, cafeteria, or dining room in order to provide meals to its students or teachers, sales tax is not collected on the cost of each meal. However, should the school or educational institution serve meals to the general public, sales tax must be collected on all meals served. Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-19-21(c); Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-20-4. Hospitals When a private or public hospital that is operated not-for-profit operates a cafeteria, lunchroom, or dining room for the sole purpose of providing meals to its employees, no sales tax is charged upon the cost of each meal. However, if the lunchroom, dinning room, cafeteria is open to the public, all meals are subject to sales tax. Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-19-21(d); Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-20-4. Railway Train The sale of a meal by a railway train operating a dining car is subject to sales tax if the food is purchased while the train is within the state. Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-19-21(e); Kan. Admin. Regs. 92-20-4. Food in Exchange for Community Service Sales of food for human consumption by an I.R.C. §501(c)(3) organization that offers such food at a price below cost in exchange for the performance of community service by the purchaser are exempt. Kan. Stat. Ann. § 79-3606(bbb). Meals for the Indigent and Homebound Kansas exempts from sales tax food used in preparing meals for delivery to the homebound elderly and to homebound disabled people. The exemption extends to meals served in a group setting for otherwise homebound elderly and disabled persons. For purposes of this exemption, the term elderly is meant to signify any person 60 years of age or older. Kan. Stat. Ann. §79-3606(v). BNA-SUTN KS 11.10.
