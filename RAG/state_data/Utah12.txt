State: Utah



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Compact Member Utah became a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement on Oct. 1, 2012. Before becoming a full member, Utah was an associate member beginning Oct. 1, 2005. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102.3;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Utah (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Utah (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN UT 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Utah is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Utah (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Utah is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). In 2015, Utah raised an issue regarding the treatment of prewritten computer software under Utah's law compared with the treatment in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Utah State Tax Comn., Letter to Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board (Sept. 22, 2015). Utah enacted 2003 S.B. 147 and 2004 H.B. 273 to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Although the bulk of the conforming provisions took effect July 1, 2004, Utah enacted 2004 S.B. 3001, which delayed the effective date for certain administrative and health-related definitions and bad debts provisions. Moreover, 2005 H.B. 107, effective July 1, 2005, delayed provisions relating to sourcing, simplified electronic returns, amnesty, monetary allowances, and rate changes. Utah enacted 2008 H.B. 206, effective Jan. 1, 2009, which brought the state into further compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. See Utah Code Ann. §59-1-801.5; Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102; Utah Code Ann. §59-1-1201. The purpose of the Agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance through improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections, having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, having uniformity of major tax base definitions, having a central electronic registration system for all member states, simplifying state and local tax rates, having uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, simplifying administration of exemptions, having simplified tax returns, simplifying tax remittances, and protecting consumer privacy. See Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN UT 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Full Member Utah is a full member of the Multistate Tax Compact. Utah Code Ann. §59-1-801.5. The purpose of the compact is to:• facilitate proper determination of state and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers; Utah Code Ann. §59-1-801.5(1). • promote uniformity or compatibility in significant components of tax system; Utah Code Ann. §59-1-801.5(2). • facilitate taxpayer convenience and compliance in filing tax returns and in other phases of tax administration; Utah Code Ann. §59-1-801.5(3). and • avoid duplicative taxation. Utah Code Ann. §59-1-801.5(4). Effective July 1, 2014, Utah repealed the statutory authorization for its tax commission to participate in audits, tax enforcement, and tax administration with the Multistate Tax Commission, a taxing official of another state, or the federal government. Utah Code Ann. §59-1-809, as amended by 2014 Utah S.B. 214, effective July 1, 2014. BNA-SUTN UT 2.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

IFTA The Utah State Tax Commission is authorized to enter into interstate agreements related to motor fuel and special motor fuels to exchange information and audit users of such fuels. Utah Code Ann. §59-13-501. Utah entered into the International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA), effective Jan. 1, 1990. Utah Admin. Code §R865-4D-23. IFTA is a tax collection agreement among the 48 contiguous states and 10 Canadian provinces bordering the U.S. The primary purpose of the agreement is uniform administration of motor fuels use tax laws affecting qualified motor vehicles operating in more than one jurisdiction. See the International Fuel Tax Association website. BNA-SUTN UT 2.3.
