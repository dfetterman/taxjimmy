State: Wyoming



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Wyoming became a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement as of Jan. 1, 2008. Wyoming Taxing Issues - December 2007. On Feb. 15, 2005, Wyoming enacted clean-up measure H.B. 14 in order to bring the state into compliance with the Agreement. 2005 Wyo. H.B. 14, effective Feb. 15, 2005. Wyoming enacted H.B. 259 on March 1, 2001, and adopted various regulations amending its sales and use tax code to conform to the requirements of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. 2001 Wyo. H.B. 259, effective July 1, 2002; Wyo. Stat. ยง39-15-403; see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Wyoming (version 2018.1); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). The purpose of the Agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance through improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections, having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, having uniformity of major tax base definitions, having a central electronic registration system for all member states, simplifying state and local tax rates, having uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, simplifying administration of exemptions, having simplified tax returns, simplifying tax remittances, and protecting consumer privacy. See Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN WY 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Wyoming is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Wyoming (version 2018.1). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Wyoming is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). Wyoming became a full member of Streamlined Sales Tax on Jan. 1, 2008. Wyoming Taxing Issues - December 2007. The state legislature enacted H.B. 259 on March 1, 2001, and adopted various regulations amending its sales tax code to conform to the requirements of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate/Project Member Wyoming is an associate/project member of the Multistate Tax Commission. Multistate Tax Comn., Member States. Associate/project members participate in Commission meetings and otherwise consult and cooperate with the Commission and its other member states or participate in Commission programs or projects. They have not enacted the Multistate Tax Compact into their state law and do not regularly participate in or financially support the general activities of the Commission. Multistate Tax Comn., Member States. BNA-SUTN WY 2.2.
