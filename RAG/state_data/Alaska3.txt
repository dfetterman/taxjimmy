Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Other Fuel

No Tax Alaska does not impose a state sales and use tax. Refined Fuel A borough may not levy or collect a sales or use tax on the physical transfer of refined fuel, unless the transfer is made in connection with a sale or use in the borough, or wholesale sales or transfers of fuel refined in the borough. A sale occurs in the borough if the fuel is delivered to the buyer in the borough. A use occurs in the borough if the fuel is consumed in the borough. Alaska Stat. §29.45.650(i). The prohibitions on the levy and collection of a sales or use tax on refined fuel does not apply to a borough if, on the effective date of the prohibition, the borough was either levying and collecting a sales or use tax on the sale, use, or transfer of refined fuel under an ordinance adopted before Jan. 1, 2003; or receiving payments in lieu of a sales or use tax on the sale, use, or transfer of refined fuel under an agreement entered into before Jan. 1, 2003. Alaska Stat. §29.45.650(j). A borough assembly may not authorize a city to levy or collect sales or use tax on the physical transfer of refined fuel, unless the transfer is made in connection with a sale or use in the city, or wholesale sales or transfers of fuel refined in the city. A sale occurs in the city if the fuel is delivered to the buyer in the city. A use occurs in the city if the fuel is consumed in the city. Alaska Stat. §29.45.700(e). The prohibitions on the levy and collection of a sales or use tax on refined fuel does not apply to a city if, on the effective date of the prohibition, the city was either levying and collecting a sales or use tax on the sale, use, or transfer of refined fuel under an ordinance adopted before Jan. 1, 2003; or receiving payments in lieu of a sales or use tax on the sale, use, or transfer of refined fuel under an agreement entered into before Jan. 1, 2003. Alaska Stat. §29.45.650(j). BNA-SUTN AK 30.1.12.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

No Tax Alaska does not impose a state sales and use tax. Boroughs may levy and collect sales tax on sales, rents, and on services provided in the borough and are authorized generally by the state to levy taxes and special assessments. Alaska Stat. §29.35.010(6). These authorizations to tax allow boroughs in Alaska to either subject utilities to tax under a general sales and use tax, or to impose a special utilities tax, either in addition to or in replacement of a general sales tax. See Alaska Stat. §29.45.650(a). For more information on taxes on public utilities in Alaska, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Alaska 11.1. BNA-SUTN AK 30.2.1.
