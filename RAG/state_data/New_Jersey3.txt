Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Taxable Receipts for the sale of electricity provided to persons in New Jersey are subject to tax. See N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-3(b)(7) (sales tax); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-6 (use tax); N.J. Admin. Code §18:24-4.3(a)(5). A business that sells, stores, delivers, or transports “energy” to users or customers in New Jersey is responsible for collecting and remitting the tax. See N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(y)(5) (property and services subject to tax include energy sold, exchanged, or delivered in state for use in state). “Energy”is a form of tangible personal property in New Jersey, but “energy”is defined narrowly to include only natural gas or electricity. See N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(g) (definition of tangible personal property); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(gg) (definition of energy). Exemptions Receipts from the sale, exchange, delivery or use of electricity are exempt from sales and use tax in New Jersey if the electricity:• is sold by a municipal electric corporation in existence as of December 31, 1995 and used within its municipal boundaries except if the customer is located within a franchise area served by an electric public utility other than the municipal electric corporation. If a municipal electric corporation makes sales of electricity used outside of its municipal boundaries or within a franchise area served by an electric public utility other than the municipal electric corporation, then receipts from those sales of electricity by the municipal electric corporation is subject to tax under P.L.1966, c.30; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(a)(1). • is sold by a municipal electric utility in existence as of December 31, 1995, and used within its municipal boundaries. However, a municipal electric utility's receipts from the sale, exchange, delivery or use of electricity used by customers outside of its municipal boundaries and within its franchise area existing as of December 31, 1995 is subject to tax. If a municipal electric utility makes sales of electricity used outside of its franchise area existing as of December 31, 1995, then receipts from those sales of electricity by the municipal electric utility is subject to tax under P.L.1966, c.302; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(a)(1)(b). or • was generated by a facility located on the user's property or property purchased or leased from the user by the person owning the co-generator and such property is contiguous to the user's property, and the electricity was consumed by the one on-site end user on the user's property, and was not transported to the user over wires that cross a property line or public thoroughfare unless the property line or public thoroughfare merely bifurcated the user's or cogenerator owner's otherwise contiguous property or the electricity was consumed by an affiliated user on the same site, or by a non-affiliated user on the same site with an electric distribution system which is integrated and interconnected with the user on or before March 10, 1997; the director may promulgate rules and regulations and issue guidance with respect to all issues related to affiliated users. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(a)(2). BNA-SUTN NJ 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Production

Exempt Cogeneration Facilities New Jersey exempts from sales and use tax receipts from sales of machinery, apparatus, equipment, building materials, or structures or portions thereof, used directly and primarily for cogeneration in a cogeneration facility. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.13(d). New Jersey defines the term “cogeneration facility” as a facility the primary purpose of which is to sequentially produce electricity and steam or other forms of useful energy that are used for industrial or commercial heating or cooling purposes and that is designated by the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission(FERC), or its successor, as a “qualifying facility” pursuant to the provisions of the “Public Utility Regulatory Policies Act of 1978,” Pub.L.95-617. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.13(d). Restrictions This exemption does not apply to otherwise taxable sales of machinery, equipment, or apparatus whose use is incidental to the activities described above. It also does not apply to energy, motor vehicles, parts with a useful life of one year or less, or tools or supplies used in connection with the machinery, equipment, or apparatus. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.13. Sales of gas, other than natural gas, water, steam, or fuel delivered to consumers through mains, lines, pipes, or in containers or bulk are exempt from sales and use tax in New Jersey. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.7. If a municipal electric corporation makes sales of electricity used outside of its municipal boundaries or within a franchise area served by an electric public utility other than the municipal electric corporation, then receipts from those sales of electricity by the municipal electric corporation are subject to tax under P.L.1966, c.30; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(a)(1). • is sold by a municipal electric utility in existence as of December 31, 1995, and used within its municipal boundaries. However, a municipal electric utility's receipts from the sale, exchange, delivery or use of electricity used by customers outside of its municipal boundaries and within its franchise area existing as of December 31, 1995 are subject to tax. If a municipal electric utility makes sales of electricity used outside of its franchise area existing as of December 31, 1995, then receipts from those sales of electricity by the municipal electric utility are subject to tax under P.L.1966, c.302; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(a)(1)(b). or • was generated by a facility located on the user's property or property purchased or leased from the user by the person owning the co-generator and such property is contiguous to the user's property, and the electricity was consumed by the one on-site end user on the user's property, and was not transported to the user over wires that cross a property line or public thoroughfare unless the property line or public thoroughfare merely bifurcated the user's or cogenerator owner's otherwise contiguous property or the electricity was consumed by an affiliated user on the same site, or by a non-affiliated user on the same site with an electric distribution system which is integrated and interconnected with the user on or before March 10, 1997; the director may promulgate rules and regulations and issue guidance with respect to all issues related to affiliated users. Natural Gas Receipts from the purchase or use of the following are exempt from the sales and use tax in New Jersey:• natural gas or utility service that is used to generate electricity that is sold for resale or to an end user other than the end user upon whose property is located a co-generation facility or self-generation unit that generated the electricity or upon the property purchased or leased from the end user by the person owning the co-generation facility or self-generation unit if such property is contiguous to the user's property and is the property upon which is located a cogeneration facility or self-generation unit that generated the electricity; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(b)(1). and • natural gas and utility service that is used for co-generation at any site at which a cogeneration facility was in operation on or before March 10, 1997, or for which an application for an operating permit or a construction permit and a certificate of operation in order to comply with air quality standards under N.J. Rev. Stat. §26:2C-1 et seq. has been filed with the Department of Environmental Protection on or before March 10, 1997, to produce electricity for use on that site. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.46(b)(2). • natural gas and utility service used by “special contract customers”for which a customer-specific tax classification was approved by a written order of the New Jersey Board of Public Utilities prior to Jan. 1, 1998; • natural gas and utility service used by agencies or instrumentalities of the federal government; • natural gas and utility service used by international organizations of which the United States is a member; • natural gas and utility service used by cemetery companies, pursuant to N.J.S.A 8A: 5-10; • natural gas and utility service used by Amtrak; and • natural gas and utility service used by Limited Dividend Housing Corporations organized under N.J.S.A. 55:16-1 et seq. New Jersey: Notice to All Natural Gas Vendors 12/28/17. BNA-SUTN NJ 30.4.
