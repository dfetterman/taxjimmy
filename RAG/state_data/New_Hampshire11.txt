Topic: Imposition of Tax Pending and Trending Developments

No Tax New Hampshire does not impose a sales and use tax. Editor's Note: The Governor of New Hampshire has announced his intention to “erect every possible and constitutionally permissible legal and procedural hurtle”to prevent this kind of tax collection and remittance, and has called for the legislature to convene a special session no later than Aug. 15, 2018; a legislative task force is expected to release their report by July 19. New Hampshire Gov.'s Office, News Release: New Hampshire to Fight Back (June 28, 2018); New Hampshire Gov.'s Office, Requested Action (July 11, 2018); New Hampshire General Court, Senate President, House Speaker Announce Joint Task Force on Wayfair Decision. In addition, Senators from New Hampshire, Montana, and Oregon have introduced a bill which would reverse the Wayfair decision. Press Release: Tester, Shaheen, Merkley, Hassan Intoduce Bill to Protect Businesses From Collecting Sales Taxes (June 28, 2018). Retailers who are contacted by other tax collection authorities are encouraged to contact the New Hampshire Department of Justice. More information is available at NHeconomy.com/nosalestax New Hampshire Gov.'s Office, Governor Sununu Announced Executive Branch Action Regarding the Wayfair Decision. On Aug. 13, 2018, the New Hampshire Department of Revenue updated its guidance relating to when “foreign”states can require New Hampshire retailers to obtain private customer information. New Hampshire TIR 2010-004 (Aug. 13, 2018). A foreign state may obtain private customer information only if the foreign state first provides the retailer with written confirmation that the foreign state has given the New Hampshire Department of Revenue Administration written notice of its intent to collect use tax on a particular retail transaction and the New Hampshire Department of Justice has made certain determinations regarding the foreign state's sales and use tax statutes. New Hampshire TIR 2010-004 (Aug. 13, 2018). BNA-SUTN NH 1.3.
