State: Missouri



Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Sales Tax

Yes In Missouri, a sales tax is imposed on sellers who engage in the business of selling or leasing tangible personal property and on sellers who render enumerated services at retail within Missouri. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.020; Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.021. The tax is imposed on gross receipts from each sale. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.020; Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.021. The primary tax burden is placed upon the seller making taxable sales of tangible personal property or services. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.021. The seller is required to collect the tax imposed and remit returns to the Missouri Department of Revenue. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.080.1. Beginning Aug. 28, 2013, sales tax is imposed on the buyer on the sale of new and used motor vehicles, trailers, boats, and outboard motors at the time of titling the property in Missouri. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.080.1, as amended by 2013 Mo. H.B. 184, effective Aug. 28, 2013. Expansion Prohibited On Nov. 8, 2016, Missouri voters approved a ballot initiative that amends the state's constitution to prohibit the imposition of state and local sales and use taxes, or similar transaction-based taxes, on any service or transaction that was not already subject to tax on Jan. 1, 2015. Mo. Const. art. X, §26, as amended by state ballot initiative effective Nov. 8, 2016. BNA-SUTN MO 1.1.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Use Tax

Yes A compensating use tax is imposed on every person for the privilege of storing, using, or consuming any article of tangible personal property that has come to rest in Missouri or is commingled with existing property in Missouri. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.610.1. The use tax is imposed on the sales price. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.610.1; see also Sw. Bell Yellow Pages, Inc. v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 94 S.W.3d 388 (Mo. 2002); Missouri Letter Ruling LR7774 (Jan. 5, 2017). Use tax is also imposed on an out-of-state seller's sales of its products stored at its in-state customer's location. Missouri Letter Ruling LR7774 (Jan. 5, 2017). If an out-of-state seller does not collect use tax from the purchaser, the purchaser is responsible for remitting the use tax to Missouri. 4340 Individual Consumer's Use Tax Return. Expansion Prohibited On Nov. 8, 2016, Missouri voters approved a ballot initiative that amends the state's constitution to prohibit the imposition of state and local sales and use taxes, or similar transaction-based taxes, on any service or transaction that was not already subject to tax on Jan. 1, 2015. Mo. Const. art. X, §26, as amended by state ballot initiative effective Nov. 8, 2016. BNA-SUTN MO 1.1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Local Option Sales and Use Tax

Local Tax Authorized Missouri authorizes counties and other political subdivisions to impose various sales taxes. See, e.g., Mo. Rev. Stat. §66.390. Further, any county or municipality may impose a local use tax to complement any of the different local sales taxes that are enacted at a rate equal to, but not in excess of, the rate of the local sales tax in effect in such county or municipality. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.757; Street v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 361 S.W.3d 355, 2012 BL 105853 (Mo. 2012); Kirkwood Glass Co., Inc. v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 166 S.W.3d 583 (Mo. 2005); see also Macon Cty. Emergency Svcs. Bd. v. Macon Cty. Comn., 485 S.W.3d 353, 2016 BL 107977 (Mo. 2016) (determining that the Macon County Emergency Services Board need not receive the same proportional revenue of the county use tax as it does of the county sales tax). To enact a use tax, municipalities with a population under 900,000 may do so by a majority vote of its governing body. Municipalities with a population in excess of 900,000 do so only with voter approval. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.757(1); see Drury v. Cape Girardeau, Mo., 66 S.W.3d 733 (Mo. 2002). No county or municipality that imposes a local use tax may repeal or amend the tax unless it is approved by the voters. However, the repeal of the local sales tax within the county or municipality will be deemed to repeal the local use tax. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.761(1); see Drury v. Cape Girardeau, Mo., 66 S.W.3d 733 (Mo. 2002). When a transaction is subject to state sales tax, the transaction is also subject to a local sales tax if a local sales tax has been adopted by the county or political subdivision where the seller's place of business is located. Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 12, §10-117.100.(1); Street v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 361 S.W.3d 355, 2012 BL 105853 (Mo. 2012); see Missouri Letter Ruling LR8031 (April 23, 2019). Local sales tax jurisdictions must hold a referendum in order to continue to impose use tax on motor vehicles, outboard motors, boats, and trailers that were purchased from a source other than a licensed Missouri dealer by November, 2022. Mo. Rev. Stat. §32.087, as amended by 2018 Mo. S.B. 773, effective Aug. 28, 2018 (extending deadline for cities to hold a referendum from 2018 to 2022); Street v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 361 S.W.3d 355, 2012 BL 105853 (Mo. 2012). The Missouri Department of Revenue maintains a list of applicable local sales and use tax rates, which can be used to calculate the amount of state and local sales and use tax that must be remitted for a transaction. Missouri Dept. of Rev., Sales and Use Tax Rate Tables. Expansion Prohibited Like Missouri's state level sales and use taxes, local sales and use taxes are prohibited from being expanded. In a Nov. 8, 2016, ballot initiative, Missouri voters approved an amendment to the state's constitution prohibiting the imposition of state and local sales and use taxes, or similar transaction-based taxes, on any service or transaction that was not already subject to tax on Jan. 1, 2015. Mo. Const. art. X, §26, as amended by state ballot initiative effective Nov. 8, 2016. BNA-SUTN MO 1.1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Excise and Replacement Taxes

Yes Excise Taxes Missouri imposes several excise taxes that function in a manner similar to the state's transactional sales and use tax. See Mo. Rev. Stat. §301.020(1); Mo. Rev. Stat. §301.055. Examples include:• an alcoholic beverage tax; Mo. Rev. Stat. §311.550. • a cigarette tax; Mo. Rev. Stat. §149.015. • a tax on the first sale of tobacco products; Mo. Rev. Stat. §149.160. • a motor fuels tax; Mo. Rev. Stat. §142.803(1); Mo. Rev. Stat. §155.080(1). • a public utilities tax; Mo. Rev. Stat. §150.020; Mo. Rev. Stat. §153.030(1); Mo. Rev. Stat. §151.010; Mo. Rev. Stat. §394.260. and • a motor vehicle use and registration tax. Mo. Rev. Stat. §301.020(1); Mo. Rev. Stat. §301.055. Replacement Taxes The Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement prohibits member states from imposing a “prohibited replacement tax” which is a tax outside of the sales and use tax that is imposed on products defined in the Agreement. Generally, the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board will make a finding regarding whether or not a member state has imposed a replacement tax. Missouri is not a member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales Tax State Members; Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 19, 2017, Section 334. BNA-SUTN MO 1.1.4.

Stat. §144.600, citing L. 1959 H.B. 35 § 1. BNA-SUTN MO 1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Pending and Trending Developments

Tax Base On Nov. 8, 2016, Missouri voters approved Constitutional Amendment 4, which amended Missouri's constitution to prohibit the imposition of any new state and local sales and use taxes “on any service or transaction” not subject to such taxes as of Jan. 1, 2015. Mo. Const. art. X, §26, as amended by state ballot initiative, effective Nov. 8, 2016. The amendment also applies to any similar, transaction-based tax. Mo. Const. art. X, §26, as amended by Amendment 4, effective Nov. 8, 2016. As for the potential effects of the amendment, the official ballot title included the following language: “Potential costs to state and local governmental entities are unknown, but could be significant. The proposal's passage would impact governmental entity's ability to revise their tax structures. State and local governments expect no savings from this proposal.” The ballot also stated that the measure, if passed, will not increase or decrease taxes. Missouri Sec. of State, 2016 Ballot Measures. In another Nov. 8, 2016, ballot initiative related to sales and use taxes (Constitutional Amendment 1), Missouri voters renewed for 10 years the 0.1 percent sales and use tax that is used for soil and water conservation and for state parks and historic sites. Voters must approve the continuation of this tax every 10 years. The tax is expected to continue to generate about $90 million annually. Mo. Const. art. IV, §47(c), as repealed and replaced by state ballot initiative effective Nov. 8, 2016. Earlier in the year, on May 4, 2016, the Missouri General Assembly enacted a law that establishes the “Missouri Task Force on Fair, Nondiscriminatory Local Taxation Concerning Motor Vehicles, Trailers, Boats, and Outboard Motors.” Mo. Rev. Stat. §32.088, as established by 2016 Mo. H.B. 2140, effective Aug. 28, 2016. Effective Aug. 28, 2016, the “Missouri Task Force on Fair, Nondiscriminatory Local Taxation Concerning Motor Vehicles, Trailers, Boats, and Outboard Motors” began meeting to review and report on:• the disparity in taxation that resulted from the Missouri Supreme Court's decision in Street v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 361 S.W.3d 355, 2012 BL 105853 (Mo. 2012). concerning the local taxation of motor vehicles, boats, trailers, and outboard motors if purchased from a source other than a licensed Missouri dealer; • the need for local jurisdictions to continue to receive revenue to provide vital services restored by 2013 Mo. S.B. 23, effective July 5, 2013; and • the need to avoid placing Missouri dealers of motor vehicles, outboard motors, boats, and trailers at a competitive disadvantage to non-Missouri dealers of motor vehicles, outboard motors, boats, and trailers. Mo. Rev. Stat. §32.088(6). The Task Force is scheduled to submit a report to the Governor and General Assembly by Dec. 31, 2017, and is set to expire on Jan. 1, 2018. Mo. Rev. Stat. §32.088(7). For more information on local taxes see Missouri's Local Government Tax Guide. BNA-SUTN MO 1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Sale' and 'Retail Sale'

Defined “Sale”means any transfer, exchange or barter, conditional or otherwise, in any manner, of tangible personal property for valuable consideration. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(10). Sale also means the rendering, furnishing or selling for valuable consideration any of the things and services defined as taxable under Missouri Revised Statute §§144.010 to 144.525. The definition includes installment sales, credit sales, every closed transaction constituting a sale, and the sale or exchange of property. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(10). “Sale at retail”is defined as any transfer made by a person engaged in business, of the ownership of or title to tangible personal property to the purchaser for use or consumption and not for resale in any form as tangible personal property, for valuable consideration. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(13) as renumbered by 2016 Mo. S.B. 1025, effective Oct. 14, 2016. Engaged in business includes any activity engaged in by a person, or caused to be engaged in by him, with the object of gain, benefit or advantage, either direct or indirect. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(2). Isolated or occasional sales of tangible personal property, service, substance, or thing, by a person not engaged in such business, does not constitute engaging in business unless the total amount of the gross receipts from such sales exceeds $3,000 per calendar year. Receipts from the sale of tangible personal property in the course of a partial or complete liquidation of a household, farm, or nonbusiness enterprise are not taxable regardless of the total amount of the gross receipts received from the sale. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(2). “Valuable consideration” is not defined in the statute, but generally means valid consideration that is a pecuniarily measurable benefit on one party and a pecuniarily measurable detriment on the other. Bus. Aviation, LLC v. Missouri Dir. or Rev., No. SC97475, 2019 BL 263428 (Mo. July 16, 2019) (adopting the definition from Black's Law Dictionary (10th ed. 2014)). “Sale at retail” includes:• sales of admission tickets, cash admissions, charges and fees to or in places of amusement, entertainment and recreation, games and athletic events; See Surrey's on the Plaza, Inc. v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 128 S.W.3d 508 (Mo. 2004); Six Flags Theme Parks Inc. v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 102 S.W.3d 526 (Mo. 2003); Missouri Letter Ruling LR5526 (Mar. 13, 2009); Missouri Letter Ruling LR3512 (Nov. 13, 2006). • sales of electricity, electrical current, water and gas, natural or artificial, to domestic, commercial or industrial consumers; • sales of local and long distance telecommunications service to telecommunications subscribers and to others through equipment of telecommunications subscribers for the transmission of messages and conversations, and the sale, rental or leasing of all equipment or services pertaining or incidental telecommunication services; • sales of service for transmission of messages by telegraph companies; • sales or charges for all rooms, meals and drinks furnished at any hotel, motel, tavern, inn, restaurant, eating house, drugstore, dining car, tourist camp, tourist cabin, or other place in which rooms, meals or drinks are regularly served to the public; and • sales of tickets by every person operating a railroad, sleeping car, dining car, express car, boat, airplane, and such buses and trucks as are licensed by the division of motor carrier and railroad safety of the department of economic development of Missouri, engaged in the transportation of persons for hire. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(13) as renumbered by 2016 Mo. S.B. 1025, effective Oct. 14, 2016. “Sale at retail” does not apply to professional health services or information services. Professional Health Services Purchases of tangible personal property by physicians, dentists, optometrists, and veterinarians that are used in their practices will be deemed purchases for use and not for resale and are therefore subject to tax. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(11). Information Services Missouri excludes certain information services from the definition of “sale at retail,” including: “[T]he selling of computer printouts, computer output or microfilm or microfiche and computer assisted photo compositions to a purchaser to enable the purchaser to obtain for his own use the desired information contained in such computer printouts, computer output on microfilm or microfiche and computer assisted photo compositions shall be considered as the sale of a service and not as the sale of tangible personal property.” Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(13)as renumbered by 2016 Mo. S.B. 1025, effective Oct. 14, 2016. But see Missouri Letter Ruling LR7373 (Mar. 12, 2014). BNA-SUTN MO 1.4.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Lease'

Defined Missouri defines a lease as any transfer of the right to possess or use tangible personal property for a term in exchange for consideration. Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 12, §10-108.700(2)(A). The definition of “lease”includes a rental. Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 12, §10-108.700(2)(A). If tangible personal property is used to provide a service to a customer and the use of the property is a necessary or mandatory part of the service transaction, then any temporary transfer of the property to the customer as part of the service transaction is not a lease or rental of the property. Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 12, §10-108.700(2)(A). Lessor A “lessor” is defined as a person who transfers the right to possess or use tangible personal property under a lease. Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 12, §10-108.700(2)(B). Lessee A “lessee” is defined as a person who receives the right to possess or use tangible personal property under a lease. Mo. Code Regs. Ann. tit. 12, §10-108.700(2)(C). BNA-SUTN MO 1.4.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Use' or 'Storage, Use, or Consumption'

Defined “Use”is defined as the exercise of any right or power over tangible personal property incident to the ownership or control of that property. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(13). Use does not include the temporary storage of property in Missouri for subsequent use outside Missouri, or the sale of the property in the regular course of business. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(13); Custom Hardware Eng'g & Consulting Inc. v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 358 S.W.3d 54, 2012 BL 105847 (Mo. 2012); see Aladdin's Castle Inc. v. Missouri Dir. of Rev., 916 S.W.2d 196 (Mo. 1996); Missouri Letter Ruling LR4196 (Mar. 13, 1990). Taxable “storage” is defined as any keeping or retention of tangible personal property in Missouri, other than tangible personal property held for sale or temporarily kept in the state for subsequent use outside the state. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(10); see, e.g., Missouri Letter Ruling LR7345 (Jan. 23, 2014) (bringing aircraft into Missouri for maintenance and upgrades prior to sale does not give rise to use tax on the aircraft). BNA-SUTN MO 1.4.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Tangible Personal Property'

Defined In Missouri, “tangible personal property” means all personal property tangible in nature that is sold for retail. See Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(11). “Tangible personal property” includes electricity or electrical current, water, and gas. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(11); Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.020(1); Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.020(1)(3). However, it does not include motor vehicles, trailers, motorcycles, mopeds, motor tricycles, boats, and outboard motors. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(11); Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.020(1); Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.020(1)(3). BNA-SUTN MO 1.4.4.

Not Defined Missouri does not define “intangible property;” however it offers several examples of what may constitute intangible property. Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Retailer' or 'Vendor'

Defined Missouri does not provide a definition for the term “retailer,”but does define “seller” and “vendor”. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010(1)(12); Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(14). Vendor A “vendor”is every person engaged in making sales of tangible personal property by mail order, by advertising, by agent or peddling tangible personal property, soliciting or taking orders for sales of tangible personal property, for storage, use or consumption in Missouri. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(14). This includes all salesmen, solicitors, hawkers, representatives, consignees, peddlers or canvassers, as agents of the dealers, distributors, consignors, supervisors, principals or employers under whom they operate or from whom they obtain the tangible personal property sold by them, and every person who maintains a place of business in Missouri, maintains a stock of goods in Missouri, or engages in business activities within Missouri and every person who engages in Missouri in the business of acting as a selling agent for persons not otherwise vendors as defined in this subdivision. Irrespective of whether they are making sales on their own behalf or on behalf of the dealers, distributors, consignors, supervisors, principals or employers, they must be regarded as vendors and the dealers, distributors, consignors, supervisors, principals or employers must be regarded as vendors for the purposes of Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.600–Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.745. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(14). Seller A “seller”is a person selling or furnishing tangible personal property, the receipts from which are subject to tax. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010.1(14) as renumbered by 2016 Mo. S.B. 1025, effective Oct. 14, 2016. Missouri further defines a seller as any person making sales, leases or rentals of personal property or services. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.1003(6). In-state companies that conduct all their business purchases and sales out of state are not required to register with the Department of Revenue for a retail sales tax license. Missouri Letter Ruling LR7772 (Jan. 5, 2017). BNA-SUTN MO 1.4.6.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Purchaser' or 'Consumer'

Defined In Missouri, “purchaser” means a person who purchases tangible personal property or to whom are rendered services, receipts from which are subject to sales tax. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.010(1)(8). Missouri further defines “purchaser”as any person who is the recipient for a valuable consideration of any sale of tangible personal property acquired for use, storage or consumption in the state. Mo. Rev. Stat. §144.605(6). BNA-SUTN MO 1.4.7.
