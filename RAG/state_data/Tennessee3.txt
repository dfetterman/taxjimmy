Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends Tennessee exempts sales of electricity and other energy fuels that are sold directly to consumers for residential use. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-334(a). Electricity is included in Tennessee's definition of tangible personal property. Sales of such utilities are generally taxable when sold for non-residential use. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(89)(A) (effective July 1, 2021, the definition for “tangible personal property”is located in Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(91)(A)); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-1-.107. In the case of taxable sales of electricity for non-residential use, tax applies only to charges on a consumer's monthly bill for metered usage, a monthly minimum bill, a monthly customer charge, or a monthly demand charge. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-334(c), as amended by 2018 Tenn. H.B. 2232, effective April 27, 2018; see Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. A sale of fuel is considered to be made directly to a consumer for residential use when gas, electricity, fuel oil, coal, or other energy fuels are furnished to single private residences, including separate private units of apartment houses and other types of multiple dwellings, the fuel is actually used for residential purposes, and the fuel is separately measured or metered, regardless of the fact that the resident is contractually bound to the supplier for the charges, actually pays the charges, or is billed for the charges. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-334(b)(1). If the owner of a residential unit fails to disclose to an electric utility that the unit is being used for a purpose that does not qualify for this exemption and would thereby render the sale taxable, the owner is liable for any tax, penalty, or interest for the sale, rather than the electric utility having liability. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-334(b)(3). For purposes of this exemption, residential use of electricity and other energy fuels does not include uses of electricity or other energy fuels that occur in hotel or motel units by transient occupants. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-334(b)(2). Additionally, this exemption does not apply to energy fuels that are sold over the counter at a seller's location. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-334(b)(4). BNA-SUTN TN 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Water and Sewer

Taxable Sales of water are taxable as sales of tangible personal property. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(89)(A) (effective July 1, 2021, the definition for “tangible personal property”is located in Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(91)(A)). The sales and use tax imposed on water sold by a public utility only applies to charges on a customer's monthly water bill for metered water usage, a monthly minimum bill, a monthly customer charge, or a monthly demand charge. 2018 Tenn. H.B. 2232, effective April 27, 2018; see Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. A seller of water must collect the local option tax at the local rate for the location where the water is delivered to the customer. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-702(a)(4) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §164, effective July 1, 2009, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Tangible Personal Property Used by Watershed Districts An exemption from the Tennessee sales and use tax is provided for all sales of tangible personal property that are made to watershed districts when the property is intended to be used or consumed by those watershed districts. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-328. BNA-SUTN TN 30.2.4.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Depends Mining machinery, apparatus equipment, and materials are exempt from sales and use taxes under the industrial machinery exemption. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-206(a), as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021); Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(44)(A)(i). Effective July 1, 2021, the definition of “industrial machinery” will be located in Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(46)(A)(i). See 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). “Industrial machinery”includes mining machinery, apparatus equipment and materials, with all associated parts and accessories, including repair parts and any necessary repair or installation labor that is necessary and primarily for:• the removal, extraction or detachment of coal from land by surface, underground, or other lawful methods of mining, including the construction or maintenance of necessary ingress and egress from the mine; • the removal, handling and replacement of overburden and spoils materials;and • the reclamation of mined areas reclaimed under state or federal laws, rules, or regulations. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(44)(A)(i) (effective July 1, 2021, the definition for “industrial machinery”is located in Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(46)(A)(i)). BNA-SUTN TN 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Production

Depends Materials and equipment purchased or used in an electric generating plant or distribution system are exempt from sales and use tax provided the materials and equipment become a component part of the plant or distribution system. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.102(1); Tennessee Letter Ruling No. 13-17 (Nov. 19, 2013). The exemption does not apply to tangible personal property or taxable service used in the process of installing the exempt property, when the tangible personal property does not become a component part of the electric generating plant or distribution system. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.102(1). Contractors and sub-contractors purchasing tangible personal property to be used as a component part of an electric generating plant or distribution system may furnish their supplier an exemption certificate for each purchase in lieu of the sales or use tax. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.102(2). Sales of Energy-producing Fuels Sales of energy-producing fuels, such as coal, coke, electricity, and natural or petroleum gases, fuel oil and other combustibles, and water are subject to sales and use tax. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-1-.15. BNA-SUTN TN 30.4.
