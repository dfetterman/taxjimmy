Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

Border States Caucus/UEIA Border States Uniform Sale for Resale Certificate The states of Texas, Arizona, California, New Mexico, and Oklahoma, together with the United Mexican States, are members of the Border States Caucus. Multistate Tax Comm., Border States Uniform Sale for Resale Certificate. The caucus has developed the Border States Uniform Sale for Resale Certificate to simplify transactions in the border area. Businesses buying goods for resale in these states or the northern border strip and border region of Mexico that will be transported across state and/or national borders may use this certificate in lieu of a state resale certificate. Goods are defined as materials and other tangible property. Multistate Tax Comm., Border States Uniform Sale for Resale Certificate. The certificate must be completed by the buyer and given to the seller. The seller must retain the document as part of its accounting records. The seller should not accept an incomplete document as it will be invalid. Similarly the seller must ensure the claim is applicable, that is, the type of goods fits the description of the purchaser's business and the goods are likely for resale in that business. If a purchase is tax-exempt for reasons other than resale, the purchaser must complete a Texas exemption certificate. Multistate Tax Comm., Border States Uniform Sale for Resale Certificate. Uniform Exchange of Information Agreement The Uniform Exchange of Information Agreement allows tax administrators to exchange taxpayer information among state agencies that are parties to the agreement. Multistate Tax Comm., Uniform Exchange of Information Agreement, Article I. Information subject to exchange includes lists of taxpayers, tax returns, nexus information and questionnaires, audit reports, collection activities, and appeals and criminal tax matters. Multistate Tax Comm., Uniform Exchange of Information Agreement, Article VI. BNA-SUTN TX 2.3.
