Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Depends New Jersey exempts from sales and use tax, sales of aircraft used by air carriers that engage in intrastate air commerce with an in-state principal place of operations; however, leases and rentals of aircraft are subject to tax. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.35; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(e); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-7(d). New Jersey exempts from sales and use tax sales of aircraft having a maximum certificated takeoff weight of 6,000 pounds or more. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-8.35(1)(b). Also receipts from any sale of an aircraft are not subject to sales and use tax if, upon the purchase, the purchaser:• is not a New Jersey resident; • does not have a permanent abode in New Jersey; • does not engage in business in New Jersey; • furnishes an affidavit to the seller; and • will not place the aircraft in New Jersey except on a transient basis or for repairs within 12 months of the purchase. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-10. Leases Leases and rentals of aircraft are subject to tax in New Jersey. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(e); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-7(d). Leases or rentals of aircraft are not included in the definition of taxable leases where an operator necessary for the proper operation of the property is provided with the property leased or rented. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa)(1)(C); N.J. Admin. Code tit. 18, §24-32.2(1). Thus, charges for chartering an aircraft, where the pilot is supplied by the owner of the aircraft, are charges for transportation and therefore exempt. BNA-SUTN NJ 29.1.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Demonstrators

Taxable The use of a vehicle by a manufacturer or dealer for demonstration purposes is subject to New Jersey's sales and use taxes. Manufacturers who withdraw vehicles from inventory for demonstration use are liable for tax. The basis for the tax is determined by multiplying 25 times the sum of $500 plus the total invoice cost to distributors or dealers of vehicles of the same make, model, and accessory equipment. The basis is then be divided by 12, and the result multiplied by 0.07. N.J. Admin. Code tit. 18, §24–7.13. Retail dealers who withdraw vehicles from inventory are required to pay a compensating use tax unless the vehicle is used full-time by a salesperson employed by the dealership. The basis for the tax is 25 times the sum of the manufacturer's suggested list price plus $500. If the vehicle is used, the basis is 25 times the average retail price listed in the N.A.D.A. Official Used Car Guide plus $500. The basis is then divided by 12 and the result multiplied by 0.07 percent. N.J. Admin. Code tit. 18, §24–7.14(b). BNA-SUTN NJ 29.3.8.
