State: Vermont



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Food under Vermont law is exempt under the sales and use tax whether consumed on or off the premises where it was sold. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13). In Vermont, sales of food and food ingredients, food stamps, items purchased with food stamps or with a 3SquaresVT EBT card, and food products and beverages sold for human consumption off the premises where sold are exempt from sales and use tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13); Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1083. Vermont also exempts from sales and use tax the sales of meals taxed or exempted under the meals and lodging tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13); Vt. Code R. §1.9741(13). BNA-SUTN VT 11.1.1.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined Under Vermont Law, “food and food ingredient” is defined as food, food products and beverages whether in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that is sold for ingestion, or for chewing, by humans and that is ingested or chewed for its taste or nutritional value. The definition does not include alcoholic beverages, tobacco, or soft drinks. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(31). Vermont incorporates the definition of food and food ingredients as found in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(31). BNA-SUTN VT 11.1.2.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Dietary Supplements and Vitamins

Exempt Dietary supplements are specifically defined as food such that the sale, storage, use, or other consumption of dietary supplements is exempt from sales tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13); Vt. Code R. §1.9741(2)(C); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Vermont (version 2018.0). “Dietary supplement”is defined as a product, other than tobacco, that is intended to supplement a person's diet. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27). It has to contain one or more of the following dietary ingredients:• a vitamin; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(A)(i). • a mineral; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(A)(ii). • an herb or other botanical; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(A)(iii). • an amino acid; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(A)(iv). • a dietary substance for use by humans to supplement the diet by increasing the total dietary intake; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(A)(v). or • a concentrate, metabolite, constituent, extract, or combination of any of the above listed ingredients; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(A)(vi). and o the product is intended for ingestion in tablet, capsule, powder, soft-gel, gel-cap, or liquid form, or, if not intended for ingestion in such forms, is not represented as conventional food and is not represented for use as the sole item of a meal or diet; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(B). and o the product is required to be labeled as a dietary supplement as required under 21 C.F.R. § 101.36. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(27)(B). BNA-SUTN VT 11.1.3.3.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Alcoholic Beverages

Depends Although alcoholic beverages are not included in the definition of exempt food and food ingredients, effective July 1, 2016, Vermont exempts alcoholic beverages that are taxed or exempted under Vermont's Rooms and Meals Tax, and exempts alcoholic beverages that are provided for immediate consumption. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(10), as amended by 2015 Vt. S. 250, § 12, effective July 1, 2016. Otherwise, sales of alcoholic beverages are not exempt from sales or use tax as they are not included in the definition of exempt food and food ingredients. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13); Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(31). Prior to July 1, 2016, alcoholic beverages were subject to Vermont's sales and use tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701(31); Vermont Formal Ruling No. 2014-02 (an alcoholic beverage is subject to the sales and use tax unless an exemption is applicable). BNA-SUTN VT 11.1.3.4.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Soft Drinks

Exempt Effective July 1, 2015, the sales of soft drinks, other than those sold through vending machines or as part of a taxable meal or purchased with a 3SquaresVT EBT card, are subject to state sales and use tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(31), as amended by 2015 Vt. H. 489, effective June 11, 2015; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Vermont (version 2018.0); Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1083; Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1080. Generally, beverages containing sweeteners are subject to state sales tax. Soft drinks are nonalcoholic beverages that contain natural or artificial sweeteners and are not any beverages containing milk, milk products, soy, rice, or similar milk substitutes or beverages containing more than 50 percent vegetable or fruit juice. Also, beverage powders and concentrates are not considered soft drinks and are exempt from sales tax. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1080. Prior to July 1, 2015, soft drinks were specifically identified as a food and food product and were, therefore, exempt from sales tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(31); Vt. Code R. §1.9741(13). For information on excise taxes on soft drinks in Virginia, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Virginia 14.1. BNA-SUTN VT 11.1.3.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Catering and Personal Chefs

Nontaxable Catering services are not subject to Vermont's sales and use tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9741(13); Vt. Code R. §1.9741(13); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Vermont (version 2018.0). When catering services are presented and billed as a package, the caterer must collect and remit a 9-percent Vermont Meals and Rooms Tax on all items and services that are components of the package. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1040. The Meals and Rooms Tax should be stated as tax included on the bill. If the caterer itemizes the components of their catering service on the bill, the following items are subject to the Meals and Rooms Tax:• food and beverage; • labor for preparing, delivery, serving, and cleaning up after the meal; • tableware, such as dishware, cutlery, glassware, linens, and other items necessary for serving the meal; • flowers and any other decorative items provided as part of the service; and • room, banquet hall, building, or any space where the meal will be served. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1040. Rented items such as tableware or linens are subject to sales tax and the caterer should pay the sales tax to the vendor. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1040. The caterer may choose to pass on the sales tax as a markup in the final bill to the customer. Where items are purchased or rented from a vendor not licensed to collect tax in Vermont, the caterer is responsible for paying use tax on the items. This situation frequently occurs when making internet purchases or purchases from vendors in states without a state sales tax. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1040. Generally, tips are not taxable. Where tips exceed 20 percent, they must be reported as taxable (even if fully distributed to service employees). If any portion of a tip is retained by the caterer, rather than by service employees, the portion retained becomes part of the charge to the customer and is thus subject to tax. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1040. BNA-SUTN VT 11.5.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Restaurant Equipment

Taxable Equipment used by retail operations, such as restaurants, to process food is subject to tax; it is not eligible for Vermont's manufacturing exemption. Vt. Code R. §1.9741(14)-2(D). BNA-SUTN VT 11.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Depends Sales of food and beverages through vending machines are treated as taxable meals subject to Meals and Room Tax. Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(C)(iv), as amended by 2015 Vt. H. 489, effective July 1, 2015; Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085. Vendors are required to charge meals tax for all vending machine sales unless a transaction qualifies for one of the meals tax exemptions. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085. Vermont's meals tax exemptions depend on where the vending machine is located, the customer served, and the organization that operates it. Vending Machines in Schools Food and beverage sales by vending machines in schools are not taxable. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines on Government Property Food and beverage sales by vending machines located on local, state, or federal government properties that serve or furnish food or beverage to only government employees are not taxable. However, when sales by vending machines are open to the public, even though on government property, are taxable. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Correctional Facilities Food and beverage sales by a vending machines in any local, state, or federal correctional facility that serves or furnishes food or beverage to only inmates and employees of the correctional facility are not taxable. However, sales by a vending machine that is open to the public, even though located on the correctional facility's property, are taxable. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Hospitals Food and beverage sales by vending machines in licensed hospitals are not taxable if the vending machine contents are prepared by hospital employees. However, sales by vending machines in licensed hospitals are taxable if the contents in the machine are not prepared by hospital employees. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Trains, Buses, and Airplanes Food and beverage sales, by vending machines located in trains, buses, or airplanes transporting passengers for hire are not taxable. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Train Stations, Bus Stations, and Airports Food and beverage sales by vending machines located in train stations, bus stations, or airports are taxable. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Summer Camps for Children Food and beverage sales by vending machines located in summer camps for children are not taxable while they are operating as summer camps for children. However, food sales made by vending machines located at the camps are taxable when camps are not operating as summer camps for children. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Continuing Care Retirement Communities Food and beverage sales by vending machines located on the premises of certified continuing care retirement communities are not taxable. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Nursing Homes and Similar Facilities Food and beverage sales by vending machines located at nursing homes, residential care homes, assisted living residences, homes for the terminally ill, therapeutic community residences, or independent living facilities are not taxable if the contents are prepared by the employees, volunteers, or contractors. However, food sales by vending machines located at these facilities are taxable if the contents are not prepared by the employees, volunteers, or contractors. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machine Sales to SNAP Beneficiaries Food and beverage purchases made using 3SquareVT/SNAP resources are not taxable if the machine is equipped to accept EBT cards. However, SNAP beneficiaries who pay with cash, even if the cash is an EBT benefit, will pay the tax. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machines in Nonprofit Organizations Food and beverage sales by vending machines located on the premises of nonprofit corporations and associations are not taxable if the nonprofit is organized and operated exclusively for religious or charitable purposes, the sale furthers the purposes of the organization, and the net proceeds are used exclusively for the purposes of the organization. However, a third party vendor must charge tax on vending machine sales located on the premises of a nonprofit if any of the net proceeds go to a person or organization other than the nonprofit. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). Vending Machine Sales by Nonprofit Organizations for Events Food sales by vending machines are not taxable when nonprofits operate vending machines at bazaars, fairs, picnics, church suppers, or similar events, on or off premises. To use this exemption, nonprofits can only sell taxable meals at these types of events for four days or fewer per calendar year. Nonprofits may rent vending machines for use at events, but they must keep all of the net proceeds. No exemptions are available if third parties keep the net proceeds from events because sales then are considered to be by the third parties and not the nonprofits. However, food sales made by an organization required to have a meals and rooms license or otherwise required to have a license because its selling events are more than four days during the calendar year, there is no exemption. Sales by vending machines must charge tax. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit §9202(10)(D)(ii)(VII). However, food sales made by an organization required to have a meals and rooms license or otherwise required to have a license because its selling events are more than four days during the calendar year, there is no exemption. Sales by vending machines must charge tax. Vermont Tax Fact Sheet FS-1085; Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9202(10)(D). BNA-SUTN VT 11.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

No Guidance Vermont does not specify the sales and use tax treatment of other food and food products. See Vt. Stat. Ann. tit. 32, §9701, et seq. BNA-SUTN VT 11.10.
