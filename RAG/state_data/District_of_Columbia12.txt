State: District of Columbia



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Nonmember Although the District of Columbia is considered an advisory member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Project, it has not yet enacted the legislation necessary to comply with the requirements of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. D.C. Code Ann. § 1-301.121, et. seq.; Streamlined Sales Tax, Streamlined State Status (Sept. 1, 2015). The District of Columbia has enacted legislation that permits it to enter into the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement. D.C. Code Ann. § 1-301.121 through D.C. Code Ann. § 1-301.128. BNA-SUTN DC 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member The District of Columbia is a Compact Member of the Multistate Tax Commission. D.C. Code Ann. §47-441;Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. Compact members are states(represented by the heads of the tax agencies administering corporate income and sales and use taxes) that have enacted the Multistate Tax Compact into their state law. D.C. Code Ann. §47-441;Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. The Multistate Tax Commission is an intergovernmental state tax agency that works on behalf of states and taxpayers to equitably and efficiently administer tax laws that apply to multistate and multinational enterprises. The goal of the Commission is to facilitate the proper determination of state and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers, including the equitable apportionment of tax bases, the settlement of apportionment disputes, promoting uniformity and compatibility in significant components of tax systems, facilitating taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration, and avoiding duplicative taxation. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Multistate Tax Commission. Tax Credit Purchasers liable for use tax on tangible personal property are entitled to a full tax credit for the amount of any sales or use taxes paid by the purchaser to another state and any subdivision with respect to the same property. D.C. Code Ann. §47-441 (Article V). Exemption Certificates Whenever a vendor receives and accepts in good faith a resale or other exemption certificate, or other written evidence of exemption authorized by the appropriate state or subdivision taxing authority, the vendor is relieved of liability for a sales or use tax with respect to the transaction. D.C. Code Ann. §47-441 (Article V). BNA-SUTN DC 2.2.
