State: Nevada



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member After enacting all provisions necessary to comply with the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement's requirements, Nevada became a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement on April 1, 2008. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Nevada (version 2018.0). For the latest version of Nevada's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (June 20, 2018). On June 9, 2003, Nevada enacted A.B. 514 in order to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. 2003 Nev. A.B. 514, effective January 1, 2006. As a full member, Nevada maintains a seat on the governing board and may vote on amendments and interpretations of the Agreement. All sellers registering under the Streamlined Sales Tax system are required to collect and remit tax on sales into Nevada. Nev. Rev. Stat. tit. 32, ยง360B.010; Nev. Rev. Stat. tit. 32, ยง360B.375;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN NV 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Nevada is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. For the latest version of Nevada's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (June 20, 2018). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Nevada is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN NV 2.1.2.

WSATA Nevada is a member of the Western States Association of Tax Administrators(WSATA), which includes Alaska, Arizona, California, Colorado, Hawaii, Idaho, Montana, New Mexico, Nevada, Oregon, Texas, Utah, Washington, and Wyoming. The Western States Association of Tax Administrators promotes cooperation among states for tax administration. Nev. Admin. Code ch. 361, ยง361.429. The Federation of Tax Administrators facilitates tax administration and compliance through the Uniform Exchange of Information Agreement. Nevada has not signed the Agreement.
