Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Property and Passenger Transportation Passenger Transportation, Taxi or Transportation Network

Nontaxable New York City generally imposes tax on the same sales and services taxed at the state level. N.Y. Tax Law §1210(a)(1); N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2001(a). However, a 50 cent tax is imposed on any taxicab that begins in New York City and ends anywhere within the boundaries of the Metropolitan Commuter Transport District. N.Y. Tax Law §1281. In addition, New York City imposes an annual tax on each medallion taxicab for use in the city and on all other motor vehicles for transportation of passengers. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-802(a)(2)(C). However, if the first use of such motor vehicle occurs on or after December 1 and before March 1 of any tax year, the annual tax is one-half the amount imposed. If the first use occurs on or after March 1 in any tax year, the annual tax for that tax year is one-fourth the amount imposed. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-802(c). For more information on how New York State taxes transportation property and services see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at New York 29.9.5. For additional discussion of tax on car services, taxi cabs and other forms of transit in New York City, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at New York City 9.5. BNA-SUTN NYC 29.9.5.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Parking and Valet

Taxable New York City's sales and use tax applies to the sale of parking, garaging, or storing motor vehicles at a special rate of 6 percent. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2001(a); N.Y. Tax Law §1105(c)(6); New York TB-ST-679; see N.Y. Tax Law §1210-A(a)(1). Combined with the state sales tax and the MCTMT, New York City imposes a total tax of 10.375 percent. New York TB-ST-679; see New York TSB-M-96(13)S (Nov. 9, 1996). An additional parking surtax at a rate of 8 percent is imposed on receipts from every sale of parking, garaging, or storing motor vehicles in every county within the city of New York with a population density exceeding 50,000 persons per square mile. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2049; New York TB-ST-679. The total combined parking tax rate in such counties is 18.375 percent. New York TB-ST-679. The 8 percent parking surtax does not apply to, among other organizations and individuals: • a garage that is part of premises occupied solely as a private one or two family dwelling; N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2049; N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2051(b); New York TB-ST-679. • a garage operated by a homeowner's association or leased to a homeowner's association and whose customers are paying members of the homeowner's association; N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2049; New York TB-ST-679. or • sales of services to an individual resident of the city where the parking or garage services are rendered on a monthly or longer basis, but only if the vehicle is owned by the resident or leased for a term of at least one year. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2051(d); N.Y.C. Rules tit. 19, §19-02; New York TB-ST-679. In addition, the Manhattan Resident Parking Tax Exemption exempts certain vehicles from the 8 percent parking surtax provided the vehicle meets the following requirements: • it is individually owned by the applicant; • it is registered to the owner's primary residence in Manhattan; • it is parked in a long-term rented space for a month or more; and • it is driven for personal use only (commercial, rental, or company vehicles are not eligible for the exemption). N.Y.C. Rules tit. 19, §19-02; see also New York TB-ST-679. For more information on how New York State taxes transportation property and services see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at New York 29.10. BNA-SUTN NYC 29.10.
