Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Depends Trucks and other motor vehicles are subject to a motor vehicle excise tax in lieu of any sales or use tax. However, the motor vehicle excise tax does not apply to vehicles that are 11 or more years old or sold for $2,200 or less, before trade-in. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §32-5B-2(11); South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales and Purchases. Certain vehicle transactions are exempt from both the motor vehicle excise tax and sales tax. These transactions include vehicles that are 11 or more years old or sold for $2,200 or less before trade in, transfers as part the sales of the assets of a business if the vehicle was previously titled in South Dakota, repossessions, or transfers to an insurance company in the settlement of a claim. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §32-5B-2(11); South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales and Purchases. Cars, trucks, vans, low speed vehicles, motorcycles, off-road vehicles, and snowmobiles are subject to the motor vehicle excise tax in lieu of any sales or use tax. However, mopeds with engines of 50cc or less are not subject to the motor vehicle excise tax and are subject to sales tax. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales and Purchases. Vehicles that will not be operated on streets and highways or waterways of South Dakota or any state and will be hauled or transported to their destination may only be subject to use tax, instead of the motor vehicle excise tax, if approved by the Department of Revenue for a title only. Use tax is not due if the vehicle is purchased from a person, instead of from a business. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales. For more information on the motor vehicle excise tax, see Excise Tax Navigator, at South Dakota 9.1. Mounted equipment, such as truck boxes, hoists, digger equipment, and cherry pickers sold with the vehicle are subject to the motor vehicle excise tax and not sales tax; however if such equipment is sold separately and added after the sale of the vehicle it is subject to sales or use tax. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales and Purchases. BNA-SUTN SD 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Repair and Replacement Parts

Taxable Sales of motor vehicle repair and replacement parts are subject to South Dakota's sales and use tax. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-2; South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services. However, items that become part of a motor vehicle that are purchased by service stations, garages, and body shops from wholesalers or jobbers, may be purchased for resale. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:45. Items that become part of a motor vehicle include automotive parts, paints, lacquers, greases, lubricants, tire patches, and cement. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:45. A lease or rental business owes sales or use tax on all replacement parts, repair parts, or maintenance items installed in its rental inventory. Sales tax applies to the full charge including labor and delivery charges. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-1.5; S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-46-2.3; South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services; South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Lease and Rentals. Repair and Replacement Parts for Dealer-Owned Vehicles in Inventory for Sale Repair repair and replacement parts purchased by dealers for vehicles owned by a dealership in preparing to sell the vehicles are not subject to tax because they are considered a sale for resale. The dealer must provide an exemption certificate indicating such resale. The addition of parts or customization of a dealer-owned vehicle that will be sold is also not subject to sales tax, unless the vehicle or additional parts are not subject to the motor vehicle excise tax at the point of sale. South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services; South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Repair and Maintenance; South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales. Replacement and repair parts are items that remain with the vehicle for an indefinite period and include items such as tires, headlights, body parts, (e.g., fenders or hoods), hoses, belts, spark plugs, and batteries. South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services; South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Repair and Maintenance; South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales. However, maintenance products sold to such dealer for dealer-owned vehicles in inventory are subject to tax because they are not for resale. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:56. Maintenance items include oils, filters, antifreeze, windshield wiper fluid, and auto detailing or cleaning products. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:03:56; South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services. BNA-SUTN SD 29.3.2.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Short-term Motor Vehicle Rentals

Taxable Sales and use tax is imposed when vehicles are rented for 28 days or less, and there is also a tax imposed at the rate of 4.5 percent on the gross receipts derived from vehicle rentals of 28 days or less. This provision applies to noncommercial motor vehicles, trailers or semitrailers and certain motorcycles. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §32-5B-20, as amended by 2015 S.D. S.B. 93, effective July 1, 2015; South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Leases and Rentals. The rental receipts from noncommercial automobiles, pickups, and vans with a manufacturer shipping weight of 10,000 pounds or less and trailers with an unladen weight of 9,000 pounds or more for a period of 28 days or less are subject to sales tax. South Dakota Tax Facts—Lease and Rental. Motor vehicles may be purchased to lease or rent without sales tax. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Leases and Rentals; see South Dakota Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018 (describing which short-term motor vehicle rentals are subject to state sales tax, municipal sales tax, motor vehicle gross receipts tax, and tourism tax). Recreational vehicles are considered motor vehicles. Leases or rentals for 28 days or less of recreational vehicles, including, ATVs, campers, camping trailers, mopeds, motor coaches, snowmobiles, trailers, and watercraft trailers, are subject to state sales tax, municipal tax, and tourism tax. South Dakota Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. Leases or rentals of motor vehicles under a single contract for more than 28 days are not subject to sales tax or tourism tax. The lease or rental of go-carts, recreational water equipment, snowmobiles, and watercraft is subject to state and municipal sales tax regardless of the length of the contract. South Dakota Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. Sales tax applies to all lease and rental payments for transportation equipment where the customer receives or take possession of the equipment. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales. For leases or rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, or semitrailers that do not qualify as transportation equipment, one payment covers the entire lease or rental. The payment is taxed where the customer receives or takes possession of the property. If the lease or rental includes more than one payment, the payment, including the first payment, is taxed at the primary property location. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Lease and Rentals (July 2016). Transportation equipment includes:• any locomotive and railcar used for the carriage of persons and property in interstate commerce; • any truck/truck-tractor with a Gross Vehicle Weight Rating of 10,001 pounds or greater and any trailer, semitrailer, or passenger bus that is: ○ registered through the International Registration Plan; and ○ operated under authority of a carrier authorized and certificated by the U.S. Department of Transportation or other federal authority to engage in the carriage of persons or property in interstate commerce; and • any container designed for use on and component parts attached or secured on any transportation equipment. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Sales. The following items may be included with a vehicle rental and the charges for these items are subject to the same tax rate as the vehicle rental:• additional driver fee and airport concession fees and facility fees; • car navigational (GPS) system and satellite radio; • child seats and drivers 24 years or younger; • drop charges; • emergency roadside service; • energy surcharges and exceeding mileage; • frequent flyer surcharge; • fuel and service charges and fuel purchase option; • loss damage waiver; • rental tax surcharges; • ski racks; • up charges for nonstandard vehicles; and • vehicle licensing fees passed onto customer. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Leases and Rentals. Rental Insurance A car rental company does not owe sales tax on the insurance premiums customers pay for insurance the rental company sells when the following criteria are met:• the insurance is from a licensed insurance company; • the entire amount charged to the customer for the premium is subject to insurance premiums tax; and • the charge is itemized on the customer's invoice. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Leases and Rentals. If the charge to the customer for the insurance is greater than the amount the insurance company pays insurance premiums tax on, the entire charge is subject to sales tax. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Leases and Rentals. Sourcing If one payment covers the entire rental, the payment is taxed where the customer receives or takes possession of the vehicle. If there is more than one payment, each payment is taxed at the primary property location (the address provided by the lessee)unless the vehicle is moved to a new location and the lessee informs the lessor. Occasional use at a different location does not change the primary property location. South Dakota Tax Facts—Motor Vehicle Leases and Rentals. BNA-SUTN SD 29.3.7.

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on limousine services. See S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-12.1 (exempting “local and suburban passenger transportation” except for limousine services); S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-46-17.3; see also South Dakota Tax Facts No. 130 (identifying several types of taxable and exempt transportation services). BNA-SUTN SD 29.9.6.

==================================================

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Property and Passenger Transportation Property Transportation, General Rule

Exempt South Dakota exempts from sales and use tax transportation of freight and cargo. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-5.2; S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-12.1; S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-46-17.3; South Dakota Tax Facts No. 130. South Dakota also exempts trucking and courier services. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-12.1; see also In re USA Tire Mgmt. Sys., Inc., 2016 BL 22819, 874 N.W.2d 510 (S.D. 2016) (upholding sales tax assessment and denying claim for trucking services exemption where taxpayer took title to, removed and transported tires and casings from foreclosed property under a service contract with foreclosing bank; taxpayer's activities constituted collection and disposal of solid waste). BNA-SUTN SD 29.9.7.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Property and Passenger Transportation Property Transportation, Moving Services

Exempt South Dakota exempts moving services from sales and use tax. See South Dakota Tax Facts No. 130 (identifying moving services as exempt and as a subset of taxable transportation services). BNA-SUTN SD 29.9.8.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Parking and Valet

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on parking space rentals and valet parking services. South Dakota Tax Facts—Storage and Warehousing; South Dakota Tax Facts No. 130 (identifying valet parking as a “personal service”). BNA-SUTN SD 29.10.

==================================================

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Boat Slip and Mooring Fees

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on the rental of boat slips for more than 28 days, where the lessor provides a night guard, gasoline service, towing service, and a check-in system for boat owners. See South Dakota Sec'y of Rev. Declaratory Ruling No. 80-9 see also S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:75 (referencing the boat slip ruling No. 80-9). BNA-SUTN SD 29.11.

==================================================

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Towing

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on receipts from towing services. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:38.01; South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services; see also S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:38 (addressing generally the taxable services that a “garage and service station” provides). The towed-to destination determines the state and municipal sales tax. Receipts for towing to a location outside of South Dakota are not subject to South Dakota sales tax. S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:38.01; South Dakota Tax Facts - Motor Vehicle Repairs and Services. BNA-SUTN SD 29.12.

==================================================

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Car Wash

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on the gross receipts derived from car wash services. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-5; S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:15; S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:38; South Dakota Tax Facts No. 129. A car wash is the consumer of the supplies and must pay sales tax or use tax on the cost of the supplies. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45-5; S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:15; South Dakota Tax Facts No. 129; see also S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:02:38 (addressing generally the taxable services that a “garage and service station” provides). BNA-SUTN SD 29.13.

==================================================

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Car Detailing

Taxable South Dakota imposes sales and use tax on car detailing services. The dealer cannot buy the detailing service for resale. Motor Vehicle Sales, Lease and Rentals, and Repairs (July 2015). BNA-SUTN SD 29.14.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Other Transportation Property and Services

Exempt South Dakota exempts transportation services provided by petroleum distributors. South Dakota Tax Facts No. 130. BNA-SUTN SD 29.15.
