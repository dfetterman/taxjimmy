State: Kentucky



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Kentucky exempts sales of food and food ingredients for human consumption from taxation. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(1). Kentucky specifically excludes food and food ingredients for human consumption from its definitions of “retail sale,” “use,” “storage,”and “consumption.” Thus, Kentucky sales and use tax does not apply to food and food ingredients. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(1); Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.200; Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.310; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kentucky (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN KY 11.1.1.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined Kentucky defines “food and food ingredients” as substances, whether in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that are sold for ingestion or chewing by humans and are consumed for their taste or nutritional value. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(2). Kentucky specifically excludes the following items from the definition of “food and food ingredients”:• alcoholic beverages, defined as beverages that are suitable for human consumption and contain 0.5 percent or more alcohol by volume; • tobacco, which includes cigarettes, cigars, chewing or pipe tobacco, or any other item that contains tobacco; • candy, defined as a preparation of sugar, honey, or other natural or artificial sweeteners in combination with chocolate, fruits, nuts, or other ingredients or flavorings in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. Any items containing flour or requiring refrigeration are not regarded as candy; • dietary supplements, which are any products that are required to be labeled as a dietary supplement, other than tobacco, intended to supplement the diet that contains a vitamin, a mineral, an herb or other botanical, an amino acid, a dietary substance for use by humans to supplement the diet by increasing the total dietary intake, or a concentrate, metabolite, constituent, extract, or combination of any these ingredients. The supplement must be intended for ingestion in tablet, capsule, powder, softgel, gelcap, or liquid form or, if not intended for ingestion in such a form, it must not be represented as conventional food and must not be represented for use as a sole item of a meal or of the diet; and • soft drinks, which are nonalcoholic beverages that contain natural or artificial sweeteners. Drinks like Gatorade, fruit juice cocktails, and flavored water all meet the definition of a soft drink and are taxable. Soft drinks do not include beverages that contain milk or milk products, soy, rice, or similar milk substitutes, or greater than 50 percent of vegetable or fruit juice by volume. Kentucky Sales Tax Facts Dec. 2014. BNA-SUTN KY 11.1.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

Taxable Kentucky imposes sales and use tax on sales of prepared food. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.200. Prepared foods are not exempt from tax as food or food ingredients, and are therefore subject to the sales and use tax. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.200; Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(2)(f); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kentucky (version 2018.0). There are three categories of foods that qualify as prepared foods: (1) foods sold in a heated state or heated by the retailer; (2) two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by the retailer for sale as a single item except food that is only cut, repackaged, or pasteurized by the retailer, eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these raw animal foods requiring cooking by the consumer as recommended by the Food and Drug Administration in Chapter 3, Part 401.11 of the FDA Food Code so as to prevent food-borne illnesses; and (3) Food sold with eating utensils provided by the retailer, including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(2). Restaurants The price of food prepared by a restaurant is subject to tax, including any gratuity, service charge, surcharge or fee that is itemized on the invoice by the restaurant. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(2); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Kentucky (version 2018.0). Restaurant employees must also pay tax on the sales price of any prepared food or taxable items purchased for the employer. 103 Ky. Admin. Regs. 27:220(3). However, food donated by a retail food establishment, such as a restaurant, to a nonprofit organization for distribution to the needy is exempt from tax. 103 Ky. Admin. Regs. 27:220(4)(2). City Tax: Fourth and fifth class cities in Kentucky may impose a 3 percent restaurant tax on retail sales by restaurants doing business in the city. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §91A.400. This tax is considered a license tax that, when passed to the customers, is included in gross receipts and subject to sales tax. 103 Ky. Admin. Regs. 27:220(5); see Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.010(15)(a)(2). Use Tax: Tangible personal property is subject to tax on the purchase price if it was purchased exempt from tax with a resale certificate or a SSUTA exemption certificate or if it was provided free of charge to an employee or otherwise used or consumed by the restaurant. 103 Ky. Admin. Regs. 27:220(4)(1); Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement - Certificate of Exemption.. However, food and food ingredients donated by a restaurant to charity are not subject to tax. 103 Ky. Admin. Regs. 27:220(4)(2). School Cafeterias and Lunchrooms Sales of food to students in school cafeterias or lunchrooms are exempt from sales tax. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.495. BNA-SUTN KY 11.2.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Definition Kentucky defines “prepared food” to include 3 categories of food. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(3)(f). There are three categories of foods that qualify as prepared foods:• foods sold in a heated state or heated by the retailer; • two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by the retailer for sale as a single item except food that is only cut, repackaged, or pasteurized by the retailer, eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these raw animal foods requiring cooking by the consumer as recommended by the Food and Drug Administration in Chapter 3, Part 401.11 of the FDA Food Code so as to prevent food-borne illnesses; • food sold with eating utensils provided by the retailer, including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(3)(g). “Prepared food”does not include:• food sold by a seller whose NAICS classifications is manufacturing in sector 311; or • bakery items, including bread, rolls, buns, biscuits, bagels, croissants, pastries, donuts, danishes, cakes, tortes, pies, tarts, muffins, cookies, and tortillas. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.485(3)(h). BNA-SUTN KY 11.2.2.
