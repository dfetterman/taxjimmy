State: New Mexico



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Nonmember New Mexico is an advisory state of the Streamlined Sales Tax Project, but has not yet enacted legislation to comply with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement; thus, New Mexico is not a member state of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales Tax State Members. However, New Mexico has authorized the Secretary of Taxation and Revenue to enter into the Agreement. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-5A-7. BNA-SUTN NM 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member New Mexico is a member of the Multistate Tax Compact. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-5-1;Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. As a member of the Compact, purchasers may receive a tax credit for the amount of sales and use tax paid to another state for the same tangible personal property. In addition, New Mexico allows sellers to accept the Uniform Multijurisdiction Exemption Certificate for some transactions. N.M. Stat. Ann. §7-5-1;Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. BNA-SUTN NM 2.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

Border States Caucus New Mexico, Arizona, California, Texas, and the United Mexican States have formed the Border State Caucus in order to promote trade in the Southwest Region of the United States. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.201.19. New Mexico will accept a Border State Sale for Resale Certificate from businesses operating within Arizona, California, New Mexico, Texas, Utah, Oklahoma, and Mexico. The Certificate is to be presented when the business purchases goods for resale across state or national borders. N.M. Admin. Code tit. 3, §2.201.19. BNA-SUTN NM 2.3.
