State: Nebraska



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Nebraska enacted all provisions necessary to comply with the requirements of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement and became a full member on July 1, 2005. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Nebraska (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax State Members. For the latest version of Nebraska's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (July 13, 2018). On May 6, 2003, Nebraska enacted L.B. 282 in order to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. 2003 Neb. L.B. 282, effective January 1, 2004. Additionally, Nebraska enacted cleanup legislation L.B. 1017 on April 15, 2004, which took effect July 1, 2004. 2004 Neb. L.B. 1017, effective July 1, 2004. As a full member, Nebraska maintains a seat on the governing board and may vote on amendments and interpretations of the Agreement. All sellers registering under the SST system are required to collect and remit tax on sales to Nebraska. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-2712.03; see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN NE 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Nebraska is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. For the latest version of Nebraska's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (July 13, 2018). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Nebraska was not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017). However, in its report dated Nov. 22, 2016, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee recommended that Nebraska be found out-of-compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). This was because the state classifies gastric bands, implanted expander - tissue and breast, intragastric balloons, and wheelchair cushions in a manner inconsistent with appendices L and M (regarding health care items) of the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board's Rules and Procedures. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). The Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board did not issue a formal notice of noncompliance. For the latest version of Nebraska's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (July 13, 2018). BNA-SUTN NE 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate and Project Member Nebraska is a an Associate and Project Member of the Multistate Tax Commission(MTC). Mutlistate Tax Commission, Member States. The MTC provides the following services:• encouraging states to adopt uniform tax laws and regulations that apply to multistate enterprises; • maintaining a Joint Audit Program; • operating a National Nexus Program to encourage voluntary disclosure; and • participating in significant court cases and working with lawmakers. BNA-SUTN NE 2.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

Yes The Department of Revenue may enter into agreements with any federal office, state agency, or local subdivision of state government for the proper administration of Nebraska tax laws. Nebraska Rev. Stat. §77-373. Nebraska is a member of the Midwest States Association of Tax Administrators (MSATA), a regional association formed to promote cooperation in tax matters amongst states located in the same geographic region. Federation of Tax Administrators, FTA Regions. Nebraska is a member of the Midwest Border States Compact. The Midwest Border States Compact seeks to increase tax compliance through informing consumers about use tax and seeking voluntary registration from out-of-state businesses. Nebraska Department of Revenue, Midwest Border States Compact. BNA-SUTN NE 2.3.
