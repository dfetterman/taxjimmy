State: North Dakota



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member North Dakota is a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.4-01;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, North Dakota Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, North Dakota (version 2018.0). North Dakota filed a Petition for Membership and Certificate of Compliance with the co-chairs of the Streamlined Sales Tax Implementing States on Feb. 28, 2005. On July 1, 2005, the Petitioning States unanimously approved North Dakota as an associate member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. North Dakota became a full-member state on Oct. 1, 2005 after having met all of the requirements for membership as set forth in the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, North Dakota Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0)Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, North Dakota (version 2018.0); see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN ND 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, North Dakota is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, North Dakota Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a Compliance Review Report finding that North Dakota is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). On April 8, 2003, North Dakota enacted 2003 N.D. S.B. 2095 and 2003 N.D. S.B. 2096 in order to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. The streamlined provisions are effective Oct. 1, 2005. 2003 N.D. S.B. 2095, §1; 2003 N.D. S.B. 2096, §1. The provisions were originally set to take effect Jan. 1, 2006, but were accelerated under 2005 N.D. S.B. 2050, effective Oct. 1, 2005. Additionally, North Dakota addressed the multiple rate issue by replacing the sales tax on items taxed at a rate different from the general rate with a separate gross receipts tax. Therefore, alcoholic beverages and farm machinery are exempt from sales tax, but subject to a 7-percent and 3-percent gross receipts tax respectively. N.D. Cent. Code § 57-39.5-02; N.D. Cent. Code § 57-39.6-02. BNA-SUTN ND 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member On Aug. 1, 1995, North Dakota became a member of the Multistate Tax Compact. N.D. Cent. Code §57-59-01. The purpose of the MTC is to:• determine the proper tax liability of multistate taxpayers; • promote uniformity in state tax systems; • make more convenient the filing of tax returns for taxpayers; and • avoid multiple and duplicative taxes. N.D. Cent. Code §57-59-01. BNA-SUTN ND 2.2.
