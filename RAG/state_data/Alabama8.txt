State: Alabama



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Taxable Alabama imposes sales tax on the sale of all food or grocery items purchased within the state. Ala. Code §40-23-2(1). Vending machine sales of food and food products for human consumption, coffee, milk, milk products, and substitutes for these products are taxable at a reduced rate of 3 percent. Ala. Code §40-23-2(5); Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.183.02. Food and food products that qualify for the special 3 percent tax rate include, but are not limited to:• sandwiches; • candy; • potato chips; and • crackers. Ala. Code §40-23-2(5); Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.183.02. BNA-SUTN AL 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Depends Whether other sales of food, and food products are subject to sales and use tax depends on where the food is consumed and who sells or purchases it. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.101. School Lunches Alabama exempts from taxation sales of lunches to school children within school buildings. This exemption spans kindergarten through high school, and encompasses both private and public schools. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.99; Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.100; Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.101. Meals Sold by Boarding Houses Sales of food and meals sold by boarding houses within the state are not considered retail sales, and therefore, not subject to taxation, if the charge for food is a lump sum charge covering the cost of food for a week or a month. The food being purchased with the lump sum payment must not be available to the public, or else the sale is considered a sale at retail subject to tax. The boarding house operator is considered to be rendering a service under Alabama law, and therefore, is responsible for paying the tax due on any products used or consumed while performing the service. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.99. Meals Sold by Transportation Companies Alabama taxes sales of meals by railroads, airlines, and other transportation companies within the state, so long as the meal is served while the recipient is still within the state. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.100(1). Sales of food and meals served by a transportation company are retail sales, and therefore, taxable when the transportation company includes within its ticket price a sum covering the sales price of the food or meal. The tax is imposed on the amount for the food or meal included in the ticket price. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-1-.100(2); see also Alabama Dept. of Rev. v. Logan's Roadhouse, Inc., 85 So. 3d 403 (Ala. Ct. Civ. App. 2011). Food Purchased by the Sheriff Alabama exempts from taxation sales of food to a sheriff for the purpose of feeding prisoners under his watch. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-3-.67. Food Banks Alabama exempts from sales and use taxation all food banks within the state, so long as the food bank is an affiliated food bank of America's Second Harvest food bank or its subsidiary distribution organizations. Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-3-.23.01(1); Ala. Admin. Code r. 810-6-3-.23.01(3). BNA-SUTN AL 11.10.

==================================================
