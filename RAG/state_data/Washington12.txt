State: Washington



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Washington became a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement on July 1, 2008. Wash. Rev. Code ยง82.58.030; Wash. Rev. Code ยง82.02.210;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Washington(version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Washington (version 2018.0). In 2003 and 2004, Washington enacted legislation to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, the bulk of which, took effect July 1, 2004. 2003 Wash. S.B. 5783, effective July 1, 2004; 2004 Wash. S.B. 6515, effective July 1, 2004. However, this legislation lacked destination-based sourcing rules for tangible personal property, resulting in nonconforming provisions that kept the state from being in substantial compliance and becoming a Member State. On March 22, 2007, Washington enacted new legislation that included the necessary sourcing rules. 2007 Wash. S.B. 5089, effective July 1, 2008 (Washington enacted S.B. 5566 on April 30, 2009, which provided missing sourcing rules for ancillary services and reenacted origin-based sourcing for direct mail). The 2007 legislation also committed to mitigating revenue losses to local jurisdictions as a result of the state joining the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Washington Dept. of Rev., Streamlined Sales Tax Mitigation Program Report (Nov. 1, 2018). The purpose of the Agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance through improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections, having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, having uniformity of major tax base definitions, having a central electronic registration system for all member states, simplifying state and local tax rates, having uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, simplifying administration of exemptions, having simplified tax returns, simplifying tax remittances, and protecting consumer privacy. See Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN WA 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Washington is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Washington (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Washington is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN WA 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member The state of Washington is a compact member of the Multistate Tax Compact, and has entered the provisions of the Compact into the law of the state. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Multistate Tax Commission. BNA-SUTN WA 2.2.
