Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Oil and Gas Machinery and Equipment

Depends Tangible personal property used in the oil and gas industry may be exempt from sales and use tax in North Dakota, depending on the specific use or activity in which the property is involved. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.6(1); N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.10. Tangible Personal Property Used to Expand or Construct an Oil Refinery North Dakota exempts from tax gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property, including machinery and equipment, used in constructing or expanding an oil refinery that has the capacity to process at least 5,000 oil barrels daily. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.6(1). The owner of the oil refinery must request an exemption certificate from the Tax Commissioner prior to the purchase of the qualifying tangible personal property. If the owner of the oil refinery does not receive a certificate exempting the tangible personal property prior to the purchase, the owner is required to pay the tax imposed and apply for a refund. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.6(2), as amended by 2011 N.D. S.B. 2202, effective July 1, 2011; see Office of State Tax Comm'r, 2015/16 Tax Incentives for Businesses. If a contractor purchases or installs the exempted tangible personal property and pays the tax imposed, the owner of the oil refinery may apply for a refund of the difference between the amount of tax paid by the contractor and the allowable exemption amount. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.6(3), as amended by 2011 N.D. S.B. 2202, effective July 1, 2011; N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-03.3(4)(c), as amended by 2011 N.D. S.B. 2202, effective July 1, 2011. Environmental Upgrades to Oil Refineries and Gas Processing Plants Effective through June 30, 2017, gross receipts from sales of environmental upgrade equipment or tangible personal property used to add environmental upgrades are exempt from tax. To qualify, the investment must amount to $100,000 and be used for machinery, equipment, and related facilities that reduce emissions, increase efficiency, or enhance reliability of the equipment at a new or existing oil refinery or gas processing plant. N.D. Cent. Code § 57-39.2-04.2, as amended by 2013 N.D. H.B. 1382, effective April 20, 2013; see Office of State Tax Comm'r, 2015/16 Tax Incentives for Businesses. A refinery or gas processing plant's owner must request an exemption certificate from the Tax Commissioner for environmental upgrade equipment before the purchase. If the system's owner does not receive a certificate exempting environmental upgrade equipment before purchase, then the owner must pay the tax imposed and apply for a refund. N.D. Cent. Code § 57-39.2-04.2, as amended by 2013 N.D. H.B. 1382, effective April 20, 2013. Materials Used to Construct a Processing Facility to Produce Liquefied Natural Gas Gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property used to construct or expand a processing facility in North Dakota to produce liquefied natural gas are exempt from sales and use tax. To be exempt, the tangible personal property must be incorporated in the structure of the facility or used in the construction process to the point of having no residual economic value. To receive the exemption at the time of purchase, the owner of the processing facility must receive from the Commissioner a certificate that the tangible personal property used to construct the processing facility which the owner intends to purchase qualifies for the exemption. If a certificate is not received prior to the purchase, the owner shall pay the applicable tax and apply to the Commissioner for a refund. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.10. If the tangible personal property is purchased or installed by a contractor subject to the tax, the owner may apply for a refund of the difference between the amount remitted by the contractor and the exemption imposed or allowed. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.10(3). Materials Used in Compressing, Processing, Gathering or Refining Gas Gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property are exempt from tax when the property is used to expand or build a gas-processing facility or used in any system that is constructed or expanded to compress, process, gather, or refine gas recovered from either an oil or gas well located in North Dakota. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.5(1). This exemption applies to the extraction and processing of coal gasification byproducts, as well as the collection/recovery of natural gas. 2013 N.D. H.B. 1134, §2,effective July 1, 2013, and 2013 N.D. H.B. 1413, effective July 1, 2013; see Office of State Tax Comm'r, 2015/16 Tax Incentives for Businesses. Additionally, gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property used to construct or expand a system used to compress, gather, collect, store, transport, or inject carbon dioxide for use in enhanced recovery of oil or natural gas are exempt from sales and use taxes. To be exempt, the tangible personal property must be incorporated in the system. Tangible personal property used to replace an existing system to compress, gather, collect, store, transport or inject carbon dioxide for use in enhanced recovery of oil or natural gas does not qualify for exemption unless the replacement property creates an expansion of the system. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.14, as added by 2015 N.D. S.B. 2318, effective July 1, 2015; see Office of State Tax Comm'r, 2015/16 Tax Incentives for Businesses. The owner of the above systems must request an exemption certificate from the Tax Commissioner for such property prior to the time of purchase. If the owner of the system does not receive a certificate exempting the tangible personal property prior to purchase, the owner is required to pay the tax imposed and apply for a refund. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.5(2), as amended by 2011 N.D. S.B. 2202, effective July 1, 2011; N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-B, as added by 2015 N.D. S.B. 2318, effective July 1, 2015. If a contractor purchases or installs the exempted tangible personal property and pays the tax imposed, the owner of the system may apply for a refund of the difference between the amount of tax remitted by the contractor and the allowable exemption amount. N.D. Cent. Code § 57-39.2-04.5(3), as amended by 2011 N.D. S.B. 2202, effective July 1, 2011; N.D. Cent. Code § 57-40.2-03.3(4)(d), as amended by 2011 N.D. S.B. 2202, effective July 1, 2011. Carbon Dioxide Gross receipts from sales of tangible personal property used to construct or expand a system used to compress, gather, collect, store, transport, or inject carbon dioxide for secure geologic storage or use in enhanced recovery of oil or natural gas in North Dakota are exempt from taxes. To be exempt, the tangible personal property must be incorporated into a system used to compress, gather, collect, store, transport, or inject carbon dioxide for secure geologic storage or use in enhanced recovery of oil or natural gas. Tangible personal property used to replace an existing system to compress, gather, collect, store, transport, or inject carbon dioxide for secure geologic storage or use in enhanced recovery of oil or natural gas does not qualify for exemption unless the replacement creates an expansion of the system. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.14(1), as amended by 2019 N.D. H.B. 1439, effective June 30, 2019 (extending exemption to secure geologic storage). To receive the exemption at the time of purchase, the owner of the gas compressing, gathering, collecting, storing, transporting, or injecting system must receive from the Tax Commissioner a certificate that the tangible personal property used to construct or expand a system used to compress, gather, collect, store, transport, or inject carbon dioxide for secure geologic storage or use in enhanced recovery of oil or natural gas qualifies for the exemption. If a certificate is not received before the purchase, the owner shall pay the applicable tax imposed by this chapter and apply to the Tax Commissioner for a refund. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.14(2), as amended by 2019 N.D. H.B. 1439, effective June 30, 2019 (extending exemption to secure geologic storage). Biodiesel Facilities North Dakota exempts from sales tax the sale of equipment to a licensed refiner, supplier or other licensed facility that enables such facility to sell diesel fuel containing at least 2 percent biodiesel or green diesel fuel. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04(51); see Office of State Tax Comm'r, 2015/16 Tax Incentives for Businesses. Straddle Plant, Fractionator, and Associated Infrastructure Effective July 1, 2019, tangible personal property used to construct or expand a straddle plant, fractionator, or associated infrastructure are exempt from sales tax. Code §57-40.2-03.3(4), as amended by 2019 N.D. H.B. 1205, §§3, 4, effective July 1, 2019; North Dakota Sales Tax Newsletter June 2019. Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Depends North Dakota provides exemptions for specified items used in wind farms and hydrogen generation facilities. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.2(1)(c)(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.2(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-04.2(1)(c)(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-04.2(2). The exemption for wind farms will expire on July 1, 2017. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.2(1)(c)(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.2(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-04.2(1)(c)(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-04.2(2); North Dakota Sales Tax Newsletter June 2017. Wind Powered Electrical Generating Facilities (Wind Farms) Until July 1, 2017, North Dakota offers a sales and use tax exemption for purchasing building materials, production equipment, and other tangible personal property used to construct or expand wind powered electrical generating facilities. To qualify for the exemption, construction must be complete before January 1, 2017. Further, the facility must have at least one single electrical energy generation unit with a nameplate capacity of 100 kilowatts or more. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.2(1)(c)(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04.2(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-04.2(1)(c)(2); N.D. Cent. Code §57-40.2-04.2(2). Legislation attempting to make this exemption permanent did not pass during the 2017 legislative session, and the exemption will expire with the fiscal year ending June 30, 2017. North Dakota Sales Tax Newsletter June 2017. Hydrogen Fuel A sales tax exemption is available for sales of hydrogen to power an internal combustion engine or fuel cell and equipment used directly and exclusively in production and storage of the hydrogen by a hydrogen generation facility in North Dakota. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04(50); see Office of State Tax Comm'r, 2015/16 Tax Incentives for Businesses. BNA-SUTN ND 30.5.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Energy Conservation

No Guidance North Dakota does not enumerate an explicit exemption for machinery and equipment purchased for energy conservation purposes, but that equipment may qualify for other exemptions. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-02.1; N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04. BNA-SUTN ND 30.6.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Wastewater Treatment

Taxable North Dakota does not enumerate an explicit exemption for machinery and equipment purchased for wastewater treatment purposes. N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-02.1; N.D. Cent. Code §57-39.2-04. BNA-SUTN ND 30.7.
