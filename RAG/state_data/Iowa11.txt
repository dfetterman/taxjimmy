State: Iowa



Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Sales Tax

Yes Iowa imposes sales tax on sales of tangible personal property, utilities, admissions, and enumerated services within the state. Iowa Code Ann. §423.2. Sellers are required to collect the sales tax from their purchasers. Iowa Code Ann. §423.14; Iowa Code Ann. §423.29. BNA-SUTN IA 1.1.1.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Use Tax

Yes Iowa imposes use tax on the purchase price or installed purchase price of tangible personal property and taxable services that are used, consumed, or stored within the state. Iowa Code Ann. §423.5. Purchases such as those made through mail-order catalogs, television shopping programs, the internet, toll-free 800 numbers, magazine subscriptions, and untaxed purchases made while in another state are subject to Iowa's use tax. Iowa Code Ann. §423.5; Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(62);Iowa Dept. of Rev., Don't Let Compliance be Taxing This Holiday Season. BNA-SUTN IA 1.1.2.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Local Option Sales and Use Tax

Local Tax Authorized Iowa permits a county to impose a local sales and services tax of not more than 1 percent on the sales price subject to tax by the state. Iowa Code Ann. §422B.8. The local sales and services tax must be imposed on the same basis as the state sales and services tax. Iowa Code Ann. §422B.8; Iowa Code Ann. §423B.5; Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-107.6, as amended by 41 Iowa Admin. Bull. No. 18 (Feb. 27, 2019), effective April 3, 2019. Iowa does not have a local option use tax. Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-107.6, as amended by 41 Iowa Admin. Bull. No. 18 (Feb. 27, 2019), effective April 3, 2019. BNA-SUTN IA 1.1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Excise and Replacement Taxes

Yes Excise Taxes Iowa imposes various excise taxes that operate similarly to the state sales and use tax. See, e.g., Iowa Code Ann. §123.3. These taxes include, but are not limited to:• an alcoholic beverage tax; Iowa Code Ann. §123.3 through Iowa Code Ann. §123.184. • a cigarette tax; Iowa Code Ann. §453A.1 through Iowa Code Ann. §453A.40. • an insurance premium tax; Iowa Code Ann. §432.1. and • a motor vehicle and aviation fuels tax. Iowa Code Ann. §452A.1 through Iowa Code Ann. §452A.86. For more information on Iowa's excise taxes, see the Iowa Excise Tax Navigator, beginning at Iowa 1.1. Replacement Taxes The Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement prohibits member states from imposing a “prohibited replacement tax,” which is a tax outside of the sales and use tax that is imposed on products defined in the Agreement. Generally, the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board will make a finding regarding whether a member state has imposed a replacement tax. Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018, Section 334; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board Rules and Procedures, as amended through May 3, 2018, Rule 334. Iowa is a full member. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Iowa (version 2018.1); Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Governing Board, State Info. BNA-SUTN IA 1.1.4.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Historical Information

Est. 1934 Iowa enacted its sales and use tax in April 1, 1934 at a rate of 2 percent. Iowa Dept. of Rev., Tax Rate History. Since 1934, Iowa has adjusted its sales tax rate several times. The last adjustment was on July 1, 2008, when the rate was increased from 5 percent to 6 percent. Iowa Tax Rate History;Iowa Dept. of Rev., 2015 Iowa Tax Expenditure Study: Initial Release, (Feb. 1, 2017). This table can be found here. BNA-SUTN IA 1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Sale' and 'Retail Sale'

Defined “Sale”means any transfer, exchange, or barter, conditional or otherwise, in any manner or by any means whatsoever, for consideration, including but not limited to any such transfer, exchange, or barter on a subscription basis. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(50), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §164, effective May 30, 2018. “Subscription”means any arrangement in which a person has the right or ability to access, receive, use, obtain, purchase, or otherwise acquire tangible personal property, specified digital products, or services on a permanent or less than permanent basis, regardless of whether the person actually accesses, receives, uses, obtains, purchases, or otherwise acquires such tangible personal property, specified digital product, or service. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(57A), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §167, effective May 30, 2018. “Retail sale”means any sale, lease, or rental for any purpose other than resale, sublease, or subrent. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(46). “Sales” do not include: • the sale of tangible personal property for an exempt purpose; Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-15.3. or • certain corporate mergers. Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-15.20. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Lease'

Defined Iowa defines “lease” or “rental” as any transfer of possession or control of, or access to, tangible personal property or specified digital products for a fixed or indeterminate term for consideration. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(24), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §156, effective Jan. 1, 2019. A lease or rental may include future options to purchase the property or extend the lease. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(24), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §156, effective Jan. 1, 2019. However, a lease does not include any of the following:• a transfer of possession or control of property under a security agreement or deferred payment plan that requires the transfer of title upon completion of the required payments; • a transfer of possession or control of property under an agreement that requires the transfer of title upon completion of required payments, and payment of any option price does not exceed the greater of one hundred dollars or 1 percent of the total required payments; or • providing tangible personal property along with an operator, who is necessary for the equipment to perform as designed, for a fixed or indeterminate period of time. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(24), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §156, effective Jan. 1, 2019. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.2.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Use' or 'Storage, Use, or Consumption'

Defined Iowa defines “use” as the exercise of any right or power over tangible personal property. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(62). Effective January 1, 2019, the definition of “use” is amended to include the exercise by any person of any right or power over or access to tangible personal property or a specified digital product incident to the ownership of that property, or any right or power over or access to the product or result of a service. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(62), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §168, effective Jan. 1, 2019. A retailer's or building contractor's sale of manufactured housing for use in Iowa is also considered a use of property for the purposes of determining use tax due. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(62). The term “use”does not include the right to sell property owned in the regular course of business, the manufacture of the property into another article of tangible personal property to be sold, or the return of a qualifying defective vehicle to its manufacturer. Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-28.1. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Tangible Personal Property'

Defined Iowa defines “tangible personal property” as personal property that can be seen, weighed, measured, felt, or touched, or that is in any other manner perceptible to the senses. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(59); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. “Tangible personal property” includes electricity, water, gas, steam, and prewritten computer software. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(59); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.4.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Intangible Property'

Not Defined Iowa does not define “intangible property.” Iowa Code Ann. §423.1; Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.5.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Retailer' or 'Vendor'

Defined Iowa defines “retailer” as every person engaged in the business of selling tangible personal property or taxable services at retail. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(47); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. A retailer also includes any person furnishing of gas, electricity, water, or communication services, and tickets or admissions to places of amusement and athletic events or operating amusement devices or other forms of commercial amusement from which revenues are derived. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(47); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. However, when in the opinion of the director it is necessary for efficient administration to regard any salespersons, representatives, truckers, peddlers, or canvassers as agents of the dealers, distributors, supervisors, employers, or persons under whom they operate or from whom they obtain tangible personal property sold by them irrespective of whether or not they are making sales on their own behalf or on behalf of such dealers, distributors, supervisors, employers, or persons, the director may so regard them, and may regard such dealers, distributors, supervisors, employers, or persons as retailers. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(47); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. Additionally, a “retailer"includes a seller obligated to collect sales or use tax. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(47); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. Iowa defines a “seller” as any person making sales, leases, or rentals of personal property or services. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(53); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. Effective January 1, 2019, the definition of “retailer” is amended to include persons engaged in the business of selling specified digital products. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(47), as amended by 2018 Iowa S.F. 2417, §160, effective Jan. 1, 2019. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.6.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Purchaser' or 'Consumer'

Defined Iowa defines a “purchaser” as a person to whom a sale of personal property is made or to whom a service is furnished. Iowa Code Ann. §423.1(42); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-211.1. BNA-SUTN IA 1.4.7.
