State: South Dakota



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member South Dakota is a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. S.D. Codified Laws Ann. §10-45C-3. Prior to 2003, South Dakota implemented many of the provisions of an older version of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. 2002 S.D. H.B. 1001, enacted Feb. 28, 2002. On March 5, 2003, South Dakota enacted S.B. 76 in order to bring the state into compliance with the November 2002 version of the agreement, effective July 1, 2003, but South Dakota joined as a full member on July 5, 2005. See 2003 S.D. S.B. 76 (accelerated the effective date for various changes to local sales tax). Effective January 1, 2004, a municipality's sales and use tax had to conform to the state tax, except with respect to rate, which may not exceed two percent. Moreover, the accelerated effective date also applied to the repeal of certain local sales tax exemptions. The South Dakota Department of Revenue expected that the Legislature would fine-tune its sales tax laws over the years to ensure compliance. See S.D. Admin. R. 64:06:01:21 to 64:06:03:63 (nonconsecutive); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, South Dakota (version 2018.0). For the latest version of South Dakota's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (June 20, 2018). The purpose of the Agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance through improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections, having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, having uniformity of major tax base definitions, having a central electronic registration system for all member states, simplifying state and local tax rates, having uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, simplifying administration of exemptions, having simplified tax returns, simplifying tax remittances, and protecting consumer privacy. See Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN SD 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, South Dakota is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. For the latest version of South Dakota's Certificate of Compliance, see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance (June 20, 2018). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that South Dakota is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN SD 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate Member South Dakota is an associate member of the Multistate Tax Compact. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. The purposes of the compact are to:• facilitate proper determination of state and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers, including the equitable apportionment of tax bases and settlement of apportionment disputes; • promote uniformity or compatibility in significant components of tax systems; • facilitate taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration; and • avoid duplicative taxation. Multistate Tax Commission, Model Multistate Tax Compact. BNA-SUTN SD 2.2.
