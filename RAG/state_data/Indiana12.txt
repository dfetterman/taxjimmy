State: Indiana



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member On Oct. 1, 2005, Indiana became a full member of the Streamlined Sales Tax Agreement. Ind. Code Ann. §6-2.5-11-5; see Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Important Documents, Indiana. On May 8, 2003, Indiana enacted H.B. 1815, effective January 1, 2004, to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. 2003 H.B. 1815 added several definitions from the Agreement, specified charges for delivery and preparation that are included in the taxable sales price, delineated taxable and nontaxable food items, provided for a bad debt deduction, provided for sourcing non-mobile telecommunications, and delineated the tax treatment of direct mail. See the Indiana Commissioner's Directive 21. 2004 H.B. 1365 further addressed delivery and installation charges, and 2005 S.B. 213 excluded tobacco from the sales tax exemption for food and permitted monetary allowances for sellers and certified service providers who collect sales and use tax; see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Indiana (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN IN 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Indiana is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Indiana (version 2018.0); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, User Guide: Online Taxability Matrix and Certificate of Compliance (Nov. 20, 2018). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Indiana was not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017). However, in its report dated Nov. 22, 2016, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee of the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board found that Indiana was out-of-compliance, because the state did not accept the Simplified Electronic Return (SER) from Model 4 sellers, or from sellers who are not registered through the Streamlined central registration system. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN IN 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate/Project Member Indiana is an associate or project member to the Multistate Tax Commission, which means that Indiana my participate in Commission meetings, programs, and projects, as well as consult and cooperate with the Commission and its members. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. BNA-SUTN IN 2.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Other Interstate Agreements

Reciprocity for Vehicle Purchases The Indiana Department of Revenue is authorized to enter into reciprocity agreements with other states regarding the taxation of purchases of cargo trailers and recreational vehicles by nonresidents. Ind. Code Ann. §6-2.5-10-6, as added by 2017 Ind. S.B. 172, effective July 1, 2017. Under such an agreement, sales of cargo trailers and recreational vehicles are exempt from Indiana sales tax if:• the purchaser is a nonresident; • within 30 days of receiving delivery of the cargo trailer or recreational vehicle, the purchaser transports it out of Indiana; • the cargo trailer or recreational vehicle will be titled or registered for use in another state or country, and not in Indiana; and • the state or country in which the cargo trailer or recreational vehicle will be titled or registered provides a reciprocal exemption from sales, use, or similar taxes for cargo trailers and recreational vehicles purchased by Indiana residents that will be titled or registered in Indiana. Ind. Code Ann. §6-2.5-5-39(c); Indiana Tax Information Sales Tax Bulletin 72. For more information about sales taxes imposed on purchases of vehicles, including cargo trailers and recreational vehicles, see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Indiana 29.3.1. BNA-SUTN IN 2.3.
