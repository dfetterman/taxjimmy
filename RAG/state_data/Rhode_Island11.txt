State: Rhode Island



Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Sales Tax

Yes Rhode Island imposes a sales tax of 7 percent on retail sales. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-18. The state also imposes an additional 1-percent local tax on meals, R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-18.1. and a 6-percent tax on hotels (consisting of a 5-percent state hotel tax and a 1-percent local tax, and is in addition to any applicable sale and use tax). R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-36.1(a). The sales and use tax base for local jurisdictions is the same as the state sale and use tax base, unless otherwise prohibited by federal law. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1-3; R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1-9. Sales tax generally applies to sales at retail in Rhode Island. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-18. “Retail sale” means any sale, lease or rentals of tangible personal property, for any purpose other than resale, sublease or subrent in the regular course of business. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-8. Tax also applies to certain designated services, such as fabricating or processing of property for customers. R.I. Code Regs. tit. 280, §20-70-20.8, replacing R.I. Regs. SU 87-88, effective June 25, 2018. However, professional and personal services are not subject to the sales and use tax. BNA-SUTN RI 1.1.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Use Tax

Yes Complementing Rhode Island's sales tax is its use tax, which is imposed upon the storage, use, or consumption of tangible personal property in the state. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-20. The storage, use, or other consumption in Rhode Island of property, the gross receipts from the sale of which are required to be included in the measure of the sales tax, is exempted from the use tax. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-34. BNA-SUTN RI 1.1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Local Option Sales and Use Tax

No Local Tax The Rhode Island legislature has not passed legislation authorizing Rhode Island municipalities to impose local sales and use tax. See R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-18; R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-20. BNA-SUTN RI 1.1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Excise and Replacement Taxes

Yes Excise Taxes Rhode Island's local meals and beverage tax are excise taxes that are applied in a manner similar to sales and use tax. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-36.1(a), as amended by 2015 R.I. H. 5900, effective July 1, 2015; R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-18.1(a); R.I. Code Regs. tit. 280, §20-70-4.6, replacing R.I. Regs. MBT 09-01, effective March 18, 2018, and amended effective July 23, 2018; Rhode Island Div. of Taxn., Meals &Beverage Tax Guidelines. For additional discussion of meals tax in Rhode Island, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Rhode Island 10.2. Replacement Taxes The Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement prohibits member states from imposing a “prohibited replacement tax” which is a tax outside of the sales and use tax that is imposed on products defined in the Agreement. Generally, the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board will make a finding regarding whether or not a member state has imposed a replacement tax. Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018, Section 334; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board Rules and Procedures, as amended through May 3, 2018; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Rhode Island (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN RI 1.1.4.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Historical Information

Est. 1947 Rhode Island enacted its sales and use Tax Act in 1947. Rhode Island Tax News Third Quarter 2012. In 2007, Rhode Island joined a national coalition of states and conformed its sales and use tax laws to the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement (SSUTA). R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1-1;Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Rhode Island(version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Rhode Island (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN RI 1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Pending and Trending Developments

Nexus/Marketplace, Renumbering, Cloud Computing Nexus and Marketplace Facilitators Effective June 27, 2019, remote sellers, marketplace facilitators, and referrers who in the preceding calendar year had gross revenue of $100,000 or more or 200 or more separate transactions involving the sale of tangible personal property, prewritten computer software delivered electronically or by load and leave, vendor-hosted prewritten computer software, and/or taxable services for delivery into the state must collect sales tax on their sales. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-15.2(a)(1); R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.2-3(E), both amended by 2019 R.I. S. 251 effective March 29, 2019. Renumbering Regulations Rhode Island is in the process of renumbering and reformatting all of its regulations into the Rhode Island Code of Regulations. According to the Rhode Island Department of State, this process will be complete by Dec. 31, 2018. Taxpayers should expect to see significantly more proposed and final rules that usual during the course of 2018, and should recognize that the majority of these rules will not include substantive changes. Rhode Island Tax News Second Quarter 2018; Rhode Island Dept. of State, State Rules and Regulations (last visited April 23, 2019). Cloud Computing Effective Oct. 1, 2018, the sale, storage, use, or other consumption of vendor-hosted prewritten computer software, is subject to the sales or use tax regardless of whether the prewritten software is downloaded or not, and regardless of whether the access to, or use of, the software is permanent or temporary. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7(15), as added by 2018 R.I. H. 7200, effective July 1, 2018; Rhode Island Advisory 2018-38 (Sept. 4, 2018); Rhode Island Tax News First Quarter 2019; see Rhode Island Dept. of Rev., Summary of Legislative Changes (July 12, 2018). Ridesharing With the advent of transportation network companies, ride sharing companies and ride-sourcing companies, which allow consumers to obtain transportation services through digital networks, Rhode Island has joined a growing number of states that have passed legislation making these services subject to state sales and use tax. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7.3, as amended by 2016 R.I. H. 7454, effective July 1, 2016. BNA-SUTN RI 1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Sale' and 'Retail Sale'

Defined Rhode Island defines the terms “sale” and “retail sale”very broadly. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7; R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-8. In Rhode Island “sale”means any one of the following:• any transfer of title or possession, exchange, barter, lease, or rental, conditional or otherwise, in any manner or by any means of tangible personal property for a consideration. Transfer of possession, lease, or rental includes transactions found by the Tax Administrator to be in lieu of a transfer of title, exchange, or barter; • the producing, fabricating, processing, printing, or imprinting of tangible personal property for a consideration for consumers who furnish either directly or indirectly the materials used in the producing, fabricating, processing, printing, or imprinting; • the furnishing and distributing of tangible personal property for a consideration by social, athletic, and similar clubs and fraternal organizations to their members or others; • the furnishing, preparing, or serving for consideration of food, meals, or drinks, including any cover, minimum, entertainment, or other charge in connection therewith; • a transaction whereby the possession of tangible personal property is transferred, but the seller retains the title as security for the payment of the price; • any withdrawal, except a withdrawal pursuant to a transaction in foreign or interstate commerce, of tangible personal property from the place where it is located for delivery to a point in this state for the purpose of the transfer of title or possession, exchange, barter, lease, or rental, conditional or otherwise, in any manner or by any means whatsoever, of the property for a consideration; • a transfer for a consideration of the title or possession of tangible personal property, which has been produced, fabricated, or printed to the special order of the customer, or any publication; • the furnishing and distributing of electricity, natural gas, artificial gas, steam, refrigeration, and water; • the furnishing for consideration of intrastate, interstate and international telecommunications service sourced in this state in accordance with R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1(15) and R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1(16) and all ancillary services, any maintenance services of telecommunication equipment other than as provided for in R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-12(b)(ii). But, telecommunication service does not include service rendered using a prepaid telephone calling arrangement; • the furnishing of service for transmission of messages by telegraph, cable, or radio and the furnishing of community antenna television, subscription television, and cable television services; • the rental of living quarters in any hotel, rooming house, or tourist camp; and • the transfer for consideration of prepaid telephone calling arrangements and the recharge of prepaid telephone calling arrangements sourced to this state in accordance with R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1-11 and R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.1-15. “Prepaid telephone calling arrangement” means and includes prepaid calling service and prepaid wireless calling service. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7. In Rhode Island retail sales include any sale, lease or rentals of tangible personal property, prewritten computer software delivered electronically or by load and leave, or services as defined in R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7.3 for any purpose other than resale, sublease or subrent in the regular course of business. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-8. Additionally, effective July 1, 2018, retail sales include any sale, lease, or rentals of vendor-hosted prewritten computer software. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-8, as amended by 2018 R.I. H. 7200, effective July 1, 2018. BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Lease'

Defined In Rhode Island a lease or rental means any transfer of possession or control of tangible personal property for a fixed or indeterminate term for consideration. The terms lease or rental may also include future options to purchase or extend. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7.1. A lease or rental does not include any one of the following transactions:• a transfer of possession or control of property under a security agreement or deferred payment plan that requires the transfer of title upon completion of the required payments; • A transfer or possession or control of property under an agreement that requires the transfer of title upon completion of required payments and payment of an option price does not exceed the greater of one hundred dollars ($100) or 1 percent of the total required payments; • providing tangible personal property along with an operator for a fixed or indeterminate period of time (a condition of this exclusion is that the operator is necessary for the equipment to perform as designed); or • agreements covering motor vehicles and trailers where the amount of consideration may be increased or decreased by reference to the amount realized upon sale or disposition of the property as defined in I.R.C. 7701(h)(1). R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7.1(o)(i) to R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7.1(o)(iv). This definition of lease or rental used by the State of Rhode Island for sales and use tax purposes will be applied to determine if a transaction constitutes a lease or rental regardless of how such terms are defined under the Internal Revenue Code, the Uniform Commercial Code, or other provisions of federal, state, or local law. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-7.1(o)(v). BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Use' or 'Storage, Use, or Consumption'

Defined Rhode Island's definition of use includes the exercise of any right or power over tangible personal property incident to the ownership of that property, except that it does not include the sale of that property in the regular course of business. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-10. BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Tangible Personal Property'

Defined In Rhode Island, “tangible personal property” means personal property which may seen, weighed, measured, felt, or touched, or which is any other manner perceptible to the senses. “Tangible personal property” includes electricity, water, gas, steam, and prewritten computer software. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-16. BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.4.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Intangible Property'

Not Defined Rhode Island does not define intangible property. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-1, et seq. However, the state defines “tangible personal property” as property which may seen, weighed, measured, felt, or touched, or which is any other manner perceptible to the senses. Tangible personal property also includes electricity, water, gas, steam, and prewritten computer software. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-16. BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.5.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Retailer' or 'Vendor'

Defined Rhode Island defines a “retailer” as every person engaged in the business of making sales at retail, including prewritten computer software delivered electronically or by load and leave and including vendor-hosted prewritten computer software. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-15(a)(1), as amended by 2018 R.I. H. 7200, effective July 1, 2018. A “retailer”also includes every person engaged in the sales of specified taxable services and sales at auctions of tangible personal property owned by the person or others. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-15(a)(1). A “remote seller” is any seller, other than a marketplace facilitator or referrer, who does not have a physical presence in Rhode Island and makes retail sales to purchasers. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-15.2(a)(1), as amended by 2019 R.I. S. 251 effective March 29, 2019. A “marketplace seller” is a person, not a related party to a marketplace facilitator, who has an agreement with a marketplace facilitator to make retail sales of tangible personal property, prewritten computer software delivered electronically or by load and leave, vendor-hosted prewritten computer software, and/or taxable services through the its marketplace. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.2-2(6), as added by 2019 R.I. S. 251 effective March 29, 2019. A “referrer” means every person who:• contracts or otherwise agrees with a retailer to list or advertise for sale in the state goods or taxable services in any forum, including, but not limited to, a catalog or internet website; • receives a fee, commission, or other consideration from a retailer for the listing and/or advertisement; • transfers, via in-state software, internet link, or otherwise, an in-state customer to the retailer or the retailer's employee, affiliate, or website to complete a purchase; and • does not collect payments from the in-state customer for the transaction. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.2-2(9), as enacted by 2017 R.I. H. 5175, Substitute A as amended, Art. 8, §18, effective July 1, 2017. BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.6.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Purchaser' or 'Consumer'

Defined Rhode Island does not define the terms purchaser or consumer. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18-1, et seq. However, as of July 1, 2017, Rhode Island defines the term “in-state customer”to mean a person who purchases tangible personal property, prewritten software delivered electronically or by load and leave, vendor-hosted prewritten computer software, or taxable services for use, storage, or consumption in the state. R.I. Gen. Laws §44-18.2-2(2), as enacted by 2017 R.I. H. 5175, Substitute A as amended, Art. 8, §18, effective July 1, 2017, and amended by 2019 R.I. S. 251, effective March 29, 2019. BNA-SUTN RI 1.4.7.
