Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Short-term Motor Vehicle Rentals

Taxable Short-term motor vehicle leases of passenger vehicles for a period of 180 days or less are subject to state sales tax at a special rate of 23 cents for each two dollars of the cost of the vehicle rental and an additional one cent on each additional nine cents of the cost of the vehicle rental. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(c). For an exact table of rate calculations, see Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(c)(2). To qualify as a short-term motor vehicle lease, the vendor must not provide a driver as part of the rental, and the vehicle must not be used by the lessee to transport individuals or property for hire. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(c)(1)(i). Dump trucks, tow trucks, and various farm vehicle leases do not qualify as short term motor vehicle leases. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(c)(1)(ii). For motor vehicle rentals lasting more than 180 days and less than one year, a flat sales tax rate of 11.5 percent is applied to monthly rental or lease payments. Interview with Office of the Comptroller of Maryland, July 28, 2010; Maryland Sales and Use Tax Return, Form 202; Maryland Tax Tip No. 8. Peer-to-Peer Car Sharing Effective from July 1, 2018, to June 30, 2020, charges made in connection with a shared motor vehicle used for peer-to-peer car sharing that is made available on a peer-to-peer car sharing program are taxable. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(c-1), as amended by 2018 Md. S.B. 743, effective July 1, 2018; Maryland Sales and Use Tax Facts July 2018 - June 2019. “Peer-to-peer car sharing” means “the authorized use of a motor vehicle by an individual other than the vehicle's owner through a peer-to-peer sharing program.” These charges are taxable at a special rate of 8 percent. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(c-1), as amended by 2018 Md. S.B. 743, effective July 1, 2018. BNA-SUTN MD 29.3.7.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Parking and Valet

Nontaxable Parking and valet charges are not taxable. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-101(m); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.01(A). Maryland law only authorizes local jurisdictions to impose a parking tax in limited circumstances. For example, the Maryland Constitution specifically authorizes the city of Baltimore to impose taxes by ordinance, and Baltimore has chosen to impose a valet parking tax, which is collected by the operator of the valet service. Md. Const. art. XI-C, § 3;Baltimore, Md., Code art. 28 §22-5(a), as amended by Baltimore City Ordinance No. 13-98 (Feb. 11, 2013). BNA-SUTN MD 29.10.
