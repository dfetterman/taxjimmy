Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Refund Available Producers of biodiesel may apply for a refund of sales or use tax paid on purchases made. Iowa Code Ann. §423.4(9). To qualify for the refund, the person must be a manufacturer of biodiesel who has registered with the U.S. Department of Environmental Protection agency, and the biodiesel must be produced in Iowa for use in biodiesel blended fuel. Iowa Code Ann. §423.4(9)(a); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-12.18(1). The refund is calculated only on the first 25 million gallons of biodiesel produced at each facility during the calendar year for the years 2012 to 2017. Iowa Code Ann. §423.4(9)(c); Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-12.18(1). The calculation is based on the total number of gallons produced during each calendar quarter multiplied by the rates as follows:• 3 cents per gallon for 2012; • 2.5 cents for 2013;and • 2 cents for 2014 to 2017. Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-12.18(1). The subsection providing for the biodiesel refund, Iowa Code Ann. §423.4(9), is repealed on Jan. 1, 2025. Iowa Code Ann. §423.4(9)(e) BNA-SUTN IA 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Water and Sewer

Depends Water Beginning July 1, 2018, charges for the sale or furnishing of a water service by a water utility are exempt from sales tax. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(103)(a), as added by 2018 Iowa S.F. 512, effective July 1, 2018; Iowa Water Service Excise Tax. Instead, such charges are instead subject to the water service excise tax. Iowa Code Ann. §423G.3, as added by 2018 Iowa S.F. 512, effective July 1, 2018; Iowa Water Service Excise Tax. “Water service” is defined as the delivery of water by piped distribution system. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(103)(b)(1), as added by 2018 Iowa S.F. 512, effective July 1, 2018. “Water utility” refers to a public utility as defined in Iowa Code Ann. §476.1 that furnishes water by piped distribution system to the public for compensation. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(103)(b)(2), as added by 2018 Iowa S.F. 512, effective July 1, 2018. Water service providers that make taxable sales in addition to tax-exempt sales of piped water service will need to separately list the sales of piped water service on receipts and bills. For example, if an entity that provides both sewage and water services is currently collecting and remitting sales tax on sewer charges to nonresidential commercial customers, they will continue to collect this portion as sales tax plus any applicable local option sales tax, and will now also collect the water service excise tax on the water service sales only. Iowa Code Ann. §423G.3, as added by 2018 Iowa S.F. 512, effective July 1, 2018; Iowa Water Service Excise Tax. Water utilities that make separately stated sales of services or tangible personal property that are not integral to the sale of water service may be responsible for collecting sales tax on those sales. Iowa Admin. Code r. 701-97.4(4), as added by 41 Admin. Bull. issue 14 (Jan. 2, 2019), effective Feb. 6, 2019. Prior to July 1, 2018, charges for water service are taxable. Iowa Code Ann. §423.2(2). For more information on excise taxes imposed on utilities, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Iowa 11.1. Sewer Charges for sewage services for nonresidential commercial operations are subject to sales tax in Iowa. Charges for other types of sewer services are not enumerated as taxable services, and therefore not subject to sales tax. Iowa Code Ann. §423.2(6); Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(32)(c). BNA-SUTN IA 30.2.4.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Mining

Nontaxable Mining is not included among the enumerated taxable services subject to sales and use tax in Iowa. Iowa Code Ann. §423.2(6); Iowa Sales and Use Tax: Taxable Services (services subject to tax only when specified by Iowa law). Quarrying and mining businesses are considered manufacturers with respect to activities performed once raw materials are extracted, including rushing, washing, sizing, and blending of aggregate materials. Thus, quarrying and mining businesses may qualify for manufacturing machinery and equipment exemptions. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(47); Iowa Sales and Use Tax on Manufacturing and Processing. BNA-SUTN IA 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Exempt The sale of wind energy conversion property or hydroelectricity conversion property to be used as an electric power source is exempt from Iowa sales and use tax. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(54) The sale of the materials used to manufacture, install, or construct such conversion property also is exempt. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(54) For purposes of this exemption, “wind energy conversion property” means any devices, including but not limited to, wind chargers, windmills, wind turbines, towers and electrical equipment, pad mount transformers, power lines, and substations, that convert wind energy to a form of usable energy. “Hydroelectric conversion property” means any device, including but not limited to a generator, turbine, powerhouse, intake, coffer dam, walls, water conduit, tailrace, any other concrete components, electrical equipment substation, poles, wire transformers, breakers, and switches used to convert water, water power, or hydroelectricity to a form of usable energy. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(54); Iowa Code Ann. §423.6(6). The materials used in the repair and maintenance of wind energy conversion property are subject to tax. The exemption only applies to materials used to manufacture, install, or construct the property. Also, equipment used to transport the wind energy conversion property is subject to tax. Iowa Revenue Policy Letter No. 14300025 (July 3, 2014). Additionally, a person in possession of a wind energy production tax credit certificate may apply for a refund of sales or use tax paid on the applicant's purchases. Iowa Code Ann. §423.4(4); Iowa Tax Credits Users'Manual. A taxpayer who receives a credit for wind energy production pursuant to Iowa Code Ann. §476B.6 is not eligible for a renewable energy tax credit certificate under Iowa Code Ann. §476C.4. Iowa Declaratory Order Docket No. 2016-300-2-0311 (Feb. 1, 2017); see Iowa Tax Credits Users'Manual. For more information on tax credits for wind energy production in Iowa, see Credits and Incentives Navigator, at Iowa 5.3. Solar Energy Iowa allows a sales and use tax exemption for sales of solar energy equipment, that is, equipment primarily used to: a) collect and convert incident solar radiation into thermal, mechanical, or electrical energy; or b) transform such converted solar energy to a storage point or to a point of use. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(90). The exemption applies to separately purchased equipment used as part of the entire solar energy system, including equipment such as racks, mounts, and wiring. Iowa Tax Protest Letter No. 16300071 (Oct. 24, 2016). BNA-SUTN IA 30.5.

Depends Iowa exempts from sales and use tax electricity sold to water companies assessed for property tax pursuant to Iowa Code Ann. §§ 428.24, 428.26, and 428.28 used solely for the purpose of pumping water from a river or well. Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(53). Iowa Code Ann. §423.3(38). Additionally, the service of testing wastewater for contamination is exempt under Iowa's environmental testing exemption.
