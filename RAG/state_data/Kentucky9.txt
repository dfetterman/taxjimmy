State: Kentucky



Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Taxation of Property

Taxable Kentucky imposes sales and use tax on the sale of all tangible personal property and digital property unless specifically exempt. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.200. There is a general presumption that all tangible personal property and digital property sold by any person for delivery or access in Kentucky is subject to sales or use tax. The burden of proving the contrary is on the person making the sale unless an exemption certificate is furnished. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.260. More information on the taxation of property is available at 1300 T.M., Sales and Use Taxes: General Principles, and 1360 T.M., Sales and Use Taxes: Retail Sales Issues. BNA-SUTN KY 9.1.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Mixed and Bundled Transactions, Application of the True Object Test Mixed Transactions and the True Object Test

Taxable Kentucky defines the retail sale of two or more products, except real property and services to real property, when they are otherwise distinct and identifiable and are sold for one nonitemized price as bundled transactions. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(3)(a). As a result, what are traditionally distinguished as mixed transactions or bundled transactions are given the same treatment in Kentucky, and all transactions of that kind are classified as bundled transactions. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(2). In a transaction where the seller provides mail services to its customer whereby the seller prints, folds, inserts, and mails informational statements on behalf of the customer to individuals, the seller is providing services and not selling tangible personal property. Kentucky Board of Tax Appeals Order No. K-25212 (Feb. 8, 2017). For more information on Kentucky's taxation of bundled transactions see the Kentucky Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Kentucky 9.3.2. BNA-SUTN KY 9.3.1.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Mixed and Bundled Transactions, Application of the True Object Test Bundled Transactions

Taxable Kentucky defines the retail sale of two or more products, except real property and services to real property, when they are otherwise distinct and identifiable and are sold for one nonitemized price as bundled transactions. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(3)(a). As a result, what are traditionally distinguished as mixed transactions or bundled transactions are given the same treatment in Kentucky, and all transactions of that kind are classified as bundled transactions. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(2). Generally, the entire charge for transactions involving taxable and exempt personal property, or taxable products and exempt services is subject to tax. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(2). Bundled Telecommunications Services, Ancillary Services, Internet Access, Audio Programming, or Video Programming Taxable and Nontaxable Products: The portion of price attributable to the nontaxable product is subject to tax unless the provider can identify, by reasonable and verifiable standards, the portion of the products that are nontaxable from its books and regards that are kept in the regular course of business for other purposes, including nontax purposes. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(1)(a). Products Taxed at Different Rates: The total price is treated as attributable to the products subject to tax at the highest rate unless the provider can identify, by reasonable and verifiable standards, the portion of the price attributable to the products subject to tax at the lower rate from its books and records that are kept in the regular course of business for other purposes, including nontax purposes. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(1)(b). Exclusions The following transactions are not considered bundled transactions:• Retail sales of any products in which the sales price varies or is negotiable, based on the selection by the purchaser of the products included in the transaction. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(4)(a). • Retail sales of tangible personal property and a service where the tangible personal property is essential to the use of the service, and is provided exclusively in connection with the service, and the true object of the transaction is the service. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(4)(b). • Retail sales of digital property and a service where the digital property is essential to the use of the service, and is provided exclusively in connection with the service, and the true object of the transaction is the service. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(4)(c). • Retail sales of services where one service is provided that is essential to the use or receipt of a second service and the first service is provided exclusively in connection with the second service and the true object of the transaction is the second service. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(4)(d). • Transactions that include taxable products and nontaxable products, if the purchase price or sales price of the taxable products is 10 percent or less of the total purchase price or sales price of the bundled products. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(4)(e). • Retail sales of exempt tangible personal property and taxable tangible personal property if the transaction includes: food and food ingredients (defined in section 139.485);drugs (defined in section 139.472);durable medical equipment (defined in section 139.472); mobility enhancing equipment (defined in section 139.472); medical supplies; or over-the-counter drugs (defined in section 139.472). The seller's purchase price or sales price of the taxable tangible personal property also must be 50 percent or less of the total purchase price or sales price. Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. §139.215(4)(f). BNA-SUTN KY 9.3.2.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Local Jurisdictions, Taxability Distinctions

No Local Tax There are no local sales and use taxes in Kentucky. Kentucky Department of Revenue: Sales and Use Tax. Further Kentucky does not provide for any taxability distinctions for local jurisdictions in Kentucky. BNA-SUTN KY 9.4.
