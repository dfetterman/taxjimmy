State: Maryland



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Maryland does not impose sales and use tax on sales of food for off-premises consumption by a food vendor who operates a “substantial grocery or market business” at the same location where the food is sold. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(c)(1). A “substantial grocery or market business” is a business where at least 10 percent of all sales of food are sales of grocery or market food items. This does not include food normally consumed on the premises of a restaurant even though it is packaged to carry out. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(a)(6); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(A)(3). “Premises” is any building, grounds, parking lot, or other area that:• is owned or controlled by a food vendor; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(a)(5)(i). or • is made available primarily for the use of the patrons of one or more food vendors. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(a)(5)(ii). BNA-SUTN MD 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Ice

Depends Ice is generally not considered food for the purposes of Maryland's sales and use tax, and is therefore taxable. However, some sales of ice are exempt. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.29. When ice is sold as a component of a food, such as a beverage, the beverage will be taxed as it would be without the ice. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.29. If ice is used to pack an item for sale during shipment, and title to the ice passes to the purchaser, the ice may be purchased as a tax-exempt sale for resale. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.29. If the item for sale is transferred to the purchaser, but the ice is not, the ice is taxable when purchased by the packer. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.29. Other sales of ice, including sales of ice sculptures, are subject to tax. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.29. BNA-SUTN MD 11.1.3.12.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Eating Utensils and Disposable Items General Rule

Depends Sales of eating utensils and disposable items to restaurants and other food vendors are generally exempt as purchases for resale, provided the items are used as containers for the food or beverage sold and are not made available for discretionary use. Maryland Tax Tip 5. Disposable items such as paper plates, bowls, cups and lids used as containers for food or beverages sold to customers are items purchased for resale. This exclusion applies regardless of whether the food or beverage sold will be consumed on or off the premises. However, if these items are made available for discretionary use, likely meaning for the use of the general public instead of customers only, then they become taxable. Maryland Tax Tip 5. Additionally, disposable items that are not used as containers for food or beverages are taxable. Examples of such items include, but are not limited to, disposable napkins, straws, utensils, and stirrers. Maryland Tax Tip 5. BNA-SUTN MD 11.3.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Mobile Food Vendors

Depends Sales and use tax does not apply to a sale of food or any beverage in a vehicle that is being operated in Maryland while in the course of interstate commerce. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(e); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(6). However, sales and use will apply to a sale of food or any beverage in a vehicle not in the course of interstate commerce. Md. Code Ann. Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Restaurant Equipment

Depends Restaurant equipment is generally subject to sales and use tax. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(a)(1); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.32-2(B)(1)(a)(i); Maryland Tax Tip 9. However, equipment bought for at least $2,000 that is used to produce baked goods is exempt from sales and use tax. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.32-2(C)(5). BNA-SUTN MD 11.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Depends Maryland does not impose sales and use tax on snack food sold by a vending machine. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(2); see also Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1) (defining the term “snack food”). Snack foods are included in exempt food items when sold for off-premises consumption by a qualifying food vendor. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(a)(3)(ii); Maryland Tax Tip 5. Snack foods are items such as:• potato chips; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(i). • potato sticks; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(i). • corn chips; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(ii). • pretzels; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(iii). • cheese puffs; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(iv). • cheese curls; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(iv). • pork rinds; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(v). • extruded pretzels; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(vi). • extruded chips; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(vi). • popped popcorn; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(vii). • nuts and edible seeds; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(viii). and • snack mixtures that contain any one or more items listed above. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(g)(1)(ix). Nor does Maryland impose sales and use tax on the sale through a vending machine of milk, fresh fruit, fresh vegetables, or yogurt. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(h). Sales of other items through vending machines, such as candy and soft drinks, are taxable. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(a)(3)(iii)(3); Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(a)(3)(iii)(2). The tax base for vending machines is 94.5 percent of the gross receipts of sales from the vending machine. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(b). BNA-SUTN MD 11.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Exempt Maryland provides that sales and use tax does not apply to certain sales of food by various organizations. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(d); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(2)-(5); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(7),(8); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(11). Sales and use tax does not apply to any food sold under the following circumstances: Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(d); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(2)-(5); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(7),(8); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(11). • to patients in a hospital when the food charge is included in the regular room rate; • by a church or religious organization; • by a school other than an institution of postsecondary education, including sales at a school by a food concessionaire that is under contract with the school or with its designated contract agent, but not including sales at events that are not sponsored by the school or that are not educationally related; • to students at an institution of postsecondary education if the food charge is for a meal plan or is included in the regular charge for room and board; • by a nonprofit food vendor if there are no facilities for food consumption on the premises, unless the food is sold within an enclosure for which a charge is made for admission; • served by a bona fide nationally organized and recognized organization of veterans of the armed forces of the United States or auxiliary of the organization or one of its units, for consumption only on the premises, and for which the proceeds are used to support the organization; or • served by a volunteer fire company or department, or its auxiliary, or a volunteer ambulance company or rescue squad, or its auxiliary, or a volunteer ambulance company or rescue squad, or its auxiliary, for which the proceeds are used to support the company, department, squad, or auxiliary. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-206(d); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(2)-(5); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(7); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(8); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.05(B)(11). BNA-SUTN MD 11.10.
