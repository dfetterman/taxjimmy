State: Oklahoma



Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Tangible Personal Property

Destination Oklahoma's sourcing rules conform to the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(1). Retail sales, excluding leases and rentals, are sourced as follows. First, if a purchaser receives the property at a seller's business location, then the sale is sourced to this business location. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(1); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(1). Second, if a purchaser does not receive the property at a seller's business location, then the sale is sourced to the location where the purchaser receives the property, except that all sales by florists are sourced to the florist's business location. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(2); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(2). Third, if the location of a sale cannot be determined under the above rules, then the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address shown in the seller's business records. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(3); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(3). Fourth, if the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, then the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address obtained during the consummation of the sale, such as the address on the purchaser's payment instrument. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(4); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(4). Fifth, if the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, then the sale is sourced as follows:• If tangible personal property or other taxable property is sold, then the sale of such property is sourced to the location from which the property is shipped. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(5); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(5). • If a digital good or computer software delivered electronically is sold, then the sale is sourced to the location from which the digital good or computer software was first available for transmission by the seller, not including any location that merely provided the digital transfer of the product sold. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(5); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(5). Sourcing Transportation Equipment Oklahoma sources retail sales, leases, and rentals of transportation equipment as retail sales under the general sourcing rules. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(D). “Transportation equipment” means:• locomotives and railcars used to carry persons or property in interstate commerce; Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(D)(1). • trucks and truck tractors that have a gross vehicle weight rating of 10,001 pounds or greater, trailers, semi-trailers, and passenger buses, if such vehicles are registered under the International Registration Plan and operated under the authority of a carrier authorized by the federal government to carry persons or property in interstate commerce; Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(D)(2). • aircraft operated by air carriers authorized by the federal government or a foreign authority to carry persons or property in interstate or foreign commerce; Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(D)(2)(b); Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(D)(3). and • containers designed for use on any of the transportation equipment described above and component parts attached to such transportation equipment. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(D)(4). Sourcing of Sales Involving Direct Mail Oklahoma also has enacted provisions regarding the sourcing rules for sales involving direct mail. Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-6. BNA-SUTN OK 4.1.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Services

Destination Oklahoma has enacted general sourcing rules for services, as well as special sourcing rules for telecommunications services and ancillary services. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3; Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(B); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. General Sourcing Rules for Services Retail sales of services are sourced according to the following provisions. First, if a purchaser receives the services at a seller's business location, then the sale is sourced to this business location. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(1); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(1). Second, if a purchaser does not receive the services at a seller's business location, then the sale is sourced to the location where the purchaser receives the services. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(2); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(2). Third, if the location of a sale cannot be determined under the above rules, then the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address shown in the seller's business records. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(3); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(3). Fourth, if the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, then the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address obtained during the consummation of the sale, such as the address on the purchaser's payment instrument. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(4); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(4). Fifth, if the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, then the sale is sourced to the location from which the service was provided. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A)(5); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(5). Telecommunication Services and Ancillary Services Sales of telecommunications services are sourced according to the following rules:• Sales of telecommunications services on a call-by-call basis are generally sourced to the taxing jurisdiction where the call originates and terminates if the call originates and terminates in the same jurisdiction, or to the taxing jurisdiction where the service address is located and where the call either originates or terminates. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(B); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. • Sales of telecommunications services on a basis other than call-by-call are generally sourced to the customer's place of primary use. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(C); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. • Sales of mobile telecommunications service, other than sales of air-to-ground radiotelephone service or prepaid calling service, are sourced to the customer's place of primary use. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(1); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. • Sales of postpaid calling service are sourced to the location where the signal of the telecommunications service originates, as first identified by the seller's telecommunications system or, if the signal is not transmitted by the seller's telecommunications system, as identified by information that the seller received from the seller's service provider. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(2); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. • Sales of prepaid calling service or prepaid wireless calling service are sourced according to the location determined under the general sourcing rules, except that when the location of prepaid wireless calling service cannot be determined according to the sourcing rules, the sale of the prepaid wireless calling service is sourced either to the location, as determined by the seller, associated with the mobile telephone number for which the service is provided or to the location from which the service is provided. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(3); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5; Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-19-331. • Sales of private communication services are sourced according to the following rules: o Sales of private communication services for a separate charge related to a customer channel termination point are sourced to the location of the customer channel termination point. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(4)(a); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. o Sales of private communication services in which all customer channel termination points are located entirely in one taxing jurisdiction are sourced to the taxing jurisdiction in which the customer channel termination points are located. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(4)(b); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. o If the segments are charged separately, the sale of a private communication service that represents segments of a communications channel between two customer channel termination points located in different taxing jurisdictions is sourced 50% to each jurisdiction. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(4)(c); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. o If the segments are not charged separately, the sale of a private communication service for segments of a communication channel located in more than one taxing jurisdiction is sourced to each such jurisdiction by a percentage determined by dividing the number of customer channel termination points in the jurisdiction by the number of customer channel termination points in all jurisdictions where segments of the communications channel are located. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.30(D)(4)(d); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. • Sales of ancillary services are sourced to the customer's place of primary use. Okla. Admin. Code §710:65-18-5(e); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-5. BNA-SUTN OK 4.1.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Leases and Rentals

Destination The first payment on the lease or rental of tangible personal property is sourced to the location determined under the general sourcing rules, except for leases and rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, and aircraft, not constituting “transportation equipment.” Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(B). Subsequent periodic payments on the lease or rental, if any, are sourced to the property's primary location indicated by an address provided by the lessee available in records the lessor maintains in the ordinary course of business. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(B)(1). Leases and rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, and aircraft, not constituting “transportation equipment,” that require only one payment, are sourced to the location determined under the general sourcing rules. If the lease or rental requires multiple payments, then Oklahoma sources the lease to the motor vehicle's, trailer's, semitrailer's, or aircraft's primary location, as indicated by the property's address. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(C). Sourcing Leased or Rented Motor Vehicles Oklahoma sources leases or rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, or aircraft that do not qualify as transportation equipment as follows. First, for a lease or rental that requires recurring periodic payments, each periodic payment is sourced to the primary property location. The primary property location shall be the address for the property provided by the lessee that is available to the lessor from its records maintained in the ordinary course of business, when use of this address does not constitute bad faith. This location shall not be altered by intermittent use at different locations. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(C)(1). Second, for a lease or rental that does not require recurring periodic payments, the payment is sourced the same as a retail sale in accordance with the general sourcing rules above. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(C)(2). BNA-SUTN OK 4.1.3.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Local Sourcing

Destination Oklahoma uses destination sourcing at the state level. Presumably, the local sourcing rules are the same. See, e.g., Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1354.27(A); Okla. Admin. Code § 710:65-18-3(1). BNA-SUTN OK 4.1.4.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Credit for Tax Paid to Another Jurisdiction

Yes Oklahoma allows a reciprocal use tax credit up to the amount of any sales or use tax paid to another state. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1404(3); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Oklahoma (version 2018.0). If the amount of tax paid in another state is not greater than or equal to the amount of Oklahoma use tax imposed, the taxpayer must pay the difference between the Oklahoma use tax imposed and the amount paid in the other state. This credit is only available if the other state grants a similar credit for taxes paid in Oklahoma. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1404(3); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Oklahoma (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN OK 4.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property First Used Out-of-state

Yes Oklahoma's normal rules apply when tangible personal property is first used outside Oklahoma. See Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1402. BNA-SUTN OK 4.3.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Temporarily Stored In-state

No Oklahoma does not impose use tax when tangible personal property is intended solely for use in other states, but is temporarily retained in Oklahoma for fabrication, repair, testing, alteration, maintenance, or other service. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1402. BNA-SUTN OK 4.3.2.

==================================================

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Purchased for Use Out-of-state

No Oklahoma does not impose use tax when tangible personal property is intended solely for use in other states, but is stored in Oklahoma before shipment. Okla. Stat. Ann. tit. 68, §1402. BNA-SUTN OK 4.3.3.
