State: Pennsylvania



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Depends Food and beverages are exempt from Pennsylvania sales and use tax depending upon the type of food or beverage or upon the location where the food or beverage is sold. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29); 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(1). The sale at retail of grocery-type items by a food retailer is exempt from sales and use tax. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29); 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(3); Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision No. 1511137 (July 19, 2016) (purchase of pasta gift set (pasta, olive oil, and balsamic vinegar) and movie snack pack (licorice, Swedish Fish, popcorn, Twizzlers)are exempt from sales tax). However, Pennsylvania imposes sales and use tax on sales of food or beverages purchased from persons “engaged in the business of operating establishments from which ready-to-eat food and beverages are sold.” 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29)(iii)(B). BNA-SUTN PA 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Grocery Items

Depends Sales of grocery-type items by a food retailer are nontaxable. If an eating establishment does not qualify as a food retailer, it is required to collect tax on grocery-type items. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(3). To qualify as a food retailer, an eating establishment must maintain a separate department or identifiable location from which grocery-type sales are made and separate records for the sale of grocery-type items. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(3); Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision No. 1614006 (June 23, 2017) (eating establishment did not qualify as a food retailer because it did not keep separate records for sales of unprepared food). But see Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision Nos. 1704217, 1704218, 1704219, 1704220 (March 6, 2018) (tax assessment and penalties abated for delicatessen that sold both nontaxable grocery items and taxable food for immediate consumption). Grocery-type items includes baked goods such as pies, cakes, bread and similar items; produce such as fresh fruit and vegetables; canned goods; meat and cheese by the pound; milk in pint, quart, 1/2 gallon or gallon quantities;prepackaged ice cream products such as ice cream by the pint, quart, 1/2 gallon or gallon quantities; ice cream cakes and pies; frozen foods; unprepared fish and seafood; bottled honey; bottled syrup;jam; jelly; artificial sweeteners; and similar items. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(a); see Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision Nos. 1716753, 17168770 (April 16, 2018). A food retailer is a business, or an identifiable location within a business, which is engaged in the sale of grocery-type items for other than immediate consumption. The following are examples of food retailers: bakeries, pastry shops, doughnut shops, delicatessens, grocery stores, supermarkets, farm markets, convenience stores, and similar businesses. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(a). Fruit Baskets Fruit baskets and other gift consisting of food are exempt if the taxpayer can show that the item consists entirely of nontaxable grocery items. Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision No. 1612975 (June 23, 2017). BNA-SUTN PA 11.1.3.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Non-Alcoholic Beverages

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Tea

Depends Bagged tea, loose tea, and unsweetened bottled tea will generally qualify as a grocery-type item exempt from tax. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29); 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(3). Hot drinks such as tea are taxable regardless of where they are sold. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29); 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(3). However, the taxability of iced tea will depend on whether the retailer is considered an eating establishment. Pennsylvania defines “eating establishment” as a business which advertises or holds itself out to the public as being engaged in the sale of prepared or ready-to-eat food or beverages, to customers for their immediate consumption on or off the premises. This holds true regardless of whether the location is mobile or permanent, and does not depend on the provision of seating. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(1). Pennsylvania also provides the following list of examples of eating establishments: restaurants, cafes, lunch counters, private and social clubs, taverns, dining cars, hotels, night clubs, fast food operations, honor boxes, pizzerias, fairs, carnivals, lunch carts, ice cream stands, snack bars, lunch trucks, cafeterias, employee cafeterias, theaters, stadiums, arenas, amusement parks, juice stands, carry out shops, coffee shops, popcorn stands, automats, vending machines and similar establishments. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(a). Under the statutory and regulatory language, iced tea prepared and sold at a grocery or convenience store (but not through a vending machine), will not be subject to tax. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29); 61 Pa. Code §60.7(b)(3). BNA-SUTN PA 11.1.3.14.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Restaurant' or 'Eating Establishment'

Defined An “eating establishment” is a business, or an identifiable location within a business, which advertises or holds itself out to the public as being engaged in the sale of prepared or ready-to-eat food or beverages, to customers for their immediate consumption on or off the premises. An eating establishment may be mobile or immobile and may or may not provide seating accommodations for its customers. 61 Pa. Code §60.7(a). Stat. §7204(29)(iii)(B). A bakery, a pastry shop, a donut shop, a delicatessen, grocery store, supermarket, farmer's market, a convenience store or a vending machine shall not be considered an establishment from which food or beverages ready to eat are sold except for the sale of meals, sandwiches, food from salad bars, hand-dipped or hand-served iced based products including ice cream and yogurt, hot soup, hot pizza and other hot food items, brewed coffee and hot beverages. 72 Pa. See, e.g., Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision Nos. 1613012, 1626457 (June 30, 2017) (dough tray washing system located in a pizza restaurant is subject to tax as a post-production activity); Pennsylvania Board of Finance and Revenue Decision No. 1523772 (March 6, 2017) (percentage of equipment-use related to maintaining minimum temperatures for raw material storage subject to tax as a pre-production activity). BNA-SUTN PA 11.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Depends The sale of food or beverages from a vending machine may be taxable or exempt depending upon the type of food or beverage or upon the basis of the location from which the food or beverage is sold. Since a vending machine does not constitute an eating establishment, only the sale of selected food and beverage items is taxable. 61 Pa. Code §31.28(d). Accessory Items Sales and use tax is imposed on “accessory items,” items purchased for a vending machine customer's enhanced convenience that are not ultimately resold. 72 Pa. Stat. §7202(b). Accessory items include napkins, disposable utensils, and straws. Thus, vending machine operators providing accessory items are responsible for paying use tax on the items provided. 61 Pa. Code §31.28(g)(1); Covenco Inc. v. Pennsylvania, 579 A.2d 434 (Pa. Commw. Ct. 1990). BNA-SUTN PA 11.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Depends Sales of food and beverages at or by a school or church, in the ordinary course of the activities of such school or church, are exempt from sales and use tax. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(29)(iii)(B). Sales by Youth Centers Beginning Jan. 1, 2020, youth centers do not have to charge sales tax on food and beverages sold to raise funds. Prior to Jan. 1, 2020, such sales are taxable. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(49), as amended by 2019 Penn. H.B. 262, effective Jan. 1, 2020; Pennsylvania State Tax Summary: July 2019. A “youth center” means “a fixed location used exclusively for programs for individuals who are 19 years of age or younger that are conducted primarily by volunteers, designed to advance recreational, civic, or moral objections, and conducted by an organization exempt from tax under I.R.C. §501(c)(3) that has obtained an exemption number from the Pennsylvania Department of Revenue. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(49), as amended by 2019 Penn. H.B. 262, effective Jan. 1, 2020; Pennsylvania State Tax Summary: July 2019. Sales by Sports-Affiliated Nonprofit Associations Food and beverages sold or used by nonprofit associations that support sports programs are exempt from sales and use tax. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(49). This exemption is generally limited to associations supporting programs organized for recreational purposes primarily for participants who are 18 years of age or younger or whose 19th birthday occurs during the year of participation or the competitive season, whichever is longer. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(49)(iv). However, this age limitation does not apply with respect to programs operated for physically handicapped persons or persons with mental retardation. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(49)(iv). Sales by a Volunteer Firemen's Organization Beginning Jan. 1, 2020, volunteer firemen's organizations do not have to charge sales tax on food and beverages sold to raise funds. Prior to Jan. 1, 2020, such sales are taxable. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(71), as amended by 2019 Penn. H.B. 262, effective Jan. 1, 2020; Pennsylvania State Tax Summary: July 2019. Food and Beverages Provided by Airlines Food and nonalcoholic beverages provided by airlines to passengers are exempt from sales and use tax. 72 Pa. Stat. §7204(61). BNA-SUTN PA 11.10.
