State: Mississippi



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Taxable Mississippi imposes sales tax on the retail sales of food and food ingredients. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17. As tangible personal property, food and food ingredients are subject to Mississippi's sales and use tax. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17. The gross proceeds of sales by restaurants, cafes, cafeterias, snack bars, drive-ins, beer parlors, lounges, night clubs, concessions, caterers or other vendors of beverages and food products are taxable at 7 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.101. However, the value of meals prepared by a restaurant and donated to an I.R.C. §501(c)(3) charitable organization that regularly provides food to the needy and the indigent is not included in the restaurants' taxable gross proceeds of sales. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.205. Additionally, sales of food made through vending machines serviced by full line vendors as well as food sold with food stamps or food instruments issued by the Mississippi Band of Choctaw Indians under the Women, Infants, and Children Program are exempt from Mississippi's sales tax. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-111(m); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-111(o); Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-111(v). BNA-SUTN MS 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Carbonated Beverages

Taxable Mississippi imposes tax on the retail sales of carbonated beverages. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17. However, bottlers, wholesalers, or distributors that withdraw soft drinks from inventory for sale through full service vending machines must remit 8 percent wholesale tax on total value when placed into the vending machine for sale. Mississippi defines “soft drinks”as “all beverages which are closed or sealed in glass, metal or any other type of container, such as Coca Cola, Sprite, Dr. Pepper, 7-Up, R C Cola, ginger ale, Canada Dry, Shasta, soda water, carbonated water, Capri Sun, Gator Aid, Hi C, Hawaiian Punch, limeade, lemonade, orangeade, artificial fruit juice, tea, chocolate drink and similar items.” Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.01.100. For information on excise taxes on carbonated beverages in Mississippi, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Mississippi 14.1. BNA-SUTN MS 11.1.3.6.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

Taxable Mississippi imposes tax on the sale of tangible personal property including prepared food. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.101. The gross proceeds of sales by restaurants, cafes, cafeterias, snack bars, drive-ins, beer parlors, lounges, night clubs, concessions, caterers or other vendors of beverages and food products are taxable at 7 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.101. Prepared Food In General Mississippi imposes sales and use tax on the sale of food, meals, or beverages by restaurants. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.101; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.501. Restaurants Restaurants are any places where prepared food and beverages are sold for consumption, whether or not the food and beverage is consumed on the premises. Restaurants do not include any school, hospital, nursing home, or any restaurant-like facility operated by or in connection with a school, hospital, medical clinic, or nursing home providing food for students, patients, visitors, or their families. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.503. School Meals Sales of food to schools or students as regular student meals are exempt. Similarly, sales of food served at banquets and luncheons for student groups are exempt from tax if the exempt entity pays the total cost of the meal provided. However, sales of food by or through a school cafeteria and dining hall to non-students, such as faculty members, employees, visitors, and the public are subject to tax. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.207. Food Ingredients Becoming a Part of the Completed Meal Purchases of merchandise that becomes a part of products resold are exempt from tax. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.302. Food Service Companies Food service companies that operate restaurants or cafeterias must include in the tax base underwriting, management, or other fees paid by the client. A client who pays for or discounts their employees' meals and for whom the service company serves as an agent for food service operations may obtain a business license for the food service operation and pay tax on the greater of the meal charges to employees or the cost to the employer of all purchases utilized in the food service operation, with employer costs for food preparation or food service management excluded from the sales. The tax applies regardless of who the food service operator is. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.206. Sales by Food Producers Mississippi exempts from sales and use tax sales by producers of livestock, poultry, fish, or other farm, grove, or garden products if the product sold is in its original state. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-103(b). BNA-SUTN MS 11.2.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined Mississippi's definition of “prepared food” includes several categories. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02. More specifically, this term includes:• food made to order upon the customer's request; • food sold in a heated state or heated by the seller; • two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by the seller for sale as a single item, but not including food that is only, cut, repackaged, or pasteurized by the seller, and eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these raw animal foods requiring cooking by the consumer as recommended by the Food and Drug Administration in Chapter 3, part 401.11 of its Food Code so as to prevent food borne illnesses; and • food sold with eating utensils “provided by the seller,” including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws—except food items that remain in their original container packaged by the manufacturer. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.504. BNA-SUTN MS 11.2.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Taxable Sales of tangible personal property made through coin-operated vending machines, other than full service machines, are subject to Mississippi's sales tax. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-111(m); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.04.03.100 However, Mississippi exempts from tax the gross proceeds of retail sales of food and drink for human consumption made through vending machines serviced by full line vendors from and not connected with other taxable businesses. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-111(m). The wholesale sales of food and drink to full service operators to be resold through vending machines located apart from and not connected with other taxable businesses shall be taxed at the rate of 8 percent. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-17; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.09.02.101. “Full service vendors” are persons in the business of making sales through vending machines by placing merchandise in the machine, taking the money, and paying the location owner a space rental fee. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.04.03.101. BNA-SUTN MS 11.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Taxable Where customers use an application on their phone or an internet website to order food delivered from a particular restaurant of the customer's choosing, the food delivery service must collect sales and use taxes on the entire charge for the food and delivery. Mississippi Sales and Use Fact Sheet -- Food Delivery Services. Charges for tips added to the bill by the delivery service are subject to sales and local taxes. These tips added by the delivery service are considered part of the taxable gross proceeds of the sale. Mississippi Sales and Use Fact Sheet -- Food Delivery Services. Tips made at the discretion of the customer where the customer chooses whether to pay a tip and how much to tip are not subject to sales and or local taxes. These tips are considered a gratuity that is not part of the taxable gross proceeds of the sale. Mississippi Sales and Use Fact Sheet -- Food Delivery Services. BNA-SUTN MS 11.10.
