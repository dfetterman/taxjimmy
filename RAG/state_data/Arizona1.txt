State: Arizona



Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Tangible Personal Property

Depends Retail sales of tangible personal property are sourced to the seller's business location if the seller receives the order at a business location in Arizona, and, except as provided in Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5008.01, to the purchaser's location in Arizona if the seller receives the order at a business location outside the state. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(A). If there is no delivery address, the sale is sourced to the purchaser's billing address. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(A), as amended by 2019 Ariz. S.B. 1019, effective Aug. 27, 2019. An order is received when all of the information necessary to accept the order has been received by or on behalf of the seller, regardless of where the order is accepted or approved. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(B), as added by 2013 Ariz. H.B. 2111, effective Jan. 1, 2015. Use Tax For purposes of the use tax, the transaction is sourced to the location where the tangible personal property is used or consumed. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5034(B). BNA-SUTN AZ 4.1.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Services

Depends Services subject to transaction privilege tax are sourced according to business classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5034(A). The services are sourced as follows: • transportation services are sourced to the location where the transportation begins; • mining services are sourced to the location where the product is extracted, mined, quarried, or pumped; • utility services are sourced to the location where the electricity, gas, or water is delivered to the consumer; • telecommunications services (other than mobile telecommunications) are sourced to the location from which the messages or conversations by telephone or messages by telegraph are transmitted; • pipeline services are sourced to the location where the oil or artificial or natural gas is delivered to the consumer; • private car line services are sourced to the location in which the cars are operated, by proportion; • job printing services are sourced to the location where the job printing, engraving, embossing, or copying is done; • restaurant services are sourced to the location where the restaurant or other establishment at which food or drink is sold for consumption is located, except in the case of dining cars, which are sourced the same as the private car line classification; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5034(A). and • mobile telecommunications services are sourced to the customer's residential or primary business street address where the customer's use of mobile telecommunications service primarily occurs. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5034.01(E)(4); Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax Ruling 04-1 (July 7, 2004). BNA-SUTN AZ 4.1.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Leases and Rentals

Depends The gross receipts from leasing or renting tangible personal property in Arizona are sourced to the lessor's business location that appears on the lessor's privilege license if the lessor has a business location in the state. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(C); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1503(A)(1); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1503(C). If the lessor does not have a business location within Arizona, the leasing or renting of tangible personal property is sourced to the lessee's residential or primary business street address. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(C); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1503(A)(1); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1503(C). If there is no lessee's address, the lease or rental is sourced to the lessee's billing address. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(C), as amended by 2019 Ariz. S.B. 1019, effective Aug. 27, 2019. Gross receipts from leasing or renting tangible personal property are not taxable if the property is shipped or delivered outside of Arizona and intended, at the inception of the lease, for use exclusively outside of Arizona. Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1503(D). BNA-SUTN AZ 4.1.3.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Local Sourcing

Depends Retail sales of tangible personal property are sourced to the seller's business location if the seller receives the order at a business location in Arizona, and, except as provided in Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5008.01, to the purchaser's location in Arizona if the seller receives the order at a business location outside the state. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5040(A). Municipal transaction privilege and use taxes are sourced to the Arizona municipality:• in which the business sales office which generated the taxable transaction is located; • in which the purchaser resides, is located or is situated at the time of the transaction;or • which imposes or claims the right to impose a transaction privilege tax on the transaction in question under its ordinance. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-6003(I)(1). For businesses subject to the transaction privilege tax under the pipeline classification, the county tax is sourced to the location where the oil and gas is delivered to the customer. For businesses in the private car classification, the county tax applies to the location where the cars are operated in the same proportion as the mileage of railroads in each county to the total mileage of railroads in the state over which the cars are operated. Arizona Publication No. 617. BNA-SUTN AZ 4.1.4.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Credit for Tax Paid to Another Jurisdiction

Yes Arizona provides an exemption from use tax for tangible personal property, the sale or use of which has already been subject to an excise tax at a rate equal to or exceeding Arizona's use tax, under the laws of another state of the United States. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(A)(2); Ariz. Admin. Code R15-5-2305; Arizona Publication No. 623. If the excise tax imposed by the other state is at a rate that is less than Arizona's tax, then the amount of Arizona tax is reduced by the amount of tax already imposed by the other state. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(A)(2); Ariz. Admin. Code R15-5-2305; Arizona Dept. of Rev. v. Ariz. Pub. Serv. Co., 934 P.2d 796 (Ariz. Ct. App. 1997); Arizona Publication No. 623. For example, if the amount of sales tax paid in another state is 3 percent of the purchase price and the Arizona use tax rate is 4 percent, the purchaser is required to pay to Arizona an additional 1 percent of the purchase price. Ariz. Admin. Code R15-5-2305(B). Arizona does not allow rental businesses to claim a credit for taxes paid in another state on the lease or rental of tangible personal property. Arizona Publication No. 606. BNA-SUTN AZ 4.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property First Used Out-of-state

Depends Tangible personal property brought into Arizona by an individual who was a nonresident at the time the property was purchased for storage, use or consumption by the individual is exempt from use tax if the first actual use or consumption of the property was out-of-state, unless the property is used in conducting a business in Arizona. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5159(A)(6); Arizona Publication No. 610. Also, tangible personal property not exceeding $200 in any one month purchased by an individual at retail outside the continental limits of the United States for personal use and enjoyment is exempt from tax. Arizona Publication No. 610. BNA-SUTN AZ 4.3.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Temporarily Stored In-state

Taxable Arizona does not exempt from use tax tangible personal property temporarily stored in the state. See Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5001, et seq. BNA-SUTN AZ 4.3.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Purchased for Use Out-of-state

Depends Beginning Jan. 1, 2015, sales to a nonresident of tangible personal property, other than a motor vehicle, shipped or delivered to a location outside Arizona for use outside the state, are subject to tax at the seller's business location. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(14); Ariz. Admin. Code R15-5-170(B). Sales of Motor Vehicles to Nonresidents Sales tax does not apply to the gross proceeds of sales or gross income from a nonresident of Arizona if the purchaser's state of residence does not allow a corresponding use tax exemption and if the nonresident has secured a special ninety day nonresident registration permit for the vehicle as prescribed by sections 28-2154 and 28-2154.01 Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(28)(a) Sales of motor vehicles to nonresidents from reciprocal states including California, Connecticut, Florida, Idaho and Illinois, that have higher tax rates than Arizona's 5.6 percent transaction privilege tax rate, are subject to Arizona taxes if the nonresident purchaser takes possession of the vehicle in Arizona. Residents of such states will be allowed a credit against their state taxes for the state transaction privilege tax paid in Arizona, when the nonresident registers the vehicle in his or her home state. For a information on states exercising reciprocity with Arizona for taxes paid on such purchases see Nonresident State Tax Rate Schedules and Arizona County Tax Rate Schedules, effective Aug. 1, 2016; Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax Changes & News January 2015. Nonresidents from Arkansas, Delaware, Washington D.C., Georgia, Maryland, Mississippi, Oklahoma, Virginia and West Virginia are not entitled to a credit for taxes paid on such purchases in Arizona. Thus, Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(28)(a) provides an exemption from Arizona transaction privilege tax and county excise tax for motor vehicle sales to nonresidents who are from these states Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5061(A)(28)(a); Nonresident State Tax Rate Schedules and Arizona County Tax Rate Schedules, effective Aug. 1, 2016; Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax Changes & News January 2015. Otherwise, sales to nonresidents are subject to tax. Ariz. Admin. Code R15-5-173. BNA-SUTN AZ 4.3.3.
