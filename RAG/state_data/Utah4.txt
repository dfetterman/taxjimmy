Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Taxable Sales of motor vehicles are subject to sales and use tax in Utah. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-103(1)(a). Sales tax due on vehicle sales by Utah vehicle dealers must be paid with a dealer's sales and use tax return, on or before the established due date. All sales tax must be collected by the dealer and paid to the tax commission with the dealer's sales and use tax returns. Purchasers of vehicles from other than licensed vehicle dealers must pay applicable sales tax to the division of motor vehicles. Utah Publication No. 5. Sales for Use Out-of-state Sales of motor vehicles that are the type required to be registered in Utah are exempt from sales or use tax if the vehicle is not registered in Utah and is not used in Utah, or if it is used in Utah, so long as the vehicle is not used to conduct business for the longer of 30 days in a calendar year or the time period required to transport the vehicle to Utah. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-104(9); Utah Publication No. 5. Tax Base The following motor vehicle dealer related transactions are taxable:• charges for dealer preparation, commissions and similar fees; • charges to customers for waste disposal, hazardous material handling or disposal, etc.; • charges for shop supplies and purchases of items used or consumed by the repair shop even though the consumer is charged separately; • charges for vehicle theft protection packages if they include window etchings, glass engravings, security devices, or other modifications or additions to the vehicle; • sales of extended warranties or service plans even though the sale of the vehicle may have qualified for exemption under the authorized carrier exemption;and • document service fees and amounts collected as deductibles at time of service. Utah Publication No. 5. The following motor vehicle dealer related items are exempt from sales tax:• amounts of manufacturer's rebates, whether paid to the purchaser or retained by the dealer as a down payment; • costs of labor and parts to honor claims against in-house warranties and service plans, or charges to third-party service companies for nationally honored plans; • fees to transfer a warranty to a new owner; • separate charges for interest or insurance when a vehicle is sold on credit; • Utah safety inspection fees, county emission testing fees and separate charges for diagnostic testing services, if no repairs are made as a result of the test; • parking or storage fees; • sales of extended warranties if the sale of the vehicle (including watercraft and off-highway vehicles) qualifies the exclusive use outside of Utah exemption or out-of-Utah delivery exemption; and • specific charges for amounts to be paid to the Utah State Tax Commission for titles, registrations, and license plates on behalf of customers, including the fee for a temporary permit. Utah Publication No. 5. Demonstrator Vehicles Owners and licensed sales staff may be assigned a demonstrator vehicle without being assessed sales or use tax. Rental charges for demonstrator vehicles are subject to tax. Rental charges include charge-backs, whether or not formally designated as a rental. Utah Publication No. 5. Motor Vehicle Leases Sales and use tax is collected and remitted as the payments related to a motor vehicle lease are received. The gross amounts of all payments under a lease agreement are subject to tax with except insurance and fees-in-lieu of property taxes. Trade-in credits are allowed to reduce the tax base of a lease transaction, so long as the lessee owns the trade-in vehicle and the trade-in value reduces the periodic lease payments. A customer who trades-in a leased vehicle must first purchase the vehicle and pay all applicable sales and use taxes before a trade-in credit will be allowed for the leased vehicle. A lease that includes a payoff of a previously existing obligation or refinancing by a lease in which title is vested in the lessor is a taxable lease, unless the transaction is considered a sale-leaseback. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102(60)(a), as renumbered by 2019 Utah S.B. 168, effective Oct. 1, 2019; Utah Admin. Code §R865-19S-32(1); Utah Admin. Code §R865-19S-32(2); Utah Publication No. 5. Leases with Terminal Rental Adjustment Clauses as defined in I.R.C. §7701(h)(1) are taxed the same as other leases or rentals. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102(60)(b), as renumbered by 2019 Utah S.B. 168, effective Oct. 1, 2019. The exercise of a purchase price option is taxable in the same manner as other lease payments. Utah Admin. Code §§ R865-19S-32(1) and (2); Utah Publication No. 5. In order to be considered a lease or rental, the purchase option price must exceed the greater of $100 or 1 percent of the total required payments. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102(60)(c)(ii), as renumbered by 2019 Utah S.B. 168, effective Oct. 1, 2019. If the option price does not exceed the greater of $100 or one percent of the total required payments, then the transaction will be treated as a financing lease and tax will be due upfront on the total amount required to be paid under the nominal lease agreement, not on lease payments as they are made. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-102(60(c)(ii), as renumbered by 2019 Utah S.B. 168, effective Oct. 1, 2019; Utah Private Letter Ruling No. 96-146 (Oct. 10, 1996). Vehicles Leased for Sport Events The lease or use of a vehicle issued a temporary sports event registration certificate under Utah Code Ann. §41-3-306 is exempt from tax. Utah Code Ann. §59-12-104(46). “Motor Vehicle” Defined A motor vehicle is a self-propelled vehicle intended primarily for use and operation on the highways. Utah Code Ann. §41-1a-102(38)(a). Motor vehicle does not include an off-highway vehicle. Utah Code Ann. §41-1a-102(38)(b). For more information on any applicable excise taxes, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at Utah 9. BNA-SUTN UT 29.3.1.
