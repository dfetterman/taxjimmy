Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Taxable In Washington, biodiesel is subject to sales and use tax as tangible personal property, unless it is used to propel a motor vehicle. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.020; Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.865(1). If biodiesel is sold to propel a motor vehicle, it would be considered special fuel. Wash. Rev. Code §82.38.020. Special fuels are generally subject to a retailer or distributor paid fuels tax, thus consumers are generally exempted from sales and use tax. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-126. Washington defines biodiesel as the monoalkyl esters of long chain fatty acids derived from plant or animal matter that meet the registration requirements for fuels and fuel additives established by the federal environmental protection agency and standards established by the American society of testing and materials. Wash. Rev. Code §19.112.010. Washington does not apply sales and use tax to the sale of vegetable oil that is used for the production of biodiesel for personal use. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.0205(1). BNA-SUTN WA 30.1.1.

Rev. Code §82.04.110(1); Wash. Rev. Code §82.04.120; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-135; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13601; Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 13-0317 (Oct. 24, 2013). BNA-SUTN WA 30.3.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Timber

Taxable Most equipment and services used in timber exaction are subject to sales and use tax, with a few exceptions. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501. Retail sales tax must be collected and remitted on all sales of logs to consumers, unless exempt by law. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(2)(d). Income from the services of persons performing extracting activities for timber harvesters is not subject to the retail sales tax. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(3). Extracting activities commonly performed by extractors for hire include, but are not limited to: Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(3). • cutting trees; • felling trees; • logging road construction or maintenance; and • activities related to and performed on timber-producing property that are necessary and incidental to timber operations, such as slash cleanup, scarification, stream and pond cleaning or rebuilding, restoration of logging roadways to a natural state, restoration of wildlife habitat, and fire trail work. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(3)(c). Persons performing timber processing for hire activities for consumers must collect retail sales tax on those services unless otherwise exempt. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(4). Equipment and Supplies Used in Timber Harvest Operations The retail sales tax generally applies to all purchases of equipment, component parts, and supplies by persons engaging in timber operations. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(7). Purchases of fertilizer and spray materials for use in cultivating timber are subject to the retail sales tax. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(7). Equipment used to extract timber is also subject to tax. Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 12-0277 (holding that Washington's exemption for manufacturing machinery and equipment does not apply to an excavator used to extract timber). The purchase of seeds or seedlings by a person cultivating timber is subject to the retail sales tax. The use of seed collected by a person cultivating timber is subject to the use tax. If seeds are provided to third-party growers in Washington, the seed owner, and not the third-party grower, incurs the use tax liability on the value of the seed. Similarly, the value of seedlings brought into and used in Washington is subject to the use tax, unless retail sales or use tax was previously paid on the seedlings or the seed from which the seedlings were grown. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(8). Conifer Seeds The retail sales tax does not apply to the sale of conifer seeds that are immediately placed into freezer storage operated by the seller if the seeds are to be used for growing timber outside Washington. This exemption also applies to the sale of conifer seeds to an Indian tribe or member for growing timber in Indian country, but only if the seed is immediately placed into freezer storage operated by the seller. The buyer must provide the seller with an exemption certificate. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(8)(b)(i). Tax Deferral If a buyer of conifer seeds is normally engaged in growing timber both within, and outside, the state and is unable to determine at the time of purchase whether the seed acquired, or the seedlings germinated from the seed acquired, will be used for growing timber within or outside Washington, the buyer may defer payment of the sales tax until the seed or seedling is planted in Washington. A buyer that does not pay sales tax on the purchase of conifer seeds and subsequently determines that the sale did not qualify for the tax exemption must remit the amount of sales tax that would have been paid at the time of purchase. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(8)(b)(ii). A buyer who pays retail sales tax on the purchase of conifer seed and subsequently determines that the sale qualifies for this tax exemption may claim a tax paid at source deduction on the buyer's tax return. The deduction is allowed only if the buyer keeps and preserves records that show from whom the seed was purchased, the date of the purchase, the amount of the purchase, and the tax that was paid. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(8)(b)(iii); Wash. Rev. Code §82.04.050(9). The use tax does not apply to conifer seeds used to grow seedlings if the seedlings are grown by a person other than the owner of the seeds. To qualify for the exemption the seedling may only be used for growing timber outside Washington or in Indian country. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(8)(b)(iii). Unloading Logs from Logging Trucks The unloading of logs from logging trucks onto rail cars at transfer points is subject to retail sale taxes when the activity involves providing tangible personal property along with an operator. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(9). Retail Sale of Christmas Trees Retail sales of plantation Christmas trees by farmers are subject to retail sales tax. Items purchased for the production of plantation Christmas trees are exempt from retail sales and use tax. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-13501(10)(a);Washington Dept. of Rev., 2016 Tax Exemption Study. BNA-SUTN WA 30.3.3.

As part of the application for a remittance, the purchaser must provide the following documentation:• a copy of the contractor's certificate of registration; • the contractor's current state unified business identifier number; • a copy of the contractor's proof of industrial insurance coverage for the contractor's employees working in Washington; • the contractor's employment security department number; • the contractor's state excise tax registration number; and • documentation of the contractor's history of compliance with federal and state wage and hour laws and regulations. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.962(1)(c)(i), as amended by 2019 Wash. S.B. 5116, effective May 7, 2019; Wash. Rev. Code §82.12.962(1)(c)(i), as amended by 2019 Wash. S.B. 5116, effective May 7, 2019. Repair parts required to restore power generating equipment to its normal working condition, which do not increase productivity beyond what would normally be produced by the equipment, are not eligible for the exemption. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.962(3)(c)(ii)(D), as amended by 2019 Wash. S.B. 5116, effective May 7, 2019. Expired Exemptions Prior to Oct. 1, 2017, a full solar energy exemption was available for solar energy systems that produced 10 kilowatts or less of electricity. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.963, as amended by 2017 Wash. S.B. 5939, effective, July 7, 2017; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-263; Washington Special Notice Sept. 24, 2013; Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 16-0091 (Mar. 8, 2016). Additionally, through June 30, 2018, a full exemption was available for machinery and equipment used directly in producing thermal heat using solar energy, if the purchaser developed with the machinery and equipment a facility capable of producing not more than three million British thermal units per day. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.963; Washington Special Notice June 26, 2018. “Machinery and equipment” is defined in Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.963 to include “industrial fixtures, devices, and support facilities that are integral and necessary to the generation of electricity or production and use of thermal heat using solar energy.” Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.963; Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 16-0091 (Mar. 8, 2016). A “support facility” is a part of a building, structure, or improvement used to contain or steady a fixture or device. A support facility must be specially designed and necessary for the proper functioning of the fixture or device and must perform a function beyond being a building, structure, or improvement. Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 16-0091 (Mar. 8, 2016). Charges for electrical and structural engineering, electrical and building permits, construction bond, plan review, project management and coordination, equipment rental, and weather station with solar radiation monitoring, do not qualify for the renewable energy machinery and equipment (M&E) exemption from retail sales tax. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.963; Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 16-0277 (Aug. 30, 2016). Biomass and Other Fuel Used by its Extractor or Manufacturer Effective July 1, 2018, Washington reinstates and expands sales and use tax exemptions for certain anaerobic digesters and landfills. 2018 Wash. H.B. 2580, effective July 1, 2018. Effective July 1, 2018, an eligible person is exempt from sales and use tax on retail sales of:• tangible personal property that becomes a component of the anaerobic digester;and • services to install, construct, repair, clean, alter, or improve the anaerobic digester. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.900, as amended by 2018 Wash. H.B. 2580, §4, effective July 1, 2018; Washington Special Notice June 8, 2018. Effective July 1, 2018, “anaerobic digester”means a facility that processes organic material into biogas and digestate using microorganisms in a decomposition process within a closed, oxygen free container as well as the equipment necessary to process biogas or digestate produced by an anaerobic digester into marketable coproducts. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.900, as amended by 2018 Wash. H.B. 2580, §4, effective July 1, 2018; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-210(9)(n)(i)(B). Effective July 1, 2018, “eligible person”means any person establishing or operating an anaerobic digester or landfill, or processing biogas from an anaerobic digester or landfill into marketable coproducts. Wash. Rev. Code §82.08.900, as amended by 2018 Wash. H.B. 2580, §4, effective July 1, 2018. Prior to January 1, 2018, Washington exempted from its use tax the use of fuel by its extractor or manufacturer directly in the operation of the particular extractive operation or manufacturing plant which produced or manufactured the fuel. Effective January 1, 2018, this exemption is narrowed to only apply to biomass fuel. Wash. Rev. Code §82.12.0263, as amended by 2017 Wash. H.B. 2163, effective Aug. 1, 2017, and by 2018 Wash. H.B. 2424, effective Mar. 15, 2018; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-121(6)(a), as amended by Wash. State Register 18-05-011, effective March 11, 2018. However, non-biomass fuel otherwise meeting the criteria for exemption continues to be exempt from local use tax. Wash. Rev. Code §82.12.0203(3), as added by 2017 Wash. H.B. 2163, effective Aug. 1, 2017, and by 2018 Wash. H.B. 2424, effective Mar. 15, 2018. “Biomass fuel” refers to wood waste and other wood residuals, including forest derived biomass. It also includes partially organic by-products of pulp, paper, and wood manufacturing processes. The term does not include firewood or wood pellets. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-121(2)(a), as amended by Wash. State Register 18-05-011, effective March 11, 2018. Refinery Fuel Gas Effective January 1, 2018, through December 31, 2018, refinery fuel gas is subject to a reduced use tax rate of 0.963 percent. This rate is then increased as follows:• 1.926 percent from January 1, 2019, through December 31, 2019; • 2.889 percent from January 1, 2020, through December 31, 2020; and • 3.852 percent from January 1, 2021, and onward. Wash. Rev. Code §82.12.0203(2), as added by 2017 Wash. H.B. 2163, effective Aug. 1, 2017, and by 2018 Wash. H.B. 2424, effective Mar. 15, 2018. Tax Deferrals for High-Technology Businesses Washington also has a retail sales and use tax deferral program to promote high technology research and development in the fields of advanced computing, advanced materials, biotechnology, electronic device technology, and environmental technology. Wash. Rev. Code §82.63.020; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-24003; Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 17-0146 (June 7, 2017). Projects to develop alternative energy sources are eligible for the deferral, however nuclear energy is not considered an alternative energy source. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-24003(3)(e)(v)(C); Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 17-0146 (June 7, 2017). The deferral applies to sales and use tax imposed on the construction, expansion or renovation of qualified buildings, and the purchase of qualified machinery and equipment. Wash. Rev. Code §82.63.045; Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-24003; Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 17-0146 (June 7, 2017). If approved by the department, taxpayers receive a tax deferral certificate stating the amount of tax deferral and the date by which the project is expected to be completed. Wash. Admin. Code §458-20-24003(7); Washington Tax Decision Det. No. 17-0146 (June 7, 2017). BNA-SUTN WA 30.5.
