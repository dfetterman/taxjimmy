Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Exempt Biodiesel fuel subject to the District of Columbia's motor fuels tax is not subject to sales and use tax. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(20); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2301(a). Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Propane

Depends The District taxes the receipts from the sale of natural or artificial gas when made to any purchaser for purposes other than resale or for use in manufacturing, assembling, processing or refining, or if directly used in a restaurant. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(11); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(11A). See D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §§444.1 to 444.12; D.C. Office of Tax & Rev., New Restaurant Utility Exemption Sales Tax Filing Requirements (July 3, 2018). In addition, sales of residential public utility services and commodities by a gas, electric, or telephone company, sales of residential heating oil or related services by any person, sales of residential natural or artificial gas by any person, or sales of residential electricity by an electric supplier are not subject to tax. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(24). BNA-SUTN DC 30.1.10.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Heating Oil

Exempt Sales of residential heating oil or related services by any person are exempt from sales tax. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(24). The District has stated that the “ordinary, common-sense meaning of the term ‘heating oil' is oil used for the purpose of providing heat or warmth”and has provided that heating oil sold for the purpose of manufacturing is not subject to taxation. District of Columbia Tax Ruling 2006–01. BNA-SUTN DC 30.1.11.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends The consumption of public utility services, including electricity, is taxable unless sold for residential use or for direct use in manufacturing and processing. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(D); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(11); D.C. Code Ann. §47-200(24); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2201(a)(1)(B); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2). Sales of electricity for use in a restaurant are also exempt from sales tax. Fiscal Year 2014 Budget Support Emergency Act of 2013, Act 20-130;D.C. Office of Tax & Rev., New Restaurant Utility Exemption Sales Tax Filing Requirements (July 3, 2018). Starting Nov. 2017, restaurants must complete an online application and attach supporting documentation to receive an exemption certificate; certificates include an expiration date and taxpayers must reapply for new exemptions prior to the expiration date. Certificates are valid only for a single location. D.C. Office of Tax & Rev., New Restaurant Utility Exemption Sales Tax Filing Requirements (July 3, 2018). For purposes of the restaurant utility exemption, a restaurant is:• a retail establishment licensed by the District of Columbia; • that is a separately metered or submetered facility; and • is in the principal business of preparing and serving food to the public. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(11A)(B), as amended by 2013 D.C. Act 20-157, effective Dec. 24, 2013; D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2). The restaurant utility exemption includes pizzerias, delicatessens, ice cream parlors, cafeterias, take-out counters, caterers, and banquet and food-processing areas in hotels. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(11A)(B), as amended by 2013 D.C. Act 20-157, effective Dec. 24, 2013; D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2). Beverage counters, including coffee shops and juice bars, are specifically excluded from the restaurant utility exemption. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(11A)(B), as amended by 2013 D.C. Act 20-157, effective Dec. 24, 2013; D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2). BNA-SUTN DC 30.2.1.
