Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Interstate and Common Carrier Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Exempt Sale of motor vehicles and trailers are not subject to sales and use tax. Rather, motor vehicles and trailers are subject to the motor vehicle excise tax, registration, and titling fees. D.C. Accessories attached to a motor vehicle or trailer at the time of sale are considered part of the motor vehicle and are exempt from sales and use tax because such accessories will be subject to motor vehicle excise tax. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(13); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2); D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §451.1. Further, extra equipment and accessories attached to a motor vehicle or trailer at the time of sale, where such vehicle will not be titled in the District and is sold to a non-resident, are not subject to tax. D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §451.2. Further, D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §451.3 states, “[i]f a chassis is purchased from a District dealer or other person by a non-resident of the District and will not be titled in the District, and subsequent to the purchase of the chassis, a body or any other equipment or accessories are attached to that chassis, reimbursement for the sales tax shall be added to the sales price of that body, other equipment, or accessories if the purchaser takes delivery within the District. The receipts from the sale shall be taxable to the vendor.” For more information on any applicable motor vehicle excise taxes, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at District of Columbia Section 8. Limousines and Buses Rental of limousines, chartered buses, and other motor vehicles, are subject to tax “when the control of the vehicle is exercised by the person to whom the vehicle is furnished, regardless of the method of charging for that use. D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §462.1. Exercise of control of a motor vehicle is when the person has:• the exclusive use of the vehicle for a given period of time; and • the right to direct the manner of the use of the vehicle. D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §462.2. Other taxable motor vehicle rentals include:• Rentals of limousines, hearses, and funeral cars to funeral directors; • Rentals of limousines for sightseeing and weddings; • Rentals of limousines or other vehicles to private parties; and • Rentals of taxicabs to funeral directors, sightseeing, weddings and other private parties. D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §462.3. BNA-SUTN DC 29.4.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Water Vessels, Interstate and Common Carrier Fuel

Exempt Sales of water vessel fuel that are subject to motor fuels tax are exempt from sales and use tax. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2005(20); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2206(2); D.C. Code Ann. §47-2301(a). Fuels defined as “motor fuels,” are exempt from sales and use tax, and instead subject to the motor fuels excise tax. “Motor fuels” include gasoline, diesel fuel, and other volatile and flammable liquid fuels produced or compounded for the purposes of operating or propelling internal combustion engines. Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Parking and Valet

Depends Charges for commercial parking services are subject to sales and use tax at a rate of 18 percent in the District of Columbia. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2002(a)(1), as amended by 2017 D.C. B. 244, effective Oct. 1, 2017; District of Columbia OTR Notice Parking Sales (Sept. 2, 2011). The rate was scheduled to increase to 22 percent on Oct. 1, 2017; however, the D.C. Council repealed this increase. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2002(a)(1), as amended by 2017 D.C. B. 244, effective Oct. 1, 2017. Residential Parking Charges for parking to a D.C. resident who is a tenant in an apartment or the owner of a condominium or cooperative unit in which he or she resides are not taxable, provided that the parking is provided on the same premises at which the tenant or owner has his or her place of residence. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(i); D.C. Mun. Regs. tit. 9, §454.1. There is not a taxable retail sale if a District resident possesses a parking sales tax exemption card and shows it to the parking service provider. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(ii)(I). This exemption extends only to District of Columbia residents who use the service for the purpose of keeping their vehicles or trailers near their place of residence; it does not extend to a resident whose motor vehicle or trailer is used for commercial purposes. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(ii)(II). The Mayor must issue a parking sales tax exemption card to a resident if the resident:• applies for the card; D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iii). • possesses a District of Columbia motor vehicle or trailer registration certificate and identification tag for the motor vehicle or trailer to be parked, if required by the motor vehicle registration provisions; D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iii)(I). • has registered the vehicle or trailer to a DC residential address (if a registration certificate is required by the motor vehicle registration provisions) located within 0.5 miles of the service-providing business or operation's address; D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iii)(II). and • provides the Mayor with the service-providing business or operation's name and address. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iii)(III). A parking sales tax exemption card states the name and address of the person to whom it is issued, the name and address of the business or operation to provide the service, and any other information, including a photograph, that the Mayor deems necessary. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iv). Valet Parking The District of Columbia exempts from sales and use tax parking charges made by a valet parking service business. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iv-I)(I). The term “valet parking service business”means a corporation, partnership, business entity, or proprietor that takes temporary control of a motor vehicle of a person attending any restaurant, business, activity, or event to park, store, or retrieve the vehicle. It does not include a garage, parking lot, or parking facility that provides parking services by parking lot attendants. D.C. Code Ann. §47-2001(n)(1)(L)(iv-I)(II). BNA-SUTN DC 29.10.
