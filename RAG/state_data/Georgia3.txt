State: Georgia



Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Depends Effective July 1, 2015, sales of biodiesel purchased to propel motor vehicles on public highways are exempt from state sales and use taxes. Sales of biodiesel purchased for any other purpose are fully subject to the state sales and use tax. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3.1, as amended by 2015 Ga. H.B. 170, effective July 1, 2015. Prior to July 1, 2015, sales of biodiesel purchased to propel motor vehicles on public highways, were exempt from the first 3 percent of state sales and use taxes imposed but subject to the remaining 1 percent of state sales and use taxes. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3.1, as amended by 2015 Ga. H.B. 170, effective July 1, 2015. BNA-SUTN GA 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Dyed Diesel

Depends Whether sales of dyed diesel are subject to the state sales and use tax depends whether it is purchased for highway use. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3; Georgia Letter Ruling SUT-2018-01- Dyed Diesel Fuel (March 7, 2018). Sales of dyed diesel for highway use, such as for the construction, reconstruction, maintenance, or repair of public highways, are exempt from sales and use taxes. Sales of dyed diesel for non-highway use are taxable. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3; Georgia Letter Ruling SUT-2018-01- Dyed Diesel Fuel (March 7, 2018). BNA-SUTN GA 30.1.5.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Taxable Georgia requires the payment of sales tax on the sale of electricity and any other energy-providing materials when made to a purchaser for purposes other than resale. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-30; Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-2(31)(A); Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.32(1). The tax, however, does not apply to deposits used to guarantee payment of utility services. Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.32(1). Additionally, artificial or natural gas used to produce electricity is exempt. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(70). BNA-SUTN GA 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Mining, Timber, Natural Resource Extraction Timber

Exempt Off-road equipment and related attachments that are sold to, or used by, persons engaged primarily in the growing or harvesting of timber and that are used exclusively in site preparation, planting, cultivating, or harvesting timber are exempt from sales and use tax. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3.3(a)(1)(B); Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3.3(b). “Primary activity”means the principal activity in which a person is engaged and to which more than 50 percent of the person's resources are committed. Georgia Policy Bulletin SUT-2016-02; Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. r. 560-12-2-.03. Equipment used in harvesting includes all off-road equipment and related attachments used in every forestry procedure starting with the severing of a tree from the ground until and including the point at which the tree or its parts in any form has been loaded in the field in or on a truck or other vehicle for transport to the place of use. “Off-road equipment” includes skidders;feller bunchers; debarkers; delimbers; chip harvesters; tub-grinders;woods cutters; chippers of all types; loaders of all types; dozers;motor graders; and all related attachments. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3.3(a)(1)(B). BNA-SUTN GA 30.3.3.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Exempt The sale or use of biomass material, including pellets or other fuels derived from compressed, chipped, or shredded biomass material, utilized in the production of energy, including without limitation the production of electricity, steam, or the production of electricity and steam, which is subsequently sold, is exempt from tax. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(83)(A). “Biomass material”means organic matter, including agricultural crops, plants, trees, wood, wood wastes and residues, sawmill waste, sawdust, wood chips, bark chips, and forest thinning, harvesting, or clearing residues;wood waste from pallets or other wood demolition debris; peanut shells;pecan shells; cotton plants; corn stalks; and plant matter, including aquatic plants, grasses, stalks, vegetation, and residues, including hulls, shells, or cellulose containing fibers. “Biomass material”does not include fossil fuels. Ga. Code Ann. §48-8-3(83)(B). BNA-SUTN GA 30.5.
