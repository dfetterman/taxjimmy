Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends New York City generally imposes tax on the same sales and services taxed at the state level. N.Y. Tax Law §1210(a)(1). However, while New York State exempts fuel, utilities (gas, electricity, refrigeration and steam) and utility services used or consumed directly and exclusively in the production for sale of tangible personal property, gas, electricity, refrigeration or steam by mining or extracting, N.Y. Tax Law §1115(c). New York City's correlating exemption is limited to fuel, utilities (gas, electricity, refrigeration and steam) and utility services used or consumed directly and exclusively in the production for sale of tangible personal property. See N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2001(b)(8); N.Y. Tax Law §1115(c)(1). Unless otherwise provided, New York State taxes the receipts from the sale of gas, electricity, refrigeration, and steam, as well as gas, electric, refrigeration, and steam services. N.Y. Tax Law §1105(b)(1); New York Notice N-09-12 (July 2009). New York City imposes tax on receipts from every sale of transporting, transmitting, distributing, or delivering gas or electricity, even if sold separately or purchased from someone other than the vendor of the gas or electricity. N.Y. Tax Law §1210(a)(1); N.Y. Tax Law §1210(b)(1); N.Y. Tax Law §1115(c); N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2001(b)(8); New York Notice N-09-12 (July 2009). New York City's rate of tax on such services does not follow the state's reduced rate for certain gas or electric services, which has been phased out Sept. 2003. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2001(b)(8); N.Y. Tax Law §1105-C(a). Rather, the city tax applies at the regular 4.5 percent. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-2002(a); New York Notice N-09-12 (July 2009). For more information on how New York State taxes utilities see the Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at New York 30.2.1. In addition, New York City imposes an excise tax upon utilities for the privilege of exercising its franchise, holding its property, or doing business in the city based on its gross income. Vendors of utility services are similarly subject an excise tax based on its gross operating income. N.Y.C. Admin. Code §11-1102(a). For more information regarding the application of the utility tax in New York City, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, at New York City 11.1. BNA-SUTN NYC 30.2.1.
