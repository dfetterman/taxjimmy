State: Wyoming



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Wyoming exempts sales of food for domestic home consumption from states sales and use taxes. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(vi)(E); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN WY 11.1.1.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined Wyoming incorporates the definition of food and food ingredients of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement and the phrase “food for domestic home consumption” includes substances, whether liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that are sold for human ingestion or chewing and consumed to meet their nutritional value and taste. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xli); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xviii); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §13(u). The definition of food and food ingredients includes dietary supplements, however, it does not include alcoholic beverages, tobacco, prepared foods, or food sold through vending machines. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xli); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xviii); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §13(u). BNA-SUTN WY 11.1.2.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Grocery Items

Depends Grocery items are not subject to tax. However, alcohol, tobacco, prepared foods, and items sold through vending machines in grocery stores are subject to tax. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xli); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xviii); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §13(u); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN WY 11.1.3.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Candy

Exempt Generally, Wyoming exempts the sale, storage, use, or other consumption of food and food ingredients including, but not limited to, candy. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(vi)(E); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). However, when candy is sold from a vending machine it becomes taxable. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. “Candy” is defined as a preparation of sugar, honey, or other natural or artificial sweetener combined with chocolate, fruit, nuts, or other ingredients or flavorings in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. This does not include a preparation that contains flour or that requires refrigeration. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xli). BNA-SUTN WY 11.1.3.2.

Exempt The sale, storage, use, or other consumption of dietary supplements is exempt. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(vi)(E); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). Dietary supplements are products, other than tobacco, that are intended to supplement a person's diet, if all of the following apply:• the product contains any of the following ingredients: ○ a vitamin; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(A). ○ a mineral; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(B). ○ an herb or other botanical; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(C). ○ an amino acid; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(D). ○ a dietary substance intended for human consumption to supplement the diet by increasing total dietary intake; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(E). or ○ a concentrate, metabolite, constituent, or extract; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(F). • the product is intended for ingestion in tablet, capsule, powder, soft-gel, gel-cap, or liquid form, or, if not intended for ingestion in such forms, is not represented as conventional food and is not represented for use as the sole item of a meal or diet; Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(F). and • the product is required to be labeled as a dietary supplement as required under 21 C.F.R. §101.36. Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(l)(i)(F). BNA-SUTN WY 11.1.3.3.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Coffee

Depends The taxability of coffee depends on whether it is purchased for home consumption or immediate consumption as prepared food. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xli); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xviii); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §13(u); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). Wyoming exempts sales of food for domestic home consumption, including ground coffee or beans typically bought at a grocery store, from states sales and use taxes. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(vi)(E); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. However, if coffee is considered prepared food, such as hot or iced coffee from a coffee shop, it will be subject to tax. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-103(a)(i)(F); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-105(a)(vi)(E); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Wyoming Taxability Matrix (version 2018.0). Venders who sell both prepared and exempt food must adopt a 75/25 policy. This means that when a food vendor's sales are comprised of both prepared food and non-prepared food, and 75 percent or more of the sales are prepared food, the vendor must impose sales tax on all food sales. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry. However, if such vendor sells items that contain four or more services packaged as one items sold for a single price, the item is considered an exempt food for domestic consumption and not prepared food. Thus, if a bag of coffee has four or more coffee servings per container, such as a one-pound bag of ground coffee, it will be considered an isolated sale exempt as food for domestic home consumption. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry; Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. BNA-SUTN WY 11.1.3.13.

Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry. Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined Prepared foods are food that are generally intended for and ready for immediate consumption. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xviii); Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p)(i). Wyoming defines the term “prepared food” to include:• food sold in a heated state or heated by the seller; or • two or more food ingredients mixed or combined by the seller for sale as a single item; or • food sold with eating utensils provided by the seller, including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p). Prepared food does not include the following:• food that is only cut, repackaged, or pasteurized by the seller, and eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these raw animal foods requiring cooking by the consumer as recommended by the Food and Drug Administration; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p)(i)(A). • food sold in an unheated state by weight or volume as a single item; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p)(i)(B). • food sold by a seller whose proper primary NAICS classification is food manufacturing; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p)(i)(C). • bakery items including bread, rolls, buns, biscuits, bagels, croissants, pastries, donuts, Danishes, cakes, tortes, pies, tarts, muffins, bars, cookies, or tortillas that are sold for other than immediate consumption; Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyo. Stat. §39-16-101(a)(xix); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p)(i)(D). or • dietary supplements including vitamins, minerals, botanicals, amino acids, and other substances used to enhance dietary health. Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §2(p)(i)(E). BNA-SUTN WY 11.2.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Restaurant' or 'Eating Establishment'

Not Defined Wyoming's sales and use tax code does not define “restaurant” or “eating establishment.” Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a). However, a Wyoming statute governing alcoholic beverages defines a “restaurant” as space in a building maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where individually priced meals are prepared and served primarily for on-premise consumption and where the primary source of revenue from the operation is from the sale of food and not from the sale of alcoholic or malt beverages. The building shall have a dining room or rooms, a kitchen and the number and kinds of employees necessary for the preparing, cooking and serving of meals in order to satisfy the licensing authority that the space is intended for use as a full service restaurant. Wyo. Stat. §12-1-101(a)(xiv). “Full service restaurant” means a restaurant at which waiters or waitresses deliver food and drink offered from a printed food menu to patrons at tables or booths. The service of only fry orders or such food and victuals as sandwiches, hamburgers or salads shall not be deemed a restaurant for the purposes of this section.” Wyo. Stat. §12-1-101(a)(xiv). BNA-SUTN WY 11.2.3.

Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry. BNA-SUTN WY 11.3.6.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Complimentary Meals

Nontaxable Complimentary meals or beverages given free of charge as a gesture of goodwill for a negative experience or similar circumstance are not subject to sales tax. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry. However, the restaurant would owe sales and use tax on the wholesale cost of the food given away as a complimentary meal. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry. BNA-SUTN WY 11.6.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Mobile Food Vendors

Taxable Sales of prepared food are subject to Wyoming's sales and use tax. No exceptions are provided for mobile food vendors. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xlvi); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. BNA-SUTN WY 11.7.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Restaurant Equipment

Taxable Any equipment, materials or supplies that are purchased by a business to perform its service are subject to tax at the time of purchase. Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §13(bb); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Publication- Restaurant Industry. A restaurant that paid sales tax on items that may have been nontaxable as a sale for resale, such as utility bills, parts, supplies, and food, was still liable for sales tax on the entire purchase price paid by the customer. In re T-Joe's Bar &Lounge, Wyoming Administrative Decision 2012-64 (Sept. 6, 2013). BNA-SUTN WY 11.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Depends Wyoming imposes sales tax on tangible personal property and prepared food and sold through vending machines. However, if the item qualifies as food for domestic home consumption, the fact that it is sold through a vending machine will not transition that product into prepared food and it will not be subject to tax. Wyo. Stat. §39-15-101(a)(xli); Wyo. Regs. ch. 2 §13(il); Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. BNA-SUTN WY 11.9.

==================================================

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Other Food-Related Transactions

Depends Wedding cakes and party trays containing cold products may be subject to sales and use tax, depending on the type of seller. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. Wedding cakes purchased from a grocer or bakery are not subject to sales tax unless the vendor also provides utensils, such as plates, forks, or serving utensils as part of the price for the wedding cake. Wedding cakes sold and served by a caterer are taxable as prepared foods. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. Party trays containing cold products like sliced or cubed cheeses, meats, fruits or vegetables with or without dip made by an entity whose primary NAICS code is that of a food manufacturer are exempt as food for domestic home consumption. Party trays assembled by the deli, grocery store or any entities other than NAICS-classified food manufacturers are taxable as prepared food. Wyoming Sales and Use Tax Bulletin- Food for Domestic Home Consumption. BNA-SUTN WY 11.10.
