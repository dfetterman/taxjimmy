State: Florida



Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Taxation of Property

Yes Florida sales and use tax is imposed on the sale, lease, rental or license to use tangible personal property. Fla. Stat. §212.05. “Tangible personal property” is defined as personal property which may be seen, weighed, measured, or touched or is perceptible to the senses. Fla. Stat. §212.02(19). Tangible personal property includes: • electric power or energy; • boats; • motor vehicles; • mobile homes; • aircraft;and • all other types of vehicles. Fla. Stat. §212.02(19). However, “tangible personal property”does not include stocks, bonds, notes, insurance, or other obligations or securities; or pari-mutuel tickets sold or issued under the racing laws of Florida. Fla. Stat. §212.02(19). More information on the taxation of property is available at 1300 T.M., Sales and Use Taxes: General Principles and 1360 T.M., Sales and Use Taxes: Retail Sales Issues. BNA-SUTN FL 9.1.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Taxation of Services

Enumerated Services Only Florida sales and use tax is imposed on the retail sale of tangible personal property and enumerated services. Fla. Stat. §212.05(1). Such enumerated services include:• detective, burglar protection and other protection services; Fla. Stat. §212.05(1)(i)(1)(a). • nonresidential cleaning services; Fla. Stat. §212.05(1)(i)(1)(b). • nonresidential pest control services; Fla. Stat. §212.05(1)(i)(1)(b). and • the installation of telecommunication services. Fla. Stat. §212.05(1)(e)1.b. Throughput Agreements Payments made pursuant to a throughput agreement are services that are not subject to sales tax. Florida Technical Assistance Advisement 16A-016 (Sept. 12, 2016). A “Throughput Agreement” is defined as an agreement to put a specified amount of product per period through a particular facility. An example is an agreement to ship a specified amount of crude oil per period through a particular pipeline. Throughput Agreement, Nasdaq (Dec. 24, 2016). Florida law provides that “when the owner of equipment furnishes the operator and all operating supplies, and contracts for their use to perform certain work under his direction and according to his customer's specifications, and the customer does not take possession or have any direction or control over the physical operation, the contract constitutes a service transaction and not the rental of tangible personal property, and no tax is due on the transaction.” See Fla. Admin. Code Ann. r. 12A-1.071(7)(d); Florida Technical Assistance Advisement 16A-016 (Sept. 12, 2016). Transactions that involve the sale or use of both taxable and nontaxable services must be separately identified and stated with respect to the taxable and exempt portions of the transaction or the entire transaction is subject to tax. Fla. Stat. §212.05(1)(i)4. BNA-SUTN FL 9.2.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Mixed and Bundled Transactions, Application of the True Object Test Mixed Transactions and the True Object Test

Taxable Mixed transactions are generally taxable on the entire price paid. Florida has not adopted the “true object test.” AT&T v. Florida Dept of Revenue, 764 So. 2d 665, 670 (Fla. Dist. Ct. App. 2000). The sale of tangible personal property is taxable on the “sales price.” The term “sales price” means the total amount paid for tangible personal property, including any services that are part of the sale. Fla. Stat. §212.02(16). However, professional and personal service transactions that involve inconsequential sales with no separate charges are exempt from sale and use tax. Fla. Stat. §212.08(7)(v). BNA-SUTN FL 9.3.1.

Topic: Overview: Taxation of Property and Services Local Jurisdictions, Taxability Distinctions

Conforms to State In Florida, the local tax is a discretionary surtax that applies to all transactions occurring in the county that are subject to the sales and use tax. Fla. Stat. §212.054(2). The surtax is not mandatory. Fla. Stat. §212.054. Charter County and Regional Transportation System Surtax Each charter county that has adopted a charter, each county the government of which is consolidated with that of one or more municipalities, and each county that is within or under an interlocal agreement with a regional transportation or transit authority created under chapter 343 or chapter 349 may levy a discretionary sales surtax, subject to approval by a majority vote of the electorate of the county or by a charter amendment approved by a majority vote of the electorate of the county in Florida may levy a charter county. Fla. Stat. §212.055(1)(a). Local Government Infrastructure Tax Each county in Florida may levy a local government infrastructure surtax of 0.5 percent or 1 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(2)(a). Small County Surtax Each county in Florida with a population of 50,000 or less may levy a small county surtax of 0.5 percent or 1 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(3)(a). Indigent Care and Trauma Center Surtax Each county in Florida with a population of at least 800,000, and is not authorized to levy a county public hospital surtax, may levy an indigent care and trauma center surtax of up to 0.5 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(4)(a)(1). County Public Hospital Surtax Any county in Florida as defined in s. 125.011(1)may levy a county public hospital surtax of 0.5 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(5). School Capital Outlay Surtax Each school board in each county in Florida may levy a school capital outlay surtax of up to 0.5 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(6)(a). Voter-Approved Indigent Care Surtax Each county in Florida with a population of less than 800,000 may levy a voter-approved indigent care surtax of up to 0.5 percent, except that if a publicly supported medical school is located in the county, the rate cannot exceed 1 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(7)(a)(1). Emergency Fire Rescue Services and Facilities Surtax Each county in Florida, other than a county that has imposed two separate discretionary surtaxes without expiration, may levy an emergency fire rescue services and facilities surtax of up to 1 percent. Fla. Stat. §212.055(8)(a). BNA-SUTN FL 9.4.
