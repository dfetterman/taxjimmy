State: Maryland



Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Sales Tax

Yes Maryland sales tax is imposed on retail sales of tangible personal property or taxable services within the state. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(a). The duty to pay sales or use tax is legally imposed on the purchaser of tangible personal property or taxable services. The vendor is required to collect the applicable tax on behalf of the Comptroller of Maryland. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-403(b), as amended by 2019 Md. H.B. 884, effective June 1, 2019, and by 2019 Md. S.B. 533, effective June 1, 2019; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-403(c), as amended by 2019 Md. H.B. 884, effective June 1, 2019, and by 2019 Md. S.B. 533, effective June 1, 2019. BNA-SUTN MD 1.1.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Use Tax

Yes Maryland imposes a complementary use tax on the use within the state of tangible personal property or taxable services. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(a). BNA-SUTN MD 1.1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Local Option Sales and Use Tax

No Local Tax Under Maryland law, a county, municipal corporation, special taxing district, or other Maryland political subdivision (local jurisdictions) may not impose a general retail sales or use tax, unless the tax was in effect on January 1, 1971. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(c). As of 2017, no local sales and use taxes are imposed in the state. However, the state has authorized imposition of local excise taxes in certain circumstances. Understanding Maryland Taxes (2013). BNA-SUTN MD 1.1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Excise and Replacement Taxes

Yes Excise Taxes The state of Maryland has a number of excise taxes, including local excise taxes. See, e.g., Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §5-102. The state's excise taxes include, but are not limited to:• an alcoholic beverage tax; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §5-102; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §5-105. • a cigarette and tobacco tax; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §12-102. and • a fuels tax. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §9-314. Local jurisdictions may also impose local excise tax on fuels, utilities, space rentals, controlled dangerous substances, and telecommunications services. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(c)(1)(ii); Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(c)(1)(iii); Md. Code Ann. Local Govt. §20-605(a). A code county may impose a tax on the sale of food and beverages in a resort area. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-102(c)(1)(iii); Md. Code Ann. Local Govt. §20-602. Although referred to as sales and use taxes, in effect, local taxes operate as excise taxes. For more information on state and local excise taxes in Maryland, see the Excise Tax Navigator, beginning at Maryland 1.1. Replacement Taxes The Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement prohibits member states from imposing a “prohibited replacement tax” which is a tax outside of the sales and use tax that is imposed on products defined in the Agreement. Generally, the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board will make a finding regarding whether or not a member state has imposed a replacement tax. Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement as amended through December 16, 2016, Section 334 and Rule 334 of the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board,Rules and Procedures. Maryland is not a member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Governing Board, State Info. BNA-SUTN MD 1.1.4.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Historical Information

Est. 1947 Maryland first enacted its sales and use tax in 1947. Understanding Maryland Taxes (2013). When the tax was initially enacted, it was imposed at a rate of 2 percent on retail sales of tangible personal property within the state. Understanding Maryland Taxes (2013). By 1977, the tax increased to 5 percent. Currently, the tax rate is at 6 percent and it now applies more broadly to sales of tangible personal property and some services. Understanding Maryland Taxes (2013). BNA-SUTN MD 1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Pending and Trending Developments

Exemptions;Taxability List From July 1, 2016, through June 30, 2026, Maryland exempts from sales and use tax sales of construction material or warehousing equipment, if the material or equipment is purchased by a person solely for use in a specified “target redevelopment area” in Baltimore County. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(B), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. Effective Oct. 1, 2019, the Comptroller will be required to publish a comprehensive list of the taxability of tangible personal property and services which will be updated quarterly. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-109, as added by 2019 Md. H.B. 454, effective April 30, 2019. BNA-SUTN MD 1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Sale' and 'Retail Sale'

Defined “Sale”is defined as a transaction for a consideration, whereby a service is performed for another person, or title or possession of property is transferred by any means, including by lease, rental, royalty agreement, or licensure. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(i)(1). “Retail sale”is the sale of tangible personal property or a taxable service. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(h)(1). BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.1.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Lease'

Not Defined Statutes and regulations governing sales and use tax in Maryland do not define the term “lease.” See Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101; Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.01, et seq. However, a transaction whereby, in exchange for consideration, a service is performed or title or possession of property is transferred by any means, including by lease is considered a “sale” in Maryland. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(i)(1); Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.28. BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.2.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Use' or 'Storage, Use, or Consumption'

Defined “Use”is the exercise of a right or power to use, consume, possess, or store that is acquired by a sale for use of tangible personal property or a taxable service. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(n)(1). “Sale for use”is a sale in which tangible personal property or a taxable service that is consumed, possessed, stored, or used in Maryland is acquired. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(j). With respect to sales of tangible personal property, only the price of the raw materials and component parts contained in the property are included in the taxable price when these raw materials and component parts are acquired for use in Maryland by a person who assembles, fabricates, or manufactures the property in Maryland. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(l)(2); Maryland Comp. of Treas. v. Am. Can Co., 117 A.2d 559 (Md. 1955). BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Tangible Personal Property'

Defined In Maryland, “tangible personal property” means corporeal property of any nature, or a right to occupy a room or lodgings as a transient guest. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(k)(1). “Tangible personal property” also includes:• farm equipment; • wall-to-wall carpeting installed into real estate, regardless of the purpose, method, or permanency of its installation; and • coal, electricity, oil, nuclear fuel assemblies, steam, and artificial or natural gas. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(k)(2). BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.4.

==================================================

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Intangible Property'

Not Defined Statutes and regulations governing sales and use tax in Maryland do not define “intangible property.” Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101. BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.5.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Retailer' or 'Vendor'

Defined Maryland defines “retail vendor” as a person who sells or delivers tangible property or taxable services in Maryland. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-701(c)(1). Effective Feb. 20, 2016, the definition of “vendor” includes an accommodations intermediary. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(o)(1), as amended by 2015 Md. S.B. 190, effective Feb. 20, 2016. An “accommodations intermediary” is a person, other than an accommodations provider, who facilitates the sale or use of an accommodation and charges a buyer the taxable price for the accommodation. A person who brokers, coordinates, or in any other way arranges for the sale or use of an accommodation by a buyer is considered to facilitate the transaction. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(a-2), as added by 2015 Md. S.B. 190, effective Feb. 20, 2016. An “accommodations provider” owns, operates, or manages an accommodation and makes the accommodation available for sale or use to a buyer. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(a-3), as added by 2015 Md. S.B. 190, effective Feb. 20, 2016. Effective June 1, 2019, the definition of “vendor” is further expanded to include a short-term rental platform. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(o)(1)(v), as amended by 2019 Md. H.B. 884, effective June 1, 2019, and by 2019 Md. S.B. 533, effective June 1, 2019. A “short-term rental platform” means an Internet-based digital entity that:• advertises the availability of short-term rental units for rent; and • receives compensation for facilitating reservations or processing booking transactions on behalf of the owner, operator, or manager of a short-term rental unit. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(j-2), as amended by 2019 Md. H.B. 884, effective June 1, 2019, and by 2019 Md. S.B. 533, effective June 1, 2019. Effective Oct. 1, 2019, the definition of “vendor”includes persons who engage in the business of a marketplace facilitator or marketplace seller. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(o)(1), as amended by 2019 Md. H.B. 1301, effective Oct. 1, 2019 BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.6.

Not Defined Statutes and regulations governing sales and use tax in Maryland do not define “purchaser”or “consumer.” See Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101, et seq; Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.01, et seq. BNA-SUTN MD 1.4.7.
