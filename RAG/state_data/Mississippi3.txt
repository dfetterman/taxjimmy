State: Mississippi



Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Taxable Mississippi imposes sales and use tax on the commercial, nonresidential, and nonagricultural sales and use of biodiesel. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(a). Mississippi imposes sales and use tax on the sales of electricity, natural gas, liquefied petroleum gas, or other fuels, and services related to providing such services, when sold by an electric power association, natural gas district, municipalities, privately owned businesses, or any other person or business. Sales of utilities are taxed at the regular tax rate of 7 percent. However, the sales of utilities are exempt when sold for residential purposes, such as heating and lighting. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(a); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.101; Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.201. Sales to manufacturers, custom processors or public service companies for industrial purposes at their plant site are taxable at the special rate of 1.5 percent. This includes utilities used to generate electricity, to operate power line substations and pipeline compressor or pump stations when not powered by internal combustion engines. Miss. Code Ann. § 27-65-19. Effective July 1, 2009, sales of naturally occurring carbon dioxide and anthropogenic carbon dioxide that is lawfully injected into the earth for use in enhanced oil recovery projects, or for the permanent sequestration of the carbon dioxide in a geological formation, is taxable at the special rate of 1.5 percent. Miss. Code Ann. § 27-65-19(1)(b)(ii), as added by 2009 Miss. H.B. 1459, effective July 1, 2009. The sequestration in a geological formation of carbon dioxide emissions from the burning of coal is part of the environmental “clean coal” effort. However, Mississippi exempts from tax fuels used to power motor vehicles including biodiesel. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.04.01.100 Residential Use Exemption To qualify for the residential exemption, all utilities must be sold to, billed to, and paid for by the owner of resident of the residential facility. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.201. Residential customers include, but need not be limited to, privately owned hunting and fishing camps, apartments, homes, cabins, and summer homes. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.202. Any residential unit which is used for both residential and business purposes is taxed at the regular rate unless there is a separate meter used to measure the business's use of the utilities. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.203. Church Exemption Sales of electricity, current, power, natural gas, liquefied petroleum gas or other fuel for heating, lighting or other use, and sales of potable water to a church are exempt from Mississippi sales tax if the sales are exempt from federal income tax. To qualify for the exemption, the church purchasing the utilities may submit an affidavit attesting that it is exempt from federal taxation, and therefore qualifies for the exemption in Mississippi. Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.302 Agricultural or Livestock Use Sales of electricity, natural gas, liquefied petroleum gas or other fuels used in the production of agricultural or livestock products for market are taxable at a reduced 1.5 percent rate. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(b)(i); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.103. Use in Enhanced Oil Recovery Sales of electricity, current, power, steam, coal, natural gas, liquefied petroleum gas or other fuel are taxable at a reduced 1.5 percent rate when sold to a producer of oil and gas for use directly in enhanced oil recovery using carbon dioxide and/or the permanent sequestration of carbon dioxide in a geological formation. Miss. Code Ann. §27-65-19(1)(b)(ii); Miss. Regs. §35.IV.06.01.103. BNA-SUTN MS 30.1.1.
