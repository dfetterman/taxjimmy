Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Gross receipts from the sale and purchase of, and storage, distribution, use or consumption of food and food ingredients are exempt from sales and use tax. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Minnesota (version 2018.0); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102A (food and food ingredients). BNA-SUTN MN 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined Food and food ingredients are substances, whether in liquid, concentrated, solid, frozen, dried, or dehydrated form, that are sold for ingestion or chewing by humans and are consumed for their taste or nutritional value. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102A; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Minnesota (version 2018.0). Prior to July 17, 2017, the exemption for food and food ingredients does not apply to candy, soft drinks, all food sold through vending machines, dietary supplements, alcoholic beverages, tobacco, and prepared foods. Bottled water for human consumption is not subject to sales or use tax in Minnesota. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102A; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Minnesota (version 2018.0). Effective July 1, 2017, vending machine sales of food are exempt unless such sale is of “taxable food.” “Taxable food sold through vending machines” means candy, soft drinks, dietary supplements, alcoholic beverages, tobacco, and prepared foods. 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, First Special Session, Art. 3, effective July 1, 2017, amending the following: Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d) (removing “all food sold through vending machines” from the list of taxable food); Minn. Stat. §297A.61(34) (defining “taxable food sold through vending machines”); Minn. Stat. §297A.67(2) (amending definition of “food and food ingredients”); Minn. Stat. §297A.67(4) (vending machine sales of taxable food at residential facilities); Minn. Stat. §297A.67(5) (vending machine sales of taxable food at schools); Minn. Stat. §297A.67(6) (vending machine sales of taxable food to persons 60 years of age or older or persons who are disabled). BNA-SUTN MN 11.1.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Candy

Taxable Candy is a taxable food item in Minnesota. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d)(3); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102A; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102B. Candy is a preparation of sugar, honey, or other sweeteners in combination with chocolate, fruits, nuts, or other ingredients or flavorings in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(33); Minn. R. 8130.4700(6)(A). Candy does not include food items that require refrigeration or that contain any type of flour as an ingredient. Minn. R. 8130.4700(6)(B); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102B. Items that contain the word “flour,” such as white, whole wheat, rice, corn, or brown flour, on the ingredient label are not taxable. Ingredients such as soy or whey used in place of flour, are not flour. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102B. Cotton candy is commonly thought of as candy, but it is not candy because it is not sold in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. Cotton candy is taxable when prepared by the seller. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102B. BNA-SUTN MN 11.1.3.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Dietary Supplements and Vitamins

Taxable Dietary supplements are taxable in Minnesota. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d)(4); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102A; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 117E (revised Nov. 2017). A “dietary supplement”is any product, other than tobacco, intended to supplement the diet that:• contains one or more of the following dietary ingredients: ○ a vitamin; ○ a mineral; ○ an herb or other botanical; ○ an amino acid; ○ a dietary substance for use by humans to supplement the diet by increasing the total dietary intake; and ○ a concentrate, metabolite, constituent, extract, or combination of any ingredient described above. Minn. Stat. §297A.67(2)(1)(i)–Minn. Stat. §297A.67(2)(1)(vi). • the product is intended for ingestion in tablet, capsule, powder, soft-gel, gel-cap, or liquid form, or, if not intended for ingestion in such forms, is not represented as conventional food and is not represented for use as the sole item of a meal or diet; Minn. Stat. §297A.67(2)(2). and • the product is required to be labeled as a dietary supplement as required under 21 C.F.R. §101.36. Minn. Stat. §297A.67(2)(3). See also SlimGenics Weight Control Centers v. Minnesota Comr. of Rev., No. 8422-R (Minn. Tax Ct. Oct. 23, 2013) (an item can only be labeled a dietary supplement for sales tax purposes through reference to its “Supplemental Facts” label and any item that does not bear such a label cannot be determined a dietary supplement). Any item required by FDA to be labeled with“Supplement Facts” is taxable, including certain teas, appetite suppressants and/or stimulants, or food supplements such as vitamins and minerals, whether sold in tablet, capsule, powder, softgel, gelcap, or liquid form. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102E. Meal substitutes labeled with “Nutrition Facts” are not taxable. Examples include unsweetened breakfast bars or bars that contain flour. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102E; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 117E (revised Nov. 2017). BNA-SUTN MN 11.1.3.3.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Specific Products Soft Drinks

Taxable Soft drinks are taxable food items in Minnesota. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d)(2); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102A; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C. Soft drinks are nonalcoholic beverages that contain natural or artificial sweeteners. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(32); Minn. R. 8130.4700(5)(A); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C. “Natural and artificial sweeteners”means an ingredient of a food product that adds a sugary sweetness to the taste of the food product. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C provides a reference list of common ingredients that, if included on the product label of a beverage, generally indicates a taxable soft drink. Soft drinks do not include beverages that contain milk, milk products, soy, rice, or similar milk substitutes. Minn. R. 8130.4700(5)(B). Also soft drinks do not include beverages that contain more than 50 percent vegetable or fruit juice. When beverages labeled as fruit juice, fruit drink, fruit sports drink, or fruit nectar do not specify the percentage of juice content, they are taxable. Minn. R. 8130.4700(5)(C). Beverage powders and concentrates are not considered soft drinks for sales tax purposes. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C. Nonalcoholic beer is taxable as a soft drink because it contains sweeteners. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C. For information on excise taxes on soft drinks in Minnesota, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Minnesota 14.1. BNA-SUTN MN 11.1.3.9.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food General Rule

Taxable Prepared food is subject to tax in Minnesota. Minn. Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined Prepared food includes is food that is sold with eating utensils provided by the seller, including plates, knives, forks, spoons, glasses, cups, napkins, or straws. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(31); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102D; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Minnesota (version 2018.0). “Prepared food”also consists of food sold in a heated state or heated by the seller, or two or more food ingredients that are mixed or combined by the seller for sale as a single item. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(31); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102D; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Minnesota (version 2018.0). Prepared food does not include:• bakery items, including, but not limited to, bread, rolls, buns, biscuits, bagels, croissants, donuts, Danish, tortes, pies, muffins, cookies; Minn. Stat. §297A.61(31)(2)(i); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102D. • ready-to-eat meat and seafood in an unheated state sold by weight; Minn. Stat. §297A.61(31)(2)(ii). • eggs, fish, meat, poultry, and foods containing these raw animal foods that require cooking by the consumer recommended by the Food and Drug Administration to prevent food borne illnesses; Minn. Stat. §297A.61(31)(2)(iii); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102D. or • food that is only sliced, repackaged, or pasteurized by the seller. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(31)(2)(iv); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102D. BNA-SUTN MN 11.2.2.

For hotels, when meals or drinks are included in the price of a room, and the room charge does not change whether or not the customer consumes the meal or drink, it is considered complimentary. Minnesota Hotels and Other Lodging Establishments Industry Guide; Minnesota Food and Bar Establishments Industry Guide. Disposable items used in providing free meals are subject to use tax as well. This includes disposable napkins, disposable cups, soft drinks in cans or bottles, straws, and paper plates. Minnesota Food and Bar Establishments Industry Guide. Employee Meals If hotel or restaurant employees receive a discount on meals, sales tax should be charged to the employee on the price paid for the meal. However, if employees receive meals, the hotel or restaurant owes use tax on any taxable items used in those meals. Minnesota Food and Bar Establishments Industry Guide. BNA-SUTN MN 11.6.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Mobile Food Vendors

Taxable Minnesota does not discuss any sales or use tax implications relating to mobile food venders. Minn. Stat. §297A.61. BNA-SUTN MN 11.7.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Restaurant Equipment

Taxable Restaurants do not qualify for the industrial production or capital equipment exemptions; therefore, restaurant equipment is subject to sales and use tax. Minnesota Food and Bar Establishments Industry Guide. Taxable cooking and serving equipment includes appliances and cookware. Minnesota Food and Bar Establishments Industry Guide. BNA-SUTN MN 11.8.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Vending Machines

Taxable Effective July 1, 2017, taxable food and drinks sold from vending machines are subject to sales and use tax. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d) as amended by 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, First Special Session, Art. 3, §5, effective July 1, 2017 (removing “all food sold through vending machines” from the list of taxable food). Thus, the only taxable food sold through vending machines, snack boxes, and honor boxes is prepared food, soft drinks, candy, and dietary supplements. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d) as amended by 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, First Special Session, Art. 3, effective July 1, 2017 (removing “all food sold through vending machines” from the list of taxable food); Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 158; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102B. Taxable vending machine sales include: candy bars, honey roasted nuts, hot coffee or cocoa, soda pop, and soft drinks. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 158. Vending machine sales that are nontaxable include: bottled water, cookies, licorice, carton of milk, and muffins. Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 158. Prior to July 1, 2017, all food, not just taxable food, and drinks sold through vending machines were subject to sales and use tax. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3), as amended by 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, effective July 1, 2017; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 158; Minnesota Sales Tax Fact Sheet 102C; see Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d)(5). “Food sold through vending machines” is defined as food dispensed from a machine or other device that accepts payment including honor payments. Minn. Stat. §297A.61(34); see also Minnesota Automatic Merch. Council v. Salomone, 682 N.W.2d 557 (Minn. 2004). However, effective July 1, 2017, the defined term changes to “taxable food sold through vending machines.” “Taxable food” refers to the items of taxable food listed under Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d). 2017 Minn. H.F. 1, First Special Session, Art. 3, effective July 1, 2017, amending Minn. Stat. §297A.61(34) (defining “taxable food sold through vending machines”); and amending Minn. Stat. §297A.61(3)(d) (removing “all food sold through vending machines” from the list of taxable food). BNA-SUTN MN 11.9.
