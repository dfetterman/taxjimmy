Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, North Carolina is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, North Carolina Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.1). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that North Carolina was not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). North Carolina enacted 2003 N.C. H. 397 on June 30, 2003, and 2004 N.C. S. 1145 on August 2, 2004, to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. The bulk of these provisions took effect July 15, 2003, and, Aug. 15, 2004, respectively. 2003 N.C. H.B. 397, §45.1, et seq., effective July 15, 2003; 2004 N.C. S.B. 1145, §§ 18-20, effective August 2, 2004. The purpose of the agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration in order to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance through improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections, having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, having uniformity of major tax base definitions, having a central electronic registration system for all member states, simplifying state and local tax rates, having uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, simplifying administration of exemptions, having simplified tax returns, simplifying tax remittances, and protecting consumer privacy. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). North Carolina relieves certified service providers, and any seller registered under the agreement that contracts with a certified service provider, from tax liability to North Carolina and its local jurisdictions for 10 days after notification by the Secretary to conform with changes to the taxability matrix. N.C. Gen. Stat. §105-164.42L(b), as amended by 2016 N.C. S.B. 729, §3.17(a), effective Sept. 1, 2016; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, North Carolina (version 2018.0); User Guide: Online Taxability Matrix and Certificate of Compliance (Nov. 20, 2018). Streamlined Sales Tax Local Boundaries Database As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, North Carolina is required by Section 305(D) of the Agreement to maintain a database describing boundary changes for all local taxing jurisdictions. The database describes the boundaries using the Federal Information Processing Standards codes and includes the effective dates of the changes for sales and use tax purposes. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Sales Tax Boundary Databases; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018, Section 305. BNA-SUTN NC 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate/Project Member North Carolina is an associate and project member of the Multistate Tax Commission. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. As an associate and project member state, North Carolina participates in commission meetings and otherwise consults and cooperates with the commission and other member states, and participates in commission projects and programs. See Multistate Tax Commission, About the Multistate Tax Commission. BNA-SUTN NC 2.2.
