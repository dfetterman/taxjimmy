Topic: Real Property, Construction Contracts and Materials, Mobile and Manufactured Homes Fixtures, Classifying Property as Real or Personal General Rule for Classification

Depends Whether items of tangible personal property become a part of real property depends principally on the intention of the person making the annexation. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(1). If the individual's intention is to permanently and substantially improve land, a building, or other real property, the material will be considered to have become real property. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(2). However, if the intention of the annexation is for a temporary purpose, that is, for the enjoyment or use of the material as chattel or personalty, the material will be considered to retain its character as tangible personal property. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(2). An installed or annexed item that is an integral, necessary, and expected part of real property constitutes a permanent and substantial improvement to the real property. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(2). Machinery used in a production activity retains its character as tangible personal property without regard to the method or permanency of its annexation to real property. Farm equipment, a foundation in support of machinery and equipment used in a production activity, and any machinery, device, or equipment required for conformance with air or water pollution laws or regulations retain their character as tangible personal property. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(2). Factors to be considered in determining the intention of the party making the annexation are:• the nature of the article annexed; Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(4)(a). • the mode of annexation; Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(4)(b). • the purpose for which the article was annexed; Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(4)(c). and • the practicality and feasibility of removal of the annexed article. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(4)(d). Generally, counters, countertops, and cabinetry installed in commercial spaces will be treated as tangible personal property. Doors, windows, molding, built-ins, and kitchen cabinetry installed in residential or commercial spaces will be treated as realty. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(C)(5). BNA-SUTN MD 28.2.1.

Topic: Real Property, Construction Contracts and Materials, Mobile and Manufactured Homes Construction Materials and Contracts Construction Materials and Contracts, Generally

Nontaxable Charges for labor under a construction contract are not subject to sales or use tax, regardless of the form of the contract. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(m); Maryland Tax Tip 18. Contractors must pay tax on the materials and supplies that will be incorporated into real property. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(A); Maryland Tax Tip 18. Contractors are also liable for sales tax on purchases of machinery and tools used in completing construction contracts. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(F); Maryland Tax Tip 18. The incidence of tax is not changed by language in a real property construction contract or subcontract such as “tax included” or “tax exempt,”except with certain exceptions for non-profit organizations. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(B). The incidence of tax is also not changed by any form of payment under the real property construction contract or subcontract, whether lump-sum, cost-plus, time and material, or otherwise. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(B). Wall-to-wall carpeting always remains tangible personal property, regardless of how it is installed. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-101(k)(2)(ii). BNA-SUTN MD 28.3.1.

Topic: Real Property, Construction Contracts and Materials, Mobile and Manufactured Homes Contracts with Exempt Entities

Depends Contractors who purchase building materials to complete a construction project for which the final recipient is a private, nonprofit, charitable, educational, or religious organization or volunteer fire, ambulance, or rescue company may purchase such materials exempt using the organization's exemption certificate. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(D). To purchase the material exempt, the buyer must certify to the supplier, in a signed statement, that the material will be incorporated into the real property of the exempt organization. The buyer must include in the statement the name and exemption certificate number of the organization, and the date and identification of the contract for which the materials are purchased. No specific format for the statement is required nor are forms supplied by the comptroller. Buyers and suppliers may use any format that contains the required information. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(d). The following is an example of an acceptable format: To: (Name of Supplier) Reference: (Supplier's Invoice Number and Date) I hereby certify that all of the materials purchased on this order will be incorporated into the real property of (name of organization), exemption certificate (number), in accordance with my contract (number or other designation), dated (date), (Signature, Title and Date). Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(d). However, contractors must pay tax on purchases of materials for all other entities, regardless of the entity's tax status, including:• the state of Maryland and its political subdivisions; • the United States government and its agencies and instrumentalities; • diplomatic embassies; and • credit unions. Md. Regs. Code §03.06.01.19(E). Maryland has enacted a temporary exemption, from July 1, 2016, through June 30, 2026, for construction material or warehousing equipment, if the material or equipment is purchased by a person solely for use in a specified “target redevelopment area” in Baltimore County and the buyer provides the vendor with evidence of eligibility for the exemption issued by the comptroller. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(b), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. Maryland defines a “target redevelopment area” as any real property owned or leased by a person in Baltimore County that was:• previously owned at any time by Bethlehem Steel Corporation, or any of its subsidiaries; Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(a)(3)(i), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. and • as of January 1, 2016, the subject of an approved application for participation in a specified voluntary cleanup program under provisions of the Environment Article. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(a)(3)(ii), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. Maryland defines “construction material”as an item of tangible personal property that is used to construct or renovate a building, a structure, or an improvement on land and that typically loses its separate identity as personal property once incorporated into the real property. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(a)(2)(i), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. Construction material includes building materials, building systems equipment, landscaping materials, and supplies. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(a)(2)(ii), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. Maryland defines “warehousing equipment”as equipment used for material handling and storage, including racking systems, conveying systems, and computer systems and equipment. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. § 11-232(a)(4), as enacted by 2016 Md. H.B. 1533, effective July 1, 2016. BNA-SUTN MD 28.7.

Topic: Real Property, Construction Contracts and Materials, Mobile and Manufactured Homes Mobile and Manufactured Homes

Taxable Sales and use tax applies only to the sale of modular buildings and to the first sale of a manufactured home; however, the rate is imposed only on 60 percent of the taxable price. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(d); Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-213. A manufactured home is a structure that is transportable in one or more sections, eight body feet or more in width and 30 body feet or more in length, built on a permanent chassis, and designed to be used as a dwelling, with or without a permanent foundation, when connected to the required utilities. Md. Code Ann. Public Safety §12-301(g). Modular buildings are single-family or multifamily houses, apartment units, or commercial buildings, or permanent additions to single-family or multifamily houses, apartment units, or commercial buildings, comprising one or more sections that are:• intended to become real property; • primarily constructed at a location other than the permanent site at which they are to be assembled; • built to comply with the standards for industrialized buildings; and • shipped with most permanent components in place. Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(f)(1); Md. Code Ann. Tax-Gen. §11-104(f)(2). BNA-SUTN MD 28.8.
