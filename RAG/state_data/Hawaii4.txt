Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Taxable The gross receipts from the sales of aircraft are subject to the Hawaii general excise or use taxes unless an exemption applies. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-13(2); Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-2. Aircraft means any contrivance used or designed for navigation of or flight in the air. Haw. Regs. §19-31.1-1. “Aircraft”includes airplanes, helicopters, and gliders. Haw. Regs. §19-31.1-1. The leasing or renting of any aircraft or the keeping of any aircraft solely for leasing or renting to lessees or renters using the aircraft for commercial transportation of passengers and goods is exempt from gross receipts and use tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-1; Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24.3(11). The use or consumption of aircraft when the transfer to, or the acquisition by, the person using or consuming the aircraft, or the rental for the use of the aircraft, has actually been or actually is taxed under the general excise tax, is exempt from use tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-3(f). Temporary Presence Temporary use of property, not of a perishable or quickly consumable nature, such as aircraft, where the property is imported into Hawaii for temporary use and not intended to be and is not kept permanently in Hawaii, is exempt from use tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-1. BNA-SUTN HI 29.1.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Taxable Sales of motor vehicles are subject to Hawaii's general excise and use taxes. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-13(2); Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-2. Motor vehicle means any vehicle, motor vehicle, or truck, except for tractors, trailers, and amphibious vehicles. Haw. Rev. Stat. §437-1.1. Motor Vehicle Trade-ins Gross income does not include the sale price of an item accepted as partial payment on any new item sold, if the full sale price of the new item is included in the “gross income”or “gross proceeds of sales. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-3(b). Therefore, the value of a trade-in may be excluded from gross income and gross proceeds of sales. Under the use tax regulations and in the context of motor vehicles, landed cost is reduced by a trade-in allowance. Haw. Regs. §18-238-2(g)(2). Landed cost consists of the invoice price plus freight, insurance, custom duty, and any other charges incident to landing the motor vehicle in Hawaii, less:• trade-in allowance for old car; • any charges for license plates outside Hawaii; and • a depreciation allowance of 10 percent for normal use outside Hawaii, but this rate may be adjusted depending upon the mileage and condition of the car. Haw. Regs. §18-238-2(g)(2). The landed value of the motor vehicle does not include any retail sales tax paid to another state or local government. Haw. Regs. §18-238-2(g)(2). Motor Vehicle Leases Where a motor vehicle is customarily rented or leased for six months or less, it is presumed that the vehicle is subject to the rental motor vehicle surcharge tax. Haw. Regs. §18-251-2-03. See the Excise Tax Navigator at Hawaii 9.2 for more information regarding the rental motor vehicle surcharge tax. Complimentary Vehicles Hawaii imposes rental motor vehicle surcharge tax on motor vehicles provided in conjunction with public relations activities, promotional activities or get-away prize packages. Haw. Regs. §18-251-1-04(b). Example: Bigtime Car Co. participates in a visitor industry project to encourage travel agents to cultivate Hawaii as a vacation destination. Bigtime Car Co. provides six cars for a one-week familiarization tour of the islands. Result: All six of the cars are subject to the rental motor vehicle surcharge tax. Haw. Regs. §18-251-1-04(b). BNA-SUTN HI 29.3.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Railroad Rolling Stock, Interstate and Common Carrier Fuel

Taxable Hawaii statutes and regulations do not provide use-based exemptions for rolling stock fuel. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24 et seq.; Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-2; Haw. Regs. §18-237-13.01 et seq. However, amounts collected by distributors as fuel taxes on “liquid fuel” imposed by chapter 243 (Fuel Tax Law) and the amounts collected by such distributors as a fuel tax imposed by any act of the U.S. Congress are excluded from the general excise tax and use tax base. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24(8). BNA-SUTN HI 29.8.6.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Property and Passenger Transportation Property Transportation, General Rule

Depends Agricultural Commodities Transportation Amounts received from the loading, transportation, and unloading of agricultural commodities shipped for a producer or produce dealer on one island of Hawaii to a person or organization on another island of Hawaii are not subject to Hawaii's general excise tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24.3(1). This exemption was temporarily suspended from July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2013. 2011 Haw. S.B. 754; Hawaii Announcement No. 2013-04 (June 5, 2013). Loading and Unloading of Cargo The gross income or gross receipts received from loading or unloading cargo from ships, barges, vessels, or aircraft, whether or not the carriers travel between Hawaii and other states or countries or between the islands of Hawaii is not subject to Hawaii's general excise tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24.3(4)(A). This exemption was temporarily suspended from July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2013. 2011 Haw. S.B. 754; Hawaii Announcement No. 2013-04 (June 5, 2013). Amounts received from the following are also excluded from the general excise tax:• the transportation of pilots or governmental officials to ships, barges, or vessels offshore; • rigging gear; • checking freight and similar services; • standby charges; and • the use of moorings and running mooring lines. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24.3(4)(C). Tugboat Services Tugboat services are exempt from the general excise tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24.3(4)(B). The exemption includes pilotage fees performed within the state and the towage of ships, barges, or vessels in and out of state harbors, or from one pier to another. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-24.3(4)(B). This exemption was temporarily suspended from July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2013. 2011 Haw. S.B. 754; Hawaii Announcement No. 2013-04 (June 5, 2013). BNA-SUTN HI 29.9.7.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Property and Passenger Transportation Property Transportation, Moving Services

Taxable There is no statutory or regulatory exemption for moving services; therefore, the general excise tax applies to moving services. See Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-13. BNA-SUTN HI 29.9.8.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Parking and Valet

Nontaxable The state of Hawaii general excise tax does not apply to parking. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-13; Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-8.6(d). The county surcharge applies to the same base as the state general excise tax. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-13; Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-8.6(d). Therefore, the county surcharge does not apply to parking. For more information on parking taxes and other storage fees in Hawaii, see Excise Taxes Navigator, at Hawaii 9.6. BNA-SUTN HI 29.10.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Boat Slip and Mooring Fees

Taxable Tangible property and services are subject to the general excise and use tax, unless there is an exemption from the tax. There is no statutory or regulatory exemption for boat slip and mooring fees therefore, the general excise tax and use tax apply to boat slip and mooring fees. Haw. Rev. Stat. §237-13; Haw. Rev. Stat. §238-2 BNA-SUTN HI 29.11.
