State: Vermont



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Full Member Vermont became a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement on Jan. 1, 2007. Vermont Dept. of Taxes, Streamlined Sales Tax. Vermont enacted H.B. 480 on June 18, 2003, and H.B. 784, on May 27, 2004, in order to bring the state into compliance with the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Vermont (version 2018.0); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Vermont (version 2018.0); see also 2006 Vt. H. 843, enacted May 31, 2006, effective on the first day of the second quarter following the date of Vermont's membership in the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. The purpose of the Agreement is to simplify and modernize sales and use tax administration to substantially reduce the burden of tax compliance through improving state level administration of sales and use tax collections, having uniformity in the state and local tax bases, having uniformity of major tax base definitions, having a central electronic registration system for all member states, simplifying state and local tax rates, having uniform sourcing rules for all taxable transactions, simplifying administration of exemptions, having simplified tax returns, simplifying tax remittances, and protecting consumer privacy. See Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, About Us; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). BNA-SUTN VT 2.1.1.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Vermont is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Certificate of Compliance, Vermont (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Vermont is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN VT 2.1.2.

Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Associate Member Vermont is an associate member of the Multistate Tax Commission. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. Associate members of the Multistate Tax Commission are states that participate in Commission meetings, and consult and cooperate with the Commission and its other member states, or participate in Commission programs or projects as project members. Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. The Multistate Tax Commission is an intergovernmental state tax agency that works on behalf of states and taxpayers to equitably and efficiently administer tax laws that apply to multistate and multinational enterprises. The goal of the Commission is to facilitate the proper determination of State and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers, including the equitable apportionment of tax bases and settlement of apportionment disputes, promoting uniformity or compatibility in significant components of tax systems, facilitating taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration, and avoiding duplicative taxation. Multistate Tax Commission, About the Multistate Tax Commission. BNA-SUTN VT 2.2.
