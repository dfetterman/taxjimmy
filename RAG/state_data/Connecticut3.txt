State: Connecticut



Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Fuel Biodiesel

Taxable Connecticut imposes sales and use tax on sales of tangible personal property. Biodiesel is tangible personal property, thus those transactions are taxable. See Conn. Gen. Assembly, OLR Research Report (Aug. 21, 2008); Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412. Connecticut has four state taxes that apply to some or all types of fuel (petroleum fuels, electricity, and natural gas) or petroleum products. These taxes are the (1) sales tax, (2) motor vehicle fuels tax, (3) petroleum products gross earnings tax, and (4) public service company tax. Conn. Gen. Assembly, OLR Research Report (Aug. 21, 2008). For more information about the other taxes, see Excise Taxes Navigator, at Connecticut 8. BNA-SUTN CT 30.1.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Electricity

Depends The sale, furnishing, or service of electricity is not subject to tax when delivered to consumers through mains, lines, pipes, or bottles if for use in any residential dwelling; or directly in agricultural production, or fabrication of a finished product to be sold, or in an industrial manufacturing plant. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(A)(i); Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(A)(ii). However, this exemption only applies to a metered building, location, or premises where not less than 75 percent of the electricity consumption is used for the purpose of production, fabrication, or manufacturing. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(A). Electricity Not Otherwise Exempt The sale or furnishing of electricity that is not otherwise exempt is not subject to tax with respect to the portion of the charges for any month of service that does not exceed $150. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(D). BNA-SUTN CT 30.2.1.

Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Utilities Natural Gas

Depends The sale, furnishing, or service of gas, including bottled gas, when delivered to consumers through mains, lines, pipes, or bottles is not subject to tax if for use in any residential dwelling; or directly in agricultural production, or fabrication of a finished product to be sold, or in an industrial manufacturing plant. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(A)(i); Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(A)(ii). However, this exemption only applies to a metered building, location, or premises where not less than 75 percent of the gas, including bottled gas, is used for the purpose of production, fabrication, or manufacturing. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(3)(A)(ii). BNA-SUTN CT 30.2.2.

Exempt Heating Fuel Sales of fuel used for heating purposes in any residential dwelling; premises utilized directly in agricultural production or fabrication; or certain industrial manufacturing plants are exempt from sales and use tax. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(16). Ice Storage Systems for Cooling Ice storage systems used for cooling, including equipment related to such systems, and services relating to the installation of such systems by a utility ratepayer who is billed by such utility on a time-of-service metering basis are not taxable. Conn. Gen. Topic: Utilities, Fuel, Mining and Natural Resource Extraction, Production, Sale Alternative Energy

Exempt Connecticut exempts from sales and use tax sales and installation of solar energy electricity generating systems and passive or active solar water or space heating systems. Connecticut also exempts sales of geothermal resource systems. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(117)(A); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2010(6); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2007(7). Further, Connecticut exempts sales of equipment related to these systems and services relating to installing the systems. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(117)(A); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2010(6); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2007(7). Connecticut also exempts sales and use of machinery, equipment, tools, materials, supplies and fuel used directly in the renewable energy and clean energy technology industries. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(117)(B); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2010(6); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2007(7). Connecticut defines the term “renewable energy and clean energy technology industries” as industries that apply technologies to produce, improve or develop solar energy electricity generating systems, passive or active solar water or space heating systems, geothermal resource systems and wind power electric generation systems. It also includes equipment related to those systems. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(117)(B); Connecticut Special Notice SN 2010(6). Services or Tangible Personal Property Used to Operate a Solid Waste-to-Energy Facility Effective July 1, 2011, Connecticut repealed its exemption for the sale or use of any services or tangible personal property to be incorporated into, used, or otherwise consumed in the operation of a solid waste-to-energy facility. Beginning July 1, 2011, those transactions and activities are taxable. Conn. Gen. Stat. §12-412(95); 2011 Conn. Pub. Acts 11-6 §166, as enacted by 2011 Conn. S.B. 1239. BNA-SUTN CT 30.5.
