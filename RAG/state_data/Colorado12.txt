Topic: Interstate Agreements Multistate Tax Commission

Compact Member Colorado is a compact member of the Multistate Tax Commission and has enacted the provisions of the Multistate Tax Compact into state law. Colo. Rev. Stat. §24-60-1301;Multistate Tax Commission, Member States. The purposes of the Multistate Tax Compact are to:• facilitate proper determination of state and local tax liability of multistate taxpayers, including equitable apportionment of tax bases and settlement of apportionment disputes; • promote uniformity or compatibility in significant components of tax systems; • facilitate taxpayer convenience and compliance in the filing of tax returns and in other phases of tax administration; and • avoid duplicative taxation. Multistate Tax Commission, Multistate Tax Compact. Exemption Certificates Colorado permits the use of exemption certificates from other jurisdictions. Colo. Rev. Stat. §24-60-1301; Colorado FYI Sales 3; 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-105.3. A seller may accept a resale license, exemption certificate, or other authorized documentation from another issuing jurisdiction. Sellers may also have an out-of-state purchaser complete an exemption certificate, affidavit of exempt sale, or the Multistate Tax Commission's Exemption Certificate. 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-105.3(a)(ii). A Colorado seller has the burden of demonstrating to the Department of Revenue that the purchaser was eligible for the exemption. 39 Colo. Code Regs. §26-105.3(c)(i). BNA-SUTN CO 2.2.
