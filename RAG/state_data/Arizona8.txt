State: Arizona



Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients General Rule

Exempt Food items sold by a qualified retailer and intended for human consumption, other than items sold for consumption on the premises, are exempt from the Arizona transaction privilege tax. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A). Certain localities, such as Phoenix, do not allow an exemption for food for home consumption. Phoenix Tax Code §14-120;Phoenix Tax Code §14-465; Phoenix Tax Code §14-610. For purposes of the exemption, a “qualified retailer” includes:• retailers who operate an eligible grocery business; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(1). • retailers who sell food in a similar manner as an eligible grocery business, but whose primary business is not the sale of food (such as department stores, drug stores, and gas stations); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(2); Arizona Publication No. 575. • retailers who are not eligible to participate in the food stamp program and who sell food without any area for consumption on the premises (such as health food stores and outlets retailing soda and other beverages); Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(3); Arizona Publication No. 575; see also Arizona Private Taxpayer Ruling LR15-002 (Feb. 24, 2015) (retailers that ship food sold via phone or internet order to residential customers qualify as retailers selling food without providing any facilities for on-premises consumption). • retailers who operate a delicatessen business from a counter that is separate from where taxable sales are conducted or who use a cash register which can separate taxable and exempt sales; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(4). • retailers who use pushcarts; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(5), as amended by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2371, effective Aug. 2, 2018. • vending machines and other automatic retailers; Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(6). and • prior to August 2, 2018, retailers who use mobile facilities, motor vehicles, or other such conveyances. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(5), as amended by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2371, effective Aug. 2, 2018. An “eligible grocery business” is an establishment that sells food and is eligible to participate in the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5101(1), as amended by 2013 Ariz. S.B. 1179, effective Sept. 13, 2013. BNA-SUTN AZ 11.1.1.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Food and Food Ingredients Definition of 'Food and Food Ingredients'

Defined “Tax-exempt foods” are generally items intended for home consumption which are eligible to be purchased with federal food coupons. Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(15)(a); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(15)(b). Tax-exempt food, at the state level, includes:• bread and flour products; • candy; • cereal; • cocoa; • coffee; • eggs; • meat; • fish; • frozen foods; • fruit and fruit products; • packaged ice cream products; • ice cubes and bottled water including carbonated and mineral water; • vegetables and vegetable products; • sugar, sugar products and substitutes; • butter, oleomargarine, shortening and cooking oils; • milk and milk products; • tea; • spices, condiments, extracts and food colorings; • soft drinks and soda (including bottles on which a deposit is required to be paid); • any ready-to-drink, nonalcoholic beverage in a closed or sealed bottle, can, or carton that is intended for human consumption at home; • dietary substitutes; and • purchases of seed and plants for use in gardens to produce food items for personal consumption. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5106(D), as amended by 2013 Ariz. S.B. 1179, effective Sept. 13, 2013; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(15)(a); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(15(c). However, some of the items listed above may be taxable at the local level. Phoenix Tax Code §14-120;Phoenix Tax Code §14-465; Phoenix Tax Code §14-610. Effective June 19, 2018, if a city, town, or other taxing jurisdiction imposes a transaction privilege, sales, use, franchise, or other similar tax or fee on the sale of food items intended for human consumption at home, the tax must be applied uniformly with respect to all food items. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-6015(A)(1), as added by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2484, effective June 19, 2018. Also effective June 19, 2018, if a city, town, or other taxing jurisdiction imposes a transaction privilege, sales, use, franchise, or other similar tax or fee on the sale of food items intended for human consumption on the premises, the tax must be applied uniformly with respect to all food items. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-6015(A)(2), as added by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2484, effective June 19, 2018. Tax-exempt food does not include:• food for consumption on the premises; • alcoholic beverages; • tobacco; • medicines;or • dietary supplements. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5106(C), as amended by 2013 Ariz. S.B. 1179, effective Sept. 13, 2013; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(14). Each year the Department of Revenue publishes a list of food items which are exempt if purchased from the above retailers. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5103(1). “Food” is defined as any item intended for human consumption. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5101(3). Food items must fall within the Department of Revenue's guidelines to be tax-exempt. Under these guidelines, tax-exempt foods are generally those items of food intended for home consumption which, if purchased from an eligible grocery business, would be eligible as of Jan. 1, 1979, to be purchased with USDA food coupons. The Department of Revenue has explicitly stated that the guidelines provided by the supplemental nutrition assistance program(“SNAP”) established under the Food and Nutrition Act of 2008 do not apply. See Arizona Publication No. 575. BNA-SUTN AZ 11.1.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Prepared Food Definition of 'Prepared Food'

Defined “Food for consumption on the premises” means hot prepared food, including items or ingredients of food which are prepared and sold or are intended to be sold in a heated condition including a combination of hot and cold food items or ingredients if a single price is charged by the retailer. Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(i). “Food for consumption on the premises” also includes:• hot or cold sandwiches; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(ii). • food served by an attendant to be eaten at tables, chairs, benches, booths, stools, counters and within parking areas (for in-car consumption); Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(iii). • food served with trays, glasses, dishes or other tableware; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(iv). • beverages sold in cups, glasses or open containers; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(v). • food sold by caterers; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(vi). and • food sold within theaters, fairs, amusement parks, bowling alleys, athletic events, and other businesses which charge admission, entrance or cover fees. Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(8)(a)(vii). BNA-SUTN AZ 11.2.2.

Topic: Food and Food Products, Eating Establishments Mobile Food Vendors

Depends Prior to August 2, 2018, transaction privilege tax does not apply to the gross proceeds of sales or gross income from sales of food for off-premises consumption by a retailer who is a street or sidewalk vendor and who uses a pushcart, mobile facility, motor vehicle or other such conveyance. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(5), as amended by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2371, effective Aug. 2, 2018; Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(12)(b)(v), as amended by 25 Ariz. Admin. Reg. issue 6, (Feb. 8, 2019), effective Jan. 14, 2019 (removing certain mobile food vendors from the list of retailers permitted to sell tax exempt food). Such retailers include:• snack mobiles; • chuck wagons; and • mobile hot dog stands. Ariz. Admin. Code 15-5-1860(12)(b)(v), as amended by 25 Ariz. Admin. Reg. issue 6, (Feb. 8, 2019), effective Jan. 14, 2019 (removing certain mobile food vendors from the list of retailers permitted to sell tax exempt food). Effective August 2, 2018, this exemption no longer applies to vendors who use a mobile facility, motor vehicle, or other such conveyance, but continues to apply to vendors who make sales using a pushcart. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5102(A)(5), as amended by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2371, effective Aug. 2, 2018. Effective August 2, 2018, the business of operating mobile food units is taxable under the restaurant classification. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-5074(A), as amended by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2371, effective Aug. 2, 2018. “Mobile food unit” means a food establishment that is licensed by the state, that is readily movable, and that dispenses food or beverages for immediate service and consumption and other incidental retail items from any vehicle. Ariz. Rev. Stat. Ann. §42-6015(C)(1), as added by 2018 Ariz. H.B. 2371, effective Aug. 2, 2018. BNA-SUTN AZ 11.7.
