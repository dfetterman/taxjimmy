State: Wisconsin



Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Tangible Personal Property

Depends Sales of tangible personal property may be subject to origin-based sourcing or destination-based sourcing depending on the circumstances of the sale. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(1). In general, the location of a sale is determined as follows. First, if a purchaser receives taxable property or services at a seller's business location, the sale is sourced to that business location. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(1). Second, if a purchaser does not receive taxable property or services at a seller's business location, the sale is sourced to the location where the purchaser receives taxable property or services. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(2). Third, if the location of a sale cannot be determined under the above rules, the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address as indicated by the seller's business records. Wis. Stat. §77.552(1)(b)(3). Fourth, if the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address as obtained during the consummation of the sale, such as the address indicated on the purchaser's payment instrument. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(4). If the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, the sale is sourced as follows:• If tangible personal property or other taxable property is sold, the sale of such property is sourced to the location from which the property is shipped. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(5)(a). • If a digital good or computer software delivered electronically is sold, the sale is sourced to the location from which the digital good or computer software was first available for transmission by the seller, not including any location that merely provided the digital transfer of the product sold. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(5)(b). • If a service is sold, the sale is sourced to the location from which the service was provided. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(5)(c). Direct Mail Sales In the case of direct mail sales, the sale is sourced to the location from which the direct mail is shipped, unless the purchaser provides the seller with a direct pay permit, an exemption certificate claiming direct mail, or information indicating the appropriate taxing jurisdiction that is the ultimate destination of the property purchased via direct mail. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(c). BNA-SUTN WI 4.1.1.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Services

Depends Sales of tangible taxable services may subject to origin-based sourcing or destination-based sourcing depending on the circumstances of the sale. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(1). In general, the location of a sale is determined as follows:• if the purchaser receives taxable services at a seller's business location, the sale is sourced to that business location;. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(1). • if a purchaser does not receive taxable services at a seller's business location, the sale is sourced to the location where the purchaser receives taxable services; Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(2). • if the location of a sale cannot be determined under the above rules, the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address as indicated by the seller's business records; Wis. Stat. §77.552(1)(b)(3). and • if the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, the sale is sourced to the purchaser's address as obtained during the consummation of the sale, such as the address indicated on the purchaser's payment instrument. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(4). If the location of a sale cannot be sourced under any of the above rules, the sale is sourced the location from which the service was provided. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(5)(c). Telecommunications Services and Ancillary Services Sales of telecommunications services are sourced according to the following rules:• telecommunications services sold on a call-by-call basis are generally sourced to the taxing jurisdiction where the call originates and terminates, if the call originates and terminates in the same jurisdiction, or to the taxing jurisdiction where the service address is located and where the call either originates or terminates; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(b). • telecommunications services sold on a basis other than a call-by-call basis are generally sourced to the customer's place of primary use;. Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(c). • mobile telecommunications services, other than sales of air-to-ground radiotelephone service or prepaid calling service, are sourced to the customer's place of primary use; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(d). • postpaid calling services are sourced to the location where the signal of the telecommunications service originates, as first identified by the seller's telecommunications system or, if the signal is not transmitted by the seller's telecommunications system, as identified by information that the seller received from the seller's service provider; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(e). • prepaid calling services or prepaid wireless calling services are sourced according to the location determined under the general sourcing rules of Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b), except that where the location of prepaid wireless calling service cannot be determined according to the sourcing rules of Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(b)(1) through (b)(4), the sale of the prepaid wireless calling service is sourced either to the location, as determined by the seller, associated with the mobile telephone number for which the service is provided or to the location from which the service is provided; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(f). • internet access services are sourced to the customer's place of primary use; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(h). and • ancillary services are sourced to the customer's place of primary use. Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(i). Sales of private communication services are sourced according to the following rules:• private communication services sold for a separate charge related to a customer channel termination point are sourced to the location of the customer channel termination point; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(g)(1). • private communication services in which all customer channel termination points are located entirely in one taxing jurisdiction are sourced to the taxing jurisdiction in which the customer channel termination points are located; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(g)(2). • private communication services that represents segments of a communications channel between two customer channel termination points that are located in different taxing jurisdictions are sourced to an equal percentage in both such jurisdictions, if the segments are charged separately; Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(g)(3). and • private communication service for segments of a communication channel that is located in more than one taxing jurisdiction is sourced to each such jurisdiction in a percentage determined by dividing the number of customer channel termination points in that jurisdiction by the number of customer channel termination points in all jurisdictions where segments of the communications channel are located, if the segments are not charged separately. Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(g)(4). If the location of a customer's service address, channel termination point, or place of primary use is not known, the location where the seller receives or hands off the signal will be deemed to be the customer's service address, channel termination point, or place of primary use, for purposes of the above telecommunications and ancillary services sourcing rules. Wis. Stat. §77.522(4)(j). BNA-SUTN WI 4.1.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Leases and Rentals

Taxable Except as provided below for leases and rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, and aircraft, not constituting “transportation equipment,” the first payment on the lease or rental of taxable property is sourced to the location determined under the above general rules. Wis. Stat. §77.522(3)(a). Subsequent periodic payments on the lease or rental, if any, are sourced to the property's primary location as indicated by an address that is provided by the lessee and that is available in records that the lessor maintains in the ordinary course of business. Wis. Stat. §77.522(3)(a). Leases and rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, and aircraft, not constituting “transportation equipment”, that require only one payment, are sourced to the location where the lessee receives the property. This is generally the lessor or dealer's location. Wis. Stat. §77.522(3)(b); Wisconsin News for Tax Professionals – Oct. 31, 2014. Leases and rentals of motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, and aircraft, not constituting “transportation equipment”, that require multiple payments are sourced to the primary location of such motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers, or aircraft as indicated by an address for the property that is provided by the lessee and that is available in records that the lessor maintains in the ordinary course of the lessor's business. Wis. Stat. §77.522(3)(b). “Transportation equipment” includes:• locomotives and railcars that are used to carry persons or property in interstate commerce; • trucks and truck tractors that have a gross vehicle weight rating of 10,001 pounds or greater, trailers, semi-trailers, and passenger buses, if such vehicles are registered under the international registration plan and operated under the authority of a carrier that is authorized by the federal government to carry persons or property in interstate commerce; • aircraft operated by air carriers authorized by the federal government or a foreign authority to carry persons or property in interstate or foreign commerce; and • containers designed for use on any of the transportation equipment described above and component parts attached to such transportation equipment. Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(a)(2). BNA-SUTN WI 4.1.3.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Sourcing Rules Local Sourcing

Same as State The local counties are subject to the same sourcing rules as the state. Wis. Stat. §77.72;Wis. Stat. §77.522(1)(a)(2). Premier Resort Area Tax A sale, license, lease, or rental is sourced where the purchaser receives the product or makes first use of a service. Wisconsin Publication 403 (Aug. 1, 2016). “Receive”means taking possession of the product. Receive does not include a shipping company taking possession of the product on a purchaser's behalf. Wisconsin Publication 403 (Aug. 1, 2016). If the purchaser receives the product at the seller's business location, the sale is sourced to that business location. Wisconsin Publication 403 (Aug. 1, 2016). If the sale cannot be sourced to the seller's location, the sale is sourced to the location where the purchaser, or the purchaser's designated donee, receives the product, including the location indicated by the instructions known to the seller for delivery to the purchaser or the purchaser's designated donee. Wisconsin Publication 403 (Aug. 1, 2016). If the sale cannot be sourced to the seller's location nor the delivery address, the sale is sourced to the purchaser's billing address, purchaser's other address, or the location where the item is shipped from. Wisconsin Publication 403 (Aug. 1, 2016). The sourcing of services for purposes of the premier resort area tax is the same as for Wisconsin county sales tax purposes. Wisconsin Publication 403 (Aug. 1, 2016). BNA-SUTN WI 4.1.4.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Credit for Tax Paid to Another Jurisdiction

Yes In Wisconsin, if a purchase, rental or lease of tangible personal property, or items, property, or service subject to use tax in Wisconsin is subject to a sales tax by another state in which the purchase was made, the amount of sales tax paid to the other state shall be applied as a credit against and deducted from the tax. Wis. Stat. §77.53(16); Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, Wisconsin (version 2018.0). A credit is also allowed for sales taxes paid to the District of Columbia and the commonwealth of Puerto Rico. However, no such credit is allowed for taxes paid to the several territories organized by congress, such as Guam. Wis. Stat. §77.53(16); Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 197. If the amount of tax paid to the other jurisdiction is less than the amount of Wisconsin tax due, the difference must be paid as Wisconsin tax. However, there is no refund or credit allowed for tax paid to the other jurisdiction in excess of Wisconsin tax due. Wis. Stat. §77.53(16); Wisconsin Tax Bulletin No. 197. However, a credit may not be applied against and deducted from a sales tax paid on the purchase of advertising and promotional direct mail, if the advertising and promotional direct mail purchaser did not provide to the seller a direct pay permit, an exemption certificate claiming advertising and promotional direct mail, or other information that indicates the appropriate taxing jurisdiction to which the advertising and promotional direct mail is delivered to the ultimate recipients. Wis. Stat. §77.53(16); see also Wisconsin Publication 234. Wisconsin also provides a credit for any person subject to sales tax on telecommunications services that terminate in Wisconsin and who has paid a similar tax on the same services to another state. The taxpayer may reduce the amount of tax due to Wisconsin by an amount equal to the tax paid to another state on the telecommunications services or by the amount due in Wisconsin on the services, whichever is less. Wis. Stat. §77.525. BNA-SUTN WI 4.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property First Used Out-of-state

Depends Businesses and individuals owe Wisconsin use tax if the business or individual purchases taxable products or services in another state or outside the United States, and subsequently brings the product, or the property on which a taxable service was performed, into Wisconsin. Wisconsin Fact Sheet 2104. BNA-SUTN WI 4.3.1.

Taxable In Wisconsin, storage includes any keeping or retention in this state of taxable tangible personal property or items, property, or goods purchased from a retailer for any purpose except sale in the regular course of business. Wis. Stat. §77.51(18); Wisconsin Publication 203 BNA-SUTN WI 4.3.2.

Topic: Jurisdiction and Sourcing Rules Temporary Presence, In-Transit Property Tangible Personal Property Purchased for Use Out-of-state

Taxable Wisconsin purchasers who receive deliveries of products from out-of-state sellers or who purchase products out-of-state for storage, use, or consumption in Wisconsin must, unless they qualify for an exemption, report and pay to this state the tax based upon the sales price of such products even though the products may subsequently be shipped outside Wisconsin. Wis. Stat. §77.51(18); Wisconsin Publication 203 However, an exemption exists for aircraft purchased for use out-of-state.
