Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Repair Services

Taxable Repair services for aircraft used intrastate or non-commercially are subject to sales tax unless an exemption applies. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-205(c)(4) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §75, effective Jan. 1, 2008, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.54(1). Labor, parts, service call charges, any minimum charges, and the cost of all parts supplied to customers are cost components to be included in the tax base. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(79)(A) (”sales price” means total amount for which a taxable service is sold, without deduction for cost of materials used, labor or service costs, or any other expense) (effective July 1, 2021, the definition for “sales price” is located in Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-102(81)(A). Repair services include occasions when no new parts are involved in the transaction and also when the customer provides any or all of the parts needed for the repair work. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.54(1). Dealers performing repair services and repairs of tangible personal property may purchase repair parts, repair services, and containers, labels, etc., without tax by using a certificate of resale. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.54(3). However, all equipment, energy, other supplies, and taxable services are subject to sales or use tax and the tax must be paid to the vendor. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.54(3). Repair services and repairs of tangible personal property include any one or all of the following for a user and consumer:• work done to preserve or restore to or near the original condition made necessary by wear, normal use, wastage, injury, decay, partial destruction, or dilapidation; • mending, correction, or adjustment made for any defect or defective portion; • alterations; • refinishing; • any cleaning that is a necessary part of any repair work; • service calls where any repair work is done or contemplated; and • changes in the size, shape, or content of the property. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.54(2). Repair service labor includes all labor in connection with repairing, maintaining, overhauling, rebuilding, or modifying aircraft engine equipment or aircraft mainframes, including any necessary test or inspection. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(c)(2)(C) as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Sales and use taxes do not apply to charges for repair and refurbishment services performed in Tennessee on helicopters and helicopter components and parts that have their situs outside Tennessee and are removed from Tennessee within 15 days after repair and refurbishment services are completed. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(h)(1)(B) as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Sales and use taxes do not apply to charges for repair and refurbishment services performed in Tennessee on airplanes and airplane components and parts that have their situs outside Tennessee and are removed from Tennessee within 30 days after the services are completed. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(h)(1)(D) as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Repair and refurbishment services are exempt from sales and use tax if the services are performed by:• the registered owner of a “supplemental type certificate” issued by the FAA; or • an authorized service facility designated by an original equipment manufacturer for service of aircraft qualifying as “transport category aircraft”under 14 CFR, parts 25, 29, 91, and 121. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(h)(1)(D) as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). “Repair and refurbishment services”includes, but is not limited to, modifications, conversions, and installations. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(h)(1)(B) as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). This exemption does not extend to fuel and other petroleum products or to shop equipment and tools. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-302(c), as amended by 2015 Tenn. H.B. 467, effective July 1, 2015; Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. Large Aircraft Repair and Refurbishment Effective July 1, 2015, Tennessee exempts from the sales and use tax, all charges for repair and refurbishment service labor performed with respect to large aircraft mainframes, large aircraft engine equipment and large aircraft accessories, when the repair services on the mainframes, equipment and accessories are initiated, contracted, performed or completed in or by an authorized large aircraft service facility within Tennessee. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(k)(1), as amended by 2015 Tenn. H.B. 467, effective July 1, 2015. Aircraft Storage Charges for storing airplanes are not subject to tax. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.04(2). BNA-SUTN TN 29.1.3.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Fuel

Taxable Prior to July 1, 2021, sales and use tax applies to the sale, use, consumption, distribution, and storage of aviation fuel that is actually used in the operation of airplane or aircraft motors. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-217 (Effective July 1, 2021, this provision will be repealed by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, effective date extended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931). Effective July 1, 2021, the sales and use tax no longer applies to the sale, use, or consumption of aviation fuels that is actually used in the operation of airplane or aircraft motors, instead the special user privilege excise tax will apply. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-386; Tenn. Code Ann. §67-4-2701. Prior to July 1, 2021, the amount of tax imposed and remitted on a person's purchase, use, consumption, or storage of aviation fuel may not exceed the following:• $21,375,000 for the period of July 1, 2015 through June 30, 2016; • $17,750,000 for the period of July 1, 2016 through June 30, 2017; • $14,125,000 for the period of July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018; and • $10,500,000 for any tax year occurring on or after July 1, 2018. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-217(b)(1), as amended by 2015 Tenn. S.B. 982, effective July 1, 2015. Gasoline Tennessee exempts from sales and use tax gasoline on which a privilege tax per gallon is paid and not refunded. However, pre-mixed engine fuel containing gasoline and oil, produced for use in two-cycle engines and not for use in the propulsion of an aircraft, vessel or any other vehicle, that is sold in containers of one gallon or less, is subject to sales and use tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-329(a)(1). Liquefied Gas and Compressed Natural Gas Tennessee exempts from sales and use tax, liquefied gas and compressed natural gas taxed as an alternative fuel under Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-1101 et. seq. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-329(a)(5), as amended by 2015 Tenn. H.B. 879, effective July 1, 2016; see also Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. “Liquefied gas” is defined as “all combustible gases that exist in the gaseous state at sixty degrees Fahrenheit and at a pressure of fourteen and seven tenths pounds per square inch (14.7 p.s.i.) absolute, but does not include gasoline or diesel fuel or compressed natural gas.” Tenn. Code Ann. §67-3-1101(3). Motor Fuel Tennessee exempts motor fuel subject to tax under title 67, chapter 3, part 2. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-329(a)(2). “Motor fuel” is defined as “gasoline, diesel fuel and blended fuel.” Tenn. Code Ann. §67-3-103(47). For more information on any applicable fuel excise taxes, see the Excise Tax Navigator, at Tennessee Section 8.1. BNA-SUTN TN 29.1.6.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Aircraft, Interstate and Common Carrier Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Exempt Tennessee exempts from sales or use tax, the sale, use, or storage of aircraft owned or leased by commercial interstate or international air carriers for use principally in interstate or international commerce. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-302(a). Tennessee exempts from sales and use tax all sales and purchases of large aircraft and related equipment, and their use, storage, or consumption within Tennessee following the sale and purchase, if the large aircraft and related equipment have or are intended to have a situs outside of Tennessee, and if the large aircraft and related equipment are in and remain within Tennessee following the sale and purchase solely for repair and refurbishment services by an authorized large aircraft service facility, and are removed from Tennessee within 15 days after the repair and refurbishment services are completed. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-313(h)(1)(A) as amended by 2015 Tenn. S.B. 847, effective Jan. 1, 2016. Code Ann. §67-6-313(h)(1)(A), as amended by 2015 Tenn. S.B. 847, effective Jan. 1, 2016. Tangible Personal Property Used by Common Carriers Common carriers pay sales tax at a reduced state rate of 3.75 percent and a reduced local rate of 1.5 percent on purchases of tangible personal property made in Tennessee but used outside the state. Tenn. Code Ann.§67-6-219; Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-702(e); Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. Common carriers must apply for and receive a certificate from the department of revenue that can be presented to Tennessee suppliers when making purchases. Common carriers will be responsible for payment of the Tennessee state and local sales tax on qualifying purchases to the department of revenue. The reduced rate of sales tax does not apply to purchases of food or fuel. If the common carrier pays the reduced rate, but subsequently uses the property inside Tennessee, the common carrier will be liable for Tennessee state and local sales tax at the full rates. The common carrier will also be responsible for the full rates of state and local taxes if the common carrier fails to maintain records to document that the purchases were in fact used outside the state. Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide—September 2018. Code Ann. §67-6-329(a)(17). Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-202 as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021); Tenn. Code Ann. §42-3-102 or Tenn. Code Ann. §42-4-103. This exemption does not apply to subleases by a lessee. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-302(b). BNA-SUTN TN 29.2.1.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Motor Vehicles, Intrastate or Non-Commercial Use Purchase, Lease, or Rental Transactions

Taxable Sales and leases of motor vehicles are subject to sales and use tax, unless an exemption applies. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-202 as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Tennessee imposes sales and use tax on the full amount charged for an automobile, trailer or other motor vehicle. The full amount charged includes labor charges for preparing a motor vehicle for sale, freight, and federal excise tax. Sales and use tax also applies to the full amount charged for accessories or parts sold with or installed on a motor vehicle, including associated labor, and to repair labor involved in installing, replacing or repairing motor vehicle parts. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.03; Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). When a trade-in is taken as credit or part payment on a sale, sales and use tax is paid on the difference between the sales price and any credit given. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-510(a); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-1-.02; Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). A trade-in vehicle must be titled in the name of the person trading it in on the purchase of another vehicle. Trade-ins must be of a like kind and character as the item purchased. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. §1320-5-1-.02(2); Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). For more information about trade-ins, see Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Tennessee 6.5. When a manufacturer's rebate is offered, sales tax is assessed on the full sales price before the application of the rebate. Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). For more information about trade-ins, see Sales and Use Tax Navigator, at Tennessee 6.5. Occasional and Isolated Sales of Motor Vehicles Casual and isolated sales of motor vehicles that are required to be registered by Tennessee or the U.S. government are subject to tax. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.09(1). Exemptions Individuals relocating to Tennessee on a bona fide change of residence can import a personal vehicle into the state without payment of use tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-210(b); Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). This exemption does not apply to motor vehicles of businesses relocating to Tennessee. Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). Further, fees paid to states to register a motor vehicle are not sales and use taxes. Thus, these fees may not be claimed as a credit for sales and use tax paid to another state. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.91(2). Military Members and Veterans Tennessee also exempts sales of motor vehicles to certain members of the armed forces engaged in active full time military service in Tennessee. An exemption is also allowed for active duty members stationed in a combat zone, although the exemption expires 90 days after release from the combat zone. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-303; Tennessee Sales and Use Tax Guide; Tennessee County Clerk's Sales and Use Tax Guide for Motor Vehicle and Boat Transactions (September 2017). Tennessee provides sales and use tax exemptions for certain U.S. military service members for their purchase and use of certain motor vehicles and adaptive equipment. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-303(a); Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-353 ; Tennessee Military Members and Veterans Tax Guide. Motor vehicles that are registered in Tennessee are exempt from sales and use tax if sold to:• a uniformed service member in active military service of the United States who is stationed in Tennessee or at a military reservation located partially within Tennessee under orders of the member's branch of service; • a Tennessee National Guard member, or a uniformed service reserve member of the United States, who is a participant in the active guard and reserve program(AGR) and is stationed in Tennessee or at a military reservation located partially within Tennessee under orders of the member's branch of service; or • a Tennessee National Guard member, or a uniformed service reserve member of the United States, who has been called into active military service of the United States and is stationed in a combat zone. S.B. 2223, §75, effective Jan. 1, 2008, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). BNA-SUTN TN 29.9.8.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Parking and Valet

Depends Charges for services rendered by persons operating or conducting a garage, parking lot or other place of business for the purpose of parking or storing motor vehicles are subject to tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-205(c)(2); Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.104(1); see also Tennessee Dept. of Rev., What Services are Subject to Sales and Use Tax in Tennessee (Jan. 6, 2017). Sales tax is not imposed on charges for parking services made by the state and political divisions for on-street parking space or when the parking garage is unattended and charges are collected by parking meters. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-205(c)(2) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §75, effective Jan. 1, 2008, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Further, parking privileges sold by colleges, universities, technical institutes or state colleges of applied technology, to students at those institutions, are exempt from tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-329(a)(7). BNA-SUTN TN 29.10.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Boat Slip and Mooring Fees

Exempt Boat slip and mooring fees are not taxable because storage services are not enumerated in the sales and use tax statutes. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-205(c)(5) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §75, effective Jan. 1, 2008, as amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021). Tennessee specifies that charges for storing motor boats are not subject to tax. Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.104(2). BNA-SUTN TN 29.11.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Car Wash

Depends Effective April 18, 2019, charges for car washing services by a car wash facility are subject to tax unless the customer remains in control of the vehicle and most of the washing is completed by the customer or automated equipment. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-205(c)(5) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §75, effective Jan. 1, 2008, and amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021), and 2019 Tenn. H.B. 84, effective April 18, 2019; Tennessee Notice No. 19-12 (June 2019). Prior to April 18, 2019, such charges are taxable. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-205(c)(5) as added by 2007 Tenn. S.B. 2223, §75, effective Jan. 1, 2008, and amended by 2019 Tenn. H.B. 931, effective April 12, 2019 (extending Tennessee's compliance with SSUTA until July 1, 2021), and 2019 Tenn. H.B. 84, effective April 18, 2019; Tennessee Notice No. 19-12 (June 2019). BNA-SUTN TN 29.13.

Topic: Transportation Property, Fuel, Services Car Detailing

Depends Purchases of detailing services and repair services performed on motor vehicles held for resale are exempt from tax, if such vehicles are held for resale by a licensed motor vehicle dealer or licensed automobile auction. Other detailing services are likely subject to sales and use tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-392; Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1320-5-1-.54(2). “Detailing service”is defined as “services that cosmetically or functionally refurbish or restore to like-new or serviceable condition or appearance and that are intended to enhance or increase the sales value of used or pre-owned motor vehicles in preparation for the vehicles being offered for sale at wholesale or retail in the ordinary course of the seller's business, or that cosmetically or functionally prepare new vehicles for sale at wholesale or retail in the ordinary course of the seller's business.” Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-392. Detailing services does not include washing and or cleaning services of motor vehicles which are subject to tax. Tenn. Code Ann. §67-6-392. BNA-SUTN TN 29.14.
