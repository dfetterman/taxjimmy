State: Oklahoma



Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Membership Status

Member Oklahoma was admitted as a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement on July 1, 2005. See 2003 Okla. S.B. 708, effective Nov. 1, 2003. As a full member, Oklahoma maintains a seat on the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board and may vote on amendments to and interpretations of the Agreement. See 2003 Okla. S.B. 708; State Guide to the Streamlined Sales Tax Project (March 1, 2019). On June 5, 2003, Oklahoma enacted 2003 Okla. S.B. 708, bringing Oklahoma into compliance with the Agreement. See 2003 Okla. S.B. 708, effective Nov. 1, 2003. As part of its membership, the Oklahoma Tax Commission has completed a taxability matrix to verify the application of tax-related statutory terms defined in the Agreement. Sellers and certified service providers shall be relieved from liability for having charged and collected the incorrect amount of sales or use tax resulting from the seller's or certified service provider's relying on erroneous data provided in the taxability matrix. Topic: Interstate Agreements Streamlined Sales Tax Conformity to Agreement

In Compliance As a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement for which the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board has not issued a notice of noncompliance, Oklahoma is considered to be in full conformity with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Oklahoma Certificate of Compliance (version 2018.0). On Oct. 25, 2017, the Compliance Review and Interpretations Committee issued a report finding that Oklahoma is not out-of-compliance with the Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2017 Compliance Review Report (Oct. 25, 2017); see also Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, 2016 Compliance Review Report (Nov. 22, 2016). BNA-SUTN OK 2.1.2.
