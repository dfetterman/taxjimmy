State: New Jersey



Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Sales Tax

Yes In New Jersey, sales tax is imposed upon the receipts from every retail sale of tangible personal property or digital property and every sale of enumerated services. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-3. The purchaser of taxable property or services is liable for the payment of sales tax on property or services purchased. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-12(a); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-14. Generally, the seller is responsible for collecting the sales tax and remitting it to the Division of Taxation. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-12(a); N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(w). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.1.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Use Tax

Yes A use tax is imposed on items acquired for use in New Jersey on which a sales tax would be due, but has not been paid. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-6; New Jersey Sales Tax Publication ANJ-7. If taxable goods or services are purchased out-of-state for use in New Jersey, and no sales tax is collected at the time of purchase, then the purchaser owes New Jersey use tax. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-6; New Jersey Sales Tax Publication ANJ-7. If taxable goods or services are purchased out-of-state for use in New Jersey, and sales tax is collected but at a lower rate than applicable in New Jersey, then the purchaser also owes use tax. The amount of sales tax paid to the other state will be credited against the amount due to New Jersey. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-6; New Jersey Sales Tax Publication ANJ-7. Use tax is further due on inventory purchased under a resale certificate that is then used by the purchaser (e.g., removed from inventory and given away as part of a promotion. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-6; New Jersey Sales Tax Publication ANJ-7. BNA-SUTN NJ 1.1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Local Option Sales and Use Tax

Local Tax Authorized New Jersey law authorizes fourth class cities to levy and collect taxes on retail sales or sales at retail. N.J. Rev. Stat. §40:48-8.15. Fourth class cities are cities bordering on the Atlantic Ocean which are seaside or summer resorts. N.J. Rev. Stat. §40A:6-4. The fourth class cities retail sales tax effectively codifies the Atlantic City luxury tax. N.J. Rev. Stat. §40:48-8.15; N.J. Admin. Code tit. 18, §25-1.4, as amended by 50 N.J. Reg. issue 22, Docket No. 2341(b) (Nov. 19, 2018), effective Oct. 24, 2018; New Jersey Sales Tax Publication ANJ-17. Cape May County imposes a 2-percent tourism sales tax in addition to the state sales tax, for a combined rate of 9 percent. The tourism tax applies to predominantly tourism related sales, defined to include hotel, motel, and boarding house lodging;food and drink sold by restaurants, taverns and similar establishments for consumption on and off premises; and admission charges to places of amusement. N.J. Rev. Stat. §40:54D-4; New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Bulletin SU-13; New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Bulletin SU-11; New Jersey Technical Bulletin TB-23; ST-375, Sales Tax Collection Schedule. In addition, New Jersey has enacted the “Sports and Entertainment District Urban Revitalization Act.” N.J. Rev. Stat. §34:1B-190. This act authorizes any eligible New Jersey municipality to establish a sports and entertainment district within the municipality and impose a sales tax, without a corresponding use tax, to finance revitalization and development of sports and entertainment facilities. N.J. Rev. Stat. §34:1B-194. For purposes of this tax, an “eligible municipality"is defined as a municipality:• that is part of an urban enterprise zone, which is a zone in a municipality subject to revitalization, designated as an urban enterprise zone by the enterprise zone authority, and that is subject to a zone wide exemption for 50 percent of the sales tax imposed under the Sales and Use Tax Act;and • has a population greater than 25,000 and less than 29,000 in a third class county with a population density greater than 295 and less than 304 persons per square mile. N.J. Rev. Stat. §34:1B-192; N.J. Rev. Stat. §52:27H-60; N.J. Rev. Stat. §52:27H-62(a); N.J. Rev. Stat. §52:27H-66.7(a). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax General Rule - Imposition of Tax Excise and Replacement Taxes

Yes Excise Taxes New Jersey imposes several excise taxes that function in a manner similar to the state's transactional sales and use tax. Examples of such taxes include:• an excise tax on alcoholic beverages; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:43-1. • an excise tax on food and drinks; N.J. Rev. Stat. §34:1B-194(a)(2). and • a hotel and motel occupancy fee. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32D-1. For more information on New Jersey excise taxes, see the Excise Taxes Navigator, beginning at New Jersey 1. Replacement Taxes The Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement prohibits member states from imposing a “prohibited replacement tax” which is a tax outside of the sales and use tax that is imposed on products defined in the Agreement. Generally, the Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board will make a finding regarding whether or not a member state has imposed a replacement tax. Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018, Section 334; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board Rules and Procedures, as amended through May 3, 2018. New Jersey is a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement. Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Streamlined Sales Tax State Members; Streamlined Sales Tax Governing Board, Taxability Matrix, New Jersey (version 2018.0). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.1.4.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Historical Information

Est. 1935 New Jersey first imposed a general sales tax in 1935. That year, the tax rate was 2 percent. You're 40: Now Get to Work—Making the State Sales Tax Pull its Weight, New Jersey Policy Perspective, June 2006. In 1938, the state's sales tax lapsed, and was not enacted again until 1966. The sales tax rate in 1966 was 3 percent. N.J. Rev. Stat. § 54:32B-3; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-6; You're 40: Now Get to Work—Making the State Sales Tax Pull its Weight, New Jersey Policy Perspective, June 2006. Effective January 1, 2017 through December 31, 2017, the rate is 6.875 percent. Effective January 1, 2018 and thereafter, New Jersey's sales and use tax rate is 6.625 percent. 2016 N.J. A. 12, effective October 5, 2016; New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Rate Change Notice Effective Jan. 1, 2017 (Feb. 24, 2017). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Pending and Trending Developments

Economic Nexus On June 21, 2018, the very day that the U.S. Supreme Court issued its decision in South Dakota v. Wayfair, Inc., New Jersey's legislature introduced A. 4261 and S. 2794, identical bills providing for the collection of sales tax from remote sellers who either make sales of $100,000 into the state or separate 200 transactions. The bill also includes a marketplace facilitator provision. It passed both houses on July 1, 2018, but has not yet been signed into law. See 2018 N.J. A. 4261. Tax Rate New Jersey's sales and use tax will be reduced in two phases between 2017 and 2018. Effective Jan. 1, 2017, the rate decreased from 7 percent to 6.875 percent, and further decreased to 6.625 percent on Jan. 1, 2018. N.J. Dept. of Treas., 2017 Tax Collection Schedule; New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Rate Change Notice Effective Jan. 1, 2017 (Feb. 24, 2017). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Sale' and 'Retail Sale'

Defined “Retail sale” means any sale, lease, or rental for any purpose other than resale, sublease, or subrent. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(e). “Retail sale”also includes sales of tangible personal property to all contractors, subcontractors or repairmen of materials and supplies for use by them in erecting structures for others, or building on, or otherwise improving, altering or repairing real property of others. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(e)(2). The term “retail sale” does not include the following:• professional, insurance, or personal service transactions involving the transfer of tangible personal property as an inconsequential element, for which no separate charges are made; • the transfer of tangible personal property to a corporation, solely in consideration for the issuance of its stock, pursuant to a merger or consolidation; • the distribution of property by a corporation to its stockholders as a liquidating dividend; • the distribution of property by a partnership to its partners in whole or partial liquidation; • the transfer of property to a corporation upon its organization in consideration for the issuance of its stock; • the contribution of property to a partnership in consideration for a partnership interest in the partnership; or • the sale of tangible personal property where the purchaser's reason for purchasing the property is to hold it as security for the performance of an obligation of the seller. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(e)(4). Additionally, the New Jersey Division of Taxation has determined that there is no retail sale of tangible property when a contribution is made through a crowd-funding website. New Jersey State Tax News Winter 2014. BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.1.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Lease'

Defined The terms “lease” and “rental” are used interchangeably to mean any transfer of possession or control of tangible personal property for a fixed or indeterminate term for consideration. A lease or rental may include future options to purchase or extend. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa). “Lease” or “rental”does not include:• a transfer of possession or control of property under a security agreement or deferred payment plan that requires the transfer of title upon completion of the required payments; • a transfer of possession or control of property under an agreement that requires the transfer of title upon completion of required payments and payment of an option price does not exceed the greater of $100 or one percent of the total required payments; or • providing tangible personal property or a specified digital product along with an operator for a fixed or indeterminate period of time. A condition of this exclusion is that the operator is necessary for the equipment to perform as designed and the operator must do more than maintain, inspect, or set-up the tangible personal property or specified digital product. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa)(1). However, lease or rental does include agreements covering motor vehicles and trailers where the amount of consideration may be increased or decreased by reference to the amount realized upon sale or disposition of the property as defined in I.R.C. §7701(h)(1). N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa)(2). New Jersey, as a full member of the Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, has adopted the definition of lease found in the Agreement. Streamlined Sales and Use Tax Agreement, as amended through Dec. 14, 2018; N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(aa). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.2.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Use' or 'Storage, Use, or Consumption'

Defined New Jersey statutes define “use” to mean the exercise of any right or power over tangible personal property, specified digital products, services to property or products, or services by the purchaser of such property or products. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(h). “Use” includes, but is not limited to, the receiving, storage or any keeping or retention for any length of time, withdrawal from storage, any distribution, any installation, any affixation to real or personal property, or any consumption of such property or products. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(h). The definition of “use” also includes the exercise of any right or power over intrastate or interstate telecommunications and prepaid calling services and the exercise of any right or power over utility service. It also includes the derivation of a direct or indirect benefit from a service. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(h). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.3.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Tangible Personal Property'

Defined In New Jersey, “tangible personal property” means personal property that can be seen, weighed, measured, felt, or touched, or that is in any other manner perceptible to the senses. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(g). “Tangible personal property” includes electricity, water, gas, steam, and prewritten computer software, including prewritten software delivered electronically. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(g). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.4.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Intangible Property'

Not Defined New Jersey's sales and use tax statutes do not define “intangible property.” N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2. BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.5.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Retailer' or 'Vendor'

Defined New Jersey defines “seller” to mean a person making sales, leases, or rentals of personal property or services. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(i). The term seller includes the following:• a person making sales, leases or rentals of tangible personal property, specified digital products or services that are subject to sales or use tax; • a person maintaining a place of business in New Jersey or having an agent maintaining a place of business in New Jersey and making sales, whether at such place of business or elsewhere, to persons within New Jersey of tangible personal property, specified digital products or services, the use of which is subject to tax; • a person who solicits business either by employees, independent contractors, agents or other representatives (including via link on an internet website) or by distribution of catalogs or other advertising matter and by reason thereof makes sales to persons within New Jersey of tangible personal property, specified digital products or services, the use of which is subject to tax; • any other person making sales to persons within New Jersey of tangible personal property, specified digital products or services, the use of which is subject to tax, who may be authorized by the Director of the Division of Taxation to collect the tax imposed; • New Jersey, any of its agencies, instrumentalities, public authorities, public corporations (including a public corporation created pursuant to agreement or compact with another state) or political subdivisions when such entity sells services or property of a kind ordinarily sold by private persons; • a person who sells, stores, delivers or transports energy to users or customers in New Jersey whether by mains, lines or pipes located within New Jersey or by any other means of delivery; • a person engaged in collecting charges in the nature of initiation fees, membership fees or dues for access to or use of the property or facilities of a health and fitness, athletic, sporting or shopping club or organization;and • a person engaged in the business of parking, storing or garaging motor vehicles. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(i)(1). See New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Bulletin SU-5; New Jersey Sales and Use Tax Bulletin SU-12 (seller includes anyone purchasing tangible personal property for lease or rent, whether in New Jersey or elsewhere). In addition, the Director of the Division of Taxation, may treat any salesman, representative, peddler, or canvasser as the agent of the seller, and hold such person jointly responsible with the seller for the collection and payment of tax due, when in his discretion, the Director finds it necessary for the efficient administration of the sales and use tax statutes. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(i)(2). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.6.

Topic: Imposition of Tax Key Definitions 'Purchaser' or 'Consumer'

Defined New Jersey defines “purchaser” as a person to whom a sale of personal property is made or to whom a service is furnished. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(c). New Jersey also defines “customer”to include every purchaser of tangible personal property, specified digital goods, or services; every patron paying or liable for the payment of any amusement charge; every occupant of a room or rooms in a hotel; every person paying charges in the nature of initiation fees, membership fees or dues for access to or use of the property or facilities of a health and fitness, athletic, sporting or shopping club or organization; and every purchaser of parking, storage or garaging a motor vehicle. N.J. Rev. Stat. §54:32B-2(x). BNA-SUTN NJ 1.4.7.
